Aston Villa 3-0 Reading: Bruce's side boost play-off hopes with win

Steve Bruce has an array of attacking talent to call on. And then there is Birkir Bjarnason.

On a night when Villa were under all sorts of pressure to overcome their recent crisis, a collectors’ item goal from their Icelandic midfielder set them on their way.

Established scorers Conor Hourihane and Scott Hogan made sure normal service was resumed against ten-man Reading, who had David Edwards sent off in the first half for two bookings. 

Birkir Bjarnason is delighted after his goal put Aston Villa ahead just after the break

Conor Hourihane was on target as he doubled Villa's advantage to boost their play-off hopes

So, crisis over. It remains to be seen whether Villa still have sufficient time to relaunch their bid for automatic promotion, but at least they have remembered how to win and how to score goals.

Blips are all about the timing and Villa could not have chosen a more destructive period than the promotion run in. 

From being in with a chance of top two to probably having to settle for a top-six place puts the season into a different perspective. 

Bjarnason's effort flew past a host of Reading defender to bring Villa Park to its feet

Eight points had vaporised in the last three matches which invited the Villa sceptics to start vocalising their fears.

Manager Bruce has seen it all before, which is why he will not be sucked in by the moans and groans. 

However, his reaction to the 0-0 draw at Hull on Saturday was to send a clear message to the under-performers by making five changes. 

Hogan is congratulated by team-mate Josh Onomah after making it 3-0 to Villa

Reading's David Edwards expresses his frustration after he was sent off in the first half

Edwards was dismissed for a second bookable offence after he caught Birkir Bjarnason

Villa are always better when Jack Grealish is in the right mood. He has vision, pace and the ability to pull strings. He might even have put the home side into an early lead with a volley from Albert Adomah’s cross but the shot was wayward.

Still, it was a positive start as Villa took early control with Grealish darting and dangerous. 

Their cause was helped by a red card for Edwards, for so long a loyal servant at a Wolves and probably the last person on the pitch to commit two rash tackles within quarter of an hour. 

Albert Adomah tries his luck on goal but Villa, despite their best efforts, couldn't find the net

Referee Darren Bond brandished the first yellow for a foul on Grealish and then had no choice but to reach for his cards again as a horribly mistimed challenge sliced into Bjarnason.

Edwards looked stunned and walked at snail’s pace to the tunnel. Paul Clement’s defensive Plan A would have to become an even more defensive Plan B. 

Vito Mannone in the Reading goal had to deal with long-range efforts from Hourihane and Robert Snodgrass which were really only routine. 

The goalkeeper also denied Hogan and caught a curling effort from Grealish. Somehow the visitors got to half-time without conceding. 

The question was whether Villa could maintain that momentum and stay patient. The answer was yes. 

Right at the start of the second half Snodgrass twisted and turned before finding Bjarnason and his finish was sublime. 

Robert Snodgrass, on loan from West Ham, goes for goal but is unable to score

Snodgrass was the provider for the second as well, chipping into the area for Hourihane to head past Mannone. 

Then Hogan took advantage of the keeper going walkabout to make it three. Two fabulous tackles by John Terry deserve mention and the arrival of fit-again Jonathan Kodjia was also a bonus.

There was a 29,000 plus crowd at Villa Park and, for once, there would have been no complaints.
