Barcelona’s own-goal luck: CHAMPIONS LEAGUE – THINGS YOU MISSED

Liverpool tore apart Manchester City at Anfield, winning 3-0 to seize control of their quarter-final tie ahead of the second leg at the Etihad.

Three goals inside the first 30 minutes from Mohamed Salah, Alex Oxalde-Chamberlain and Sadio Mane gave Jurgen Klopp’s men a comprehensive early lead. Pep Guardiola’s side offered little in the way of an attacking threat in the second half, giving Liverpool a commanding victory and a clean sheet to take to Manchester.

Barcelona all-but secured their place in the semi-finals with a dominant victory against Roma at the Nou Camp but were helped out somewhat by their Italian opponents, who scored two own goals. 

Gabriel Jesus endured a torrid time at Anfield as he struggled to impact the quarter-final

Here, Sportsmail brings you a round-up of all the things you may have missed from Wednesday evening's Champions League quarter-final first-leg matches.

Kick-off delay

The mouth-watering tie between Liverpool and Man City is no ordinary fixture. A Champions League quarter-final at Anfield against a domestic rival is no small matter, and fans marked the occasion prior to kick-off with flares and fireworks as the City players arrived.

Those celebrations spilled over to kick-off too, as the start time between the Premier League’s two highest-scoring teams was delayed for around a minute because of fireworks.

Scenes outside Anfield saw Liverpool's clash with Manchester City delayed by one minute

Messi reaches 100

Cristiano Ronaldo’s incredible bicycle kick against Juventus reminded us of his unrivalled achievements in the Champions League. 

The Portuguese forward has 119 goals, but Messi isn’t far behind him and is also the only other player to score a century of goals in European football’s premier competition.

Ahead of kick-off against Roma, Carles Puyol presented Messi with a plaque to commend the Argentine on reaching 100 goals in the Champions League - which he managed against Chelsea in the previous round.

Lionel Messi was handed a plaque celebrating him reaching 100 goals in the competition

Messi got a hug from his former team-mate Carles Puyol after being presented with the plaque

Sir Alex checks in

Sir Alex Ferguson watching a Champions League quarter-final involving Manchester City, and not, United, won’t make for natural viewing.

During his time in-charge at Old Trafford, any big European night involving a Manchester side involved him on the touchline, but since retiring, the dynamics have changed somewhat and Pep Guardiola’s side are now the top dogs in English football.

Spotted in the stands at Anfield, the Manchester United legend may have been torn over who to support – the ‘noisy neighbour’ or their long-standing Premier League rivals?

Legendary ex-Manchester United manager Sir Alex Ferguson (right) watched on at Anfield

Milner king of the assists

The leading assister in this season’s Champions League has seven assists, three more than any other player. It may come as a surprise to some that the player in question is none other than James Milner.

In doing so, Milner sits alongside some elite company when it comes to laying on goals in Europe. The only other players with at least seven assists in a Champions League season are Neymar (2016-17) and Zlatan Ibrahimovic (2012-13).

The retired England international set up Alex Oxalde-Chamberlain’s long-range rocket for Liverpool’s second goal, helping the Reds reach another milestone. Klopp’s side have now scored 30 Champions League goals this season, beating their previous record of 29 from the 2007-08 campaign.

James Milner is in elite company when it comes to laying on assists in the Champions League

Gabriel Jesus’ quiet evening

An injury to Sergio Aguero days before this clash meant Gabriel Jesus was certainly going to start at Anfield, but the Brazilian failed to make the most of the occasion with an extremely quiet performance.

Jesus’ heat map tells you all you need to know. The striker failed to register a single shot on target, was booked and had just six passes in the first half, with three of those coming at kick-off.

Jesus' heat map shows he passed the ball just six times in the first half - three from kick-off

Barca’s luck continues

It comes as no surprise that Lionel Messi is Barcelona’s top scorer in the Champions League this season with six strikes in 2017-18 so far.

Their next highest-scorer has five — own goals. Two more from Roma at the Nou Camp helped Barca reach that figure. It’s just the fourth time in competition history that a side have scored two own goals in the same match.

One more gift from the opposition will mean Barcelona’s joint-top scorer in Europe would be Messi and six unlucky incidents from opposition players.
