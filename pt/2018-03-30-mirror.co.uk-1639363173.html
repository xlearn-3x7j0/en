Liverpool handed boost over transfer target Max Meyer

Liverpool have been handed a boost in their pursuit of Schalke midfielder Max Meyer.

The German has attracted interest from Liverpool and Arsenal with his contract expiring this summer.

Talks over a new deal have stalled and now Schalke have confirmed they are preparing for him to depart.

"At the moment we can and must expect that Max leaves the club in the summer. Everything suggests that," sporting director Christian Heidel said.

"At the moment there is no offer from us and no wish from Max to have a conversation again."

Roma name price for Liverpool target Alisson

AS Roma chiefs have named their price for Liverpool's Brazilian target Alisson - and it's not the quoted £80million, writes Mirror Football's Liam Prenderville.

The highly-rated keeper has long been rated by Jurgen Klopp - who know he'll have to break the transfer record for a keeper to land him.

According to Corriere Dello Sport President James Pallota has instructed the Italians not to even consider selling the keeper to Real Madrid, Liverpool or anyone else for less than €60 million.

Pallota said about clubs raiding Roma for players: "I feel frustrated when people say that Rome is a supermarket, things are not simple when a footballer moves.

He then added about the Brazilian: "I've been a Alisson fan since the first day he was with us and I'm glad he's finally succeeded in show how good he is."

But Italian sources are sure it will cost Liverpool around £52m to prise him away.

Club legend Ian Rush picks Liverpool's main man - and it's not Mohamed Salah

Roberto Firmino, not Mohamed Salah is Liverpool’s main man, according to Reds legend Ian Rush, writes Mirror Football's David Anderson.

Salah may be Liverpool’s top scorer with 36 goals this season, but Rush claims Firmino is the star the team can rely on most.

Rush is impressed by how Firmino leads Liverpool’s high press in addition to his 23 goals and 10- assists this campaign.

“Everyone knows how amazing Mohamed Salah is, but if you’re looking at one player Liverpool rely on it’s Bobby Firmino,” said the former hitman.

“With his closing down he reminds me a little bit of myself. Not only does he work hard for the team, he’s also very skilful. He does everything.

“He may not be regularly getting 30 goals a season, but he makes the opposition make mistakes.

“He’s a rare breed of a Brazilian and I think his talents suit the Premier League down to a T.

“He’s tireless, every manager would love a player like that in his team. We are very, very lucky that we have him in our side.”
