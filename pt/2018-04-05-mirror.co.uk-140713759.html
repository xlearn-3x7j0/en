Chelsea announce plans to celebrate Ray Wilkins as John Terry pays own tribute

Chelsea will celebrate Ray Wilkins’ life and career with a number of tributes ahead of their game with West Ham on Sunday.

Fans at Stamford Bridge will get their chance to pay their respects to the legend who began his career at the club and had two spells as assistant manager there.

Wilkins, aged 61, passed away on Wednesday.

Chelsea have been working - ever since learning of the news - on the best ways in which to give their former midfielder the best send-off.

The club will stage a minute’s applause before kick-off while the players will wear black armbands.

The official programme will pay homage to Wilkins and his image, while the club montage of his career is going to be shown on the big screens inside the stadium.

Flags are already being flown at half-mast at Chelsea’s Cobham training ground. The Blues’ Under-18’s wore black armbands during their 3-0 win at Birmingham in the FA Youth Cup semi-final on Tuesday night. A minute’s applause was also held.

Former Spurs midfielder Graham Roberts, who played with Wilkins for England, said: “You could always see the love and respect that people throughout the game and across the clubs had for him.

“He was just so kind. Nothing was ever too much trouble. My thoughts are with his wonderful family at this time.”

Blues legend John Terry returned to Stamford Bridge on Thursday, as he paid his own respects to Wilkins.

Terry, who captained Chelsea to the Premier League title when Wilkins was assistant manager to Carlo Ancelotti, remarked on social media: "At Stamford Bridge today paying my respects to the great man Ray Wilkins @chelseafc. I placed my captains armband on one of Rays pictures as a mark of respect. Ray was our youngest ever permanent captain at 18 ©️LEGEND"

Ex-QPR midfielder Nigel Quashie, handed his senior debut by Wilkins, has also paid his own tribute with a heartwarming tale.

"I will always be proud to say how lucky I am to have been coached, managed and even meet you," the former Portsmouth and Southampton star wrote on his Instagram page.

"If it wasn’t for you I would never have started my career playing football and will never forget going to Manchester United as a YTS trainee to do all the kit and boots in the changing room for the first team squad.

"1hr 30 mins before kick off you name the team and I was in it and that’s how my debut went, I didn’t even get to think about it because all you said was your number 18, here are your boots and shin pads , just go and play, have fun and treat it like your over the park with your mates.

"My mum never had a mobile phone back then because we couldn’t afford anything like that and everything went through her work or home phone.

"I asked you after the game if I could call home to speak to her, you said use my mobile and call this number, it was my mum on the other end of the line and she said she was outside by the away team bus, I went outside to see her and asked how did you get here because I knew she couldn’t afford it, she told me Ray had got the club to pay for her train ticket to come to the game and then told me Ray got the club to give us our first mobile phone in life during the week and he wanted to surprise me knowing I was making my debut.

"I was given a chance by Ray Wilkins one of the legends of the game and to this day you are the first person I mention when it comes to football and that will never change. You will be missed so much not just in the football world but as a real human being with such class.

"Thanks for everything and my thoughts go to all the family. Sleep well Sir Ray Wilkins."
