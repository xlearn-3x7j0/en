Perez Hilton worries dances class will make his son gay

Perez Hilton says he won't sign his five-year-old son up for dance class because it might make him gay, and he'd 'prefer' him to be straight.

The openly gay showbiz commentator candidly confessed he wanted his young boy Mario to be heterosexual because it's an 'easier' life. 

'I would prefer my son to be heterosexual because that's easier,' he said in a video on his website on Sunday.

Hilton said he wasn't ashamed of being gay, but being homosexual or transgender in the U.S. was still 'a harder road to travel on'.

'We're still discriminated against, and I don't want my son or daughters [Mia, two, and Mayte, five months] to have a harder path in life,' he said.

The 40-year-old said he wouldn't change his sexuality today, but if given a choice when he was a teenager he'd have chosen to be straight as it was hard growing up.

'If my son turns out to be gay, or if he turns out to be gay but not able to articulate that yet because he's only five, I will love my son unconditionally and support him,' he said.

'If my son is transgender, I will love him and support him unconditionally.'

The controversy stemmed from Hilton saying in a podcast last week that he wouldn't enroll Mario in dance class because it might make him gay.

By Monday's video (pictured) he appeared to have an epiphany: 'Maybe there are so many gay dancers because they were already gay going into dance class and dance just spoke to them'

Hilton pictured with his mother Teresita Lavandeira, son Mario, and two-year-old daughter Mia

Defending his comments on Sunday, he said at least half of boys who took dance classes turned out to be gay, and he didn't think that view was homophobic. 

'Just like I don't think it's a homophobic thing to say that a disproportionate amount of male singer/actor/dancers on Broadway are gay,' he said.

'There are more gay men drawn to that profession. That's not homophobic, that's just a fact.'

Hilton said his hypothetical ban on dance classes, which his son hadn't asked to take, was just about protecting him.

'I may be digging my grave even further here, but every decision I make for my son is what I think is best for his future. I don't want to do anything that could potentially hurt him,' he said.

'I said what I said because I know I'm a good dad.'

However, he conceded that: 'If he begged and pleaded, if I saw that would make him truly happy, I would do that.'

Hilton, seeming less sure of himself than usual, then admitted he was still processing it all and his views may change 'down the line'.

By Monday he appeared to have an epiphany as he briefly commented on the online furore his comments kicked up, as part of his celebrity bulletin.

'Maybe there are so many gay dancers because they were already gay going into dance class and dance just spoke to them,' he said.

However, he said Mario hadn't expressed interest in taking dance classes and Hilton wasn't going to volunteer him for it without such interest.
