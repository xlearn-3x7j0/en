New Zealand 124-4 at lunch on day 5, 2nd test vs England

CHRISTCHURCH, New Zealand (AP) Stuart Broad took two wickets with the first two balls of the final day to catapult England toward victory in the second cricket test against New Zealand.

Set 382 to win after England declared its second innings at 352-9, New Zealand had seen off the threat of the new ball to begin the fifth day at 42-0. But its hopes of winning or even drawing the match to complete its first series victory over England at home crumbled when Broad dismissed Jeet Raval (17) and Kane Williamson (0) with his first two deliveries Tuesday.

Left-armer spinner Jack Leach claimed his first test wicket when he dismissed Ross Taylor (13) and James Anderson removed Henry Nicholls (13). At lunch, New Zealand was 124-4, its innings held together by a half century by Tom Latham, who was 65 not out, and wickekeeper B.J. Watling was 15.

Beginning the day with all 10 wickets still in hand, New Zealand had been confident of being able to save a test in which England has made most of the running and to clinch the series after winning the first test by an innings and 49 runs. But that confidence slumped with Broad’s opening salvo.

Raval, who had survived a tough test before stumps Monday, played in front of his pads at a delivery from Broad angled into leg stump and chipped a catch to Mark Stoneman at mid-wicket.

Williamson was undone first ball by a superb delivery from Broad which bounced and jagged off the seam. Williamson played back instinctively in defense and the ball grazed the outside edge on its way to wicketkeeper Jonny Bairstow, who claimed his sixth catch of the test.

As the senior remaining batsman, hope fell on Ross Taylor who appeared in good touch despite offering a chance to James Vince off Anderson at 6. He took boundaries from drives through long on and cover before sweeping at a ball from Leach and picking out Alastair Cook at short backward square.

Nicholls, treating with great respect deliveries from Leach which had begun to turn sharply out of footmarks, lost that discipline and chased a wide ball from Anderson to give Cook a catch at first slip.
