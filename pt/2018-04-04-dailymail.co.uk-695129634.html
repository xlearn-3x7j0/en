More snow could be headed towards the Northeast this weekend

The calendar may say its spring, but winter still has a firm grip on much of the Northeast region. 

According to a Wednesday weather report, New York and other areas in the northeast could see some more snow this weekend.

PIX11 meteorologist Byron Miranda says there are multiple weather systems headed towards the region, with the first expected to hit Thursday night and continue into Friday morning.

While this may be bad news for some, the current update calls for a coating or less with the northern interior region seeing up to two inches of snow. However, if the storms moves further south than expected the interior region should see mostly rain.

Friday, people in the Northeast could face a 'quick burst of snow' that could change into rain during the morning commute. 

Whatever snow does fall is expected to melt by Friday afternoon, much like it did earlier in the week when a mild wintry storm rolled through the area.

During that storm, Central Park saw 5.5 inches of snow blanket the area, making it the snowiest April day since 1982. 

Unfortunately, winter isn't quite ready to disappear for good just yet. There's a possibility that Monday and Tuesday will also see snow but it's a little too early to know how that storm will play out.

While many areas in the Northeast will be bracing for snow later this week, Minnesota will enter the weekend with frigid temperatures. 

WCCO meteorologist Matt Brickman said Minnesota could see record cold temperatures in the 30s starting Thursday and ending Sunday, CBS Minnesota reports. 

Earlier in the week, Minnesota suffered the heaviest April snowfall in 34 years. Records show that Minneapolis-St Paul International Airport measured nine inches of snow and the northeast area was hit with over 10 inches. 

Many other parts of the Twin Cities reported more than a half foot of snow.
