Impala is surrounded by wild dogs and a hippo

  • Impala caught between wild dog and hippo at Kruger National park, South Africa
  • Tourist catch rare  scene while out with guide Arun Rao of Legend Safaris
  •  Amazingly, hippo tries to save the impala calf by pushing it back to shore 

Published: 11:41 EDT, 3 April 2018 | Updated: 12:09 EDT, 3 April 2018

Gripping video footage shows the moment a heroic hippo attempts to save a lone impala calf from water, only for it to be eaten by a wild dog.

Safari guide Arun Rao, 53, of Legend Safaris was taking guests around when they spotted the unusual scene.

'We were out on a drive and there happened to be a pack of wild dogs running alongside us on the road, approximately 1km North of the waterhole,' he said. 

'The pack then ran off into the bushes and down to the waterhole where the safari guests noticed a lone impala.'

The young impala calf jumps into the watering hole to get away from the wild dog who is waiting at the water edge, left

The heroic hippo swims quietly beside the impala and gently nudges it along until it reaches the shore

The video starts as the jeep pulls up beside the watering hole and see the impala swimming in the hole to get away from the wild dog who is watching him from the water edge. 

We then see the hippo emerge on the surface and swim quietly beside it and gently nudges the impala along until it reaches the shore.

Unluckily, it was not a happy ending for the young impala as the dog started chasing him again once he reached the shallow water

Tragically, when the young calf reaches the shore the wild dog immediately starts chasing it again as the hippo seems to swim after them.

But it was too late and from the distance we see the unlucky antelope get caught in the jaws of the wild dog.
