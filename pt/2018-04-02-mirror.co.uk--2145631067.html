Will Ospreay could miss WrestleMania weekend after scary neck injury

British wrestler Will Ospreay could miss his WrestleMania weekend bookings after suffering a scary neck injury.

The IWGP Junior Heavyweight Champion took a sickening blow to the head during a match with fellow Brit, Marty Scurll.

The pair battled on the ring apron during a bout at New Japan Pro Wrestling's Sakura Genesis show in Tokyo on Sunday.

Fans at the Ryogoku Kokugikan winced as Ospreay tried to hit a Spanish Fly, a move often performed from the top rope.

The 24-year-old bashed his head on the apron as he rotated through the air and was busted open during the incident.

Ospreay, from Havering, London, continued the match and retained his title against Scurll, 29, from Littleport, Cambridgeshire.

But the Aerial Assassin then took to Twitter to say he might not be able to compete in New Orleans during WrestleMania week.

The high flyer said: "Hey guys I’m ok. But I might not be fit to compete for NOLA. I’ll update you once I know more."

Ospreay later added: "Back at my hotel now. Time can only tell. Thanks for the messages, can’t help but tell you how much I love you guys.

"P.S I could really do without the whole “I told you so” or “This is why”"

He later posted a picture of his bloodied face on Instagram, with the caption: "Elevate the Junior Championship. Or die trying."

If Ospreay does have to pull out of his bookings in Louisiana this week, that will cause problems for several companies.

He is set to face AR Fox at Evolve 102, Matt Riddle at the WWN Supershow and Dezmond Xavier at CZW’s WrestleCon show.

The in-demand talent is also scheduled to participate in two matches for PROGRESS Wrestling and in a six-man bout for Revolution Pro Wrestling, as they venture across from London to the States.
