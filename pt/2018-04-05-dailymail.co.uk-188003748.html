Jose Mourinho arrives at Lowry Hotel clutching protein supplements

  • Jose Mourinho and Manchester United are preparing for Saturday's derby clash
  • The manager arrived at Manchester's Lowry Hotel early on Wednesday evening
  • Manchester City can win the Premier League title with victory at home to United
  • Mourinho said City winning the Premier League title 'wasn't important to him'

Published: 19:27 EDT, 4 April 2018 | Updated: 20:23 EDT, 4 April 2018

Jose Mourinho set an example for his Manchester United players on Wednesday when he arrived at the Lowry Hotel clutching two tubs of protein supplements. 

Mourinho is infamous for demanding a remarkable level of hard work from his players to ensure they are in physically top condition to carry out his tactical plans.

And the United boss showed he doesn't neglect his own fitness goals as he was spotted carrying supplements into the Lowry Hotel ahead of Saturday's derby.

Jose Mourinho clutched two tubs of protein supplements as he arrives at the Lowry Hotel

JOSE MOURINHO'S PROTEIN 

Mourinho was clutching a tub of Branched Chain Amino Acids (BCAAs) by Science in Sport as he walked into the Lowry Hotel. 

BCAAs contain eucine, isoleucine and valine, three of the 20 amino acids that the body needs to create protein and support muscle recovery. 

The United manager was also holding a container of what appeared to be Optimum Nutrition's Gold Standard Whey Protein. 

Whey protein isolates are the purest form of protein isolates and are the most popular supplement for those looking to aid muscle gain after exercise.

Mourinho was pictured with a tub of Science in Sport Branched Chain Amino Acids and a larger container of what appeared to be Optimum Nutrition Gold Standard Whey Protein.

The Portuguese boss is preparing for Saturday's match against City in which United can deny their local rivals the Premier League title. 

Pep Guardiola's side will clinch the Premier League with victory at the Etihad with City currently 16 points ahead of United with seven games remaining. 

Mourinho insisted City winning the title 'was not important' to him after United's 2-0 home victory over Swansea at the weekend. 

'Manchester City is not important for me,' Mourinho said. 'Important for me is that since the moment we left the first position and went to second, we stayed there for the whole of the season.

'We deserved to feel second in spite of what you can say, the critics you make, you all say the third, fourth, fifth, sixth are better than us but they are not better than us because we have more points than them.'

Mourinho insisted it 'was not important to him' if Manchester City win the title against United

City will be looking to bounce back from their chastening 3-0 loss to Liverpool in their all-Premier League Champions League quarter-final first leg. 

Guardiola's men have a mountain to climb ahead of next week's return leg at the Etihad and the City boss may be tempted to rest players for Saturday's visit of United to ensure his players are fresh for the Liverpool clash on Tuesday.

The latest rearranged TV fixtures sees United heading down to Brighton's Amex Stadium on Friday May 4 for a match which will be shown live on Sky Sports.
