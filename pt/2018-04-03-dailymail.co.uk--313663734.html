Cat Greenleaf files disability discrimination lawsuit against NBC

Emmy Award-winning talk show host Cat Greenleaf filed a disability discrimination lawsuit against NBC on Tuesday alleging the broadcasting company terminated her because she suffers clinical depression.

Greenleaf, 46, who interviewed a manifold of famous names from her Brooklyn brownstone on 'Talk Stoop,' was replaced by TV and radio personality, Nessa Diab, 33, this month.

The married mother-of-two was fired from her position back in December and now claims it was due to her emotional state.

Greenleaf claims she had confided in supervisors about the recent passing of a dear friend prior to her termination, according to the lawsuit.

Greenleaf, who spoke openly about her battle with depression since her childhood, informed supervisors she relapsed following her loved one's death.

She alleged in the disability discrimination suit her employer cited her termination was for 'not effectively communicating,' 'insubordination' and 'intimidation of co-workers'.

Bubble MTV personality, Diab, replaced Greenleaf for the hosting gig this month - and an NBC affiliate expressed the company's thrill about welcoming her.

Meredith McGinn, senior vice president of LX.TV Productions, a division of NBCU Owned Television Stations, gloated about Diab in a recent statement.

'Nessa's funny, honest and unfiltered personality is exactly what "Talk Stoop" is all about,' McGinn said of the MTV personality.

'When people are talking about something or making noise on social media, you can be sure that Nessa will ask the right questions to get audiences the answers they're looking for.

'We are thrilled that she's joining our "Talk Stoop" family and excited for fans everywhere to fall in love with her, just like we did.'

Diab is also known as the girlfriend of NFL free agent and racial justice activist, Colin Kaepernick. 

'Talk Stoop' first aired in 2009 and grew popular across the United States. Episodes broadcast in unconventional places like NYC cabs and gas stations.

The talk show currently has an audience of over 12million viewers each month.

The former host re-tweeted a message to her Twitter page after filing the lawsuit against NBC on Tuesday.

Greenleaf shared a tweet about depression that was posted by fellow host, Cooper Lawrence.

The message said: 'If you've never had to wait for the whole family to go to bed so you can privately fall apart, then you have no idea what real depression feels like.'
