Olympiacos president Marinakis fines team £350,000 - exiles players

  • Evangelos Marinakis took action after his side's  1-1 draw at Levadiakos
  • He is calling up players from U20 team to replace disappointing first teamers 
  • Greek owner slammed players for being to blame for sacking of three coaches 
  • He added: 'You think more about the nice houses you live in and your cars'

Published: 04:52 EDT, 3 April 2018 | Updated: 05:26 EDT, 3 April 2018

Olympiacos president Evangelos Marinakis has taken the remarkable step of sending under-performing stars into exile for the rest of the season.

The Greek shipping magnate has become so furious at his side's lacklustre displays that he's planning to call up players from the Under 20s for the remaining games. 

Marinakis, who also owns Nottingham Forest, went on an extraordinary rant condemning who he deemed as entitled performers. 

Olympiacos owner Evangelos Marinakis (right) is fuming with his team's performances

Olympiacos are currently third in the Greek league and first team players have been sent away

According to Greek media, the 50-year-old told the dressing room: 'I will build Olympiacos from the beginning and it will become the team we are dreaming of. 

'I and the rest of the fans have tolerated you enough. You will leave today and go on holiday.' 

'The amateur teams live with the basics, they love Olympiacos and their supporters much more than you do.

The president and owner has presided over periods of success but the team are struggling

'[You think more about] the nice houses you live in and your cars and don't care about the team.

'I pay millions for you to have everything. With your actions I fired three coaches. But at the end it looks like you were at fault.'

In addition to banishing first team players until next pre-season, Marinakis fined the squad a total of £350,000. 

There are three games remaining this season and Olympiacos lie third in the table, six points adrift of leaders AEK Athens. 

Marinakis is accustomed to success having overseen his hometown club win the Greek League title for seven successive seasons from 2011 to 2017, which perhaps explains his dramatic response to a fallow period.

In June 2015, Marinakis was banned from all football activity after being bailed for £174,000 over five charges.

They were: establishing and directing a criminal organisation, fraud, attempted extortion, bribery with the aim of fixing matches and instigating an explosion endangering human life.

The explosion in question came at the bakery owned by a referee who had been told what the result of an upcoming match should be. 

Nobody was injured but the referee, Petros Konstantineas, had his business destroyed. 

Marinakis was accused of being leader of a match-fixing ring known as 'The System' dating back to 2011. 

Last month 58 people were sentenced to jail terms ranging from 30 months to 10 years for their involvement but Marinakis was not among them.
