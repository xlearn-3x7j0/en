Terence Crutcher's family file wrongful death lawsuit against Tulsa 

The parents of Terence Crutcher have filed a wrongful death lawsuit against the City of Tulsa, Oklahoma. 

Crutcher's estate, representing parents Joey and Leanne Crutcher, is suing the Oklahoma city for the deadly September 16, 2016, killing by then Tulsa Police Officer Betty Shelby.  

Shelby was charged with first-degree manslaughter for Crutcher's death but was ultimately acquitted in May 2017. While resigning from the Tulsa Police Department, she has since become a deputy for the Rogers County Sheriff's Office.

Crutcher had his hands in their air and wasn't combative when he was shot- part of which was confirmed by police video taken from a dashboard camera and helicopter that showed Crutcher walking away from Shelby, hands held above his head. 

Shelby, who has said she does not regret what she did, claims that training warned her that when someone walks back to their vehicle and reaches inside, they 'can pull out guns and kill you.' 

'Everyone saw the video,' said Leanne Crutcher in a press conference on Wednesday, according to News 9. 'I will never get over that. Don't tell me to get over that.'  

The parents' attorney explained how his clients' son lived with them, went to church and cooked for his family. 

'Due to the City's negligence in hiring and training Shelby, Rev. Crutcher and Mrs. Crutcher will live the rest of their lives without their constant companion, star choir member, and loving son,' a release details.

Reverend Joey Crutcher also had a statement included in the proceedings. 

'Every night, my wife and I cry because we see our son with his hands up when we close our eyes. So, while we are frankly tired of all of the legal proceedings, we must continue to fight to hold those responsible for the murder of Terence accountable and to get some sort of justice for Terence,' he said. 

Crutcher's estate has filed a civil lawsuit against Shelby and Police Chief Chuck Jordan, already. 

They accuse Shelby of shooting Crutcher based on racial bias that has been promoted by the department. 

The family sought the additional wrongful death lawsuit because the civil rights lawsuit doesn't include claims for the parent's 'for their suffering, grief, and loss of companionship with Terence,' WJAC-TV reports. 

They've already detailed their annoyance that the police department attempted to demonize the fact that Crutcher was in possession of PCP to try to get over the fact that a gun wasn't found in his SUV.
