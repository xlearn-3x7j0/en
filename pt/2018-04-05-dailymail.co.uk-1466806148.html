Tour bus driver charged with DUI after flipping bus over in Georgia

  • Steven F Hoppenbrouwer was charged with a DUI after flipping a tour bus over
  • Hoppenbrouwer was driving 18 people to the Masters Tournament at the Augusta  National Golf Club Thursday morning when he flipped the bus on I-20
  • At least five passengers are reported to have suffered serious injuries 
  • Police charged Hoppenbrouwer with DUI and failure to maintain lane

Published: 12:36 EDT, 5 April 2018 | Updated: 13:07 EDT, 5 April 2018

A driver who flipped a tour bus over on a Georgia highway Thursday morning injuring at least a dozen people has been charged with driving under the influence.

Steven F Hoppenbrouwer, 61, was taking a group of 18 people to the Masters Tournament at the Augusta National Golf Club when the bus flipped over on I-20 in Columbia County around 8:45am. 

The accident caused the entire eastbound of the highway to close Thursday morning. 

A tour bus traveling to the Masters Tournament at the Augusta National Golf Club flipped over Thursday morning 

At least a dozen people were injured, with five suffering serious injuries. Police said 18 people were on the bus 

The driver of the bus, 61-year-old Steven F Hoppenbrouwer, was charged with DUI and failure to maintain lane 

Officials at Augusta University Medical Center told KRDW they were treating seven people involved in the crash. Five of them have serious injuries and two were in fair condition.

Doctors Hospital in Augusta also said an additional seven people were being treated at their facility.

Columbia County Sheriff's Office charged Hoppenbrouwer with driving under the influence and failure to maintain lane. 

They said he was traveling on the highway when he ran off the right side of the road and tried to overcorrect causing the bus to overturn in the median.

In a press release, authorities said the bus is owned by Jet Executive Limousine. It's not clear if Hoppenbrouwer is an employee with the company or had been contracted to drive the bus.
