Brownlees finish outside the medals in Commonwealth Games triathlon

  • Alistair and Jonny Brownlee both failed to make the podium in Gold Coast
  • Defending champion Alistair finished 10th, with Jonny coming home seventh
  • South Africa's Henri Schoeman dominated the race to claim gold medal
  • Scotland's Marc Austin claimed the bronze medal behind Jacob Birtwhistle 

Published: 01:18 EDT, 5 April 2018 | Updated: 01:18 EDT, 5 April 2018

The Brownlee brothers suffered a shock defeat as they failed to make the podium in the triathlon at the Commonwealth Games.

Defending champion Alistair fell out of contention at the start of the run, eventually coming 10th, while Jonny finished seventh in one of the brothers' worst days.

But Scotland's Marc Austin took the bronze medal behind gold medal winner Henri Schoeman of South Africa and Australia's Jacob Birtwhistle, who claimed silver.

It was a disappointing race for the Brownlee brothers Jonny (left) and Alistair as they finished seventh and 10th respectively at the Commonwealth Games in Australia

Jonny Brownlee (left) and his brother Alistair (right) both show disappointment at the finish

The two brothers were in firm contention entering the cycling phase but fell away in the run

Alistair Brownlee said of his race: 'About three quarters of it went alright. I knew I was going to struggle on the run and I was downplaying my chances. I've only run a handful of times in the last month or so.

'The last few weeks has been making sure the calf is alright to make sure I could get round properly. The swim and bike went well and I was just trying to pull that gap out as much as possible to give Jonny the best chance. It was a shame it didn't work out for him.'

There was gold medal delight for South Africa's Henri Schoeman on the Gold Coast

Australia's Jacob Birtwhistle (left) took silver with Scotland's Marc Austin (right) third

Austin claimed Scotland's first medal of the Commonwealth Games with a superb race

Jonny Brownlee, reflecting on a disappointing race for him, said: 'It was pretty terrible today. I didn't feel great. I've had ups and downs and I was injured in January so I missed a lot of training but I thought I was going to be better than that.

'I dived in and felt pretty weak on the swim and then I'm normally one of the strongest on the bike but I didn't feel great. Then I started running and thought 'oh dear, I'm in trouble here.''

Austin, savouring winning Scotland's first medal of the Games, said: 'I was obviously scared to dream about that kind of thing because it's way beyond what I've done before but I knew I was capable of it.'
