Romeo Beckham shows off tennis potential as he hits with Wozniacki

Romeo Beckham looked to prove his genuine potential as a budding young tennis player on Monday as he enjoyed a session with World No 2 Caroline Wozniacki.

While father and former England captain David Beckham would have been disappointed his son rejected football, Romeo's natural tennis ability and passion for the sport will please his father.

Wozniacki, fresh from her second-round defeat by Monica Puig at the Miami Open, became the latest pro to hit with Beckham's son, following on from Grigor Dimitrov and Andy Murray.

Beckham posted a short clip of their session on his Instagram account, writing: 'Fun hitting with @carowozniacki'.

He put a string of neat forehands together as he and the world's second best female tennis player exchanged shots over the net. He certainly handled himself well, even if Wozniacki was holding back ever so slightly. 

His ability, though, was impressive and is testament to the natural athletic prowess passed on to him by his father.

Beckham Jnr, whose Instagram account is flooded with videos and photos of him in action, has made a name for himself as a budding young star and has hit with some of the world's most elite stars.

Dimitrov and Murray shared a court with the 15-year-old, with the former praising the youngster as they traded shots before last year's Wimbledon.

The Bulgarian World No 11, who won four ATP tour titles last season including the season finale at the O2 arena in November and is a friend of Beckham Snr, followed up his session with Beckham claiming 'he's a very talented kid with a lot of potential'. 

Romeo's contacts in the tennis world - courtesy of his father admittedly - can only be beneficial for him, but it's the advice he is given that he will treasure the most. 

He's had all the exposure that every young, aspiring tennis player dreams of, and he will certainly hope to push on from here.
