Mkhitaryan swapped United for Arsenal as he missed attacking football

  • Henrikh Mkhitaryan swapped Manchester United to join Arsenal in January 
  • The Armenian reveals he did not have to think twice when offered the move 
  • He 'missed playing offensively' as he enjoys new attacking freedoms at Arsenal
  • Mkhitrayan also believes he was not brought in solely to replace Alexis Sanchez 

Published: 04:58 EDT, 5 April 2018 | Updated: 04:58 EDT, 5 April 2018

Henrikh Mkhitaryan revealed he had no hesitations in joining Arsenal because after a tumultuous 18-month spell at Manchester United, he missed playing attacking football. 

The Armenian playmaker departed Old Trafford in January with Alexis Sanchez heading in the opposite direction and he has started life well in London in a free role.

With the feeling split when he left the north west, Mkhitaryan looked to spark life into an ailing Arsenal side on his debut with three assists in the rout over Everton. 

Henrikh Mkhitaryan reveals he opted to join Arsenal as he began to miss offensive football

Mkhitaryan left Manchester United in January after struggling to get regular match time

But the Armenian has impressed so far at Arsenal and managed three assists on his debut

'When I heard I could swap Man United for Arsenal, I said, "Yes, I want to do that", I didn't think twice,' Mkhitaryan told FourFourTwo. 

'It's important for me to play in an offensive team. I couldn't have imagined a better start. I'd missed playing offensively.'

Despite the January move appearing to be a swap deal, Mkhitaryan made clear he feels he was not signed purely to replace Chile ace Alexis Sanchez and that Arsene Wenger wanted him regardless. 

'I joined Arsenal because (Arsene) Wenger wanted me, not because he wanted to replace (Alexis) Sanchez,' the Armenian continued. 

However, Mkhitaryan says he was not signed by the Gunners purely to replace Alexis Sanchez

'We are different players and characters, with different abilities and skills, so I'll try my best to do everything for the club.' 

With a place in the Premier League top four beyond Arsenal for a second successive season, Mkhitaryan and his team-mates are looking to go all the way in the Europa League.

It is a competition where the Armenian thrives having excelled for United on route to winning the competition against Ajax in Stockholm last season and he will hope for similar impact against CSKA Moscow on Thursday evening.
