'Abused' three-year-old girl dies while in the care of her stepfather

Behind the door of an apartment in Queens still decorated with cut-outs of Easter bunnies, police on Monday came upon a man performing CPR on his unconscious three-year-old stepdaughter, who later died.

Bella Edwards was rushed to St John's Episcopal Hospital in Far Rockaway, where it was determined that the toddler had signs of trauma consistent with physical abuse, according to the NYPD.

Edwards was left in the care of her stepfather, who was the only adult in the sixth-floor apartment. A three-month-old baby, believed to be Bella's brother, was also present and was later taken to a hospital for evaluation.

Bella' smother, Shamikaa Gonzalez, posted a heart-breaking status update on Facebook saying she was sorry that she was not there to protect her.

‘This feels like a bad dream I cant [sic]wake up from,’ the mother-of-two wrote. ‘You was loved by everyone and shared so many Funny memories. I'm going to miss all the times you kissed and hugged me everyday...'

NYPD officers responded to the apartment building on Beach 105th Street after getting a 911 call at 6pm from Bella's stepfather reporting an unresponsive child, reported New York Daily News.

The three-year-old passed away at the hospital an hour later. Sources tell New York Post Bella‘s lower extremities showed signs of trauma.

Bella's mother, Shamikaa Gonzalez, was not home. Both of her children (pictured) were left in the care of their stepdad

Gonzalez and Bella's stepfather were taken to the 100th Precinct for questioning, reported ABC7, but as of Tuesday morning, no charges have been filed in connection to the toddler's death.

The medical examiner will perform an autopsy on the girl to determine the cause and manner of death. NYPD’s Special Victims Unit is handling the case.

Friends and family will light candles and release pink and red balloons in Bella's honor on Wednesday evening.
