New study reveals the trickiest driving manoeuvre for UK motorists 

Motorists have had their say about what is the most difficult driving manoeuvre they face on the road.

Unsurprisingly it's one that fills the most confident of learners with dread, and even concerns some of the most experienced licence holders.

It is, of course, the parallel park - and some drivers admit they're willing to burn extra fuel just to avoid having to attempt to slot their car between two other vehicles.

But a top ten list of things drivers hate to do has also revealed some surprising results, with some even claiming that they find navigating a roundabout hard. 

Parallel parking problems: UK motorists said this was the most difficult driving manoeuvre they face every day

A poll of 2,000 UK drivers conducted by Accident Advice Helpline found that some would drive hundreds of metres to find a wider parking space in fear that they won't be able to prise their car into the gap under the gaze of other road users. 

According to the study, 47 per cent of drivers have at some point attempted a parallel park that went so disastrously wrong they simply gave up and drove on rather than giving it a second go. 

Some 15 per cent of respondents claim parallel parking leaves them feeling anxious, and one in 10 believe a difficult manoeuvre makes them feel stressed or nervous.

Around a quarter of the men surveyed said they feel confident when tackling a tough parking move, though just half of the female responded said the same. 

David Carter, director of marketing at Accident Advice Helpline, said the most hated task has been one of drivers' most common struggles for generations.

'Parallel parking has been a thorn in drivers’ sides since the invention of the motor car,' he said.

'We’ve all felt the pressure of getting into a tight parking space on a busy street when there are others watching.

'So it’s no surprise that twice as many people said they dread parallel parking as the next most difficult manoeuvre.'  

Almost half of motorists surveyed said at some point they've attempted to parallel park and driven off in embarrassment having failed to complete the manoeuvre

THE TOP 10 TRICKIEST DRIVING MANOEUVRES FOR BRITONS 

1. Parallel park

2. Reverse into a parking bay

3. Reversing around a corner

4. Turn-in-the-road/three point turn

5. Driving forward into a parking bay

6. Reversing in a straight line

7. Parking close to the curb

8. Navigating a roundabout

9. Emergency stop

10. Pulling up on the right of the road

Source: Accident Advice Helpline survey of 2,000 motorists

Parallel parking is just one of the requirements that might be called upon during a driving test

But it wasn't the only manoeuvre motorists confided that they struggle with.

Reversing into a parking bay, one of the new elements added to the driving test from 4 December 2017, with a quarter of motorists admitted they have let someone else back their vehicle into a vacant spot.

Reversing around a corner - which was omitted from the test at the same date reversing into a bay was added - was also high on the list of driving hates.

Other manoeuvres that cause consternation included pulling up on the right hand side of the road, driving forwards into a parking bay and even reversing in a straight line.

Women admitted they felt less confident about difficult parking actions than men did

Drivers are hunting for cars with reversing cameras to help them park with more ease

Unsurprisingly with almost the entire top 10 of most hated manoeuvres being a requirement of the driving test, either before or after last December's update, just under a third of motorists quizzed said they had failed attempts at getting their licence because of the intricate requirements.

In fact, almost one in 10 said they flunked the test multiple times because they couldn't park adequately during the examination. 

NEW DRIVING TEST CHANGES FROM 4 DECEMBER 2017

1. Independent driving part of the test will increase to 20 minutes

2. Drivers will need to follow directions from a sat nav

3. 'Reverse around a corner' and the three-point 'turn in the road' will be replaced by:

  • parallel park at the side of the road 
  • park in a bay - either driving in and reversing out, or reversing in and driving out (the examiner will tell you which you have to do) 
  • pull up on the right-hand side of the road, reverse for 2 car lengths and rejoin the traffic

4. Drivers will be asked to answer two vehicle safety questions while driving

Two in five UK drivers also think that manoeuvres are the hardest part of driving, beating out road awareness and knowing what all the signs mean.

With reversing being a commonly occurring theme of motorist struggles, it's unsurprising that drivers are now looking for models with rear-facing cameras to help them navigate into a space.

A quarter of drivers also said they wanted to have someone on standby to get out of the vehicle to help guide them into a tight spot. 

Carter added: 'The cliché of women being less confident drivers than men seems to have been borne out by this study, presuming that men answered honestly.

'Our results found this was particularly true when it comes to manoeuvres, with 56 per cent of women saying they struggled with them more than anything else, compared to 31 per cent of men.

'But of course, accidents can happen to anyone at any time, while a low speed bump when parking might not cause serious harm, accidents at roundabouts or when reversing around corners can be more dangerous.'
