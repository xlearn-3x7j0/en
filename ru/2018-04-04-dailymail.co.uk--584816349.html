Leicester set to welcome back three England players from injury

Leicester will go into the 'do-or-die' phase of their crusade to reach the Premiership play-offs for a 14th successive season buoyed by positive injury bulletins on three of their England backs.

Jonny May has come through his return-to-play protocols after being knocked out by a blow to the head against Wasps 10 days ago and the wing will play in 'The Clash' against Bath at Twickenham on Saturday. 

There is also a strong possibility that Manu Tuilagi could return to action before the end of the league campaign.

Tigers head coach Matt O'Connor is tasked with getting his side to a 14th successive play-off

The Tigers' head coach, Matt O'Connor, yesterday confirmed Sportsmail's story from last week, that the centre's chest damage is merely a pectoral strain, surgery is not required and he should be fit for selection again later this month. 

And long-term casualty Ben Youngs is expected to return to training this week, with the scrum-half on course for a comeback against Northampton on April 14.

These are the positive developments Leicester sorely need as they seek to maintain their remarkable sequence of top-four finishes in the English elite division. With that record comes pride and sharp-end experience, but also the pressure of expectation.

'We have to keep winning and that's the only thing that's important to us,' said O'Connor. 'If we win all our remaining games, we'll probably be in the top four, if we don't, we probably won't - it's that simple. 

'The guys understand the stakes are high. We won't be putting a ceiling or an end-point on it. We will look at it as if we have potentially six games to go.' 

Asked about Saturday's opponents at HQ - eighth-placed Bath, he added: 'They will bring everything because it's do-or-die for them, as it is for us. It's going to be an intense occasion.'

Scarlets head coach Wayne Pivac, the Kiwi who has guided the region to a Champions Cup semi-final on the back of their Pro12 title success last season, has revealed that he has held informal talks with the Welsh Rugby Union about succeeding his compatriot, Warren Gatland, as national coach. 

Pivac also confirmed that George North will not be returning to Llanelli when he leaves Northampton at the end of this season.

Saracens owner Nigel Wray has dismissed reports that the club are up for sale as 'rubbish', after reclaiming a 100 per cent stake by buying out the shares owned by South African billionaire Johann Rupert's company, Remgro, for an undisclosed fee. 

Wray insisted that potential new investors have already come forward.
