Liverpool vs Everton referee revealed for Merseyside derby

Michael Oliver has been appointed to take charge of this Saturday's Merseyside Derby between Liverpool and Everton.

The clash at Goodison Park will be the second time the 33-year-old has taken charge of the high profiled rivalry, having officiated the 2013 fixture at Anfield which ended a goalless draw.

Oliver has reffed 35 matches this season across the Premier League, Championship, Champions League, FA Cup, Carabao Cup and internationals - flashing 125 yellow cards and six reds.

He has only taken charge of the Reds once this campaign, a 1-1 draw with Chelsea in November, and two Toffees matches - October's away draw with Brighton and the 4-0 thrashing of West Ham.

The Merseyside Derby is infamous for being the Premier League's most bad tempered fixture, with 21 red cards being produced in 26 years of the division.
