South Africa close on Test series victory over Australia

Published: 13:02 EDT, 2 April 2018 | Updated: 13:02 EDT, 2 April 2018

Captain Faf Du Plessis led from the front with a century to leave South Africa on the brink of their first home Test series win over Australia since 1970.

Du Plessis turned his overnight 34 into his eighth Test hundred, finishing with 120 as the Proteas closed in on a victory in the fourth and final Test which would seal a 3-1 series triumph.

The hosts finally declared on 344 for six in their second innings, setting Australia a mammoth victory target of 612 in Johannesburg.

Captain Faf Du Plessis scored 120 as South Africa declared on 344-6 in the fourth Test

And when bad light forced an early finish on day four, the tourists were already struggling on 88 for three.

Morne Morkel - in his final Test - trapped both Australian openers Matt Renshaw (five) and Joe Burns (42) lbw, with Keshav Maharaj accounting for Usman Khawaja (seven) in the same fashion.

That left Peter Handscomb (23 not out) and Shaun Marsh (seven not out) attempting to delay the inevitable when proceedings were brought to a premature end.

Paceman Morne Morkel trapped both Australian openers Matt Renshaw and Joe Burns lbw

Australia must score 524 runs in a single day to claim a world-record run chase, and now surely only poor weather will halt South Africa.

Dean Elgar hit South Africa's second-highest score in their second innings, with 81, while Temba Bavuma (35) and Vernon Philander (33) added an unbroken 71-run stand before the home side declared at tea.

Pat Cummins' four for 58 proved the only return of note for Australia's bowlers, as a Test series mired in so much controversy neared its conclusion.
