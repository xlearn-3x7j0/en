Hearn reveals who Wilder will fight next - and it's not Joshua

Eddie Hearn suspects Deontay Wilder will snub Anthony Joshua to fight Dominic Breazeale next.

Hearn is unimpressed by claims from Wilder’s co-manager Shelly Finkel that the WBC king is prepared to face Joshua this summer at Wembley for the undisputed heavyweight crown.

Breazeale, who Joshua beat in his first defence, pulled out of a final IBF eliminator and Hearn believes it is because he will fight Wilder next this summer.

“Wilder’s going to fight Breazeale next,” said Hearn. “There’s no doubt about that because the IBF ordered a final eliminator between Kubrat Pulev and Breazeale and Breazeale pulled out.

“Breazeale had a final eliminator to fight Pulev, a massive opportunity to become mandatory to Joshua and if he had fought Pulev, he would probably have won.

“So why pull out if he had nothing else on? The answer is because he’s fighting Deontay Wilder.

“If Wilder then fights in June or July against Breazeale, then our fight with Wilder would not take place until November or December.”

Hearn claims Wilder could be jeopardising his historic showdown with WBA, IBF and WBO champ Joshua if he waits too long because the mandatories will then be due.

“Really, if we don’t make this fight by the end of 2018, or by January-February 2019 at the very latest, it can’t happen for all of the belts because the mandatories will be due,” he said.

“It would still be big, but it would not be historic.”

Hearn claims negotiations with Wilder’s camp are complicated because the American has three managers.

The Matchroom MD has little time for Finkel, who he dismissively calls Shirley, and says he has been talking to Wilder’s other co-manager Al Haymon for weeks.

“What Shirley says doesn't really mean anything because Shirley is not really involved in the decision,” he said.

“It’s difficult because Wilder has three managers - Al Haymon, Shirley, and Jay Deas. They don’t really like each other and each one will feel they don’t need the others.

“I’m speaking to Al Haymon’s people. I’ve got a good relationship with them and I can do business with them.

“But with Shirley, I can’t see an agreement taking place because we don’t like the way he does business.

“Every time Shirley comes out in the media, it takes things further away and people don’t realise it’s really difficult.”
