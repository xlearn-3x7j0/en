It doesn't take many guesses what Tyson Fury's son, Prince John James, wants to be when he grows up.

As Fury continues to make his comeback to the ring, the heavyweight appears to be helping his son become a world champion in his own right after Prince was pictured in a pair of boxing gloves on his dad's Instagram account.

On Wednesday afternoon, the 29-year-old proudly announced that his boy was 'starting young' as he flexed his muscles in their living room.

Tyson Fury says his son, Prince John James, is 'starting young' as he poses in boxing gloves

The heavyweight boxer proudly posted the picture to his Instagram page on Wednesday

The youngster even has his own WWE replica championship belt with his name on as he looks to emulate his father. 

And he doesn't have to look far for inspiration as a painting hangs on the living room wall which shows Fury beating Wladimir Klitschko to become a heavyweight champion.

The self-proclaimed 'Gypsy King' has made it no secret that he wants to get back to where he once was after a three-year absence from the ring. 

The former world champion was even briefly called out by Anthony Joshua following his win over Joseph Parker last Saturday.

Following Joshua's unanimous points decision over Parker, Fury took to Instagram to post 'there can only be 1' as he posed with the four belts Joshua now has.

With a fight against Deontay Wilder more likely Fury will have to turn his attentions somewhere else for the time being.
