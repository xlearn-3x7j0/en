Mourinho could hand Luke Shaw another chance

Luke Shaw could be set for a reprieve from Manchester United boss Jose Mourinho this weekend.

Shaw was hauled off at half-time in United's 2-0 FA Cup win over Brighton, with Mourinho claiming he needed players on the pitch capable of carrying out their defensive duties.

It was the latest setback for Shaw in his troubled United career, and one which seemed to push the 22-year-old further towards the Old Trafford exit door this summer.

But with Ashley Young struggling after picking up a knee injury on England duty, Mourinho admitted Shaw is an option at left-back, despite the episode against Brighton.

“If he's available, Luke Shaw is always an option,” said Mourinho. “Ashley Young was available to play against Brighton, so I started with Luke Shaw, so that's not a problem.

“Young had a problem in the last part of the game against Italy, but we think there is a chance for him to recover.”

Mourinho also revealed United's second-choice keeper Sergio Romero is out for several weeks after picking up an injury on international duty.

It means David De Gea, who had not featured in United's FA Cup run this season, is likely to start the semi-final against Tottenham at Wembley on April 21.

“Romero is injured, and I think he will be for a few weeks,” said Mourinho. “I think in the end he was lucky because was a strong contusion and will take a little bit of time to recover.

“Daley Blind came back from his treatment in Holland, start training with us in the last few days and is the only one not available.”

Mourinho paid tribute to Zlatan Ibrahimovic, who left United for LA Galaxy last week. “For me it's always sad when the big players go in the direction of the end,” said the United boss.

“This period with Galaxy, these couple of years, will be very good for him and American football in terms of what he can do, teach, attract, his personality and his passion for football.”
