CM Punk WWE return latest ahead of former champion's next UFC fight

It's fair to say CM Punk 's UFC debut didn't exactly go to plan.

They hype around his first fight in the Octagon hinted at the promise of a big display when he finally stepped into the cage.

CM Punk - real name Phil Brooks - had announced in December 2014 that he had signed a multi-fight contract the promotion and he began training in Milwaukee.

It was later announced that he would fight in the welterweight division but a shoulder injury in October 2015 delayed his debut.

Even after his fight with Mickey Gall was announced, CM Punk befell yet more bad luck, undergoing surgery for a herniated disc.

And when he was finally cleared to compete, he fell victim to a rear-naked choke midway through the opening round at UFC 203 in September 2016.

That wasn't enough to dishearten him however, and he looks set to return to the cage this summer.

His opponent for UFC 225 - to be held in CM Punk's home city of Chicago - has yet to be announced.

And any WWE fans who are hoping he turns his back on mixed martial arts for a dramatic return to wrestling look set to be disappointed.

WWE manager Paul Heyman told Digital Spy: "I don't think Phil Brooks (CM Punk’s real name) thinks about WWE at all anymore. I would hope that he doesn't.

"I would hope that he is of the focused mindset in propelling himself into the ranks of UFC.

"I don't think it's a matter of positioning or money or spotlight anymore.

"I think it's a matter of his dream is to become a successful UFC fighter and he doesn't even have a thought regarding WWE, at least I would hope that he doesn't.

"Because I don't know how it would be considered productive for him to do so."
