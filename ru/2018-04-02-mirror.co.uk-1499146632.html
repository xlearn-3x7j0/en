How Sterling has proven himself to become one of Man City's key men

The week ahead isn’t just the biggest for Manchester City since Pep Guardiola arrived but the biggest since Sheikh Mansour got his hands on the club.

I know they have won a couple of Premier League titles since the Abu Dhabi crew walked into the Etihad in September 2008.

And I know a lot of people will say that, in terms of ‘bigness’, the week Sergio ‘Aguerooo’ led City to the first of them in May 2012 won’t be topped.

But if results go their way between now and next Tuesday then the next eight days will supersede any that have gone before them over the last 10 years in terms of importance because of the messages City will be sending to both their domestic and European rivals.

Winning the title against Manchester United at home on Saturday would be frustrating enough for the red half of the city.

But should Pep Guardiola’s men wipe the floor with Jose Mourinho’s then they could well set the tone for the next two or three seasons.

And should they get past Liverpool over two legs, tomorrow and next Tuesday, which I expect them to after extra-time, then they will have reached the Champions League semi-finals twice in three seasons and that will leave the giants of Europe little choice but to sit up and take note.

I’ve been critical of the way City have gone about achieving their recent success but I’ve also been quick to praise Guardiola for the things he has done well.

And I have to say he deserves a hell of a lot of credit for the way he has improved some of his players, not least Raheem Sterling, who also deserves a pat on the back.

I wrote at the start of the season that it was make or break for Sterling, who was on the fringes at City and looked to be on his way out.

I wanted to see him show Guardiola and England boss Gareth Southgate what he was capable of in a World Cup year, and everyone else with a passing interest, too.

He has done just that, kicking on, learning and understanding his position much better.

And, fingers crossed, he will go to Russia injury free and still in good form, and as a Premier League winner to boot.

The biggest compliment I could pay Sterling is that England played to his strengths as much as they did to anybody else’s in the recent international break.

And his progress just goes to show any young player, particularly those who have had loads of money thrown at them, that if you listen to your coaches, if you do the things you’re good at, you can be incredibly successful in a very short period of time.

Sterling has opened his mind to everything Guardiola has had to tell him, and it has been a classic mix of pupil and teacher working in fantastic harmony.

He has been one of the three mainstay players in City’s team in what has been a very impressive season.
