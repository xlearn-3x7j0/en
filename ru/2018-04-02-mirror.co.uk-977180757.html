Surprise contender for West Brom job emerges

Michael Appleton has emerged as the bookmakers' favourite to take over at West Brom following Alan Pardew's dismissal.

Pardew was axed on Monday after a disastrous run that leaves the Baggies hurtling towards the Championship.

The Premier League basement-boys are 10 points adrift of safety, with just six games remaining.

Darren Moore has been placed in caretaker charge for Saturday's showdown with Swansea, but Leicester assistant Michael Appleton is the frontrunner to be installed on a permanent basis.

Appleton, who had a temporary spell in charge of the Baggies back in 2011, is the 2/1 favourite for the job.

Former Hull and Watford boss Marco Silva (10/3) and Northern Ireland chief Michael O'Neill (6/1) are also in the frame.
