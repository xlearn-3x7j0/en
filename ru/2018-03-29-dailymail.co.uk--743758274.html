Ex high school football coach charged with sexual assault

Brad Elliot, 37, of Waleska, Georgia, who is a former teacher and football coach, has been charged with one count of sexual battery and one count of sexual assault

A former teacher and football coach from Georgia has been charged with one count of sexual battery and one count of sexual assault.

Brad Elliot, 37, of Waleska, who was a teacher and football coach at Buford High School, is charged with sexual battery for 'unwanted touching of the victim’s buttocks,' police said.

He is also charged with sexual assault for 'consensual sexual contact' with a second female, who is older than 16, AJC.com reported.

Gwinnett County Police Department detectives revealed the two alleged victims were both students in Elliott’s classes.

Both incidents occurred on Buford High School property during the current school year.

It is illegal for a teacher to have sexual contact with a student at their own school, even if both parties are over the age of consent.

Elliott recently resigned from Buford High School.

The ex football coach turned himself in Wednesday evening and was in custody at the Gwinnett County Detention Center. 

His bail was set at $22,000, according to a release from the police department. 

Detectives have asked others to come forward as they investigate the possibility of additional victims. 

Anyone who has information about additional victims or crimes involving Elliott is encouraged to contact GCPD detectives at 770-513-5300.
