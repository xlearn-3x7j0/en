Milner shares video from inside Liverpool bus showing incredible Anfield welcome

Liverpool midfielder James Milner has shared a video from inside the Reds' teams bus showing the incredible Anfield welcome prior to their Champions League quarter-final first leg win over Manchester City.

Much was made of the Liverpool supporters' plans to create an atmosphere outside the ground as their players arrived on the coach.

And they were true to their word with thousands lining the streets - some with flares and smoke bombs on a memorable night for the Reds.

Milner tweeted a clip from inside the bus, showing the fans outside and his teammates on the coach with the caption: "No words needed."

However, the fans also came under criticism for their behaviour as the Man City coach was pelted with missiles causing damage.

The Blues claim they had to draft in a replacement bus and their original team coach was seen leaving Anfield at half-time.

UEFA are poised to launch an investigation, but City midfielder Kevin De Bruyne insists he didn't mind the scenes.

“It was okay," he said. "I don't mind supporters doing that – I've had it a couple of times for my team and it's a nice feeling.

“Breaking windows is probably not done but who am I to say something? I am fine with it as long as no-one gets hurt.

“It doesn't change anything. As long as you keep the violence down, there's nothing wrong with it."

On the pitch, it was an incredible night for Liverpool who put one foot in the last four.

Mo Salah, who limped off with a groin problem in the second half, opened the scoring early on before strikes from Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain and Sadio Mane gave the Reds a 3-0 half-time lead.

That's how it remained until the end with City now facing a huge task to book their place in the semi-finals.
