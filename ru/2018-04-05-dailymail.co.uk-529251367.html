Prince Harry becomes patron of Walk Of America expedition

Prince Harry has been announced as the patron of the Walk Of America expedition, which will raise funds for armed forces charity Walking With The Wounded.

The royal, 33, will attend the launch event for the 1,000 mile hike - which will see a team of six veterans walk from the west coast to the east coast of America - in London next Wednesday.

The expedition will start in LA - Meghan Markle's former home - on June 2, before finishing in New York on September 6.

There are suggestions that Harry and Meghan could now choose the US for their first overseas tour following their wedding in May. 

However, Kensington Palace has made it clear that the couple will not be joining or visiting the Walk Of America expedition.  

Prince Harry (pictured with fiancée Meghan Markle at the Endeavour Fund Awards) has been announced as the patron of the Walk Of America expedition

Harry has previously taken part in two treks for Walking With The Wounded, including a expedition in the South Pole in 2013.

He joined a team of injured British servicemen and women in the gruelling race, against groups from the US and the Commonwealth.

In 2015, he also joined a group of wounded Afghanistan veterans for part of an epic 1,000-mile hike across Britain.

In a statement, Kensington Palace said: 'The Walk Of America will create a platform to tell the stories of wounded veterans and their individual journeys from injury to the present day, as well as encourage a wider conversation around veteran mental health.

'The expedition will also raise vital funds for veterans both sides of the Atlantic.'

At the launch event next week, the prince will meet the three British team members taking part in the trek.

Kensington Palace said Harry's fiancée Meghan will not be joining the prince at the engagement.

It comes after the former Suits actress reportedly jetted back to LA over the Easter weekend, to spend time with her mother Doria ahead of the royal wedding in May.  

Walking With The Wounded helps veterans re-integrate back into society and retain their independence.

The charity is supported by the Endeavour Fund, which was founded by the Royal Foundation to help servicemen and women.

One of Harry and Meghan's first public engagements together was at the Endeavour Fund Awards in London earlier this year.
