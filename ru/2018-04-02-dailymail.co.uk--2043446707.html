Accrington Stanley owner defends funding team trips to McDonald's

  • Accrington Stanley owner Andy Holt treats his players after they win games
  • He gives them £200 to visit fast food franchises and buy food after the matches
  • The EFL asked for an explanation after claiming it broke one of its regulations
  • Holt, however, claimed that the money was his own and not the club's

Published: 11:20 EDT, 2 April 2018 | Updated: 11:34 EDT, 2 April 2018

Accrington Stanley owner Andy Holt has stood by his decision to fund his team's trips to McDonald's following victories after he received a letter from the English Football League.

Holt revealed that he gives the team £200 to buy their meals at fast food chains such as McDonald's after they win – but the players must pay for their own when they lose.

On Twitter, Holt claimed he had received a letter from the EFL, who claimed the post-match bonuses did not form any part of the players' contracts.

Accrington Stanley owner Andy Holt has defended his decision to fund team trips after wins

Holt said he gave £200 to his players and they go to 'McDonald's and the like' after games

The EFL claim Holt's actions are in violation of regulation 61.6, which states: 'Full details of all payments to or benefits paid in cash or in kind on behalf of players must be included in the standard contract.'

'As a result of a communication made by Mr Holt in relation to the payment of additional monies, the EFL has written to Accrington Stanley and asked them for their observations,' an EFL spokesman said.

'The club has provided its response and the matter will be considered further in due course.'

And ahead of Monday's clash against Notts County, the Stanley owner took to Twitter once again to staunchly defend his stance, claiming it to his money and not the club's.

Holt took to Twitter to say he had received a letter from the EFL 'demanding an explanation' 

'You have to wonder what these folk would do with their life if it wasn't for my errors of judgement,' he added.

In response to another tweet, he said: 'It's my money John, not the club's. I spend it how I want, been known to give a free bar to our fans for 10 minutes too on the odd occasion.'

Holt continued: 'I don't for one minute think anything untoward in my actions. At least the EFL keep an eye on my twitter feed.

'I'm relaxed about it. Should put an expenses claim in, but I'd have to pay the bill either way.'
