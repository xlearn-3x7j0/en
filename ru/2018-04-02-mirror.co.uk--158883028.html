Paedophile footballer Adam Johnson's sister can't wait to 'celebrate' release

The sister of disgraced footballer Adam Johnson has one again taken to social media to tell how she can't wait to 'celebrate' the paedophile footballer's release.

Johnson has 12 months left until he is eligible for release after being handed a six year sentence for grooming and sexual activity with a star-struck teenage girl.

The former England, Sunderland and Manchester City hopes to resurrect his career on his release - however he may have to look outside of Europe.

And Faye Johnson has once again told how she can't wait for him to get out.

Wishing her son Lucas a happy birthday, she said: "7 year (sic) ago the most adorable little boy came into my life! Happy birthday lucas This time next year uncle Adam will be celebrating with us."

It comes just a few days after Faye took to social media to say she can't wait for him to be freed so they can go on a 'boozy night out' together.

Posting a picture with her boyfriend, Kamil Didi, prior to a evening out near her home in Castle Eden, County Durham, Faye said she is excited for the ex-Sunderland and England winger to join them.

Responding to a comment from a friend which read: "Won't long before Adam is in the picture with you", Faye said: "I can't wait".

The friend added: "You will then have another one in the gang for drinks in the Inn."

Faye responded with: "Exactly" and a smiley face and bicep emoji.

Faye stuck by her disgraced brother and was behind two failed legal bids to appeal his conviction for sexual activity with the 15-year-old fan.

In November, she hinted Johnson may be freed soon, tweeting: "Not long till we have him back home where he belongs."

The ex-Sunderland man said earlier this month that he wants to sign for a big-named side and had touted America as a destination to get back into the game.

But his plans to snap up a lucrative MLS contract were kicked into touch as the US has strict visa laws meaning the sex offender would not be able to enter the country.

The 30-year-old hopes of signing for a European team are slim, with most clubs seeing his signing as a PR disaster meaning Johnson has little option to sign for a team in Asia or the Middle East.

Johnson was found guilty in February 2016 of one count of sexual activity with a 15-year-old female Sunderland fan.

The former Sunderland player had previously admitted grooming the girl and one charge of sexual activity

He was jailed for six years in March 2016.

Johnson has so far served two years of his six-year sentence, and remains behind bars at Moorland Prison in Yorkshire. He is not eligible for automatic parole until March 2019.

Johnson was cleared of one charge relating to another sexual act.
