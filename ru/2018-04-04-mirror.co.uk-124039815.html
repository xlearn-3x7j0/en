How Spanish newspapers reacted to Cristiano Ronaldo's sensational overhead kick

Just when you think Cristiano Ronaldo has done it all, he goes and proves you wrong.

His overhead kick in Real Madrid's win over Juventus was one of the greatest goals that the Champions League has ever seen.

Not only was it one of the most impressive goals he has ever scored, it helped his side to one of the most remarkable away results in European competition this season.

He was even applauded by the entire Juventus Stadium.

It comes as no surprise that Ronaldo features on the front pages of most of Spain's newspapers on Wednesday morning.

Each with a varying take on the overhead kick, possibly the most apt headline came from AS.

They simply ask: "What planet did you come from?"

Ronaldo's goal is also featured across the entirety of the Marca front page, claiming that he "made history" with his stunning strike.

What is perhaps not a surprise is the fact that the Catalonia-based newspapers still splashed on Lionel Messi.

The Ronaldo goal was nothing more than a banner at the top of Mundo Deportivo's front page, as they preferred to give their hero the limelight, despite the Portuguese's heroics.

It wasn't just the Spanish newspapers that led their coverage on Ronaldo's goal - other papers around Europe celebrated it too.

Daily Italian publication Corriere dello Sport described it as a "work of art," while Tuttosport added: "On another planet."
