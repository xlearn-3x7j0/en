Man City stars look terrified as they arrive at training for dressing-down

Kevin De Bruyne has admitted Manchester City need "something special" to turn around their Champions League quarter-final tie against Liverpool.

De Bruyne conceded Pep Guardiola's side have a huge task to make the semi-finals after being humbled 3-0 in the first leg at Anfield on Wednesday.

But De Bruyne, City's outstanding player this season, said the tie is still not over and said clinching the Premier League title in Saturday's Manchester derby will give the Blues the boost they need for Tuesday's return leg.

"It's going to be difficult but it's not impossible," said De Bruyne. "You never know. The way this team plays, everything is possible.

"I have confidence in this team and the way we play but it’s going to need something special.

"We need to focus on the derby on Saturday first and then we will try everything in our power to get a result against Liverpool.

"They're going to be the big favourites but I think we have played a lot of games where it was possible to score three goals so why not? But obviously they have a big advantage."

Goals from, Mo Salah, Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain and Sadio Mane in a stunning 19-minute first-half spell crushed City, who failed to register a single shot on target at Anfield.

But De Bruyne said City's improved second-half display - even though they failed to score - has given them encouragement ahed of the decisive second leg.

"I don't think we played badly," said De Bruyne. "It was okay, but if you lose 3-0 people are not going to say that. We started really well and they hit us on the counter attack and that is what they do best.

"They scored three goals out of four chances and then it becomes difficult. I think the way we reacted in the second half is good - not that we created that much.

"In the end, offensively we could be a little bit better but that's the way it goes. They have a big advantage but there's another 90 minutes and you never what's going to happen in football. We have a lot of confidence in our qualities so we'll see. If they go through and they deserve it, that's the way it goes."

De Bruyne says City were not fazed by Liverpool fans pelting their team bus with missiles on the way to Anfield, with a replacement coach was needed to take them back to Manchester after the windscreen of the original one was cracked.

"I've seen things like this before," said De Bruyne. "It happens. I don't think it's something bad. Obviously it's not nice if you see windows breaking that's not a good thing. But I think if supporters do that without being violent then I think it's okay."
