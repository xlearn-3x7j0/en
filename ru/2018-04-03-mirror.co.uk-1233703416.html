West Brom caretaker boss makes appeal to players and fans

Darren Moore has appealed for “unity” at crisis club West Brom as their fourth boss this term.

Popular ex-Baggies defender Moore is in charge at the Premier League basement outfit for their remaining six games.

Albion have endured a nightmare season with just three League wins under Tony Pulis, Gary Megson and Alan Pardew.

Pardew was axed on Easter Monday after an eighth successive defeat at home to Burnley.

His reign was dogged by controversy after four senior pros broke a curfew and allegedly stole a taxi in Spain, while Chris Brunt and Grzegorz Krychowiak openly questioned Pardew's tactical decisions.

Ahead of Swansea's visit, Moore, who took his first training session on Tuesday, said: “We need to get the unity back among everyone.

“The players, the staff, the fans, everyone connected with Albion, because that is the only way forward.

“I felt the pain on Saturday just like everyone. It’s their club and it’s their passion and love for it which makes it so painful at the moment.

“But let’s get that unity back. We’ve had tough days before and we’ve always bounced back and that’s what we will do again."

Leicester No.2 and former Baggies coach Michael Appleton is favourite for the permanent role in the summer. But highly-rated Graham Potter of Swedish club Ostersunds, Aberdeen's Derek McInnes, ex-WBA coach Nigel Pearson at OH Leuven and Brentford's Midlander Dean Smith can expect a shot too.

CEO Mark Jenkins has told technical director Nicky Hammond to establish a blueprint using younger players and a new possession-based style.

Nacer Chadli took part in full training for the first time since he sustained a thigh injury against Stoke in December.
