Mauricio Pochettino joins in Tottenham training ahead of Stoke clash

  • Mauricio Pochettino joined in with his Tottenham players in training on Thursday
  • The Spurs manager took part in a one-touch game and match-scenario exercise
  • He is preparing his side for their trip to Stoke in the Premier League on Saturday

Published: 12:40 EDT, 5 April 2018 | Updated: 12:40 EDT, 5 April 2018

Tottenham boss Mauricio Pochettino couldn't resist the opportunity to wind back the clock and join in with training on Thursday afternoon.

The former Argentina defender took part in a one-touch drill and a match-scenario exercise with his players as they prepare for their Premier League clash with Stoke on Saturday.

After putting his players through their paces, Pochettino decided to step back as they changed their focus to shooting drills.

Christian Eriksen, who scored a stunning strike in the vital win against Chelsea last weekend, was pictured honing his dead ball skills by whipping the ball over a free-kick mannequin.

Harry Kane also took the opportunity to sharpen up his shooting, having not fully recovered yet from the ankle injury he sustained against Bournemouth last month. 

All of Tottenham's stars were out on display for the session at their Enfield Training Centre facilities, including Dele Alli, who fresh from his heroics against Chelsea looked to be brimming with confidence as he shared a joke with team-mate Eric Dier.

The mood in the camp appeared to be high but Pochettino's men also looked switched on with the knowledge another three points this weekend would put them one step closer to Champions League qualification for next season.

Tottenham will be confident of picking up the victory, having won the last four fixtures against Stoke by a four-goal margin.

The Potters on the other hand, cannot afford to lose, with them sat in second bottom and three points adrift of Premier League safety.
