The Budapest derby between Ferencvarosi and Ujpest produced some spectacular visual scenes on Saturday after the game was played in front of both sets of ultras for the first time in four years. 

Ferencvarosi's die-hard supporters had been boycotting their fixtures because of increased security measures that were imposed by the club when their new stadium opened.

However, after reaching an agreement with the club earlier this season, they made their highly-anticipated return to the stands.

Finally derby day arrived as Ferencvaros played away at their fierce city rivals and both sets of supporters were awarded a heavy police presence as they made their way into the ground.

However, that did not prevent them from letting off flares in the street.

The hosts displayed a tifo before kick-off at the Ferenc Szusza Stadium with a masked ultra forming the centre piece of the choreographed display.

As the game unfolded, both sets of ultras let off numerous flares and fiery objects as they sought to intimidate both teams on the pitch. 

Smoke filled the stadium while loud and fierce chanting heightened the atmosphere even further.

Unlike the crowd, the game failed to ignite and finished as a goalless draw. A point was good enough for Ferencvaros to extend their lead at the top of the Hungarian League.
