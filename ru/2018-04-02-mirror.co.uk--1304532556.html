Deontay Wilder responds to Anthony Joshua's ultimatum to fight him in the UK

Deontay Wilder is prepared to fight Anthony Joshua in the UK this summer.

Joshua added another world title to his already burgeoning collection on Saturday when he outpointed Joseph Parker in front of 80,000 fans at the Principality Stadium.

Immediately after the fight, the Londoner called out Wilder , the only other world heavyweight champion, and the American is ready to accept the challenge.

"We are really glad that Anthony Joshua said for the first time 'I will fight Deontay next...' and we believe he is a man of his word. Deontay accepts the challenge," his manager Shelly Finkell told the Daily Telegraph .

"We want to make it public that Deontay wants the fight, and we are prepared to come over, or meet here and get the deal done. Deontay is ready to sign and come to the UK to fight this summer."

Joshua now holds the WBA, WBO and IBF belts while Wilder is the WBC champion.

The two men could meet in one of the richest heavyweight fights of all time as they bid to become the first unified champion since Lennox Lewis.

After beating Parker, Joshua said: "UK fighters have had to go to America. The fans have had to spend a lot of their money going to Vegas. We can do it here — in London, at Wembley. We can do it right here.

"Does he need me more than I need him? Yes. Without being big-headed, that's 100 per cent true. He needs British boxing. He’s bigger here than he is in the States, because of us.

"I'm in a great position because I can still fight top-10 heavyweights, it’s not an issue. Either he will step up or not, but my career will still go on. But he’s in a position after 10 years as a pro and he hasn't done what I've done.

"You have to look at him and ask him does he really want it?"
