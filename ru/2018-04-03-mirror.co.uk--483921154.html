17:17

Ronaldo...just like Trezeguet, says Buffon

In his 23 seasons of top flight football, Gianluigi Buffon believes he’s only seen one player who finishes like Cristiano Ronaldo - former team-mate David Trezeguet.

The Juventus shot-stopper will once again come up against the Real Madrid star in the Champions League quarter-finals on Wednesday night.

Last time the Italian faced Ronaldo, the Portugal captain put two goals past him as Real Madrid won the 2017 Champions League final in Cardiff.

Los Blancos’ number seven has been on fire since the turn of the year, scoring 21 goals in 11 games, and Buffon reckons his style is similar to that of Trezeguet.

“I admire him because he’s a person who knows what he’s doing and he is pragmatic,” Buffon told Marca .

“In recent years he has shown that he is intelligent. He has changed his position, he wastes less energy, yet he is just as lethal.

“In front of goal he is a real assassin.

“The only player I’ve seen have this clarity when shooting on his own is David Trezeguet.”

The Champions League quarter-finals are upon us, and the action kicks off with a mouthwatering, monstrous, epic clash of the titans in Turin.

Real Madrid, the 12 time winners of the European Cup, the reigning champions and back-to-back winners, face Juventus, the six-times in succession Italian champions and finalists in two of the last three seasons.

In a rematch of last year’s final in Cardiff - won 4-1 by Los Blancos - Juve will be gunning for revenge, hoping to avenge that final no show in Wales.

Real Madrid boss Zinedine Zidane is eyeing more history for himself and his side however, knowing that failure to win Europe’s biggest prize may well result in his dismissal after a lacklustre league campaign.

Madrid have lost only one knockout tie since 2014, the 2015 semi-final against tonight’s opponents.

Max Allegri will be hoping for a repeat in the next eight days, but knows his side must garner a positive result on home soil this evening to have any chance.
