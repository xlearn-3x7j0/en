Anthony Joshua receives offer to leave Matchroom for De La Hoya's Golden Boy

Anthony Joshua has received an offer to leave Matchroom and join Golden Boy with Oscar De La Hoya promising to make him a star in the United States.

The unified heavyweight world champion outpointed Joseph Parker last week in front of 80,000 fans in Cardiff.

But AJ, whose deal with Eddie Hearn and Matchroom expires at the end of this year, is yet to crack the highly lucrative US market, with the PPV model able to expand the Brit's earnings.

Hearn and Joshua have hinted that a deal is close for an extension to their current deal, having worked together since his debut in 2013 after winning Olympic gold the previous year.

A former world champion in six weight divisions, De La Hoya insists the "real money" remains in the United States, despite AJ's huge reported payday of £18m to fight Parker.

De La Hoya said: "If Anthony Joshua and Deontay Wilder want to become household names in the US of A and stop doing mediocre ratings on Showtime, call me and I’ll make you superstars in the beautiful USA."

Some fans hit back at the Golden Boy, insisting Joshua's stadium fights at the Principality Stadium or Wembley Stadium bring the UK to a stand-still.

But De La Hoya replied: "I guess he’s fine making 15 million when he can make 40+."

Golden Boy's top fighter, Saul 'Canelo' Alvarez, who has pulled out of the mega fight vs Gennady Golovkin due to failing a drugs test for clenbuterol, is earning $50m (£36m) per fight now, according to his promoter.

The offer was also put out to WBC champion Wilder, who De La Hoya previously promoted, after his modest, yet career-high $2.1m payday vs Luis Ortiz this year.

AJ and the Bronze Bomber have been called on by fans to fight each other next this year in what would decide the undisputed heavyweight world champion.
