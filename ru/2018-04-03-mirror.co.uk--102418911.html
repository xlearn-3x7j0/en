Tiger Woods' net worth revealed amid golfing superstar's latest comeback

Golf fans can rejoice as fan favourite Tiger Woods is well and truly back.

The 14-time Major winner started the year ranked at world No.656 after years out of the game with back injuries.

Woods spent the whole of 2016 missing while a comeback in 2017 proved fruitless.

But after taking time back from his latest operation, spinal-fusion surgery last April, Woods has now participated in five 2018 tournaments.

Having eased himself back at the Farmers Insurance Open (T23) and the Genesis Open (CUT), the 42-year-old finished 12th at The Honda Classic.

Woods' form continued to improve with a Tied-2nd finish at the Valspar Championship last weekend.

As a result of his form, the 14-time major winner entered the Arnold Palmer Invitational as the tournament's favourite.

Woods again put in a strong performance but was bested by Henrik Stenson and Rory McIlroy to finish Tied-5th.

With the 2018 Augusta Masters fast approaching (April 5-8), all eyes will be on Woods to see if he can close in on the great Jack Nicklaus's tally of 18 majors.

What is Tiger Woods' net worth?

According to Forbes, Woods' net worth is a staggering $740million.

The figure is nearly double the $375m countryman and fellow golfer Phil Mickelson is worth.

Woods is seventh on the site's America's Wealthiest Celebrities list, ranked between Diddy ($750m) and Dr. Dre ($710m).

Michael Jordan is the only athlete ranked above Woods, with his $1.2billion fortune.
