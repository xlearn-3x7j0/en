Trump is entertaining friends at the White House

President Trump may not dine out on the town much in Washington, D.C. – but he's happy to play host. 

Politico is reporting new details about the president's entertaining style, in which Trump invites notable personalities to the White House for dinner several times a month, never including the gatherings on his public schedule.

Early on, he hosted former Alaska Gov. Sarah Palin and musician Kid Rock. This week he brought in Peter Thiel, his most prominent Silicon Valley backer, along with Oracle's Safra Catz.

While President Trump doesn't go out in Washington much, he has been hosting several dinner parties with prominent guests every month 

Early on he invited Kid Rock (left), Alaska Gov. Sarah Palin (center) and Ted Nugent (right) to the White House as guests 

President Trump has also hosted Matt Drudge (left), the founder of the right-leaning Drudge Report, and also Fox News Channel's Sean Hannity (right) 

He's hosted media personalities – though not the ones he includes in his rants against 'fake news' – including Fox News Channel's Sean Hannity, Newsmax CEO Christopher Ruddy and Matt Drudge, the founder of the right-leaning Drudge Report. 

Politico, talking to eight current and former administration officials who've attended the meals, reported that if Trump is feeling 'upbeat' he'll give his guests a tour. 

He's taken them to the Lincoln Bedroom, or show off the White House's copy of the Gettysburg Address. 

Another favorite artifact: a rug first installed by a Republican favorite, President Ronald Reagan. 

'Have you ever seen luxury like this?' Trump – whose entire business brand centers on selling luxury – will often say, an administration official told Politico.  

The meal will often include some of the president's favorites, the publication wrote. 

Dishes like steak, chicken, salmon and salad, with American desserts like strawberry shortcake or chocolate cake. 

They usually kick off around 6:30 p.m.

And being ever the celebrity the president will sign a book, or one of the meal's menus, as a souvenir, Politico wrote. 

Like many presidents, Trump will use the gatherings to get perspective on what's going on outside the White House bubble. 

He's used them to catch up with old friends.  

He brought's in New England Patriots owner Bob Kraft, media mogul Rupert Murdoch and real estate developer Richard LeFrak, a New Yorker Trump has known for years. 

Last month, he gathered his 2016 campaign team together, hosting former campaign manager Corey Lewandowski, deputy campaign manager David Bossie, and Brad Parscale, who did social media work during the last campaign. 

Trump has already tapped Parscale to run his 2020 re-election bid.   

Besides bringing in news from the outside, Trump's fellow diners often affirm his world view, with several exceptions. 

He invited JPMorgan Chase CEO Jamie Dimon to one of the meals. 

Dimon was one of President Obama's closest allies. 

He also hosted a number of Democratic senators at the White House over the summer.     

When he leaves the White House for dinner he's only been seen at his Trump International Hotel in Washington, located within four blocks of the White House. 

Or the president will dine at a private residence like he did Wednesday night, when he spent time at City Center, the posh new section in downtown D.C. where many of his aides live.
