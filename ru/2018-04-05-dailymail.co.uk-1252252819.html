Man wielding a metal pipe is shot dead by NYPD cops

Published: 22:08 EDT, 4 April 2018 | Updated: 23:11 EDT, 4 April 2018

A mentally ill black man armed with a metal pipe has been shot dead by New York police after they responded to reports that he was wielding a gun on the street.

NYPD officers responded to several 911 calls on Wednesday afternoon reporting that a man in a leather jacket was pointing what appeared to be a silver gun at residents in the Brooklyn neighborhood of Crown Heights.

The man has been identified as 34-year-old Saheed Vassell.  

Five officers arrived on the scene at the intersection of Montgomery St and Utica Ave when the man 'took a two-hand shooting stance' and pointed a metal object at them, NYPD Chief of Department Terence Monahan said.

The officers fired off 10 rounds between them and struck the man at least once.

He was rushed to a nearby hospital where he was pronounced dead.

Police said no gun was recovered from the scene and officers determined that the man had been holding a 'metal pipe with a knob at the end of it'.

Security cameras from the intersection showed the man appearing to point a weapon at the officers, Monahan said. 

None of the officers were wearing body cameras at the time.

The shooting sparked anger from residents in the neighborhood who described the victim as a quirky character who wasn't known to be violent, the New York Daily News reports.

'He's a little touched in the head. But he's not crazy enough to go around hitting people,' a witness who knew Vassell said.

Another resident said: 'All he did was just walk around the neighborhood. He speaks to himself, usually he has an orange Bible or a rosary in his hand. He never had a problem with anyone.

'The officers from the neighborhood, they know him. He has no issue with violence. Everybody just knows he's just mentally challenged. This shouldn't have happened at all.'
