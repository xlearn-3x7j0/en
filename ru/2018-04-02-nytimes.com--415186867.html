Yankees’ Home Opener Postponed by Snow

The Yankees postponed their home opener on Monday after an early-spring storm dropped about six inches of snow inside Yankee Stadium and made clearing the field and the seats in time for a scheduled 1:05 p.m. first pitch impossible.

The game between the Yankees and the Tampa Bay Rays was rescheduled for 4 p.m. Tuesday.

“I thought, this is going to be interesting,” Yankees manager Aaron Boone said. “But I’m obviously not surprised. It was going to be quite an ordeal to have any kind of a chance.”

The decision to postpone the game was made shortly before 9 a.m., about an hour before the stadium gates were to open. Gates will open to fans at 2 p.m. on Tuesday. A Yankee spokesman said the later 4 p.m. start time was chosen to account for a forecast of rain in the morning. A pregame ceremony will begin at 3:30 p.m.

Lefthander Jordan Montgomery will start Tuesday for the Yankees, although Boone said he and the pitching coach Larry Rothschild considered starting Luis Severino, who would be on his standard five days’ rest.

The Rays, who had planned to start Austin Pruitt and a procession of relievers on Monday, instead will start their ace, Chris Archer, on Tuesday.

Monday’s storm gave Boone a one-day respite from what might have been a frosty reception from the fans after a questionable bullpen decision cost the Yankees a game against the Toronto Blue Jays on Sunday.

With the Yankees leading by a run in the bottom of the eighth inning, Boone chose to have reliever David Robertson intentionally walk Josh Donaldson to load the bases and pitch to Justin Smoak. But after a tense nine-pitch standoff, Smoak hit a grand slam on 3-2 pitch to give the Blue Jays a 7-4 victory.

“We identified the matchup we preferred,” Boone said of his call. “Larry and I were comfortable with that, but Smoak’s a really good hitter and they got us there. It’s going to happen. One more pitch and we’re out of that thing.”

Boone said he had spoken to his father, Bob Boone, a former major league manager, after Sunday’s game.

His fatherly advice?

“Welcome to managing,” Boone said.
