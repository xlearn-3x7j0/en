Live stream information, team news, odds and more for Barcelona vs Roma

Barcelona will be hoping for more Lionel Messi magic in their attempt to reach the Champions League semi-finals.

Barca host Roma in the first leg of their quarter-final tie this week as the La Liga leaders target the treble.

The Catalan giants were indebted to Messi's influence as they almost lost their unbeaten league campaign to Sevilla at the weekend - with the Argentine helping his side come from 2-0 down to draw.

Ernesto Valverde's side have already edged past Juventus, Sporting Lisbon, Olympiakos and Chelsea as they hunt for a first piece of continental silverware since 2015.

Serie A's third placed side Roma have seen off Athletic Madrid, Qarabag, Chelsea and Shaktar Donetsk to reach the last eight.

Where and when is the game?

Roma travel to the Nou Camp on Wednesday. Kick off will be at 7:45 BST.

Early team news

Barcelona

Sergio Busquets is back in Barca training ahead of the clash having missed games against Athletic Bilbao and Sevilla with a broken foot.

He is likely to return straight into the starting XI, despite Ivan Rakitic impressing as deputy.

Lucas Digne is Barca's only star definitely missing the clash at the Nou Camp.

Roma

AS Roma's attacking midfielder Cengiz Under will miss Wednesday's Champions League game at Barcelona because of injury, but Radja Nainggolan will make the trip.

Under, whose five league goals this season have all come since the winter break, suffered a thigh injury while he was with Turkey during the international week.

Nainggolan limped off during the first half of Roma's 1-1 draw at Bologna on Saturday, but Roma said the combative Belgian had been included in the 21-man squad for the quarter-final first leg.
