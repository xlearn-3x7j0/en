One dead, 15 injured when bus traveling from Houston crashes in Mexico

  • A tourism bus traveling from Houston crashed in Mexico on Wednesday morning
  • The Pegasso Tourism bus left Houston on Tuesday night and rolled over and crashed early Wednesday morning on Highway Reynosa-Monterrey 
  • One person was killed in the crash and at least 15 were injured, police said
  • There was no immediate information on the identity of the victim or what caused the accident 

Published: 15:06 EDT, 3 April 2018 | Updated: 16:12 EDT, 3 April 2018

One person has died and more than a dozen injured when a tourism bus traveling from Houston to Mexico rolled over and crashed on a rural highway. 

The Pegasso Tourism bus left Houston, Texas on Tuesday night headed towards Monterrey. 

Early Wednesday morning, the bus rolled over on Highway Reynosa-Monterrey crashing into a ditch.

A tourism bus rolled over and crashed on Highway Reynosa-Monterrey Wednesday morning killing one person and injuring at least 15 others 

The bus left Houston Tuesday night headed towards Monterrey, Mexico 

Federal police in the northern Mexico border state of Nuevo Leon confirmed that one person died in the crash and at least 15 others were injured. 

ABC 13 reports that injuries vary and people were being transported to area hospitals.

Photos from the crash show the large bus on its side in a ditch on the side of he highway.  

There was no immediate information on the identity of the victim or what caused the accident.
