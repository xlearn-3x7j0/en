Ferdinand reckons Ronaldo's overhead was better than Rooney's

Rio Ferdinand has trolled his former Manchester United teammate Wayne Rooney by insisting Cristiano Ronaldo's stunning overhead kick against Juventus was better.

Real Madrid superstar Ronaldo pulled off an incredible bicycle kick to help the La Liga giants beat Juve 3-0 in the first leg of their Champions League quarter final.

The strike, the Portuguese's second of the game and 119th in his Champions League career, was an audacious acrobatic effort to convert Dani Carvajal's cross.

It sparked wild celebrations on the pitch and in the away end while boss Zidane looked speechless - and even Juve fans were left in rapturous applause.

The goal brought back memories of Rooney's celebrated Manchester Derby strike from 2011.

The current Everton front man produced a tumbling volley after Nani had slipped a ball to the back post to shock Manchester City.

And former colleague of both Rooney and Ronaldo Ferdinand has given his reasons for picking the Real ace's effort over the England hit man.

"I was in the stadium fortunate enough to see a goal similar to that, Wayne Rooney's.

"His one was off his shin pad but the occasion and where he is, to score this goal.

"He turns up every time he is asked to deliver on the big stage, he delivers.

"In his relentless pursuit of greatness, he produces an absolute moment of brilliance.

"This lit up this studio, imagine what it did for people around the world to see one of the greatest, if not the greatest player on the planet, to produce a moment like this against Juventus, the magnitude of the game, the occasion.

"The athleticism, look how high he is off the floor, it is a joke."
