What happened between Henderson and Sterling that caught England's attention

Now now lads, you've got a big summer coming up.

The World Cup was probably the furthest thing from the minds of Jordan Henderson and Raheem Sterling in the closing stages of Wednesday night's Champions League quarter-final clash at Anfield, where Liverpool amassed a 3-0 lead in the first leg .

Mohamed Salah, Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain and Sadio Mane all found the net in the first half as the Reds ran riot against the soon-to-be Premier League champions, with Manchester City boss Pep Guardiola surprisingly opting to leave ex-Liverpool star Sterling on the bench against his former club.

And when the England winger came on in the second half, he was met with a chorus of boos from Reds fans, and an icy reception from Henderson too.

As the pair went head-to-head in the closing stages of the game, Henderson clattered into Sterling and earned a yellow card, ruling him out of next week's second leg at the Etihad Stadium.

But it was also apparent that Sterling had a little kick out at his former Liverpool and current England teammate, something that Henderson didn't seem to appreciate as he fronted up to him.

And the exchange caught the attention of the England Twitter account, who threw in the ever-popular eyes emoji to show that they were watching.

We'll have none of this in the summer please lads.
