Real Madrid's thrilling high-scoring victory over Real Betis in La Liga was a landmark occasion for the European giants.

Not only did Madrid come from 2-1 behind to win 5-3 on Sunday, but they also became the first side in La Liga to score an incredible 6,000 goals.

Youngster Marco Asensio opened the scoring for Zinedine Zidane's side at the Benito Villamarin but they soon fell behind after Aissa Mandi's strike and a Nacho own goal.

Marco Asensio scored Real Madrid's landmark 6,000th goal in La Liga on Sunday night

Cristiano Ronaldo and his Madrid team-mates came from 2-1 behind to beat Real Betis 5-3

However, Madrid responded emphatically when captain Sergio Ramos headed home an equaliser before Asensio popped up with the milestone 6,000th goal. 

Cristiano Ronaldo and Karim Benzema then finished off the comeback either side of a Sergio Leon goal on Sunday night.

Victory for Madrid on the weekend ensured they kept the pressure on third-placed Valencia, sitting just one point behind the Mestalla outfit in the Spanish top flight.

Despite their constant struggles so far this season, Zidane's men appear to have found some strong form all of a sudden after winning their last three games in all competitions.

Madrid, who will be desperate to finish the season strongly, have scored 13 times in their last three matches against Real Sociedad, Paris Saint-Germain and Betis.
