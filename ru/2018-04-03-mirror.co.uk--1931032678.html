How The Undertaker could return with popular gimmick for WrestleMania 34

The Undertaker is set to make his return to the WWE ring to face John Cena at WrestleMania 34 - and it could be in the guise of his popular 'American Bad Ass' gimmick.

The WWE legend appeared to 'retire' after losing to Roman Reigns at last year's WrestleMania.

The Deadman - real name Mark Calaway - left his trademark black leather cloak and hat in the centre of the ring in a symbolic move, although he has never officially announced his retirement.

Taker did return in Raw's 25 Years anniversary special in January. And Cena has made it his personal mission to set up a mega-fight at the 'Grand Daddy of them all' in Ohio next month.

But there is speculation among the Internet wrestling community that Taker will make his comeback in the guise of his 'American Bad Ass' role, which he portrayed during the famous Attitude Era.

This could coincide with Kid Rock being named in WWE's Hall of Fame with officials confirming he will perform live at Mania, prompting rumours that it could be Taker's original 'Bad Ass' entrance theme.

Cena is desperate for the match to happen - and it could headline the event on April 8.

“I challenge The Undertaker to a match at WrestleMania," 16-time champion Cena had said on RAW, in a bid to lure the Undertaker into a fight and defy WWE officials.

"I was told this was impossible, even though the fan in me wants to see this. I was told this is impossible and I just accepted it.

"There is one individual keeping a match at WrestleMania with The Undertaker from happening. And that's The Undertaker.

"So allow me to address The Undertaker himself: Get over your own ego.

"You see, the difference between you and me is when I fail I get back up, I put a smile on my face and I go to work to kick ass the next day.

"When you fail, you hide your head in the sand never be seen again, you're so ashamed and embarrassed of what people will say about you.

"Amazing that a symbol so strong is really so fragile."

Cena followed his gauntlet up by simply tweeting: "#OneMoreMatch" and it appears to have worked.
