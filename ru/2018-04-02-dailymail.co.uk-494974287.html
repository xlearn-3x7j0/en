Dani Alves reached yet another personal milestone at the weekend by adding more silverware to his trophy cabinet. 

Paris Saint-Germain's Coupe de la Ligue triumph over Monaco on Saturday means the experienced Brazilian star has now won 36 trophies in his professional career. 

The triumph has seen him overtake compatriot Maxwell, who he had gone level with after winning the French Super Cup in August.

Alves celebrates after claiming the 36th trophy of his career, with PSG beating Monaco in final

But the latest victory appears to have made him the most decorated footballer in the world, with Andres Iniesta and Zlatan Ibrahimovic the next active players in line with 33 trophies apiece.

Alves stunning journey to personal glory started in 2002 when he won the Copa do Nordeste in his second season with local side Bahia.

His impressive performances in his homeland saw him move to Sevilla in 2002, and while he had to wait a few years to collect his first trophy in Spain, more followed.

The right back won back-to-back UEFA Cups along with the UEFA Super Cup, the Spanish Super Cup and Copa del Rey. 

Six successful years at Sevilla came to an end in 2008 when he joined Barcelona, where he enjoyed his most successful spell.

The Brazil star claimed 23 trophies at the Nou Camp including six La Liga titles and three Champions Leagues.

He brought an end to his eight-year Barca spell in 2016 to join Juventus, spending just one season in Italy. 

Alves did, however, win the Serie A title and Coppa Italia in his single season in Turin before joining PSG at the beginning of this season.

He has already won two trophies — the French Super Cup and Coupe de la Ligue — and is on track to add the league title to his collection this season.

The French giants are 17 points clear at the top of Ligue 1, and another triumph will see Alves further cement his place among the greats.
