Boy, 4, drowned in pool while father was doing meth

A four-year-old New Orleans boy drowned in a filthy backyard swimming pool while his father was doing meth inside, police said Wednesday.   

Paramedics were called to a home in Slidell just before 6pm on March 5, when Timothy Brooks was found unresponsive in the pool by his father Zachary, 24. 

The boy was rushed to the hospital where he was pronounced dead. 

'This is the worst case that I've seen,' Police Chief Randy Fandal said. 'I've done this 38 years and this is disgusting.'

Zachary Brooks, 24 (left), was arrested on Saturday after his four-year-old son Timothy (right) drowned in a backyard pool while he was allegedly doing meth inside

The pool where Timothy drowned (pictured above) was black, covered with green pond scum, and full of all kinds of trash

Brooks was arrested Saturday on charges of second-degree cruelty to juveniles and negligent homicide and ordered held in lieu of $200,000 bond. 

Multiple witnesses told police that Timothy was left unattended while his dad did meth with other adults inside the house. 

After noting several 'disturbing findings' at the scene, police executed a search warrant on the home. 

Investigators said they found large amounts of drug paraphernalia and suspected drugs throughout the house, which had no running water and was littered with piles of trash and decaying food, according to the police news release. 

Brooks has had full custody of his son since the boy's mother has been in jail on drug charges since January (father and son pictured above in 2014)

The pool where Timothy drowned was black, covered with green pond scum, and full of 'all kinds of trash,' said a police spokesman, Detective Daniel Seuzeneau, in a phone interview. He said the yard was fenced, but there was no safety fence around the pool itself.

'There is no question in my mind that the drug use, the deplorable conditions of the house, and the way Timothy was being cared for, contributed to his death,' Fandal said at a news conference Wednesday.

Seuzeneau said Brooks was the primary caregiver, adding the child's mother is being held on drug-related charges. He said he did not know whether Brooks has an attorney who could speak for him.

A GoFundMe has been started to help raise money for Timothy's funeral. So far, the fund has raised more than $1,000.
