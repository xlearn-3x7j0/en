DAILY BRIEFING: Apple poaches Google executive John Giannandrea

Published: 16:54 EDT, 4 April 2018 | Updated: 19:28 EDT, 4 April 2018

Google's John Giannandrea, has been poached by Apple

CHIEF SWOOP A top artificial intelligence executive from Google, John Giannandrea, has been poached by Apple to lead its machine learning and AI strategy.

BIG JUMP The taxi app firm Uber could buy Jump Bikes for more than £70million. 

They have already been working together on a pilot that lets users book bikes through Uber’s phone app.

FLYING HIGH Budget airline Ryanair’s passenger traffic grew 6 per cent last month to 10m customers. Lower fares increased the proportion of seats occupied by 1 per cent to 95 per cent.

LUXURY OPENING Luxury department store Fortnum & Mason is opening a new shop and restaurant in London’s financial district. The restaurant will seat 90 people.

EASTER BONANZA Department store John Lewis and its sister supermarket, Waitrose, enjoyed soaring business due to the early Easter. 

Sales were up 21 per cent compared to the same week last year.

AUDIT CHANGE Beancounters may have to audit everything in big companies’ annual reports under new rules. 

At present, they only have to look at certain parts of the documents. The changes have been proposed by the Financial Reporting Council watchdog.

LEGAL BOOM Law firm DLA Piper enjoyed a bumper year in 2017 despite a cyber-attack. 

Revenues rose 6.6 per cent to £1.9billion and profits climbed 10.2 per cent to £505million. Its partners earned an average £1.3million.

CARD SWITCH Around 94 per cent of customers who used a prepaid card from online bank Monzo have upgraded to a full current account. 

The account now has 568,000 users. The prepaid scheme, which is shutting down, allowed a Mastercard to be loaded up with cash but did not offer full banking services.

PASSENGERS SOAR Low-cost eastern European carrier Wizz Air increased the number of seats available by 23.7 per cent to 2.7m in March compared to a year earlier. It flew 2.5m passengers.

DEAL SLUMP Banker Goldman Sachs has slipped down the dealmaking league table in the City. 

The US behemoth was top of the tree for mergers and acquisitions in the first three months of 2017 but has now slipped to 12th place. 

Morgan Stanley was top after advising on ten deals worth £113billion.
