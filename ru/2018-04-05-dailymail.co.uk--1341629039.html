Bud Light sends 1,000 Dilly Dilly shirts to the Masters after ban

Bud Light has scored a hole-in-one against the Masters. 

The beer company took aim at the prestigious Georgia golf course Augusta National after it dared try to ban the crowd from uttering the words 'Dilly Dilly'. 

Now the company plans to send 1,000 'Dilly Dilly' shirts to be worn during the golf competition. 

Bryce Ritchie, who writes for British golf site Bunkered, was the first to reveal that security staff at the course had been given a sheet of banned phrases for the Masters.

Right up there on the list was Dilly Dilly, a phrase that has become popular at sporting events after it was first used in a Bud Light commercial in 2017. 

Ritchie reported that, according to the list, any spectator who yells 'Dilly, Dilly' during the Masters will be removed from the event this week. 

When Bud Light heard the news, it decided to take a swing of its own. 

The company released a letter supposedly written by King John Barley IV, the main character in the Bud Light 'Dilly Dilly' commercials.  

'Your king hath received word that the guard of the Green Jacket plan to escort any patron who dare utter Dilly Dilly off yon premises,' writes the king.

'Except for myself, I am against tyranny in all forms. So, I have instructed my royal tailors to make 1,000 Dilly Dilly shirts that shall be delivered to Georgia in time for the festivities.' 

'For if thou cannot say Dilly Dilly, thou can still wear Dilly Dilly.' 

It remains unclear exactly what 'Dilly Dilly' means. 

But the phrase quickly went viral when it was featured in a slew of medieval-themed commercials about King John Barley IV and his deep love for Bud Light. 

Bud Light VP Andy Goeler told Thrillist that the phrase was 'sort of a medieval form of cheers'.

'The phrase has taken on a life and a meaning of its own, thanks to fans of the commercials,' he said. 'We're more than happy to just be along for the ride.'

And the Masters is sure to be a crazy ride this year, Dilly Dilly shouting or not. 

The chase for the green jacket begins on Thursday, and this year's competition has been hotly anticipated with the return of Tiger Woods for the first time since 2015.

'When Tiger walked onto the range today, you can tell there's an anticipation and an excitement from the crowd to watch him compete again,' English golfer Justin Rose told the Associated Press on Sunday. 

Many are hoping to see Woods compete with current golfing stars like Jordan Spieth, Rickie Fowler, and Rory McIlroy, who is trying for a career Gland Slam.

The Masters is the one missing competition keeping McIlroy, 28, from becoming the sixth golfer to have ever won all four of golf's majors during his career.

Woods, Nicklaus, along with Gene Sarazen, Ben Hogan, and Gary Player, have all earned the Grand Slam.

'I'm an avid fan of the history of the game, and I know a win here and what that would mean and where that would put me in history alongside some of the greatest that have ever played this game,' McIlroy told ESPN.

'And that would mean an awful lot to me.'
