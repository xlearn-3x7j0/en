FTSE LIVE: UK stocks struggle after overnight falls on Wall Street

European markets fall

Europe has been hit by the market falls today, too.

The German Dax is down 1.18% at 11,953 – a fall of 152 points this morning – while the French Cac is down 0.72% at 5,130 – a loss of 36 points.

What happened on Wall Street last night?

Traders dumped stocks on Wall Street last night as the world lurched closer to a full-blown trade war.

The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell by 1.9 per cent, or 459 points, after the Chinese slapped tariffs on £2.1 billion of US exports, including politically sensitive food products.

It is feared this could be just the start of a damaging tit-for-tat battle between the two superpowers.

It takes the Dow into correction territory as it is now more than 10 per cent below its previous peak earlier this year. The index closed at 23,644.19.

Spotify to take first step into public market

Music streaming app Spotify will list on the New York Stock Exchange today, a first for the company. 

It has opted, unusually, for a direct listing without the help of investment banks - handing the market more freedom to set its price. 

Laith Khalaf, a senior analyst at Hargreaves Lansdown, said it was likely to be a choppy float given the company hasn't yet turned a profit.

He says: 'Indeed it may be some time before Spotify is profitable, as the company is resolutely committed to prioritising growth over profit, and will be channelling money into investing in services for its users, and building on its scale.

'This makes sense as a business plan, but with profits in the long grass it does make the stock particularly susceptible to swings in sentiment, and to any perceived failure to deliver on growth.'

Two possible futures for Sky News

Sky News could be sold off to Walt Disney or ring-fenced in plans released by 21st Century Fox as it attempts to buy out the media firm.

Fox, which is attempting to buy the 61% of Sky it does not already own, faces a number of regulatory hurdles after the UK’s competition watchdog found the £11.7 billion deal was 'not in the public interest'.

It has now proposed either a legal separation and comprehensive ringfencing of Sky News or a sale of the loss-making channel to Walt Disney, which is itself attempting to acquire Fox’s entertainment assets.

The sale of Sky News to Disney would take place 'whether or not Disney’s proposed acquisition of 21st Century Fox proceeds', Fox said on Tuesday.

It comes as the Competition and Markets Authority weighs up whether to recommend Fox’s deal for Sky to Culture Secretary Matt Hancock, who must decide to either approve or block it.

FTSE opens lower

UK stocks have started the day off lower after struggling to shake off an overnight plunge on Wall Street. 

Tech stocks in New York suffered another sell off. 

The FTSE 100 is at 7,013 - down 0.61% at opening.
