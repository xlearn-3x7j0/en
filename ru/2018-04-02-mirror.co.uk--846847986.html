Chambers explains why Premier League still remains important for Arsenal

Calum Chambers insists Arsenal won’t take their eye off the ball in the league.

The Gunners have little to play for in the Premier League after falling well off the top four pace, 13 points behind fierce rivals Tottenham.

Their priority has now become winning the Europa League with that competition offering Arsene Wenger’s men their only realistic route back into the Champions League.

After seeing off Stoke on Sunday to stay sixth Arsenal face CSKA Moscow in a quarter-final first leg tie on Thursday and defender Chambers said: “We want to finish as high as we can in the league so we are going to keep fighting until the end of the season with that.

“We want to win every game so at the moment we are going to prepare for every game and try to get the results we need from each game.

“You could let your mind drift to the Europa League.

“But we are a professional side and do want to finish as high as we can in the league so these league games are still important for us.

“We got the job done today so now we can put that to one side and focus on Thursday.”
