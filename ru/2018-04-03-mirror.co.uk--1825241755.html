Conor McGregor hits out at Tony Ferguson in typically crude fashion

Conor McGregor wasn't going to stay quiet for long after Tony Ferguson was forced out of his fight with Khabib Nurmagomedov .

Ferguson withdrew from Saturday's lightweight title fight at UFC 223 after suffering a knee injury at a TV studio last week.

And McGregor - the former 155lb champion - hit out at his rivals on social media.

"These fools pull out more times than I do with my d***," he tweeted.

His post comes hours after Nurmagomedov accused the Irishman of talking the talk but not walking the walk.

McGregor tweeted "stay ready" last week, to which Nurmagomedov replied "be ready to stay on the line".

But instead it is Max Holloway who will replace Ferguson, and the Russian tweeted to McGregor: "Where are you now?".

The former two-weight world champion could return to the Octagon in September.

UFC president Dana White revealed McGregor was not ready to step in at such short notice, but that he is set to face the winner of Saturday night's clash.

He said: "I don’t think Conor McGregor could make the weight on six days' notice. That's a tough weight cut for him.

"And Conor is not the guy I want to throw in as a late replacement fighter.

"He’s a guy I want to make a fight with and have the right amount of time to promote the fight.

"I think Conor could return in September, and it could be against one of these guys."
