Brock Lesnar is coming back to the UFC, says president Dana

  • Brock Lesnar retired from MMA two years ago after he failed drugs tests
  • The WWE star has been linked with a comeback with his contract set to expire
  • Dana White has revealed that the 40-year-old will return to the octagon
  • Lesnar still has six months to serve on his USADA doping suspension  

Published: 12:42 EDT, 5 April 2018 | Updated: 12:42 EDT, 5 April 2018

Brock Lesnar will make a stunning return to the UFC, the promotion's president Dana White has revealed.

The 40-year-old's contract with the WWE is due to expire after he defends his Universal Championship against Roman Reigns at Wrestlemania 34 on Sunday.

Multiple sources in the US have reported a deal is already in place for the former heavyweight champion to return to the octagon and White has confirmed that he will indeed be coming back.

Dana White (right) has revealed that Brock Lesnar is set to make a return to the UFC 

A deal is thought to be in place for the former heavyweight champion to return to the octagon

'Yeah, Brock Lesnar's coming back,' White told Daniel Cormier on UFC Tonight. 'I don't know when, but yes he is.' 

The speculation surrounding 'The Beast's' potential return intensified when he was pictured wearing a UFC shirt alongside White in Las Vegas back in February.

The UFC president went on to say the 'chances were very, very, very good' that the wrestler would be coming out of retirement in the near future.

Lesanr has not fought since 2016 at UFC 200 when he beat Mark Hunt, but the victory was marred by failed drug tests.

The 40-year-old is a former UFC champion and one of the biggest stars in MMA history

The fan-favourite was banned for a year after he twice tested positive for anti-estrogen drugs clomiphene and hydroxy-clomiphene.

It will be six months before he is eligible to fight again as he would first need to re-enter the USADA drug testing pool.

The UFC are in need of a big name like Lesnar's to draw in pay-per-view buyers. Conor McGregor is currently locked in negotiations and Ronda Rousey has moved to the WWE after two devastating losses.
