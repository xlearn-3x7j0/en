Russell Crowe bought a skull from Leo DiCaprio when intoxicated

When you're an Hollywood star with spare change, nothing's off limits.

And ahead of his 'divorce auction' on Thursday, Russell Crowe revealed one of the items he was selling was a dinosaur skull he initially purchased from Leonardo DiCaprio when he was over at his home back in 2008.

Speaking to KIIS FM's Kyle and Jackie O Show, the 53-year-old recalled the night that saw him splash out over $35,000 on the historic fossil.

'We were on the sauce one night and next thing you know a cheque was signed and I had a dinosaur skull,' he explained.

'We were at his house yeah, and he’s got an incredible collection of Basquiat art and a lot of other stuff and I was admiring it and he said "well funnily enough I want to move that".

'And one thing led to another and next you know I’ve got a dinosaur skull.'

According to Sotheby's, the fossil,  originating from Kansas, is from a Monasaur's skull and comes from the Cretaceous Period 62 million years ago.

The opening bid for this piece, which has been in the hands of two of the world's greatest actors, starts at $28,000.

During his interview with the radio duo, Russell also revealed that his ex-wife Danielle was aware of the sale and the fact that he was selling the jewellery he had gifted to her.

Among the jewellery up for sale in Russell's extravagant 'The Art Of Divorce' auction is an 18-carat white gold diamond ring that cost the Gladiator star '$160K or something at the time'. 

'She's worn it a couple of times and that's the thing with Dani, she's a very humble person,' he explained to Kyle and Jackie O.

'I'd given her diamond rings, and necklaces and bracelets... she felt that she was putting her friends in an uncomfortable position.  

'So she put stuff away - she put the comfort of her friendships and stuff before her own enjoyment.' 

Russell and Danielle began an on-again, off-again relationship in 1989, when they co-starred in the Australian movie The Crossing, before marrying in 2003. 

The pair who have two sons Charles, 14, and Tennyson, 11, announced their separation in October 2012.  

The New Zealand-born star is selling 227 items from his personal collection on 7 April in a $3.6 million auction hosted by Sotheby's Australia.
