Shocking moment high-speed police chase ends in fiery crash

A fiery crash which killed one person and injured four more after a high-speed police chase has been caught on CCTV.  

Mark Padgett, 37, fled from state troopers in Indianapolis after they told him to pull over for a traffic violation while driving his Oldsmobile at 7.15pm on Tuesday.

This prompted a pursuit which ended five minutes later when Padgett struck a BMW before slamming into a pick-up truck and then catching on fire. 

This shocking CCTV captures the moment Mark Padgett, 37, collided with a pick-up truck in a fiery crash that ended a police chase

Padgett was already under surveillance over a drug transaction involving 'heroin, possibly laced with the deadly drug Fentanyl', when he was caught breaking the speed limit and making illegal lane movements.

He refused their call to stop and sped off, before throwing objects from his car at officers during the chase, according to a police statement seen by 13 WTHR.

Padgett was seen on CCTV colliding with the BMW when approaching an intersection, before hitting a GMC pickup truck and bursting into flames. 

The crash killed 32-year-old Michael Ramsey, who was in the front seat next to Padgett, and hospitalised the BMW's driver and passenger as well as two people in the pick-up truck.

He has been initially charged with resisting law enforcement resulting in death, reckless homicide, and failure to return to lawful detention. He is also wanted on an outstanding theft warrant.
