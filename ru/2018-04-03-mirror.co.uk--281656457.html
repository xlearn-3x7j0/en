Accrington Stanley chairman investigated by EFL for buying his players McDonalds

Accrington Stanley owner Andy Holt has hit out at the Football League after they demanded an explanation over the club's burger bonus.

Holt revealed on Twitter than he received paperwork from the league's governing body asking about the money he gives his players after a win.

The League Two side are cruising to promotion, three points clear of second place Luton with two matches in hand.

After admitting splashing the cash on a handful of Big Macs and McNuggets, Holt said: "So. Sometimes when we win I give £200, less the £21.37 I get back in change, to Sean (McConville, the captain) to get the lads McDonalds or the like.

"If we lose they get their own. It's just the way we roll.

"Then I receive a letter from EFL demanding an explanation because the McDonalds is not specified in the players contracts.

"You have to wonder what these folk would do with their life if it wasn't for my errors of judgement (sic)."

The bonus goes against rules which state: "Full details of all payments to or benefits paid in cash or in kind on behalf of players must be included in the standard contract."

"It's my money, not the club's. I spend it how I want, been known to give a free bar to our fans for 10 minutes too on the odd occasion," Holt added sarcastically.

"I don't for one minute think anything untoward in my actions. At least the EFL keep an eye on my twitter feed.

"I'm relaxed about it. Should put an expenses claim in, but I'd have to pay the bill either way."
