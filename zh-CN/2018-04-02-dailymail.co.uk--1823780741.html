Tottenham's Alli takes to social media to taunt Chelsea fans again

  • Dele Alli hit a brace as Tottenham ended long wait for win away to Chelsea 
  • Alli celebrated his second goal by cupping his ear in front of the Chelsea fans 
  • The midfielder took to Twitter after the game to further mock Blues supporters
  • Alli also posted a message for Tottenham fans on the club's Instagram account

Published: 06:04 EDT, 2 April 2018 | Updated: 06:36 EDT, 2 April 2018

Dele Alli was the hero as he scored a brace to help Tottenham beat Chelsea 3-1 on Sunday to secure their first victory at Stamford Bridge for 28 years and after the game the midfielder took the opportunity to further taunt Blues supporters.

After scoring his second goal, Alli cupped his ear to the Chelsea fans and he posted a picture of the celebration on Twitter, accompanied by three zip-it emojis. 

Alli also posted another Tweet in which he praised the team's overall performance. 

Dele Alli scored twice as he helped Tottenham to end their Stamford Bridge hoodoo

He wrote: '28 years since we've beaten Chelsea at Stamford Bridge, it was a pleasure to be involved. Great performance from the lads' 

In addition, Tottenham's official Instagram account posted a short message from Alli in which he reflected upon the game.

Alli said: 'Hey guys, it's Dele. I just want to thank you all for your fantastic support today. 

'Happy to score the two goals but even happier to win the game and hope you have a great Easter.' 

Alli gave a message to Tottenham supporters via the club's official Instagram account

The midfielder was delighted with his brace but said he was even happier with the victory

Alli celebrated in front of the Chelsea supporters after he scored to put Tottenham 3-1 up

Alli has endured some difficult moments this season, but he provided a reminder of the undoubted quality that he possesses with an impressive display against Chelsea.

The result saw Tottenham open up an eight point gap on their London rivals and significantly boost their chances of securing a top four finish in the Premier League.

From a personal perspective, Alli will have been delighted to have produced such an influential performance at a time when his starting berth for England at this summer's World Cup has been placed under threat by Manchester United's Jesse Lingard.

Tottenham are back in action on Saturday when they travel to face struggling Stoke and Alli will be aiming to impress again.
