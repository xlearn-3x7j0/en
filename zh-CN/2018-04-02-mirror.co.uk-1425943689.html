Man City defender to take 'once in a lifetime' opportunity vs Man United

Aymeric Laporte is urging his Manchester City teammates to take their "once in a lifetime opportunity" and win the Premier League title against massive rivals Manchester United.

'Noisy neighbours' City could claim the crown against United at the Etihad Stadium next Saturday.

Pep Guardiola' side would have an unassailable 19 point lead with six matches to go after routing the division with just one defeat and three draws all season.

The victory would also take the record for the earliest in the season a club had won the league - beating Arsenal's Invicibles along with United's 1999/00 and 2012/13 sides who both won with four games to spare.

And January recruit Laporte is hoping that despite the title seeming to be a formality, they can stick it to United in style.

"Obviously that is a very important game on Saturday," the defender said.

"I think it is a once in a lifetime opportunity and we must take advantage of it. We must try to win and get the Premier League we all want."

City face Liverpool in the Champions League quarter-final first leg on Wednesday.

And Laporte is expected to be utilised at left back, rather than his preferred centre back, as he was against Everton which the Frenchman is happy to do for the good of the team.

"It felt really good. It is a different position but we worked over the week in training sessions and I felt fine," Laporte added.

"We fight every game like it is the last one and it was very important to win. You always try to play the best you can. But we need to keep on improving.

"We know Liverpool is a difficult game. They have great players and are in great shape. We need to be 100 percent focused to have a chance to win."
