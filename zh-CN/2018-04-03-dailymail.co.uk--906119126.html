Saracens up for sale as South African co-owners look to sell stake

Saracens' future stability has been thrown into doubt as their South African co-owners want to put their stake up for sell, according to English reports.

The English giants, who were knocked out of the Champions Cup by Leinster on the weekend, are set to join a host of other sides either up for sale or seeking investment.

According to the Times, the South Africans, who arrived in north London 10 years ago, want to sell their 50% stake in the club after once again operating at a loss last year.

Saracens will look for new investors as their South African co-owners want to sell their stake

The north London outfit recorded a downfall of £2.74million, which however, was a considerable improvement on years gone by. 

Saracens were just one of 11 Aviva Premiership clubs that operated at a loss last year, with Exeter Chiefs the only club to come out healthy.

Chairman and owner of the other half of Saracens, Nigel Wray, insists the South Africans' decision to sell will make no immediate difference as he can buy Saracens out.

However, Wray is searching for other investors to come forward as he cannot financially run the club on his own in the long-haul. 

The South Africans invested in Saracens 10 years ago, led by businessman Johann Rupert 

The South Africans investment in Saracens came 10 years ago, led by wealthy businessman Johann Rupert.

They helped change the fortunes of the Allianz Stadium side, with Saracens winning three Premiership titles since 2010-11 and being crowned Champions Cup winners for the past two seasons.

However, a hat-trick of European titles is now off the cards after they were defeated 30-19 by Leinster in the quarter-finals on Sunday afternoon.

Mark McCall's side were the last English side left standing in Europe and their hopes of emulating Toulon by claiming a third successive title were buried in Dublin by a stampede of Leinster’s rested Grand Slammers.
