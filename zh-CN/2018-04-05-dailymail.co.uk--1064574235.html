Bill O'Reilly loses battle to keep terms of settlements sealed

Legal loss: Former Fox News host Bill O'Reilly on Tuesday lost a legal battle to keep the terms of his settlements with his accusers sealed

Former Fox News presenter Bill O'Reilly has lost a legal fight to keep the terms of his confidential settlements with multiple women who accused him of sexual misconduct or verbal abuse a secret.

US District Court Judge Deborah Batts on Tuesday ruled that O'Reilly, 68, has failed to provide a compelling reason to block public access to the terms of the agreements, which are now part of a defamation lawsuit.

On Wednesday, attorneys Neil Mullin and Nancy Smith, who are representing plaintiffs Rachel Bernstein, Rebecca Gomez Diamond and Andrea Mackris in the defamation case brought against O’Reilly last year, unveiled in court the settlement agreements that O'Reilly had reached with them after being accused of misconduct.

The documents revealed that Mackris, a former Fox News producer who filed a sexual harassment lawsuit against the then-host of The O'Reilly Factor in 2004, was required to lie that any of the evidence in her case were 'counterfeit or forgeries,’ should it ever come to light, reported CNN.

According to a separate court filing by the women's attorneys, the agreement with O'Reilly called for Mackris to 'lie' even under oath about the evidence and discredit it as fake.

In her original complaint, Mackris claimed she had been sexually harassed by O'Reilly and detailed their phone sex conversations in which he allegedly listed off his female conquests.

O'Reilly also allegedly detailed a sex fantasy he had about Mackris using 'that little loofah thing' in the shower, but towards the end accidentally called it a 'falafel thing'.

According to the complaint, O'Reilly called Mackris one night to inform her he was watching pornography and then began to engage in phone sex fantasy about having her come to the Caribbean with him.

'So anyway I’d be rubbing your big boobs and getting your nipples really hard, kinda' kissing your neck from behind ... and then I would take the other hand with the falafel thing and I’d put it on your p***y, but you’d have to do it really light, just kind of a tease business,' said O'Reilly according to court papers filed by Mackris.

Her complaint went on to say that during his monologue O'Reilly also suggested Mackris perform oral sex on his 'big c***' and that 'it became clear that he was using a vibrator upon, himself and had ejaculated.' 

According to Wednesday's court filing, as part of her settlement with O'Reilly, Mackris was 'forced to relinquish audio and video tapes recording O'Reilly's harassment and abuse.' The same condition applied to the other accusers. 

Her agreement also revealed that Bo Dietl, a private investigator, former Fox News contributor and failed candidate for the mayor of New York City, 'surveilled, investigated and amassed information about Ms Mackris, including tapes, photographs, emails, letters, calendars and diaries.'

Dietl denied any wrongdoing but admitted that he was hired to do investigative work by the law firm that represented O'Reilly and Fox News.

Mackris and Gomez Diamond, a former Fox Business Network host, joined a defamation suit first filed by their colleague Rachel Bernstein in December 2017.

O'Reilly (pictured on a shopping trip last year) was ousted from Fox News last year after New York Times published a story about his settlements with five accusers  

The three women sued O'Reilly over his comments suggesting that they were political operatives hell-bent on destroying his career, as well as liars whose sole intent was to shake him down for money. 

O’Reilly made those claims despite the fact that he settled with Mackris for $9million after she accused him of sexual harassment, and paid Diamond $3.25million in 2011 - an amount that had not been disclosed until this week - after she, too, accused him of sexual misconduct.

The New York Times broke the story about the misconduct allegations agaisnt O'Reilly, which caused a mass exodus of advertisers from his top-rated Fox News show, and ultimately resulted in his firing.   

Bernstein accused O'Reilly of discrimination and verbal abuse in 2002 and received a small payout that was far less than the other five known women whom O'Reilly settled with over the years.

In total, O'Reilly paid over $45million to six women as part of separate settlements, including an unnamed former colleague who was awarded $32million last year.

O'Reilly and his lawyers have repeatedly said he only agreed to settle to protect his teenage son and daughter from embarrassment. 

The confidential agreement stipulated that if Mackris were to disclose the details of the settlement to the public, she would be forced to repay the full amount and any ancillary benefits, plus O'Reilly's legal fees. 

Separate case: In late March, former Fox News reporter Laurie Dhue filed a separate defamation lawsuit against O'Reilly

Attorney Fred Newman said in a statement to CNN on Wednesday that 'confidentiality and arbitration' were two critical terms for which his client 'bargained in good faith,' and now that the agreements have been breached by the plaintiffs, he intends to 'take all appropriate legal actions to enforce the agreements.' 

In late March, Laurie Dhue, who worked as a reporter on the television show Geraldo at Large and was the host of Fox Report Weekend for eight years, filed a separate defamation lawsuit against O'Reilly.

Dhue was one of the five alleged harassment victims and received $1million payout from Fox News over the summer.

Dhue's case was not mentioned in this week's court ruling that unsealed the terms of O'Reilly's settlements' with the other accusers.
