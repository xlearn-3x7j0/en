Baby monkey cries as it hugs the electrocuted body of its mother

  • A mother monkey died hopping between power poles in southern India
  • Her devastated baby was seen crying over her body at her memorial 
  • It refused to let go of her as she sat on a wooden box in a religious farewell 

Published: 11:15 EDT, 3 April 2018 | Updated: 11:15 EDT, 3 April 2018

A baby monkey refused to let go of its mother's dead body as it cried over her memorial in an Indian town.

The distraught primate witnessed earlier its mother be electrocuted while hopping between power poles in Karnataka, southern India.

Villagers in Lakshmeshwara assembled a roadside tribute for the mother's body to give her a religious farewell.

She was placed on a wooden box propped up against a pole with a flower garland around her neck

They were moved to tears by the heartbreaking scene as the little monkey sat in her lap and wrapped his arms around her middle.

The child appeared to be desperately trying to wake his mother up despite her being dead for at least a day.

It was a similar scene to a year ago when another grief-stricken baby monkey was seen unable to let go of its mother who was hit by a car.

The youngster was photographed slumped on top of the mother's body and curled up next to her face in a long goodbye.
