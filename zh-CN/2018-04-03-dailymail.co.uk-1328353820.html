Hoda Kotb admits she was once terrified she would never be a mom

As a proud mother and an anchor on Today, Hoda Kotb is thriving—but she used to panic her personal life wouldn't pan out as well as it has, and felt that her life was 'falling apart'.

Hoda, 53, opened up about how she learned to let go of perfection and let her milestones happen in their own time in a touching essay for Today, looking back on her tough teenage years, her former marriage, and her cancer diagnosis.

She recalled how at school, she can still 'physically feel the angst' she used to feel when her name came up during roll call, leaving teachers convinced there was a 'typo' on their list.

'I was the trifecta of weird,' the anchor said. 'I had stop-sign-sized glasses, frizzy hair and a weird name so I was that girl. I wished to be Jane Smith. 

'I would've given anything to have had a regular name. But as you grow older, you go: "Oh, that's cool. It's unique." It makes you unique, you know? It's a cool thing. It took me a minute to understand that.'

When Hoda got her start as a news reporter, she was scared of making a mistake on camera and was constantly on the lookout for a possible mishap.

'I've spent so much of my career trying not to mess up. I remember if I stumbled on a live shot or if I would mess up anchoring, I'd think, "Ugh, what is wrong with me?"' she recounted.

'I would try and try and try to be perfect, and then I just eventually realized that perfect isn't real. It's not a real thing. You can't be perfect and why would you want to be — we're actually attracted to each other's flaws. 

'I'm attracted to someone not because they're perfect, but because I feel what they're feeling or I've been through something similar to what they've been through.'

Letting go of the pursuit of perfection was liberating for Hoda, who allowing herself not to be perfect gives her more opportunities to connect with others.

She credited her colleague Kathie Lee for giving her the courage to be herself in front of the camera, and recounted how they once both got rids of their cards, leading to a 'joyful and fun' moment.

'You just realize being yourself is probably the easiest thing you can be,' Hoda added.

'We spend half of our lives trying to do it right, be perfect, and you're like, "I'm perfect and it's so not me." I'd rather be flawed and a little messed up and have it be authentic.'

Self-acceptance, for Hoda, also meant letting her personal milestones happen in their own time.  

She became a mother at 52 last year, after adopting her beloved daughter, Haley Joy, on Valentine's Day. Hoda is also in a happy relationship with her longtime partner, 60-year-old financier Joel Schiffman.

'All of my blessings seem to be coming later in life,' she said. 'At 30, I was panicking about not being married and not having kids. Then I did get married and it didn't work. I got breast cancer. Everything was falling apart. And you're thinking to yourself, my windows are closing left and right — I don't know what to do or where to go.'

Hoda was previously married to tennis coach Burzis Kanga. The two tied the knot in 2005 and split in 2007.

During that same year, Hoda also faced a breast cancer diagnosis, and treatment left her unable to conceive. She had almost given up on her desire to be a parent when she asked Joel whether he would like to explore adoption with her—and he agreed right away.

'Things happen right on time and when you're ready—or at least when the universe is ready and it's meant to be,' Hoda added.

'I believe that with everything. Because who would've thought at 53 I would have the thing I love the most in life? Like I'd be feeling new emotions? Now? At 53, who feels something brand new? I can't believe I can feel like this. And I also treasure it in a way I'm not sure I would have if I were younger.'

Hoda previously shared intimate details about adopting a daughter at the age of 52, candidly admitting that she has 'done the math' and thought long and hard about what becoming a mother at an older age means for her beloved Haley in the future. 

Speaking about the little girl, Hoda said that, despite the barriers and struggles they might face as older parents, she will be 'forever thankful' to have Haley Joy in her life, and is focused completely on 'being grateful for [however many] years we have together'.

'I think any older mom who says they haven't done the math is probably lying. I've absolutely thought about it and what it means. What it means for the future,' she told the DailyMail.com in a previous interview.

'But my dad passed away when he was in his early fifties and I was in my early twenties… I was a junior in college. It was sudden, and scary, and terrible. But the seeds he lay for me during those 20 years, remain with me today. The things he taught me are still a part of me now.' 

Hoda, who recently shared her joy at hearing Haley say 'Mama' for the first time, is focusing on the present, and in transmitting her key values to her cherished little one.  

'My goals are just to be with her for as long as I can,' she added. 'To love her forever – of course – and to help her become the person that she is meant to be. Which I can already see is going to be someone who is kind, and good, and smart, and funny, and wonderful. 

 'I will work with what I have, I will be grateful for the years that I have, and I thank God that I have her in my life at all. I'm so thankful for the time that I get with her.'
