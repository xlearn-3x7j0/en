Brazilian fashion designer sheds 128lbs dropping ELEVEN dress sizes

A woman who had gastric bypass surgery after tipping the scales at 308lbs (22st) has shed nearly half her body weight - and revealed her model-esque physique. 

Thayna Pacheco Barboza, 24, who is from Juiz de Fora, Brazil, began putting on weight from the age of five and developed an appetite for junk food in her teens.

Her weight spiralled and, by her late teens, she was wearing a size 28 (UK 32).

Following the loss of her grandmother to heart disease, and then seeing her mother endure a heart attack, Thayna, then 18, was shocked into losing weight to save her own health.

However, the fashion designer, who has now shrunk to a svelte 128lbs (9st 2Ibs) and a size 2-4 (UK size 6-8) was forced to have even more surgery.

Thayna, unhappy with excess saggy skin left behind from the gastric bypass, has had to pay $2,391 (£1,700) to remove it.

From previously doing no exercise at all, Thayna now works out six times a week doing boxing, bodybuilding exercises and cardio in a bid to burn more fat than gain muscle mass. 

She swapped her high sugar, junk food diet for a balanced diet full of healthy natural foods. 

At her biggest Thayna explained that she was 'always confident and never felt less than anyone' but felt she needed to change following her mother's health scare.

'I started to get fat when I was five but I never had a problem with it because I was really young so I was used to it. Obviously, I didn't like my body, but it was normal for me,' Thayna said. 

'I think every dream for a fat person is become skinny so I didn't like to be fat but I also didn't put too much effort into it.'

She added 'I always liked to eat real food. Of course, I used to eat fast food and junk food but most of the time my problem was dairy food. If I could, I would have lunch for every meal of the day.'

Thayna was dealt a difficult blow just before she turned 18, when her grandmother died from heart disease, and she then received a devastating warning from her mother, who revealed she had a heart attack just three years previously due to genetics.

That is when she decided to have gastric bypass surgery, a procedure where surgical staples are used to create small pouch at the top of the stomach. 

The fashion designer admitted that she didn't do a lot of research about the drastic surgery instead just following her gut and booking in with a surgeon.  

'Since I started to get older I started to worry about my health and life so I took it more seriously.' 

Thayna said following the surgery it was difficult to adjust: 'I used to use my diet as medicine, so I followed everything my doctors said. 

'After this, I started to become interested in all types of diet and gym workouts.'

Thayna said she found it difficult to adjust to her new diet following surgery but soon became comfortable with eating differently

Thayna is now embracing her body and wants to have further surgery to remove more excess skin

Now she is embracing her body along with the support of her 54,000 followers on Instagram and is planning on doing whatever it takes to rid herself of her excess skin.

She regularly uses massage to tighten her skin and wants further surgery which will mean time away from the gym.

'I really like my body now, but I have some excess skin and this is a process that demands a lot of surgeries.

'But as I always say, I weighed 140kg for eighteen years of my life and now I can wait another eighteen years to have the body of my dreams.'

She advises anyone looking to go through similar procedures to make sure they do their research: 'After I started to understand why I ate so much, my passion for eating healthier food only grew.

'It's a slow process, as I say, it took me years of bad habits with sugar, saturated fat, flours and industrialised food.

'My biggest tip is to avoid eating anything industrialised. Our body is natural, and our food should be too.'
