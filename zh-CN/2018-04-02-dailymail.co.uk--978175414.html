YouTuber dresses like four different Bratz Dolls for a day

Nothing screams nostalgia like Bratz Dolls - at least for one YouTuber who decided to dress like the fashion figurines for four days. 

Safiya Nygaard, 26, who lives in Los Angeles, shared in a 17-minute video on her YouTube channel that said she was always a big fan of Bratz Dolls fashion as a youngster, but she was never allowed to dress like them back in the 2000s. 

So, in an attempt to relive her youth, she decided to fulfill her childhood dream of dressing like a Bratz doll with four outfits that mirrored the outfits of four of the Bratz dolls from 2001-2003.

For her first outfit, she picked Yasmin as her 'fashion muse'. 

She said: 'Yasmin's main outfit comes with a purple velour-ish velvet tube top, a pair of white jeans with orange embellishments at the bottom, purple platform shoes, a woven backpack and a brown bandana headband.' 

She added that the outfit was pretty hard to find and even tried to make her own version of Yasmin's bandana but it came out looking like a 'nun in training habits'. 

She also mentioned that everything about the outfit reminded her of clothes from the early 2000s that she wasn't allowed to wear but really wanted to. 

And although the fashion description on the back of Yasmin's box said that the doll's style is 'bohemian', Safiya said that it reminded her more of styles worn by pop and hip-hop artists of the time. 

Overall, Safiya said that the outfit was really fun but 'mildly uncomfortable' because the tube top and pants required a lot of 'adjusting on the go'.

Fashion muse: For her first outfit, she picked Yasmin as her 'fashion muse'

Sparkle: Next, she donned an outfit inspired by the Cloe Bratz Doll, which had a sparkly skinny scarf and an orange tank top

'The platform shoes were kind of like miniature stilts,' she said. She also added that the shoes made her stick out because they made her taller than everyone else. 

Next, she donned an outfit inspired by the Cloe Bratz Doll. The doll's outfit included a sparkly skinny scarf, an orange tank top, a denim maxi skirt with a lace inset, a bedazzled red belt, cowboy boots and a red and brown handbag with the word 'Angel' on the front. 

Safiya said that the outfit was pretty easy to find except for the lace inset on the skirt, which she ended up sewing in. 

Safiya said: 'This outfit to me, reads more like that boho style that Yasmin's box mentioned earlier.'

'Cloe's makeup kind of contrasts with her boho-esque clothes as her lips are frosted and lined with a hot pink color,' she added.

Winter Wonderland: The third outfit featured a blue faux fur hat, blue sunglasses and a green turtleneck

 Final outfit: The last outfit had a yellow-glittery newsboy cap, a purple faux fur jacket and a yellow tank top with the word 'chick' on it

Safiya also noted that she never had anything like this outfit growing up but she did enjoy wearing it because it's one of the more comfortable outfits. 

The third outfit was the 'winter wonderland' ensemble courtesy of the Sasha Bratz Doll.

The doll's outfit included a blue faux fur hat, blue sunglasses, a green turtleneck with a sparkly snowflake applique, a white vest, a blue-sequined belt, a green messenger bag, medium wash jeans, blue faux fur legwarmers and white platform boots. 

'This out was pretty difficult to put together,' Safiya said. 'Some things like the jeans and the vest were pretty easy to find but a lot of the embellishments had to be DIY'd and I couldn't find a green messenger bag like hers anywhere.'

But other than that, Safiya said she felt she was able to stay true to the 'spirit of her outfit'.

Overall, she said she was pretty unprepared for the snow and cold in the outfit with the jeans being the least warm. However she mentioned that the hat was handy and kept her ears warm. 

For the final outfit, Safiya wore clothes inspired by the Jade Bratz Doll. 

The outfit included a yellow-glittery newsboy cap, a purple faux fur jacket, a yellow tank top with the word 'chick' on it, a bedazzled yellow belt, dark wash flared jeans, silver platform shoes and a blue spherical-beaded handbag. 

Safiya said: 'This outfit was medium hard to find. We were able to buy everything but a lot of the sparkles and bedazzles had to be DIY'd on that hat and on the boobs.'    

'I never owned anything quite like this,' she added. 'But I did have a cap or two and I think everyone had a bedazzled T-shirt - I'm pretty sure that was just a right of passage.'

Timeless: Safiya said that although the Bratz style has faded, it 'will always have a place in our hearts'

Style pro: Safiya has done many style challenges in the past on her YouTube channel 

'I think this ended up being my favorite outfit because of the giant, purple and fuzzy jacket - it surprisingly had some swagger to it.'

And even though the Bratz style has faded, Safiya said the dolls will remain timeless. 

'Even though the popularity of Bratz dolls has faded over time I think the Bratz Dolls will always have a place in our hearts as the dolls that knocked Barbie off her pedestal and irritated our parents,' she said.

This isn't the first time Safiya has done a style challenge for her YouTube subscribers as back in December, the vlogger decided to wear only her boyfriend's clothes for a whole week to see if she could pull off his fashion and make it her own.

She explained: 'I've done many style challenges for a week, but I've never tackled menswear, so I decided to wear my boyfriend Tyler's clothing for a week to see what would happen!'

And in February, she went on a 'street style quest' and revealed how different other US cities are when it comes to fashion, by travelling to Miami, Dallas and Portland to get a makeover in each place.
