Sheffield United 1-1 Cardiff: Pilkington score 92nd-minute equaliser

They played the theme music from the Rocky movies before kick-off at Bramall Lane last night.

How appropriate given that, here, a veteran scrapper dragged his side off the canvas to show they were still in the fight for the title.

A last-minute draw for Neil Warnock’s Cardiff - thanks to Anthony Pilkington - means the Welsh side host Wolves on Friday and go toe-to-toe for a shot at the Championship crown.

Cardiff's second-half substitute Anthony Pilkington scored the equaliser in the 92nd minute

The winger wheels away in celebration as United players hold their head in their hands

A flick-on from substitute Gary Madine - a former Sheffield Wednesday man, who was booed relentlessly - found fellow replacement Pilkington who hit home to send the visiting fans potty in the 92nd minute.

‘You never say never with our lot,’ exclaimed a jubilant Warnock, whose side extended their unbeaten run on the road to 13 games. ‘But when you can grind out a result like we did it’s a great attribute.

‘That could be the best part of our season looking at that. A thousand fans coming on a wet Monday night, you can see what it means to them.

Pilkington is congratulated by his Cardiff team-mates as they snatch a point at Bramall Lane

Defender Sol Bamba applauds the fans as Neil Warnock's side kept up their push for promotion

‘If you came in our dressing room now you’d think we’d won the World Cup. We know that with seven games to go we’ve got that in our locker.

‘Our substitutes made the difference, Gary had a great header and Pilks has done nothing wrong - I’ve just not been able to get him in the team!

'That’s for his hard work and he’s been a credit to himself and he deserves that. We’ve come some lads coming back for Friday and we are really looking forward to it now. All credit to the lads, they’ve showed a lot of guts.’

For much of this contest Cardiff looked down and out, and not the side in contention for the Championship title.

That accolade would fall to Chris Wilder’s team, who were the more organised, more disciplined, and offered more in attack as Leon Clarke’s 27th-minute effort gave them a deserved lead.

John Lundstram picked the ball up in the Cardiff area with his back to goal. Looking over his shoulder he surveyed the options ahead of him and found Clarke.

With one touch on his right foot, he cannoned his shot with his left into Bruno Manga and the ball rippled across a sodden pitch and past Neil Etheridge’s outstretched arms.

The Blades could well have doubled their advantage just before the interval too, when John Fleck’s corner was met by Jack O’Connell only for Joe Bennett to clear the ball from the line.

Manager Warnock enjoys his homecoming as Cardiff leave it late on Easter Monday

Warnock’s men meanwhile, had little opportunity to get forward and exhibit the football which has taken them to second in the table. But whatever he said said to his players at the break seemed to work.

They came out firing and the match was played at an almighty pace from thereon in. Meaty tackles aplenty, two irate sets of supporters and end-to-end action.

In the space of three minutes, Cardiff could well have taken the lead. Aron Gunnarsson was first with a low, well-driven effort which veered just wide of the United post.

Kenneth Zohore followed his team-mate a moment later, blasting at Simon Moore who was able to tip the ball up and over the bar.

Leon Clarke's deflected shot trickled past Neil Etheridge in the Cardiff goal to make it 1-0

The Blades striker broke the deadlock before the half-hour mark with his first goal in 11 games

The 33-year-old celebrates with his United team-mates on a chilly Monday evening in Sheffield

United too had their chances to put the game beyond doubt. More pertinent perhaps was their appeal for a penalty when Lee Evans was felled in the Cardiff box by Sol Bamba.

The hokme fans protested at the challenge as their man lay on the ground, but referee Michael Oliver waved away their objections.

‘We’ve dominated a side thats heading towards the Premier League,’ said a deflated Wilder.

‘It was a penalty and and you’re hoping that he sees that - and the linesman who’s looking straight down the barrel of the gun. But in the lead up to that we’ve had our chances and we have to look at ourselves and be a bit more clinical.’

Blades manager Chris Wilder shakes Lee Evans hands after a fine performance on the left

Wilder was right. Although perhaps a penalty, United were their own worst enemy.

Clayton Donaldson hit the woodwork and Clarke should have done better with his last chance to score and put the game beyond Cardiff.

The visitors hung in there and as full-time approached there was a sense among those in the ground that they were ready to strike.

As added time ticked by, Madine - nodded goalward and Pilkington was on hand to send the ball rippling past Moore and send the Cardiff fans wild.

To borrow from the Rocky movies once more, this game showed, and perhaps the race for the Championship title as a whole, ‘is not over until it’s over.’
