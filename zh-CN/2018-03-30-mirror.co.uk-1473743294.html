Ringwalk time for Anthony Joshua vs Joseph Parker

Anthony Joshua can take another step towards becoming the undisputed heavyweight champion of the world when he faces Joseph Parker.

WBA, IBF and IBO champion Joshua will meet the New Zealander, who holds the WBO belt.

Victory would mean only American WBC champion Deontay Wilder stands in AJ's way for the ultimate collection of prizes.

The announcement brought an end to months of fractious negotiations with Parker, who defended his crown with a points win over Hughie Fury in Manchester last September and was holding out for a bigger percentage of the purse.

It will be Joshua's first fight since a gruelling late stoppage win over late replacement Carlos Takam last October.

Here is all you need to know about the fight...

Where and when is Joshua vs Parker?

The Englishman and Kiwi go to way on Saturday, March 31 at the Principality Stadium in Cardiff.

It's the same venue where Joshua overcame Takam in October.

Joshua and Parker are expected to walk to the ring at 10.30pm.

Who is on the undercard?

Alexander Povetkin vs David Price

What an opportunity for the lovable Liverpudlian who was last seen in action on a small-hall show in Essex last December. He takes on the dangerous Povetkin who is currently the mandatory challenger to Joshua and whose sole defeat came against Wladimir Klitschko back in 2013.

Ryan Burnett vs Yonfrez Parejo

Burnett's WBA bantamweight world title will be on the line when he takes on the Venezuelan. Parejo lost a split-decision to Zhanat Zhakiyanov three years ago before the man from Kazakhstan lost to Burnett last year in a unification clash.

Josh Kelly vs Carlos Molina

Kelly has long been earmarked as the best of the 'next generation' crop and he faces former world champion Molina in just his sixth fight as a professional.

Joe Cordina vs Hakim Ben Ali

Cordina is another highly-rated prospect and the Welshman should have little problem dispatching Ben Ali.

Anthony Crolla vs Edson Ramirez

Former lightweight world champion Crolla makes his return as he bids to launch one final shot at the top level.

Morgan Jones vs Mose Auimatagi Jnr

South Africa-born Jones lives in Wales and gets a chance to box on the biggest of stages in Cardiff.
