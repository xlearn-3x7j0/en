Tom Daley on concussion that puts Commonwealth Games golden hat-trick in doubt

Tom Daley's bid for a hat-trick of Commonwealth Games titles has been thrown into doubt after he was concussed in a training mishap, writes Alex Spink on the Gold Coast .

England's 10m platform champion of Delhi 2010 and Glasgow 2014 hit his head on the water at high speed while practising last month.

It left him with “headaches, nausea, dizziness, the whole shebang” and kept him out of the pool for almost a week.

“I missed my hands before the Beijing World Series and smashed my head at 35mph on to the water,” he revealed. “It was concussion.”

Daley only returned to the high board on Wednesday, after getting the all-clear.

“I haven’t had the preparation that I would’ve dreamed of,” admitted the 23-year-old, who starts his campaign next Friday (13th).

“But you have to make the best out of every situation and of course I want to go out and defend my title.”
