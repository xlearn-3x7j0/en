Will Brock Lesnar return to UFC? WWE contract expiry date revealed

The UFC seems to be an itch Brock Lesnar just can't scratch.

The 40-year-old has already had two spells with the mixed martial arts promotion, but looks set for a third later this year.

Lesnar first fought with the UFC between 2007 and 2011 when he became heavyweight champion.

Following a debut stoppage win over Min-Soo Kim, he was submitted by Frank Mir eight months later.

But victory over Heath Herring set him up for a shot at the world title against Octagon legend Randy Couture.

Lesnar needed just over a round to stop Couture and wrap the famous belt around his waist.

A revenge victory over Mir and a submission win over Shane Carwin saw him successfully defend his title on two occasions almost a year apart.

But as he struggled with injury and illness, Lesnar lost first to Cain Velasquez and then to Alistair Overeem.

Despite saying his defeat by Overeem was "the last time you'll see me in the Octagon", Lesnar was back in 2016.

The UFC paid him $2.5million to take on Mark Hunt at their flagship UFC 200 show - and Lesnar won by unanimous decision.

But if he thought his victory was going to be the start of another run at the title, Lesnar was soon disappointed.

He was notified that he had failed a drugs test the month before his victory over Hunt and was later given a one year ban.

Lesnar then notified the UFC last year that he was retiring from MMA once again.

But rumours have been rife in recent weeks that he has made another U-turn, before UFC president Dana White revealed the pair had held talks.

Asked about Lesnar's chances of returning to the sport, White said: "They're very, very, very good."

And with Lesnar's WWE contract set to expire after he faces Roman Reigns at Wrestlemania 34 on April 8, he will be free to make the switch,

But he will have to be granted permission by Vince McMahon if he wants to do so before August.

However, it's not that simple.

Because he retired midway through his drugs ban, Lesnar would have to complete the remaining six months of his suspension before he could return.

He would also have to re-enter the USADA drug testing pool for six months before he could walk out the Octagon.
