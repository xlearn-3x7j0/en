Who Is Mark Anthony Conditt, the Austin Bombing Suspect?

The man suspected in a series of bombings in Austin, Tex., died Wednesday morning when he blew himself up in his vehicle as officers approached, the authorities said.

A law enforcement official identified the suspect as Mark Anthony Conditt, a 23-year-old white man.

Here’s what we know

• Mr. Conditt resided in Pflugerville, Tex., about 20 miles from downtown Austin. He lived with two roommates who were both cooperating with officials, Gov. Greg Abbott told a local television station.

• Law enforcement entered the home of Mr. Conditt’s parents in Pflugerville.

• The suspect’s vehicle was traced to a hotel in Round Rock, outside Austin and near Pflugerville. A SWAT team surrounded the hotel and called for help from additional teams, but the suspect left the hotel before those units arrived.

• The explosion that killed Mr. Conditt detonated inside his vehicle and slightly injured a police officer, Austin’s police chief, Brian Manley, said.

• Mr. Conditt is believed to be linked to six bombs that killed at least two people and injured five others. Four explosions hit locations in Austin where bombs were left. An additional device detonated at a FedEx distribution center in Schertz, Tex., near San Antonio, while the sixth was found undetonated in a facility near Austin-Bergstrom International Airport.

What we don’t know

• The suspect’s motive is still unclear. The first explosions hit African-American residents whose families are well-known in the city’s black community, though two white men were injured by an explosive triggered by a tripwire on Sunday.

• It is not yet clear whether Mr. Conditt acted alone or was working with others. Officials have not ruled out the possibility that he had accomplices.

• It is not known whether the suspect mailed or placed other explosive devices that have yet to detonate.
