Man City stars EGG birthday boy Gabriel Jesus... and Bernardo Silva

  • Manchester City stars throw eggs at Gabriel Jesus to celebrate his 21st birthday
  • They also chased midfielder Bernardo Silva despite his birthday being in August
  • Goalkeeper Ederson posted a video of playful attacks on his Instagram account
  • Manchester City play Liverpool in a Champions League quarter-final this week

Published: 07:03 EDT, 3 April 2018 | Updated: 07:20 EDT, 3 April 2018

Manchester City face the biggest week of their season but the stars were behaving as if they were about to break up for the summer as they attacked Gabriel Jesus and Bernardo Silva with eggs.

The playground prank was played on the Brazilian striker as part of his 21st birthday celebrations on Tuesday. Ederson and Fernandinho splatted raw eggs on his head as he walked into the dressing room.

Silva then found himself falling victim to the same prank and was cornered by the Brazilian duo in the toilet. The Portuguese midfielder doesn't celebrate his birthday until August, so appears to have been attacked for little or no reason.

The striker appeared to take the yolk well as Manchester City trained on Tuesday morning

The videos were posted to Ederson's Instagram account. The first, featuring Jesus, comes complete with the message: 'Happy Birthday @dejesusoficial. God bless, man'. The second simply reads '@bernardocarvalhosilva also deserves to participate' in his native language.

Although there was clearly time for joking around, there would have been plenty of serious work undertaken at Manchester City's Etihad Campus on Tuesday morning. Indeed, it was the last training session ahead of their crunch game against Liverpool.

Pep Guardiola's men travel to Anfield on Wednesday for the all-English Champions League quarter-final first leg in a game that could take them one step closer to a trio of triumphs this season.

Bernardo Silva became the next target for Ederson and Fernandinho for little or no reason

Ederson and Fernandinho use the rest of their eggs on the Portuguese midfielder

Manchester City also have the chance to seal the Premier League title against local rivals United on Saturday, making this week by far the biggest of the season so far. 

Having beaten City at Anfield in the Premier League earlier this season, Jurgen Klopp says his team will make it very difficult for City if they can repeat the same game plan.

'The way we attack the opponent, the way we defend high is unpleasant (for our opponents). If we do that well they will have some difficulty coping with it,' the German told UEFA.com.

'It will be very exciting. There's no guarantee this will work. In the end it's the lads on the pitch who will decide the game,' he added.
