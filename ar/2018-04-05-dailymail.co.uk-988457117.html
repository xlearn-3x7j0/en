Real Madrid 'reignite interest' in Man United goalkeeper David de Gea

  • European champions Real Madrid are preparing for the summer transfer window
  • Spanish giants have come close to signing David de Gea in the past
  • Manchester United also monitoring Gareth Bale, Luka Modric and Toni Kroos

Published: 02:41 EDT, 5 April 2018 | Updated: 06:06 EDT, 5 April 2018

Real Madrid are keeping tabs on Manchester United trio David de Gea, Paul Pogba and Anthony Martial ahead of the summer transfer window.

The Spanish giants are keen to freshen up their squad after a disappointing domestic campaign.

And with Real monitoring the situations of three key first-team players, Jose Mourinho is believed to be looking at Gareth Bale, Luka Modric and Toni Kroos.

Real Madrid have 'reiterated their interest' in Manchester United goalkeeper David de Gea

The Spanish giants are also believed to be interested in Paul Pogba and Anthony Martial

Real have 'reiterated their interest' in de Gea and offered United first refusal on Bale, according to the Manchester Evening News.

They claim United are seeking to sign two central midfielders ahead of next season, with Michael Carrick and Marouane Fellaini set to move on, and could use Modric and Kroos in de facto swap deals.

Real have come close to signing the Spanish No 1 De Gea in the past and his contract expires next year - though United have the option of extending it until 2020.

Bale has fallen down the pecking order at the Bernabeu after another injury-plagued season and United have previously shown interest in the Wales star.

Though a deal for the winger is workable, Real are believed to have no intention of letting Kroos and Modric go.
