Why the Masters 2018 winner given the Green Jacket after victory in Augusta

Ian Woosnam has had one, Jack Nicklaus has had six, Rory McIlroy really really wants one and Tiger Woods has had four but is desperate for another.

It is one of the most iconic, and sought after, pieces of attire in sport, but why does the winner of the Masters get handed a Green Jacket?

The Masters winner in 2018 will win $11million and become an honorary member of the exclusive Augusta National Golf Club but it is the jacket that will be most highly prized.

Members started to wear the garment from 1937, after the idea was borrowed from Merseyside's Royal Liverpool course - who wore jackets to show their position.

Originally they were not popular with members, who hated the thick material which made things uncomfortable in the hot Georgia heat.

It wasn't until 1949 that the tradition to give the Green Jacket to the winner began, when Sam Snead won the first of his three tournaments around Augusta.

From then on it became custom for the previous year's winner to hand their contemporary a new Green jacket - the presentation firstly made in the Butler Cabin before repeating it in front of supporters on the 18th green.

Winners only keep the jacket for a year before returning it to Augusta, where they only wear it when returning to the course - although Gary Player "forgot" to hand it back from his home in South Africa following his 1962 triumph and Seve Ballesteros kept one in his home in Pedrena.

Interestingly, the one which is given to the winner on Sunday evening isn't the jacket they will keep.

In fact that one is owned by another member at Augusta, who shares a similar build, before the victor is measured up for the version he gets to treasure.

The big question is, who will Sergio Garcia be inducting into one of the most exclusive clubs this Sunday?
