Here Are the Top Story Lines for Friday’s Games

Best Game: Clemson vs. New Mexico State (9:40 p.m. ET)

These are teams with similar styles coming from opposite ends of the Division I spectrum. You can bet this game is going to be a battle. The Aggies are a stalwart defensive team, led by senior guard Zach Lofton (19.8 points per game), coming in with a six-game win streak. Clemson is also a tough defensive team that started 20-4 but lost five of eight down the stretch. Expect a close game.

Strange Seeds: No. 7 Nevada vs. No. 10 Texas (4:10 p.m. ET)

One game where the seeding seems topsy-turvy is No. 7 Nevada against No. 10 Texas. Nevada, from the Mountain West, played only one top 25 team all year (it lost). Texas is not a great 3-point shooting team, but that hardly matters when it has 6-11 Mohamed Bamba, the best shot blocker in the country. He is said to be fully back from a toe injury.

Marcus Foster, Creighton’s best player, got his start at Kansas State, but after two years was kicked off the team. “My sophomore year, I thought I’d figured everything out when really I didn’t,” he told CBS. “I grew up a little bit too fast. I wasn’t working out as hard as I was. I was looking forward to the future, going to the N.B.A., rather than worrying about getting through the year. And it hurt me. I wasn’t coachable.”

He headed to Creighton, where he has averaged 20 a game this season. Now he faces his old team in a close 8-9 game.

Best Chance for a Memorable Coach Moment: Georgia State vs. Cincinnati (2 p.m. ET)

Well, Georgia State Coach Ron Hunter already had his March moment when he literally fell out of his sideline chair after a game-winning shot by his son, R.J., sent the No. 14-seeded Panthers past Baylor in 2015. This year, he won’t have the chair (which he required after tearing his Achilles tendon), or his son (who’s playing in the G League) but his team could still pull off something memorable against No. 2 Cincinnati.

Best Chance for an Upset: Marshall vs. Wichita State (1:30 p.m. ET)

We already mentioned New Mexico State and Clemson, but keep an eye on Coach Dan D’Antoni’s Thundering Herd. They love to push the pace and spread the floor. And this is not your typical Shockers team. They’re ranked 110th in defensive efficiency, the lowest it’s been since 2008-09.

Best Free-Throw Shooter: Zach Thomas, Bucknell (vs. Michigan State, 7:10 p.m. ET)

Thomas, a senior for 14th-seeded Bucknell, toiled in relative obscurity in his college career. But this season he cracked the 20 point a game barrier, won Patriot League player of the year, and earned a national distinction as well. No player went to the line more often, and none made more free throws. He’ll have to make a lot to keep the Bison competitive against Michigan State, the best passing, rebounding and shot blocking team in the country.

Michigan State has two potential top 10 draft picks: dominant 6-11 freshman Jaren Jackson Jr. and 17-points-a-game athletic forward Miles Bridges.

Hardest Working: Syracuse vs. T.C.U., 9:30 p.m. ET.

Don’t be surprised to see Tyus Battle and Franklin Howard on the floor for Syracuse at crunchtime. And garbage time. And every other time as well.

Guards Battle and Howard played 39 minutes per game each, first and second in all of Division 1. And forward Oshae Brissett averaged 38. Sophomore Battle ranked third in the A.C.C. in scoring. But in part their heavy use was because Syracuse lacks depth. In their play-in game, the Orangemen starters played 40, 40 38, 37 and 34 minutes. The two bench players who got in played 6 and 5.

It has worked so far this season for the 11th-seeded Orangemen, but against 6th-seeded T.C.U., Syracuse might finally hit the wall.

Best Name: Seventh Woods, North Carolina (vs. No. 15 Lipscomb, 2:30 p.m. ET)

Not surprisingly, he’s the youngest of five children for Louis and Monica Woods. He’s also a key role player off the bench for the No. 2-seeded Tar Heels.

Best Defense: Cincinnati

Cincinnati ranked as the best defense in the country this season, holding opponents to 86 points per 100 possessions, the best figure in three years, since Kentucky (undefeated in the regular season) in 2014-15.

Frank Kaminsky finally cracked that Kentucky defense in the Final Four, but 15th-seeded Georgia State is going to have a tougher time in the first round. Georgia State did light up one opponent for 115 points this season. But it was a team called Tennessee Temple, a Christian university in Division II in Chattanooga.

Best Player: Gary Clark, Cincinnati

The American Athletic Conference’s player of the year, Clark won’t wow you offensively (13 points per game), but he does it all for the No. 2-seeded Bearcats. He led the league in rebounding, was sixth in blocks, and seventh in steals. Two years ago, the 6-8 forward was named the conference’s defensive player of the year.

Best Player You’ve Never Heard Of: Jordan Stark, Murray State

He was 16th in the nation in scoring (21.8 points per game), while shooting nearly 42 percent from 3-point range and 88 percent from the free-throw line.

Sloooooowest Team: Virginia (vs. Maryland-Baltimore County, 8 p.m. ET)

Virginia is the nation’s slowest team, plodding along to 60.5 possessions a game. That suffocating pace has led to a 31-2 record and opponents putting up totals like 42 (Vanderbilt), 37 (Wisconsin and Pitt) and 36 (Clemson). Virginia is a 21-point favorite over Maryland Baltimore County, a 16 seed who could wind up with a similar total.
