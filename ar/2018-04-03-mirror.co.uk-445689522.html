Tiger Woods profiled ahead of latest bid to win The Masters

Just a few months ago the idea of Tiger Woods being a contender for a 15th major title and first since 2008 would have been far fetched.

Spinal fusion surgery in April 2017 was his fourth back operation in the space of three years and surely a last roll of the dice, but it was a gamble which appears to have paid off.

The former world No.1 has finished 12th, second and fifth on his last three starts in 2018 and looks to be pain free, with only some understandable rustiness arguably preventing him winning for the first time since 2013.

Erratic driving is a problem but Woods was never the most accurate off the tee and few players know Augusta National better.

Here is the lowdown on his chances...

The good

Five tournaments into his latest comeback since back surgery last April, he is trending in the right direction. And the 42-year-old has won four times at Augusta and knows the course. He finished tied fourth in 2010 in his first tournament back after scandal hit his personal life.

The bad

The former world No.1 has not won since 2013 – and did not refind his old killer touch when he got in contetntion at the Valspar Championship and the Arnold Palmer Invitational. He is still struggling with his driver. And for all his course knowledge, he has not won at Augusta since 2005.

The Dougherty

Tiger Woods winning the Masters would be his biggest Major success. Because to win on the back of the bad stuff is more impressive than his magic in his prime.

He has done all the amazing things in the past but arguably this is as amazing. With all that personal baggage, all the professional baggage, the history, all the physical stuff in terms of injuries and four back surgeries. Having had my game and do what I did, and struggle with my game at the end and knowing how difficult it was to be remotely decent, to return to this level is truly remarkable.

The chipping – he had the yips. Now he has never chipped it better. People don’t do that, it just doesn’t happen in golf. People don’t come back from it and be brilliant again. He is as good as he has ever been. More than anything he has done before. I can see him winning another one. I don’t think he will get to Jack’s record.

But the thing about Tiger is that he spent he has spent his whole life making statement’s like I have just made false. He does have that capability: you never know with Tiger Woods.

What is Tiger Woods' net worth?

Woods has consistently ranked as one of the highest-paid athletes in sport and was most recently valued at $740million (£530m). Given his recent injury troubles and lack of tournament victories, Woods does not earn as much from playing golf as he did at his peak when he took him around $50m (£36m) each year.

But thanks to endorsement and sponsorship deals, Woods has created one of the biggest brands in sport, despite losing several sponsors in the wake of his sex scandal in 2009, including Gatorade and AT&T. But his biggest backer Nike stood by the golfer having been by his side since 1996, even though they stopped making golf equipment in 2017.

Woods also still has deals with TaylorMade, Bridgestone, Full Swing, Upper Deck and Rolex, among others.

Is he married?

Woods is no longer married having divorced Swedish Model Elin Nordegren in 2010 following six years of marriage, following his infidelity scandal which rocked the sport. Woods has one daughter from the marriage, Sam Alexis, who was born in 2007, and a son, Charlie Axel, who was born two years later.

Since his divorce, Woods has dated several high-profile women, including Olympic skier Lindsey Vonn. He is currently dating restaurant manager Erica Herman following his split from Kristin Smith. Erica and Tiger made their first public appearance at the 2017 Presidents Cup.
