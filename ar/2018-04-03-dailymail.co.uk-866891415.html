Expert reveals how 'envelope system' can help those on a tight budget

We live in a society where cash is now virtually non-existent. This means everything is paid for using a swipe of a card or a tap of a smartphone.

And while this makes day-to-day transactions easier, some still find it difficult to keep track of their spending habits.

Speaking on the Today show, Australian financial advisor Canna Campbell said unless you're taking the time to keep a close eye on where your money is going, it's easy to blow your budget and potentially end up in debt. 

Australian financial advisor Canna Campbell (pictured) revealed some simple ways you manage your money as we move towards a cashless society

Canna shows how her 'envelope system' can be used to budget and reveals the best apps that can help track your daily spending.

How does the envelope system work? 

Budgeting is one of the easiest ways you can get a handle on your expenses as it gives you clarity as to how much money you need in order to cover your costs. 

Canna said one of her top recommendations, which works especially well for those living pay cheque to pay cheque, is to use her 'envelope system'.

She explained the process was as simple as withdrawing the funds you need and placing this into an envelope - which corresponds to a particular expense.

She said you need to do this as soon as you have been paid.

The envelope system is as simple as putting cash in an envelope which corresponds to a particular expense and doing this as soon as you have been paid

'You then grab the envelope when you need to pay for something, and you have got enough money to survive,' she said

While Canna recommends using cash, it's also possible to create electronic envelopes too - a series of accounts linked to your main account that you can name accordingly.

Using tech to track spending

The financial expert said one of the problems people faced when using cards to pay for goods and services was they tended to spend mindlessly - and as a result often found they had less money available.

She said one simple way to take control of this was to use an app that allowed you to track your spending at the time of each purchase.

'One is the ASIC's Track My Spend - which is a fantastic app. Every time you spend money you put in what you have bought and the date and you can review it every time you log in.'

Canna also recommended her budgeting app, Sugar Budget, which she described as a 'proactive app.'

She explained this app works by allowing you to put in your living expenses and your income and then following the instructions as to what money you need to transfer to cover your costs.

'It also matches up to a calendar to remind you when your rego is due or if it's someone's birthday or it's Valentine's Day - whatever it might be,' she added.

Tips for adapting to a cashless world

How you can manage your spending: 

* Use a debit card instead of a credit card 

* Check your account balance and transactions every day

* Use an app to track your spending and record expenses at the time

 * Set a budget and review this regularly

* Think about setting some financial goals

Although paying by cash is still an option, many countries are steadily moving towards a cashless system.

Canna said there are some definite advantages to this, the main one being it's very easy to see and monitor transactions.

Her main tip for managing cashless spending is to make sure you use a debit card where possible - and check your account balance on a daily basis.

'We are getting back a lot of power and control but you do need proactively manage your money and understand where it goes,' she said.

'My financial mantra on my YouTube channel is "only buy what you love, value, use and appreciate".'
