Thrill-seeker rides down a motorway his bike in a superman pose

  • The risk-taker sped down the Pan-American highway in Buenos Aires, Argentina 
  • The motorbike flies past other vehicles with its rider lying down face-first
  •  Local media have reported that the daredevil was doing speeds of up to 80mph

Published: 15:02 EDT, 2 April 2018 | Updated: 15:05 EDT, 2 April 2018

Superman is usually spotted flying through the skies however this time he was noticed riding along a road in Argentina 'doing 80mph'.

The daredevil was filmed laying horizontal on his motorcycle as he sped down the Pan-American highway in the Argentinian capital, Buenos Aires.    

In the video, filmed from a moving car, the motorbike flies past other vehicles with its rider lying down face-first on the seat.

The biker keeps his legs horizontal off the back of his bike as he holds on to the side of the vehicle with his hands.

The risk-taking biker then begins kicking his legs up and down as he speeds along the Panamerican highway in the Argentinian capital, Buenos Aires.

The video has received over 34,000 views on social media in just 3 days.

One commenter was unimpressed by the stunt, commenting: 'Perhaps it is fun but it is extremely dangerous for him and other people.'

Local media report the man was doing over 80mph when he performed the reckless stunt.

The biker has not been identified by local media and no arrests have been reported.

The Pan-American Highway is a network of roads measuring around 19,000miles in total length.

According to the Guinness World Records, the Pan-American Highway is the world's longest 'motorable road'.
