Moyes set for contract talks with West Ham once safety is secured

  • West Ham are set to offer David Moyes a two-year deal once safety is secured 
  • The Scot has taken the club five points clear of the Premier League drop zone
  • The 54-year-old wants a hands-on role when it comes to player recruitment

Published: 19:26 EDT, 4 April 2018 | Updated: 20:20 EDT, 4 April 2018

West Ham manager David Moyes will hold talks over a new contract as soon as their Premier League status is secured and wants a hands-on role in player recruitment.

The Scot is likely to be offered a two-year deal to stay in charge of the Hammers after impressing the club’s hierarchy and has already started scouting summer transfer targets.

Moyes was at Craven Cottage on Tuesday night to watch Fulham striker Aleksandar Mitrovic in the flesh for the second time in a week. The 54-year-old also saw the Serb face Nigeria during the international break and he scored in both games in front of the former Manchester United boss.

Co-owners David Sullivan and David Gold have been impressed by Moyes, who was appointed in November when the club were inside the bottom three.

He has steered them five points clear of the Premier League’s relegation zone and oversaw a crucial 3-0 win over Southampton at the London Stadium on Saturday.

Moyes promised ruthlessness at his first West Ham press conference and has delivered, such as when Michail Antonio was dropped against Crystal Palace for turning up late to a team meeting.

He has also got Marko Arnautovic firing. The Austrian had not scored under Slaven Bilic following his £25million move from Stoke but was the star of the show in Saturday’s win over Southampton.

Moyes was left frustrated with West Ham’s business in January as they missed out on a midfielder but he will look to have a significant say in their transfers this summer.
