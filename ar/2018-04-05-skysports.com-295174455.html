Mick Fanning just misses out on fifth Pro Bells Beach title on surfing farewell

By Andy Charles

Last Updated: 05/04/18 8:09am

Surfing great Mick Fanning was narrowly denied a dream farewell to the sport in his final event in Australia.

Three-time world champion Fanning, who made headlines for fighting off a shark on live television in 2015, was beaten by Brazil's Italo Ferreira in the final of the Pro Bells Beach event.

The 36-year-old announced last month he would be retiring after the competition, which took place at a venue where he kicked off his rise to stardom in 2001 and had won on four occasions.

Fanning fought his way through the rounds to take on Ferreira, but he was unable to overcome the 23-year-old who impressed on both of his efforts to beat Fanning 15.66 to 12.83.

"It's really special, I always wanted to finish here at Bells just because I felt calmer," Fanning said.

"It's a very special moment with the crowd, all my best friends are here and then to be out in the final with Italo and just see how much it meant to him...it sort of made me feel better.

"It's been an amazing career and I want to thank everyone along the way."
