F1 overtaking is more difficult this year, claims Daniel Ricciardo

  • There were just five genuine on-track passes after the first lap at Australian GP 
  • Formula One has often been criticised for a lack of overtaking on the track
  • And Daniel Ricciardo has suggested a lack of overtaking will 'hurt' the sport
  • He said width of 2018 cars has made it even harder for drivers to entertain fans

Published: 08:15 EDT, 2 April 2018 | Updated: 09:03 EDT, 2 April 2018

Daniel Ricciardo has suggested a lack of overtaking will 'hurt' Formula One this season, believing the width of the 2018 cars has made it even harder for the drivers to entertain fans.

F1 has often been criticised for a lack of on-track passes since the sport shifted back to wider, high-downforce cars in 2017, and the curtain-raiser in Melbourne didn't provide much hope for supporters this term.

There were just five genuine overtakes after the first lap at the Australian Grand Prix, with Ricciardo and Co finding few opportunities due to a lack of space on the track

He believes the width of the 2018 cars has made it even harder for the drivers to entertain fans

And the Red Bull star admits a lack of action is concerning for the sport, and called for F1 chiefs to think less about lap times and more about entertainment. 

He told Autosport: 'I feel now with the wide tyres and wide cars, they already take up a lot of space on the track.

'It's hard to find clean air. It's getting to a point where I think some racetracks are going to be hurt by the racing. There's not going to be much.

'I think narrower cars were great. It's like motorbikes, because they're so narrow there's always room to get past. And they lap 30 seconds slower than us.

'I think it proves it's not necessarily about the lap time. We do need the raceability, because that's the spectacle.'

Ricciardo now turns his attention to this weekend's Bahrain Grand Prix, after battling to a fourth-placed finish in his home race Down Under.
