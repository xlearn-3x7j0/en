Everything you need to know about WWE Hall of Fame 2018

The iconic Goldberg will headline the Class of 2018 WWE Hall of Fame Induction Ceremony to kick off WrestleMania 34 weekend.

The WCW legend who went on to earn two WWE world championship titles will be inducted by master orator Paul Heyman.

Heyman is of course the advocate for Brock Lesnar - the man who waged war with Goldberg - and was key to booking their feud.

The former NFL player overcame Hulk Hogan to become WCW world champion during an incredible 173-match unbeaten streak.

And he will be joined at the event at the Smoothie King Center in New Orleans, Louisiana on Friday night by yet more ring greats.

The most decorated tag team of all time The Dudley Boyz aka Bubba Ray and D-Von, are renowned for their table-smashing antics.

They will be inducted by Edge and Christian - their famous rivals in classic TLC battles of the Attitude Era. But will the Hardy Boyz appear too?

Ex-WWE Women's Champion Ivory will be inducted by her former opponent Molly Holly, a performer who is deserving of the honour herself.

Ivory competed in Gorgeous Ladies of Wrestling, the 1980s programme which inspired the hit Netflix show GLOW, before her WWE career.

A moment fans thought they would never see will transpire when six-time WWE Intercontinental Champion Jeff Jarrett is inducted by Road Dogg.

Jarrett quit WWE to become a WCW world champion and later founded rival company TNA Wrestling, much to the chagrin of boss Vince McMahon.

An old ally of Hulk Hogan, the 6ft 7in country boy Hillbilly Jim has been associated with WWE for 30 years as a performer, manager and ambassador.

He will be welcomed into the hall by Jimmy 'Mouth of the South' Hart in what should be one of the more colourful segments of the glamorous evening.

Fans hope Mark Henry will don the salmon pink jacket he wore in his fake retirement speech before attacking John Cena when he earns his gold ring.

The 'World's Strongest Man' spent years inducting people into his 'Hall of Pain' as a WWE world champion, but now the powerlifter joins a hall himself.

One of the most controversial stars of the Attitude Era - just Google "Mae Young" and "hand" - will receive a heartfelt speech from close friend Big Show.

The newest entrant to the 'celebrity wing' of the Hall of Fame will be Kid Rock - the rockstar who performed at WrestleMania 25 in 2009.

It is not yet known who will induct the singer who famously provided the entrance theme for The Undertaker during his 'American Bad Ass' phase.

Meanwhile the Warrior Award will be received by 15-year-old Jarrius 'JJ' Robertson, an inspirational double liver transplant survivor.

You can watch the entire ceremony, which always provides laughter, tears and some fascinating wrestling anecdotes - for absolutely NOTHING.
