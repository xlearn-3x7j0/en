Liverpool 3-0 Manchester City PLAYER RATINGS

Liverpool scored three goals inside the opening 31 minutes as they ripped apart Manchester City in the Champions League quarter-final, first leg tie at Anfield.

Mohamed Salah - with his 38th goal of the season - Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain and Sadio Mane all scored in front of The Kop. 

Sportsmail's Dominic King rated the players on Wednesday night.

Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain saves his best for City and his super strike put Liverpool in control

LIVERPOOL (4-3-3)

Loris Karius – 7

Could not have foreseen being so underworked for the bulk of the game. Composed in the moment he needed to be.

Trent Alexander-Arnold – 8

Biggest test of his young career and he passed with it flying colours; provided the ball that opened City’s defence up for the first time.

Dejan Lovren – 8

When he’s good, he is very good and one crucial interception, which took the ball off Silva in the second half, was superb.

Virgil Van Dijk – 8

Looked a £75million defender, breaking up play with perfectly timed interventions and always providing a calming presence.

Defender Dejan Lovren has had his critics this season but he produced an awesome display

Andrew Robertson – 8

One of Anfield’s most popular figures, tackled and chased as if his life depended on it.

Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain – 9

Saves his best performances for when City are in town. Fabulous strike put his team in control.

Jordan Henderson – 7.5

As ever with the captain, nothing flash or fancy but a crucial role in setting the tempo. Always available for a pass.

James Milner – 8.5

Wise old head in midfield was never going to flustered by the occasion, made several telling tackles and covered miles.

Veteran James Milner played key role with several telling tackles and covered miles

Mohamed Salah – 8

Ran Laporte ragged and looked at times as if he could settle the tie on his own. Does what he always does: score.

Roberto Firmino – 7.5

Not many opportunities to score for the Brazilian but that hard running and persistence upset City’s defence.

Sadio Mane – 8

Being sent-off at the Etihad Stadium last September has haunted him but another goal and top display against City should finally exorcise that memory.

MANAGER: Jurgen Klopp – 8.5

True to his word and let Liverpool off the leash. The result were spectacular and he will be overjoyed with the result but he will know there is still work to do.

MANCHESTER CITY (4-3-3)

Ederson – 6

Hard to pin any blame on him for the three goals; looked as shocked as the rest of his team-mates over what happened.

Kyle Walker – 5

Completely caught out as Mane got on the wrong side of him to score the third goal in a frenzied spell.

Vincent Kompany – 6

With his experience, you would have expected authority and leadership but he couldn’t get to grips with Firmino.

Nicolas Otamendi – 5

Looked like the defender over whom there have been questions in the past. Not once in his comfort zone.

Kyle Walker was completely caught out as Mane got on the wrong side of him to score the third

Aymeric Laporte – 4.5

Didn’t know whether he was coming or going as Salah spun him one way then the other. Surprise to see him continue.

Kevin De Bruyne – 6

No chance to get into the areas where he can inflict damage; every time he got the ball two red shirts pounced on him.

Fernandinho – 6

One fabulous ball in the second half almost created an opening but couldn’t wrestle authority from Milner.

David Silva – 6

It looked in the early stages like he would rip Liverpool to shreds but then a tide washed over City and he was helpless.

City defender Aymeric Laporte didn’t know whether he was coming or going at Anfield

Ilkay Gundogan – 5

Shock to see him included at the expense of Raheem Sterling, no surprise when he was later substituted.

Gabriel Jesus – 4.5

One overhead kick late in the second half flew wide. No physical presence up front and was booked when his frustrations boiled over.

Leroy Sane – 5.5

Two bad mistakes contributed to two of Liverpool’s goals; he is fighter, though, and never hid from a pass despite his off night.

MANAGER: PEP GUARDIOLA – 5.5

Provided one of the images of the night when he turned around, arms out wide, wondering what had gone on. The manager who often has the answers had none this time. Leaving Sterling out a huge call.

REFEREE: Felix Brych (Germany) – 8.5

Let the game flow and it created a fabulous spectacle. Never saw him, which is the biggest possible compliment.
