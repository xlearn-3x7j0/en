Coleman pledges future to Sunderland even if they are relegated

  • Sunderland are bottom of the Championship and eight points adrift of safety
  • Chris Coleman is preparing his side for Saturday's clash trip to Leeds
  • The Black Cats boss insisted he wants to remain in the job if they are relegated 

Published: 11:25 EDT, 5 April 2018 | Updated: 11:44 EDT, 5 April 2018

Chris Coleman wants to remain Sunderland manager even if the club is relegated into League One.

The Black Cats currently sit at the foot of the Championship, eight points adrift of safety with just six games remaining, but the man who guided Wales to the Euro 2016 semi-finals still sees his future at the Stadium of Light.

Speaking ahead of Saturday's trip to Leeds, Coleman said: 'It's not about the division we are in, it's about who owns the club and what the plan is.

'It won't be because I'm saying I don't want to be here, not at all. I wanted to manage Sunderland whichever league. It will be what is the future, who is the owner and what are the plans for the club?

'Am I in the plans? Am I not? The answer to where my future is, is with someone else. I don't know where we will be yet or who will be with me or who will be here full stop.

'I hope to God I am here. I want to be here.'

Coleman's continued presence could depend largely on what owner Ellis Short decides to do at the end of the season with the club up for sale and little prospect of significant investment if it remains in the American's hands.

However, the Welshman insists any new owner would not have any difficulty in drawing up a blueprint for the future.

He said: 'All that anybody has to do who buys this club has to do is look at it, the last four or five years, and do it completely different.'
