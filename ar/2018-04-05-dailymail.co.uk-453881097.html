Fernando Alonso insists McLaren can fight for F1 podiums

  • Neither Fernando Alonso nor McLaren have been on the F1 podium since 2014
  • Alonso remains optimistic that the team can achieve a top three finish this term
  • Spaniard finished a distant fifth last time out at the Australian Grand Prix
  • Sakhir Circuit will hold second race of the year on Sunday for Bahrain Grand Prix
  • READ: All the latest F1 news, features and points tables 

Published: 11:16 EDT, 5 April 2018 | Updated: 12:37 EDT, 5 April 2018

Fernando Alonso has challenged McLaren to improve the package of his car in a bid to return the Spaniard and the Woking outfit to the podium in Formula One.

The two-time world champion finished a credible fifth place in the season opener in Australia but McLaren still looked short of pace compared to the front running teams of Mercedes, Ferrari and Red Bull.

It has been four years since either Alonso or McLaren have been on the podium but the two-time world champion believes the team face a crucial couple of months to tap into the potential of the MCL33.

Fernando Alonso claims McLaren face a crucial two months to see how their season will fair

Alonso was joined in Sakhir by Kimi Raikkonen (left) and Valtteri Bottas for Thursday's press conference ahead of the Bahrain Grand Prix weekend

'It's quite a reasonable gap to close but it is up to us now,' Alonso said in Sakhir on Thursday ahead of the Bahrain Grand Prix. 

'The next two months are crucial for us. If it is not a world championship fight, it will hopefully be some podium positions.

'It is probably the first time in the last three years it is up to us - the car has the potential.'

Those three years were a result of McLaren's disastrous partnership with Honda, which saw the power unit supplier hold much of the blame for the team's slump down the constructor standings - with McLaren finishing second from last in ninth last term.

Alonso is optimistic that with Renault power now on board, the team once again have a realistic chance of gatecrashing the top three.

'Yes. We were two places from the podium in Australia. Anything can happen,' Alonso added. 'If you are close to that position, sooner or later that opportunity will come.

'We had some ups and downs in [pre-season] testing and then in Australia it was the same thing and the qualifying was not smooth enough to show the potential. It was a good race, help from the safety car, but we take this fifth place and move on.

'It is up to the team to deliver the performance in the next four, five, six races.'

Alonso finished ahead of Max Verstappen's Red Bull to claim fifth at the Australian Grand Prix
