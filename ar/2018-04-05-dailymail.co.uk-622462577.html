CMU student 'who shot dead both his parents' UNFIT to stand trial

The attorney for a 19-year-old student accused of killing his parents at Central Michigan University says his client has been found incompetent to stand trial.

Court-appointed attorney Joshua Blanchard says James Davis Jr. was found incompetent to stand trial March 23. 

Blanchard told MLive.com on Wednesday that Davis Jr. will receive treatment in an attempt to have him regain competency.

Davis Jr's parents had just picked him up from a hospital that Friday morning. 

Davis Jr. will receive treatment in an attempt to have him regain competency 

They were believed to have been helping him move out for spring break on March 2 when he allegedly grabbed his father's pistol from their vehicle.

It was then that the teen went back to his room on the fourth floor of Campbell Hall and shot both his parents.  

He was captured after a 16-hour manhunt in Mount Pleasant, Michigan, and had been using railroad tracks to make his escape. 

He's charged with two counts of open murder and a firearm violation. The open murder charges include both first- and second-degree murder.

The night before the shootings, Davis Jr. told police someone was out to kill him. He exhibited erratic behavior that police suspected was drug-induced. 

Davis Jr at appeared in Isabella County Trial Court via video to be arraigned from McLaren Central Michigan Hospital. 

A motive is still unknown but police are working to see if there was one.
