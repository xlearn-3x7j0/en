Eagle-eyed Liverpool fans spot what Salah said to Laporte at half-time

Mohamed Salah was spotted apologising to Manchester City's Aymeric Laporte just moments after the half-time whistle was blown during Liverpool's Champions League quarter-final clash on Wednesday.

The hosts entered the interval 3-0 up after a blistering first half.

The Egyptian opened the scoring before Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain and Sadio Mane made it 3-0 after just 31 minutes.

It was an emphatic first 45 from Liverpool, but the home fans were still composed enough to spot what Salah said to Laporte just before walking off the pitch.

The Egyptian was seen on camera walking up to the French defender, holding out his hand as a peace offering and saying "I'm sorry".

While the exact reason behind his apology is unclear, Liverpool fans firmly believe it was because he gave the defender a very tough 45 minutes.

One Reds fan tweeted: "Did Mo Salah just apologise to Laporte for making him look stupid for 45minutes?"

Another posted: "Salah just apologised to Laporte for giving him the toughest 45 minutes of his life. What a man what a player."

Liverpool fans have good reason to think the way they do as it's not the first time Salah has apologised to an opposing player for wreaking havoc.

The 25-year-old smashed four past Watford last month and was seen saying sorry to Orestis Karnezis at full-time.
