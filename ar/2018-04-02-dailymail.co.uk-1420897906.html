Get fit in 15 minutes: Strengthen your core

  • Celebrity personal trainer Nadya Fairweather says you should try flamingo stand
  • For an additional challenge, try lifting the standing foot’s heel off the floor.
  • To improve your balance, try adding more spinach and ginger to your diet

Published: 20:28 EDT, 1 April 2018 | Updated: 05:20 EDT, 2 April 2018

WHAT TO DO 

To improve your sense of balance and strengthen your core, try a flamingo stand, says celebrity personal trainer Nadya Fairweather (u-shape.co.uk).

Stand on one foot, with both arms outstretched to the sides.

Once you’ve found your balance, close your eyes and hold this position for 10-20 seconds. Switch feet and repeat on the other side.

For an additional challenge, try lifting the standing foot’s heel off the floor.

Hold 5-10 seconds. Repeat three times on both sides.

WHAT TO EAT  

Our balance is dramatically affected by our inner ear health and diet can help with this, says nutritionist Shona Wilkinson (shonawilkinson.com).

Ginger is often used to ease motion sickness, as it was historically taken by sailors for sea sickness. This is because it can help our balance.

Try having a ginger tea or adding grated ginger to your food.

Spinach is also high in Vitamin B6, which can help to reduce the dizziness that often comes with poor balance.

It is beneficial when added either cooked or uncooked to meals, so make sure to eat plenty of this.
