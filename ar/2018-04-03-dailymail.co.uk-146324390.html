Philadelphia suburb uses mystery grease to keep fans off street poles

Philadelphia Police officer greases a traffic light pole as security measure for Super Bowl LII fans on February 4

The police in the Philadelphia suburbs are not expecting the same delirious outpouring that followed the Eagles' Super Bowl victory over the New England Patriots, but the Radner police department is on alert with Villanova's men's basketball team playing for a national title against Michigan in San Antonio on Monday night.

Following in the footsteps of the Philadelphia police, who had the street poles greased ahead of the Eagles' final two postseason games of the 2017 campaign, the Radner police also ordered that the poles be greased. But unlike Philadelphia, which used Crisco before ahead of the Eagles' NFC Title Game matchup with the Minnesota Vikings, Radner's grease is currently a mystery.

'I don't know what they're using but some poor guy from Villanova has to smear it all over the place,' Radnor Police Lt. Chris Flanagan told the Philadelphia Inquirer. 'We just don't want anybody falling, that's the biggest problem.'

The police in Ann Arbor, Michigan – home of the Wolverines – are also taking precautions.

Ann Arbor police made four arrests after Michigan's Final Four win over Loyola-Chicago 

Fans hold signs during the last Villanova Wildcats Championship Parade on April 8, 2016

The Ann Arbor police already made four arrests on Saturday night as hundreds of Michigan students and Wolverines fans poured onto the streets surrounding the campus after the school's Final Four victory over Loyola-Chicago in San Antonio. Should the Michigan men beat Villanova, the police could see thousands of fans mixing with traffic, which is why the department posted a warning on Facebook Sunday evening.

'As we prepare for Monday night's big game we'd remind you that we love to see fans celebrate their team,' read the social media post, per MLive.com. 'However, there are some things which WILL earn you a time out.

'Climbing poles, trees, signs or buildings; throwing anything; interfering with traffic; damaging property; lighting fires; and any other behavior that puts others at risk will not be tolerated, police said.'

Ann Arbor's sanitation department removed trash bins and benches near the campus ahead of Monday's 9.20 tip-off, according to MLive.com.

As in Philadelphia, the Ann Arbor police are looking to keep fans safe in the event of a victory
