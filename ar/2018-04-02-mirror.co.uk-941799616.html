8 contenders for The Masters as Tiger Woods bids for 5th Green Jacket at Augusta

The Masters is here with the world's best players set to compete for the Green Jacket in the year's first major.

The usual excitement has been ramped up by Tiger Woods' return to contention, having overcome back surgery.

A plethora of elite players have won on the PGA tour this season, too, including Rory McIlroy, Justin Thomas, Dustin Johnson, Paul Casey, Jason Day, Bubba Watson and Phil Mickelson.

Former champion Jordan Spieth has also rediscovered his form with a third placed finish at last week's Houston Open, which was dramatically won by Ian Poulter, enabling the fiery Englishman to sneak into the field at the final opportunity.

Sergio Garcia sparked emotional scenes with his first major title last year at Augusta, edging out Justin Rose, and the Spaniard will hope to put up a good defence, despite talk centered on a host of other players.

Here are eight contenders primed to make a run at the Green Jacket on Sunday.

Rory McIlroy

After an excellent start to the season in Abu Dhabi and Dubai, McIlroy's form suffered across the Atlantic as he missed the cut in two of his first four PGA Tour events.

However, a putting lesson from Brad Faxon before the Arnold Palmer Invitational paid instant dividends with an impressive three-shot victory at Bay Hill, courtesy of some superb putting in a closing 64.

McIlroy's three attempts to complete the career grand slam by winning the Masters have resulted in finishes of fourth, 10th and seventh, although he has yet to be truly in contention on Sunday afternoon. Could thrive this time with self-confidence restored.

Jordan Spieth

The 2015 champion has gradually rediscovered his game in recent weeks, concluding with a third placed finish at the Houston Open.

A lengthy par save on the 18th sounded a warning to his rivals that he is once again deadly with the short stick and when Spieth is hot on the putting greens, it's difficult to imagine anybody hanging with him.

Justin Thomas

Still to reach the pinnacle of the sport atop of the world golf rankings, though many would pick JT as the best player in the world over the last 12 months.

An incredible 2017, which included a maiden major victory at the PGA Championship and the FedEx Cup, has been backed up with a win this year at the Honda Classic.

Second and fourth placed finishes have followed, confirming JT as one of the game's most formidable players and a serious contender to win a second major at Augusta.

Tiger Woods

Just a few months ago the idea of Woods being a contender for a 15th major title and first since 2008 would have been far fetched.

Spinal fusion surgery in April 2017 was his fourth back operation in the space of three years and surely a last roll of the dice, but it was a gamble which appears to have paid off.

The former world number one has finished 12th, second and fifth on his last three starts in 2018 and looks to be pain free, with only some understandable rustiness arguably preventing him winning for the first time since 2013. Erratic driving is a problem but Woods was never the most accurate off the tee and few players know Augusta National better.

Phil Mickelson

Almost as unlikely a contender as his great rival Woods, Mickelson claimed his first win since the 2013 Open Championship with his play-off success over Justin Thomas in the WGC-Mexico Championship.

And it was no fluke either, the 47-year-old left-hander having finished in the top six of each of his previous three events.

Three times a winner at Augusta, Mickelson has also recorded 12 other top-10s, the most recent being a tie for second behind Jordan Spieth in 2015.

Dustin Johnson

Johnson was a 5/1 favourite to win the Masters 12 months ago after victory in the Genesis Open took him to the top of the world rankings for the first time and was promptly followed by further success in the WGC-Mexico Championship and WGC-Dell Technologies Match Play.

Johnson sensibly withdrew from the Houston Open to rest, only to then fall down some stairs at his rental house in Augusta and - after a last-gasp fitness test - be forced to withdraw from the year's first major.

The 33-year-old had finished sixth and fourth at Augusta in his previous two starts and began 2018 with an eight-shot win in the Sentry Tournament of Champions.

Paul Casey

Casey has recorded nine top-10 finishes in major championships, with five of them coming in the Masters and three in succession.

Failing to convert the 54-hole lead in last year's Tour Championship again highlighted questions over the former Ryder Cup player's ability to close out victories in tight situations, but he finally ended a nine-year win drought on the PGA Tour with victory in the Valspar Championship, with Woods in a share of second.

Bubba Watson

Bubba Golf is back in 2018 with two wins at the Genesis Open and the WGC Match Play.

The two-time winner of the Green Jacket is aiming to replicate Phil Mickelson's run at Augusta of three wins in six years after victories in 2012 and 2014.

His brutal 7 and 6 victory over Kevin Kisner in the final of the Match Play proves his shot-making ability is on point. Bubba's incredible ability to manipulate the shape of his ball will always give him a chance at Augusta.
