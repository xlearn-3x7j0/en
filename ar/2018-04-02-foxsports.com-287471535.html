The Stanley Cup playoffs are almost here.

The Minnesota Wild are on pace to extend their playoff streak to six straight seasons, but things are heating up in the Western Conference playoff race.

Check out our thoughts on the week ahead below, from first place in the Central Division through a few playoff hopefuls battling for the two wild-card spots. First, a reminder: The top three teams in each division make the playoffs, as well as two wild-card teams.

AP

If the season ended today the Wild would be …

In. The Wild are in the Central Division's third playoff spot, three points ahead of the Colorado Avalanche and the second wild-card spot. Like last week, the Wild draw the Winnipeg Jets in the first round.

USA TODAY Sports Christopher Hanewinckel

Nashville Predators (113 points)

Record: 51-17-11

This week: at Florida, at Washington, vs. Columbus

Notables: Eeli Tolvanen is here. The Preds' uber-prospect debuted Saturday in an anticlimactic loss to Buffalo, slotting in on Nashville's top line.

USA TODAY Sports Christopher Hanewinckel

Winnipeg Jets (106 points)

Record: 48-20-10

This week: at Ottawa, at Montreal, vs. Calgary, vs. Chicago

Notables: Are the Jets … maybe a little vulnerable, or at least, a little more vulnerable than they seem? They won six straight at home against playoff contenders, but four went to overtime and two of those went to a shootout. The Jets followed that up with a 6-2 road loss to Chicago in which they were shut out in the third period by a 36-year-old accountant.

AP Photo AP

Minnesota Wild (96 points)

Record: 43-25-10

This week: vs. Edmonton, at Anaheim, at L.A., at San Jose

Notables: That last stretch was looking scary before Ryan Suter went down, but the Wild are very close to clinching. A win Monday over the Oilers and a regulation win for Los Angeles would do it.

USA TODAY Sports Marilyn Indahl

WC 1 - Los Angeles Kings (94 points)

Record: 43-28-8

This week: vs. Colorado, vs. Minnesota, vs. Dallas

Notables: There's upside for the Kings all over the place heading into this final homestand. They're 3-0 against the Avalanche this season, the Wild will almost surely be down two key defensemen and Dallas is bad. The Kings are one point behind Anaheim for the third playoff spot in the Pacific Division.

USA TODAY Sports Robert Mayer

WC 2 - Colorado Avalanche (93 points)

Record: 42-28-9

This week: at L.A., at San Jose, vs. St. Louis

Notables: This spot could be on the line until the final day of the regular season, when the Avalanche host the Blues. Can Nathan MacKinnon drag this team across the finish line? Probably. Give the man his Hart Trophy already.

AP Photo AP

St. Louis Blues (92 points)

Record: 43-29-6

This week: vs. Washington, vs. Chicago, at Chicago, at Colorado

Notables: The Blues are just a point behind the Avalanche after earning at least a point in seven straight. Then they fell 6-0 to the Arizona Coyotes. Again, that season finale is looming pretty large down the stretch.

AP Photo AP

Dallas Stars (88 points)

Record: 40-31-8

This week: at San Jose, at Anaheim, at L.A.

Notables: St. Louis has a game in hand on the Stars, who would probably need to beat San Jose, Anaheim and L.A. on the road in their final three to even have a shot. They won four games total in the month of March.
