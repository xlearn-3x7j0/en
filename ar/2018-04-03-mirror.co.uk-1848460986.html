Justin Holbrook heaps praise on Matty Smith but admits keeping him will be tough

St Helens coach Justin Holbrook wants to retain in-demand scrum-half Matty Smith but admits it is becoming more difficult each week.

Two-time Grand Final winner Smith made his first appearance of the season as a sub against Widnes on Monday, having struggled to force his way past Danny Richardson , Jonny Lomax and Theo Fages this year.

The 30-year-old former England international has been linked with loan moves to Catalans, Hull KR and Salford, but his coach still wants him to stay.

Holbrook said: “My opinion hasn’t changed - I’d love to keep him here because he’s not just covering half-back, he’s covering a number of positions.

“We’ve got the luxury of Jonny being able to move if he’s needed anywhere else, which then brings Matty into play, whether he’s starting or on the bench.

“I see him as a real core part of our 19, but at the moment he’s not sneaking into our 17, which is hard for him.

“He’s been in the 19 every week, so he’s only one opportunity away from playing every week.

“But that’s easy for me to say, and hard for him to not play.

“I know he wants to play, as a lot of players do, and it’s not good for anybody that’s Super League standard not to be playing Super League.

“But with his experience as a Grand Final-winning player, and a man of the match in those kind of games, I understand it’s even harder than a 19 or 20 year old kid knocking on the door.

“He’s been great at training for us and it was great to give him a run on the weekend.

“I’m not sure if he’ll feature against Hull on Friday or not yet.”

Holbrook says Smith’s attitude around the club cannot be questioned despite playing the role of understudy to rising talent Richardson.

Holbrook added: “Matty’s been great not just for Danny but for the whole group - I can’t fault him.

“He’s been fantastic for the squad, the way he trains and the way he picks the boys up.

“Even before games he goes round and wishes every player the best.

“Everybody likes him including myself - he’s a really likeable guy.

“My ideal scenario is that he stays here, but it gets harder and harder for him.”
