Sunderland splashed out £4.4m on agents' fees this season

  • Sunderland spent £4.37m on agents' fees in the summer and January windows
  • Yet the Championship strugglers spent only £1.25m on players this campaign
  • Aston Villa spent the most on agents' fees in the Championship with £5.51m
  • Sunderland are second-bottom of the Championship five points from safety 

Published: 14:09 EDT, 3 April 2018 | Updated: 15:06 EDT, 3 April 2018

Sunderland have spent more than three times more on agents' fees than they have on new signings since the closure of the January 2017 transfer window. 

Figures released by the FA show that Sunderland splashed out £4.37million on intermediary and agents' fees, the second-highest total in the Championship behind Aston Villa's £5.51m. 

Yet despite the outlay for agents, Sunderland have spent just £1.25m on players in the last two transfer windows - summer 2017 and January 2018. 

Sunderland have spent £4.37m on agents' fees since the closure of the January 2017 window

That figure was reached with the signings of James Vaughan and Jason Steele for £500,000 each and Aiden McGeady for £250,000. 

The 12 other signings Sunderland have made this season have been free transfers or loan deals while they have also changed their manager, bringing in Chris Coleman from his position with the Welsh national team. 

Sunderland are currently battling against relegation to League One and sit second-bottom of the Championship table with 31 points from 40 matches. 

Sunderland spent just £1.25m on new signings - including Aiden McGeady for £250,000

The Mackems have endured a disastrous campaign and are looking to avoid being relegated in consecutive seasons after finishing bottom of the Premier League last season. 

Supporters have begun voting with their feet with attendances at the Stadium of Light dwindling, Sunderland will now close their upper tier for next season in a bid to improve atmosphere inside their 49,000-capacity ground. 

Sunderland owner Ellis Short is willing to relinquish control of the club for free if another buyer is prepared to take on their debts - which was last reported at £137m. 

Bolton spent the least on agents' fees in the Championship with £223,000 while top-of-the-table Wolves spent £2m.
