Neuer completes first Bayern Munich training session for SIX months

  • Bayern Munich released pictures of Manuel Neuer in goalkeeper training
  • Neuer's keeper session is his first since suffering a broken foot in September
  • Club captain is now hoping to regain full fitness for the World Cup this summer
  • Neuer also skippers Germany who will travel to Russia as defending champions 

Published: 06:02 EDT, 5 April 2018 | Updated: 07:21 EDT, 5 April 2018

It has been a miserable season for Manuel Neuer but despite missing most of the campaign with injury the German goalkeeper could make a timely return for the World Cup. 

It is the sight that Bayern Munich and Germany fans have been waiting for most of the season to witness as the 32-year-old was pictured in training with the German champions.

Neuer was seen practising his handling and general goalkeeping skills as he nears a return to action.

Manuel Neuer has completed his first goalkeeper training session in over six months

The Bayern Munich captain has been out since September following surgery on a broken foot

The club stated that the goalkeeper-specific session was the first completed by the stopper for the first time in over half-a-year.

Neuer has not featured for club nor country since the middle of September when he played the full 90 minutes in Bayern's 4-0 win over Mainz.

After breaking his left foot last April, the keeper suffered a setback to the same foot in a training session shortly after, forcing him into surgery. Initial estimates claimed Neuer would be out until January.

But recovery time has proved slower and he is set to complete a full return ahead of the final four league games of the season. 

Neuer was seen back in training for Bayern Munich on Thursday as he nears a return

The Bayern keeper will be desperate to return to full fitness for this summer's World Cup finals

Germany's No 1 is targeting to get back to full fitness for this summer's tournament in Russia, where he will hope to lead out the defending champions in his role as captain.

His replacement at club level in Sven Ulreich has managed to fill the void well, with Bayern on the cusp of winning the German top flight for the sixth season in succession, needing just one more win from their final six games.

The German giants are also on the brink of the Champions League semi-finals, after winning 2-1 at Sevilla in their first leg tie away from home in the last eight.
