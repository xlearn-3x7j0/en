Video shows three armed men trying to force way into home in Chicago

Surveillance video has emerged showing three armed men attempting to burglarize a home turn back because the resident of the house pushed the door from the inside.

The incident took place Monday morning in the Irving Park section of Chicago’s North Side.

In the video, three men seen wearing clothing that conceals their faces sneak into the backyard of a home on West Warner Avenue.

The men are seen with backpacks. One of the men wields what appears to be a metal crowbar.

As they climb up the wooden stairs, they approach the backdoor entrance to the home.

During the entire time, they are being filmed by surveillance cameras from Ring.com, the popular home security system that allows home owners to keep tabs on their properties by looking at their phones.

As the men tried to force their way inside, they were met with resistance.

The resident, described as a 25-year-old man, was holding the door as the three men kicked and pushed from the outside.

After about a minute, the three men give up and leave the area.

The surveillance video was sent to the local web site CWBChicago.

Authorities are now searching for the three men.

Police believe at least two of the suspects were armed with handguns.

One of the men seen in the video wearing a dark sweater is described as a white male in his thirties. He is about 6-feet tall and weighs 220 pounds.

Another would-be burglar was described as a black male in his twenties. He is 6-feet tall and weighs 150 pounds.

Police believe the black man was armed.

The third suspect is a white male, but no description was available.
