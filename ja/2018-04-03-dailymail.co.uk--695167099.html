Katherine Jenkins reveals her holidays of a lifetime

  • The 37-year-old recalls the first holiday she can remember, to France
  • A trip to Port Elizabeth in South Africa is picked out as her favourite trip
  • And her dream destination? That would be Bora Bora or Fiji 

Published: 17:01 EDT, 31 March 2018 | Updated: 17:01 EDT, 31 March 2018

Continuing our series featuring the holiday memories of famous people, this week opera singer Katherine Jenkins, 37, recalls her life of adventures…

First holiday I can remember: In a caravan in the South of France, on a campsite with my dad cooking on a barbecue, sitting outside playing cards using pistachio nuts instead of gambling chips.

My first trip abroad: I’ve only got a vague snapshot memory of going to Spain as I could only have been about four or five years old. It was with my mum, dad and my nana.

Sun lover: Katherine, with husband Andrew in 2017. She recalls here how she honeymooned with him in the Maldives

My first school trip: Was to Penscynor Wildlife Park in South Wales. It’s since closed but it used to be our local bird and animal sanctuary.

My honeymoon: Andrew and I went to the Maldives and had a bungalow on stilts over the water. There were no cars so you cycled around the island and it was absolutely romantic and the perfect break. I’m really hoping my husband will take me back there one day.

Best holiday: I went to Port Elizabeth in South Africa when I was pregnant with my daughter and we had a chance to dart a rhino. My husband and I are involved with conservation, in particular trying to halt the illegal horn and ivory trade, and this was an opportunity for us to be on safari but most importantly to learn how they’re trying to deal with this. Sitting with a rhino who’d been tranquillised and feeling her heartbeat when I was expecting a child was one of the most incredible things I’ve ever experienced.

Katherine is pictured here as a child, boating with her father, Selwyn

Worst trip: Usually I do a lot of research to make sure I know all the pros and cons of where I’m going, but I went to Gran Canaria at short notice and thought I was getting a beach holiday. While the hotel was technically close to a beach – it was via a massive cliff face. To get there you had to go down thousands of steps.

My essential item: Sarah Chapman Skin Insurance sun block. I love lying out in the sun but you have to make sure you’re looking after yourself and this sunblock is lovely.

Dream destination: I haven’t been to Bora Bora or Fiji or that part of the world yet. Continuing the beautiful paradise beach theme, I think it would be lovely and a real getaway.
