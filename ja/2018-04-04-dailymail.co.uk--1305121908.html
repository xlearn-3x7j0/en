This 'bad mom' is shutting down mommy shamers

A 29-year-old mother shut down everyone who has judged her parenting skills by posting a spoof photo of herself chugging a bottle of wine while her kids sit next to her looking dirty and disheveled.

Personal trainer and fitness blogger, Sia Cooper from Destin, Florida has been a victim of mommy shaming on social media as people continue to judge everything she does as mother.

After being called a bad mom for just about everything from working out during pregnancy to having tattoos, Sia finally responded to the haters saying mothers who judge other women are the real 'bad moms.'

Mommy shaming: A 29-year-old mom is shutting down people who judged her parenting skills by posting this spoof photo of her chugging wine from the bottle while her kids sit next to her

Bad mom: Fitness blogger, Sia Cooper from Destin, Florida has been called a 'bad mom' on social media so she posted this photo portraying the things she's been shamed for 

The real bad moms: After being called out for everything from working out during pregnancy to having tattoos, the mother-of-two said moms who judge are the real 'bad moms'

Sia posted a photo of herself with her two kids to Instagram and it immediately went viral with over 23,000 likes. 

In the photo Sia is pictured sitting on her front porch wearing a sports bra and leggings while chugging a bottle of wine.

With one hand she's guzzling the alcohol while the other arm is resting on her hip with the words, 'Bad Mom' written on her tricep.

Sitting next to Sia is her two-year-old daughter Everly, with chocolate all of her face and next to the little girl is her brother Greyson, four, who is playing on his iPad not looking at the camera.

The photo was meant to be a spoof of Sia doing all of the things people have judged her for in the past. 

The caption to the photo reads, 'If I had a nickel for every time I've been called a "bad mom," I would be soooo rich! 

'It seems almost impossible to be a textbook or politically correct good mom these days because everywhere you turn another mom is judging your parenting choices. Am I right?'

She listed all of the insane reasons she's been judged before explaining that the ones who judge are the real bad people. 

'I've learned that the true "bad moms" out there are the ones who constantly tear other moms down by judging them. 

'Those moms are the ones who are truly insecure and have strong feelings of inadequacy because why else would they do that? Misery loves company.

'There's no one right way to parent or to be a mom. We all are running in the same race and doing the best that we can. 

'Motherhood is not a one size fits all-what works for one family may not work for the next. So who are we to judge another mom's choices or reasoning?' she wrote. 

All in the same boat: Sia (pictured with daughter Everly) shared that she's been shamed for working out while having kids and caring about her looks

Be nice: The fitness blogger admitted that she's finally 'learned that the true bad moms out there are the ones who constantly tear other moms down by judging them'

Reasons for being judged: The personal trainer said she's been judged for just about everything including not covering up around her kids and having abs

Sia continued to list the reasons she has been shamed, 'I've been called a bad mom for: Working out during pregnancy, working out while having kids... period, for caring about my looks and health and working out in Target.'

She continued to list some more reasons, 'Using canned goods and plastic crockpot liners, having tattoos and piercings and enjoying wine every now and then,' which is why she is chugging wine in the photo. 

'Not "covering up" around my kids, running a full time business from home, co-sleeping with my kids, collecting sports cars and motorcycles aka having a hobby, taking time for myself and having abs,' are just some of the other reasons she listed.

Aside from those reasons she was also bashed for letting her kids use technology and for letting them have sugar which is why she made her daughter eat sugar in the photo while her son is on his tablet. 

'Being a mom is hard enough and if all the following make me a "bad mom" then I'll gladly wear it proudly! Here's to all the bad moms out there,' Sia concluded her post. 

After Sia's original post did so well she shared a follow-up photo of her looking into the camera holding her breasts with 'Bad Mom' written across her stomach.

In the background her son is sitting on a toy motorcycle while her daughter is half naked in a diaper.  

Sia captioned the new photo, 'In my last post, I mentioned several ways that I've been judged against as a mom and mainly it's because I find the time to workout.'

The reason why: Sia shared that she's been told her kids are 'probably neglected' and that she 'puts them off on a nanny'

Horrible comments: People have called her selfish for prioritizing fitness as a mom

Pregnancy workouts: Another reason she's been judged is for working out while pregnant and received hateful messages saying her baby was 'going to die and get shaken up in the womb'

Whatever: Despite all the hate she said you can't please everyone and: 'It seems like you're damned if you do, damned if you don't'

'I've been called selfish a thousand times because I choose to make fitness a priority as a mom. 

'I've been told that my kids are probably neglected and that I put them off on a nanny. 

'The hateful comments normally come from other moms who can't or won't put forth the same effort for whatever reason.

'I've also been judged for working out during pregnancy and received hateful messages saying my baby was going to die and get shaken up in the womb. 

'I've been told my baby would have brain damage. I had gone viral during pregnancy for maintaining my six-pack so some people commented that my abs were going to choke my baby. Yes I'm dead serious guys.

'And of course there's the Target workout judgement where I filmed a fun and playful workout video while shopping at Target that went viral last year and people called me a lunatic and crazy.

'My point is? You can't please everyone and y'all... other moms are the WORST! You'd think that moms would support one another instead of tearing each other apart. 

'It seems like you're damned if you do, damned if you don't.

'If another mom or a person in general wants to call ME a "bad mom" - vain, selfish, whatever - for putting my health first and for "having abs" then so be it! 

'I encourage you all to take what you've been called a bad mom for and wear it proud. Own it! To the haters, you can kiss my abs.

'Being a mom isn't easy and there is no wrong way to parent IF your kids are loved, healthy and being taken care of,' she concluded. 

Fitting it in: Sia was shamed after sharing a video of workout tips while shopping at Target

Ups and downs: Sia came up with the idea while shopping with her husband one Saturday morning

Tip to toe: Sia is seen demonstrating some body weight exercises in the empty aisles

The workout video Sia recorded while shopping in Target with her kids caused outrage on the internet. 

Talking to Daily Mail Online, Sia says she decided to make the video 'when my husband and I were shopping early one Saturday morning. 

'The store was literally dead and the idea popped into my head how most moms do spend their time shopping at Target, Walmart, etc with their kids,' she adds. 

'I wanted to show a way to kill two birds with one stone and how a busy Mom could sneak a quick sweat in!'

The moves themselves are nothing that Sia wouldn't be doing at home, as she often focuses on body weight exercises that require little to no equipment. 

However, the trolls came out and ripped the workout video apart.

'This is the most ridiculous thing I've ever seen!! Target is a sacred place of awesomeness!! Don't make me feel bad for roaming the aisles while snacking on cheese doodles!!' raged one user. 

'Seriously as if any woman would actually do that,' read one comment, while another said: 'Go to your house to work out. You are obstructing the aisle.' 

All strength: Sia has gained more than 630,000 Instagram followers for her tips and tricks

One to another: Sia also regularly posts about body positivity, but is still often the target of trolls, who 'took a fun and lighthearted video and trashed it like they always do'

Happy mom: Despite the trolls, other moms thanked Sia for giving them new ideas

Not bothered: Sia believes that the people leaving the hateful comments have 'some issue deep within themselves'

One cruel commenter even branded Sia 'a lunatic' simply for offering creative workout advice. 

'I'm not surprised that my workout has bothered so many people,' she says. 'They took a fun and lighthearted video and trashed it like they always do.' 

She adds: 'Many moms thanked me for it as it's given them ideas outside of the box and that's all that matters to me! But those who couldn't personally see themselves doing it just wanted to judge and mock it. 

'Nope it's not the traditional workout in the traditional setting, but busy moms need to multitask and get it in where they can.'

As for the trolls, Sia believes that they must have 'some issue deep within themselves' in order to 'hide behind a keyboard and spew such hateful comments.' 

'Whatever happened to if you don't have anything nice to say, don't say it at all?'
