Five options for Anthony Joshua's next fight after win over Joseph Parker

Anthony Joshua took another step towards becoming the unified heavyweight world champion with victory over Joseph Parker.

The WBA and IBF belt holder added Parker's WBO strap to his collection with a points victory after he was taken the 12-round distance for the first time in his professional career.

Joshua has now won all 21 of his fights but questions will still be asked of the Londoner after what was at times a lacklustre performance.

But where does the London 2012 gold medalist go from here?

We look at five possible fights for Joshua as he bids to cement his legacy in the ring...

Deontay Wilder

The one everybody wants to see, between the only two heavyweight world champions. Wilder was due to be ringside in Cardiff as part of Showtime's broadcast team but declined to travel when he was told he wouldn't be able to get in the ring with Joshua.

Wilder overcame a tough challenge from Luis Ortiz earlier this year to retain his WBC belt and has also been offered a fight with Dillian Whyte. But a clash between him and Joshua - probably in Las Vegas towards the end of the year - is the only option for most boxing fans who crave a return to the days of a single champion.

Dillian Whyte

Play it again, Sam? Whyte was knocked out by Joshua in the seventh round of their first fight in 2015 but has worked his way back into contention. Most recently he delivered a sickening KO in the sixth round of his clash with Lucas Browne to keep hold of his WBC Silver title.

That could lead to a fight with Wilder, but so far the American has rejected all offers to fly over to London to take on the Brixton man, and if Wilder also can't agree terms with Joshua, the two Brits could square off again in a fight that would at least have a tasty build-up.

Alexander Povetkin

The Russian was already the mandatory challenger to Joshua before he KO'd David Price on the Cardiff undercard. Price had fought back well from a third-round knockdown but was sent crashing to the canvas in the fifth round. That left Povetkin aiming for a shot at Joshua having previously been WBA world champion until he ran into Wladimir Klitschko.

Povetkin has talked about having the fight in a stadium in Russia and he would certainly a pose a test for Joshua - although Whyte was also calling out Povetkin after the final bell in the Welsh capital.

Tyson Fury

There's little chance of this happening- but we can dream, can't we? The former world champion is expected to return to the ring in June following close to three years on the sidelines since his famous win over Wladimir Klitschko in Germany. And Fury has claimed he'll then be ready for Joshua in a fight that would surely break all records on UK soil.

But it remains to be seen if Fury can return at a sufficient level following his time out - and whether his promoter, reported to be Frank Warren, can thrash out a deal with Joshua's promoter Hearn.

Jarrell Miller

A name that may not be familiar to every UK boxing fan but promoter Eddie Hearn has mentioned Miller as a possible opponent for Joshua's American debut, should he make it in the autumn. With 20 wins (with 18 knockouts) and one draw from his 21 professional fights, the New Yorker could provide a stern test, but the fight is unlikely to bring the excitement of many of Joshua's other options.

Miller must first overcome Johann Duhaupas at the end of the month, and it wouldn't be a surprise to see Joshua in a ringside seat at the Barclays Centre in Brooklyn.
