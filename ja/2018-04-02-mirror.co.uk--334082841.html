Liverpool star Van Dijk reveals key to beating Man City in Champions League

Even City boss Pep Guardiola calls Mo Salah almost unstoppable, and that is the potent psychological weapon Liverpool are ready to unleash.

Most observers make the Premier League champions-elect overwhelming favourites in the tie of the round.

Yet according to team-mate Virgil van Dijk, Salah can induce fear in any side.

“That’s what everyone knows, that with a player like Mo we can do damage to any team. That’s how we feel as well,” he explained.

“Everybody can see how much quality he has got, how many goals he has been scoring, and that’s important.

“It makes us better. That’s what we want, that’s what it’s going to be like at a top club. When he is 100% and in the zone then he can beat anyone.”

It is the confidence Salah brings which gives Liverpool a chance of stopping City taking a famous treble this season.

For skipper Jordan Henderson, he can take Liverpool to another level, just as Luis Suarez did in 2014.

“For a start, it’s the confidence it brings. You have someone like Mo who does the hardest part of the game, which is putting the ball in the back of the net,” Henderson explained.

“You know then, as a team, if we work as hard as we possibly can, then Mo – or Sadio and Bobby – they are going to finish it in the final third.

“We had that with Luis. You know when you are walking out there are so many goals out there for you. It’s just about defending well as a team because you know you will create chances.”

For Guardiola, it is the trio that is the headache. “They are fantastic players. The way Liverpool play is so complicated for us. They are so good.”
