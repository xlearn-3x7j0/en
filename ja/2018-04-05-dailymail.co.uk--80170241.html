Passengers thrown into walls as wild seas cause chaos on P&amp;O cruise

Rough seas might make for the best sailors but they can easily ruin a relaxing cruise. 

As the P&O Aria battled rough seas off the coast of Queensland passengers were thrown into walls, children were sea sick and seafarers were treated to an unexpected wave pool.

Footage shared by a passenger Jessica Grace was posted on social media and shows the normally calm pool had become a surfers heaven. 

A photo taken from a window on the ship shows the huge swells, reportedly 6 to 7 meters high, outside 

Passengers can be seen lulling around the pool near the windows as the water flows over the sides 

 'Yesterday people were swimming in the pool until staff said it was no longer safe and netted it off,' she told Daily Mail Australia.

The footage shows calm passengers lulling around as the pool churns up creating waves that rock back and forth. 

In the footage she shared, a statue of five dolphins can be seen holding steady as the waves lap up against it.

'The weather has been bad since we started the cruise. But the really bad weather, that caused all the outdoor areas to be closed off, started yesterday.'

Jessica said the rough waters were making it a little difficult for passengers to get around the cruise ship. 

'Everyone walking are being thrown around seen several people thrown into walls while walking.'

While the rough weather caused some chaos on the ship many passengers remained in good spirits and mingled in the bar 

Some of the footage Jessica captured showed doors and draws opening as the ship battled against the heavy waves

'It’s very rough, the stair cases are best to be avoided. Yesterday my 3 year old fell down a small stair case when we hit a big swell.'  

‘She’s fine, besides being a bit sea sick. Just had a big spew which clogged our room toilet so we are upstairs now while it gets fixed.'   

But no body is abandoning ship just yet. At least nine people can be seen in the video relaxing by the pool-side and Jessica says that the passengers are in good spirits. 

'Everyone’s around the bar having a drink and a laugh. I know a lot were disappointed by our stop at Hamilton Island as it rained the whole day,' said Jessica. 

Tropical cyclone Iris is now in her last stages and is moving away from the Queensland coast

'The kids would be most upset as it closes off all the edge activity’s as they’re outdoors on the ship.'    

The ship's arrival into Brisbane has been delayed by six hours due to the seven meter swells. 

Tropical cyclone Iris is now in her last stages and is moving away from the Queensland coast. 

It is expected to change direction and begin to track northwest by Friday, with some wild weather still predicted.

A spokesperson for P&O told Daily Mail Australia Aria was nowhere near Cyclone Iris, and had sailing away from the weather system to ensure their passengers' safety.

'The ship will arrive later than scheduled in Brisbane simply because it slowed down to maximise passenger comfort in the conditions, which it is well able to handle,' he said.
