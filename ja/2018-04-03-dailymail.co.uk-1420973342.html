Warnock manages Terry and Sessegnon in EFL Team of the Season

Despite most sides having at least seven matches remaining, the EFL named the Championship, League One and League Two Teams of the Season on Tuesday.

Cardiff City boss Neil Warnock topped the vote to manage a Championship XI which featured Aston Villa centre back John Terry and Fulham wonderkid Ryan Sessegnon.

Sessegnon was also nominated for the Championship Player of the Season award, alongside Fulham team-mate Tom Cairney and Wolverhampton Wanderers midfielder Ruben Neves.

Seventeen-year-old Sessegnon, who can play at left back or on the wing, has scored 14 goals and provided five assists in 39 Championship appearances this season.

Such impressive form has attracted interested from elsewhere, with Tottenham among his list of admirers.

Warnock's recognition comes after his Cardiff side claimed 80 points from their first 39 games this season, leaving the Welsh club well on course for promotion.

Terry has also shone this term, commanding the Villa backline as captain in his first campaign since leaving Chelsea on a free transfer.

The winners of the Player of the Season gongs will be announced at the 2018 EFL Awards which are taking place on April 15 at the London Hilton on Park Lane.

Blackburn midfielder Bradley Dack, Peterborough United striker Jack Marriott and Wigan attacker Nick Powell have been nominated for League One's top gong.

Adebayo Akinfenwa, Mohamed Eisa and Billy Kee are up for the League Two prize.

As well as being in the running for the Championship's Player of the Season award, Sessegnon and Neves have been nominated in the EA Sports Young Player of the Season category — along with Norwich's James Maddison. 

All three divisions are represented in the Mitre Goal of the Year competition, which will be settled by the audience at the awards ceremony on April 15.
