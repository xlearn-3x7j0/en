Woman is saved by doctors who remove scissors embedded in her head

  • Ms Shen, from China, had a pair of scissors stuck in her head at 1.5 inches deep
  • The incident happened when the 57-year-old tried to harvest leaves from a tree
  • The scissors attached to the end of a bamboo pole fell sending blades first
  • However, the woman calmly took a bus and admitted herself to a hospital

Published: 13:01 EDT, 5 April 2018 | Updated: 13:59 EDT, 5 April 2018

A Chinese woman was luckily saved after having a pair of scissors lodged in her head at home in central China. 

The unfortunate incident happened when the 57-year-old woman was harvesting Chinese mahogany leaves using a pair of scissors tied on a bamboo pole and the bamboo snapped.

She took a bus to the hospital where doctors removed the scissors after two minimal invasive operations. 

A woman had a pair of scissors anchored in her head when she was cutting leaves from a tree at home in China (left). Being home alone, she took a bus to the hospital herself (right)

According to Pear Video, the woman, named Shen Xiaoying, took a bus from her home in Xiangyang of Hubei Province to Xianyang No.1 Hospital after the accident on April 4.

She explained that she could not reach the Chinese mahogany tree and decided to tie a pair of scissors to the end of a long bamboo pole to harvest the leaves.

However, the bamboo snapped, sending the sharp blades towards her and lodged onto her head. 

There were no one at home and Ms Shen decided to rush to the hospital herself. 

An X-ray shows one of the blades lodged about 1.5 inches deep inside Ms Shen's skull

Doctors had to perform two invasive operations to remove the scissors from her head

It's believed that Ms Shen was harvesting the Chinese mahogany leaves for vegetables

A doctor said in the video that the blades were about 1.5 inches deep in Ms Shen's head and had reached the brain.   

Surgeons successfully removed the scissors after two invasive operations.

'She is regaining her consciousness but the anaethetics are giving her numbness for a short period of time,' said the doctor.

Ms Shen is expected to be discharged in a few days.

Chinese mahogany is commonly used for furniture and instrument making, while its leaves are eaten as vegetables.
