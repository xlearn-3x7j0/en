League Two round-up: Accrington and Luton keep up their promotion push

Accrington remained top of League Two after a solitary goal from Mark Hughes saw them beat Notts County.

Defender Hughes stroked the ball home in the ninth minute, latching on to Seamus Conneely's looping pass into the penalty area, for his fourth goal of the season.

The 1-0 win was Stanley's sixth successive victory and their ninth on the bounce at home - a club league record.

It also gave them an 11-point cushion over Exeter in fourth place, further strengthening their automatic promotion hopes.

Luton stayed on their coattails in second place, though, after coming from behind to win 2-1 at home against Mansfield.

Kane Hemmings scored to give the Stags the lead, but second-half goals from James Collins and substitute Glen Rea ensured the gap to Accrington stayed at three points.

Wycombe are a further three points back after they too came from behind to win 2-1 at home against struggling Grimsby, who led through Martyn Woolford's early strike.

Adam El-Abd and Scott Kashket scored in quick succession to turn the game on its head.

Exeter moved above Notts County after goals from Jordan Storey and Robbie Simpson saw them beat Cheltenham 2-1 at St James' Park. The visitors had led through Carl Winchester's effort.

Matt Green's 16th goal of the season gave Lincoln a 1-0 victory at Carlisle, ending the Cumbrians' eight-game unbeaten run. It got worse for the hosts in injury time when Mike Jones was sent off.

Coventry remained in the play-off places, in seventh, despite being thrashed 6-2 at home by Yeovil, who led 3-0 after only 15 minutes.

Alex Fisher, Francois Zoko and Sam Surrige bagged braces for the visitors, while substitute Maxime Biamou also scored twice for the Sky Blues.

Swindon were unable to take full advantage of the defeats for Mansfield and Coventry as they had to settle for a 1-1 draw at Crawley. In fact they needed an 85th-minute equaliser from Marc Richards to salvage a point after Jimmy Smith had put the hosts ahead.

Coventry City's Jonson Clarke-Harris vies for the ball with Yeovil Town's Omar Sowunmi

Colchester kept their pay-off hopes alive with a 2-1 success at Forest Green. Drey Wright scored after only 16 seconds, but Rovers were level through Reuben Reid's 37th-minute penalty. However, Ben Stevenson struck early in the second half to win it for the visitors.

With Chesterfield not playing and Grimsby and Forest Green both losing, bottom-of-the-table Barnet had the chance to give their survival chances a boost. However, Martin Allen's men lost 4-1 at Stevenage.

Alex Revell and Danny Newton both bagged a brace for Borough, while Shaquile Coulthirst replied for the Bees.

Charlie Kirk grabbed an injury-time equaliser for Crewe as the derby honours with Port Vale were shared after a thrilling 2-2 draw.

Ryan Wintle put Crewe ahead, but Tom Pope levelled things up from the penalty spot. It looked like Antony Kay's 71st-minute strike would win it for Vale, but Kirk popped up late on.
