Liverpool to assess Mo Salah after groin injury suffered vs Man City

Liverpool are to assess Mo Salah's injury after Egyptian exited the Champions League win vs Man City on Wednesday.

The 25-year-old added his 38th goal of the season and assisted his 13th strike as the Reds thrashed Pep Guardiola's side 3-0 at Anfield.

But Salah was substituted for Gini Wijnaldum in the 53rd minute and quickly headed down the tunnel for treatment.

The former Roma star was seen grabbing his groin area, which will concern Reds fans, who will await news on the severity of the problem, likely later on Thursday.

Jurgen Klopp insisted the outlook was positive when quizzed immediately after the game.

"I don't know at the moment. He came to the sidelines and said he felt something," Klopp revealed.

Salah told Klopp, "all fine, all good," but the extent of the injury will be revealed ahead of the weekend.

The Merseyside derby vs Everton on Saturday appears a risk not worth taking.

Then, Klopp will hope the medical team can rush Salah back in time for Tueday's second leg.

With the 3-0 lead in hand, Klopp may well prefer to keep the game tight and then unleash Salah as a substitute as City push for a route back into the game.
