Essam El-Hadary could become oldest player to feature at a World Cup

  • Faryd Mondragon of Colombia is the oldest player to have played at a World Cup 
  • Essam El-Hadary would make his World Cup debut if he is included for Russia  
  • El-Hadary trains every day with the hope of being included in the Egypt squad 

Published: 13:53 EDT, 2 April 2018 | Updated: 14:04 EDT, 2 April 2018

Goalkeeper Essam El-Hadary could become the oldest player to feature at a World Cup if he is included in Egypt's squad for the tournament.

El-Hadary, 45, plays for Al-Taawoun FC in Saudi Arabia and has made 156 appearances for Egypt since 1999.

Egypt qualified for the World Cup after Liverpool forward Mohamed Salah scored a late penalty in the 2-1 win over Congo.

El-Hadary says it would be a dream come true if he was selected at this summer's competition in Russia.

'I'm very happy to play at the World Cup, and that is a message to all footballers and others around the world, that you should believe in your dreams and fight to make them come true,' El-Hadary told BBC Sport.

'My age is 45 years old, but for me, this is just a number on paper.'

Faryd Mondragon of Colombia is the oldest player to have played at a World Cup - he was 43 years and three days when he played against Japan in Brazil in 2014. 

'I train hard every day, I don't know what the word 'impossible' means. I will keep fighting until the World Cup to have the chance to play and defend my homeland colours,' he said.    

Egypt are in Group A for the World Cup and will play Russia, Saudi Arabia and Uruguay.
