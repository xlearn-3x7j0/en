England's Barmy Army &amp; Kiwi fans mock Australia ball-tampering saga

  • England's Barmy Army mocked Australia for the recent ball-tampering scandal
  • New Zealand fans in Christchurch dressed up as members of Australia's leadership group ahead of their second Test with England
  • New Zealand batsman Ross Taylor signed a piece of yellow sandpaper too

Published: 08:54 EDT, 30 March 2018 | Updated: 09:03 EDT, 30 March 2018

While cricket enthusiasts in Australia continue to mourn the state of their game, across the Tasman Sea there are some who are revelling in it.

An explosive fallout this week has seen Australia captain Steve Smith, vice-captain David Warner, and opening batsman Cameron Bancroft sent home from their tour of South Africa in disgrace and banned long-term for their roles in cheating by illegally tampering with the ball with sandpaper during the third Test in Cape Town.

Australia coach Darren Lehmann resigned on Thursday in light of the findings too.

Members of England's Barmy Army have mocked Australia for their ball-tampering scandal by uploading this picture of four New Zealand fans dressing up as the Australian leadership group

New Zealand's Ross Taylor signed a piece of their sandpaper during Friday's Test vs England

Smith was reduced to tears during a press conference on Thursday but over in New Zealand his plight was greeted by cheers from England supporters. 

England's long winter Down Under is currently concluding with the second Test against New Zealand in Christchurch.

And ahead of the first day on Friday at the Hagley Oval, members of England's Barmy Army took great pleasure in mocking Australia's situation right now - along with New Zealand supporters.

A quartet of New Zealand supporters dressed up in Australia's Test whites, accompanied with the baggy green cap, a cricket ball and some yellow sandpaper - dubbing themselves as the 'Australian leadership group'.

That Kiwi quartet also got New Zealand batsman Ross Taylor involved with their antics by signing a piece of sandpaper while out in the field.

'Quality,' England's Barmy Army account tweeted as they showed off the 34-year-old's signature.
