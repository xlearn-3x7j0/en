Mom 'on drugs' filmed trying to drive with young daughters in car

A video has emerged of a mother from the US struggling to stay awake and attempt to drive her car while appearing to be under the influence of drugs while two young girls are in the vehicle.

‘I’m ready,’ one of the girls in the passenger seat says as she hands the keys over to the woman in the driver’s seat.

The woman, who says she’s a ‘single mother’ going through ‘a boyfriend who broke up with me’, looks to be drifting in and out of sleep.

When she talks, it is difficult to understand what she’s saying.

In recent years there have been a rash of horrific images posted to the internet showing parents incapacitated as a result of suspected use of opioids while in the presence of their children.

In September 2016, the police department of East Liverpool, Ohio disseminated images of a couple passed out in the front seats of a car while their four-year-old son sat in the back.

In December 2016, a young couple drove three hours from their home in Vermont to Massachusetts to buy heroin with their nine-month-old baby boy in the backseat.

They were found in Lawrence, Massachusetts passed out in their car after an apparent overdose.

The baby was sound asleep in the back seat.

In January 2017, a couple from Sarasota, Florida was arrested after they were found passed out in their car from an apparent overdose while their two infant children – ages five months and one year – were in the backseat.

The United States has been in the grip of an opioid epidemic in recent years.

Opioids were involved in more than 64,000 overdose deaths nationwide in 2016, according to the National Institute on Drug Abuse.

Hundreds of lawsuits by states, counties and cities have accused drugmakers of pushing addictive painkillers through deceptive marketing and wholesale distributors of failing to report suspicious drug orders.

President Donald Trump last month called for a federal lawsuit against opioid companies and stiffer penalties for drug dealers, arguing that the government must take a strong approach to combat an opioid addiction epidemic.
