New Orleans Saints quarterback Drew Brees sues a jeweler for $9million

Drew Brees is seeking 'at least $9million in damages' from a San Diego jeweler after he said he was scammed and sold several items that were undervalued.

The New Orleans Saints quarterback and his wife Brittany filed a lawsuit on Monday in the Superior Court of California against Vahid Moradi of CJ Charles Jewelers, according to TMZ Sports.

In the lawsuit, Brees claims he has been buying pieces from Moradi for years, including watches, rings, and earrings, spending a total of approximately $15million.

However, the 39-year-old said he recently had the items appraised independently and discovered they were worth $9million less than what he paid.

According to the suit, Brees stated that the most expensive piece he purchased from Moradi was a 4.09 carat blue diamond ring he bought to the tune of $8.18million in 2015.

However, after an independent appraisal in March 2017, the ring was discovered to allegedly only be worth $3.75million.

Another item listed in the lawsuit is a pink diamond ring Brees bought for his wife in December 2012 for $1.75million as a 10th wedding anniversary present.

The appraiser valued the piece at $1.28million - $470,000 less than the purchase price.

The couple met Moradi in 2003 and remained customers through 2016. Eight pieces were purchased from late 2012 to 2016 as long-term financial investments, which Moradi allegedly said would increase in value over the years. 

In the lawsuit, Brees said he confronted the jeweler, who admitted to charging 'a substantial markup' but stated that, at the time pieces were sold, the marker was as 'weak as it has ever been', reported The Advocate.

'Moradi insisted unabashedly that he had done nothing wrong because he charged plaintiffs the price at which Moradi expected the jewelry could be resold in 10 to 15 years because Moradi knew (the Breeses) wanted a "long-term investment",' the filing states.  

The jeweler, claims that Brees 'aggressively purchased' diamonds and is merely upset that their values hasn't increased as quickly as he hoped.

According to Moradi's attorney. Eric George, the blame falls solely on Brees's shoulders. 

'Drew Brees aggressively purchased multi-million dollar pieces of jewelry. Years later, claiming to suffer "cash flow problems" he tried to bully my client into undoing the transactions,' he said in a statement.

'Mr Brees's behavior and his belief that he was wronged because the jewelry did not appreciate in value as quickly as he hoped both demonstrate a lack of integrity and contradict basic principles of both economics and the law.

'He should restrict his game-playing to the football field, and refrain from bullying honest, hard-working businessmen like my client.'

Brees recently signed a two-year, $50million contract to return to the Saints. 

The 11-time Pro Bowler was chosen as the first pick of the second round of the draft by the San Diego Chargers. He spent five seasons with the Chargers from 2001 to 2005 until he left and signed a deal with the Saints.

With the Saints, he won the Super Bowl in 2009 and was named Super Bowl MVP 

According to Spotrac, Brees's earnings over his 17-year-career in the NFL amount to more than $194million.
