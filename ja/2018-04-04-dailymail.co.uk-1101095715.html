Russian skating prodigy's mother beats him and locks him in a cupboard

The millionaire mother of a Russian skating prodigy has revealed she beats her son and locks him in a closet in her quest to make him 'the best skater on the planet'. 

Self-made businesswoman Yana Rudkovskaya, 43, is facing a public backlash after she told of the exceptional pressure she puts on son Alexander 'Sasha' Plushenko.

Sasha has been in the spotlight almost all his life - becoming a fashion model before his first birthday and taking to the ice while still in nappies.  

Yana Rudkovskaya, 43 (right), mother of five-year-old figure skating prodigy Alexander 'Sasha' Plushenko (left), admits beating him and locking him in a cupboard

Rudkovskaya explained that Sasha begins his skating training every morning at 9.15am and is usually finished by around 4pm

Every day Sasha is treated to half an hour of cartoons and is given a toy if he performs well in a show, Rudkovskaya explained

To date Sasha has been in 20 shows, including a permanent position performing with his Olympic-skater father Yevgeny Plushenko (pictured) in his Nutcracker-2 production

But if Sasha misbehaves he is whipped with a 'narrow belt' by his father, Rudkovskaya said, or else locked in a dark cupboard

He is now being groomed for figure skating stardom by father Yevgeny Plushenko, a four-times Olympic medalist who won the European championships seven times.

Rudkovskaya told Spletnik, the Russian version of Tatler, that the pair raise Sasha with 'iron gloves'. 

'Many people reprimand me for excessive cruelty,' the Putin supporter admitted.

Referring to her son by his nickname, which means 'gnome', she added: 'Recently I gave an interview which brought hundreds of links on the internet arguing that Yana Rudkovskaya is cruel in how she raises Gnom Gnomych.'

In the interview Rudkovskaya was accompanied by her son who was asked to name the punishments.

He immediately said: 'The belt.'

She admitted it 'happens sometimes' with a 'small narrow belt' usually administered by her 35 year old husband.

As well as corporal punishment, the boy also told how he was forced to sit in a 'dark room'.

Rudkovskaya was unapologetic about her parenting style, saying she was treated the same way by her parents. 'And haven't I grown into a decent human being?,' she added

Rudkovskaya is now facing a backlash across Russia with people accusing her of being cruel to her son and asking for social services to intervene

The mother is a self-made millionaire who started out running salon businesses and now manages music artists

Rudkovskaya refuses to take pity on her son, saying that she will only comfort him if he falls down and hurts himself, but not when it comes to pushing his talents

His mother said this was punishment 'when he behaves the wrong way with his nanny' or other children.

She asked: 'And so what?

'My parents also put me in a dark room, and used a belt on me, and I was banned from leaving home… and haven't I grown into a decent human being?'

Her comments caused a storm in Russia with critic demanding: 'Where the hell are our social services?

'She is publicly admitting violence. This is madness.'

Another said: 'Haven't I grown into a decent human being?' Oh yes, a very decent sadist, you have.'

But Rudkovskaya - millionaire producer for Russian singer Dima Bilan - is adamant that she is using the right approach.

'Don't take a pity of a child if you want to be the best on the planet,' she told Dozhd TV. 'Sasha is a sportsman. He has three ice training sessions a day.

'At 9.15am he is already on the ice warming up, and by 4pm he is just about finishing.

Describing her son's future, Rudkovskaya said she wants him to become a great athlete, but also a 'good man' who is educated and kind

Sasha is pictured wearing one of the outfits from his Nutcracker performance while attending the theatre with his mother

Before becoming a skater, Sasha had a brief career as a fashion model, which is another of his mother's interests

'He is prepared to take this pressure on - so that he can be the best in the world.

'Am I scared that he will grow up and confront me with "What was it all for?"

'No I am not scared,' she said, arguing that her husband went through a similar routine when he was trained for greatness.

'His mother told me how she raised him. In order to be the best on the planet, don't take pity on your child. 

'You can take pity when he is asleep, or when he scratched himself, or bruised his knee. Then you can kiss and feel sorry for the boy.

'When he gets up, you have to tell him that he has to train hard today, be the best and not let his father and his trainer down.

'Yes we constantly inspire him, he is indeed a five-year-old child who is charged to become a champion.

'I want my son to grow up into a real man. We want to raise first and foremost a man and a good athlete.

Sasha's father, Evgeny Plushenko (right) won four Olympic medals and came top at the European championships eight times during his career

Sasha has several hundred thousand followers on Instagram and has been in the limelight virtually since he was born

While away from training, Sasha's Instagram feed shows the family traveling all over the world

Sasha pictured with father Evgeny, who was also a world-class figure skater

'When I say "a man", it means "a human being", too - decent, honest and well-educated.'

He has starred in 20 major shows and has a permanent position in his father's Nutcracker-2 ice show.

'He loves it,' she said. 'It's impossible to kick him off ice rink.

'People come to watch him. He worked through 20 ice shows at the Olimpiysky arena (in Moscow). He will soon enter his first competitions.

'Twenty seven cameras are accredited, four Japanese TV channels among them. He cannot do badly.

'When Sasha enters his dad's museum, he says: "How hard will I have to work to earn so many medals?".'

Aside from training, Sasha is allowed to watch 30 minutes of cartoons per day and is made to earn toys by taking part in shows and performing well. 

Rudkovskaya went on: 'He has one attitude to toys that he was gifted, and a completely different one to the ones he worked for.

'He earned them. He took part in a show and got toys.

'There is no doubt that the child has motivation in sport. And I am simply an angel compared to other parents.'

Where parents are too soft, their children turn into 'drug addicts', she claimed.
