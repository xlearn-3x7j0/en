West Brom identify ideal manager to replace sacked Alan Pardew

West Brom are weighing up a summer move for Michael Appleton after sacking Alan Pardew.

The doomed Premier League basement club yesterday brought Pardew’s miserable four-month reign to an end after just one win in 18 league games.

Former Albion player and coach Appleton - currently No.2 to Claude Puel at Leicester - is high on Albion’s list to spearhead their recovery in the Championship next season.

Ex-defender and current development coach Darren Moore will be in caretaker charge for their remaining six games.

But the Hawthorns club are planning to bring in a permanent new boss in the summer to shake up the club.

New CEO Mark Jenkins has promised an overhaul after a desperate season with just three League wins.

Jenkins knows Leicester assistant boss Appleton from the ex-midfielder’s playing days at the club before he moved into coaching.

He is highly-regarded by Albion as is fellow former Baggies midfielder Derek McInnes, now in charge at Aberdeen.

Appleton left League One Oxford, where he was boss, to assist Craig Shakespeare at Leicester last summer, before being kept on by Puel.

He but took sole charge of Leicester for two games when Shakespeare was sacked in October, winning them both.

A West Brom club statement said: “West Bromwich Albion and Alan Pardew have agreed to mutually part company today following discussions between both parties. Assistant Head Coach John Carver will also be leaving.

“The Club would like to thank Alan and John for their efforts and wish them well in their future endeavours.”

Other names in the frame include Solihull-born Graham Potter of Swedish club Ostersunds, ex-West Brom coach Nigel Pearson at OH Leuven and Walsall coach Dean Smith.
