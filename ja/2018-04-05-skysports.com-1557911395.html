Castleford winger Greg Eden signs new three-year deal

Last Updated: 05/04/18 1:00pm

Castleford winger Greg Eden has signed a three-year contract extension with the Tigers until the end of the 2021 season.

Eden began his career with the Tigers, making his Super League debut in 2011 before moving to Huddersfield Giants.

The 27-year old also spent time with Hull KR and Salford Red Devils before heading to Australia at the end of the 2014 season to join NRL side Brisbane Broncos.

Eden said: "Last year I was really enjoying my rugby and I feel like I slotted straight back into the Castleford way of playing rugby. I've really enjoyed my time here since I've been back and I'm ecstatic to have extended my contract.

"I've not had the best of luck so far this season with a hamstring injury keeping me out for a few weeks but I'm excited to get back out there with the boys."

Cas head coach, Daryl Powell added: "It's great news for the club that Greg Eden has committed his future for the next three years.

"He had a fantastic first season with the club and I have no doubt this season it will be the same when he gets consistent game time under his belt. He is a natural try scorer and is a lightning-quick player who can play both on the wing and at full-back.

"I'm looking forward to working with him and seeing him develop further."
