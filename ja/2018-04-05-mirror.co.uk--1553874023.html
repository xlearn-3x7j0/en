How Cristiano Ronaldo and Real Madrid have turned their seasons around

Real Madrid have turned their form around with a simple change of mentality.

That's the opinion of Spanish football journalist Rik Sharma, as he joined the Mirror Football podcast this week.

With the La Liga title out of reach, Real have realised that they have nothing to lose - and are going all out for Champions League glory.

The 3-0 win at Juventus has all but secured their place in the semi-finals and extends their recent record to 11 wins in their last 12 games in all competitions.

And Cristiano Ronaldo is back playing at his absolute peak.

Speaking on the podcast , Sharma said: "In the first half of the season, Ronaldo had about three goals in 14 or 15 games or something like that.

"He looked like he was struggling, he looked tired and he looked like he physically was not up to it anymore.

"But since the turn of the year, he has gone berserk! He's got 23 goals in his last 12 games for Madrid, four in his last La Liga game and is just unstoppable in front of goal at the moment."

The upturn in Ronaldo's form has also been replicated by the rest of the team - and it could be down to a change of system.

Sharma added: "Ronaldo was playing badly at the start of the season and Madrid were set up so strongly around Ronaldo that if he didn't play well then they were going to struggle.

"I think Benzema is a great player, very underrated, but in terms of goalscoring he has been very poor this season.

"But now Ronaldo is scoring again, Madrid look a lot more dangerous.

"I think they have improved as well because mentally they have realised that La Liga has gone and obviously the Copa del Rey has gone.

"The Champions League they have won it twice in a row, they are the first team to do that, and you really can't bet against them doing it again."
