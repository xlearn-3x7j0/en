Delirious Villanova fans light fires and climb street poles

Thousands of Villanova Wildcats fans poured into the streets of suburban Philadelphia after the school beat Michigan on Monday night to win its second men’s national basketball title in the last three years. 

Jubilant supporters lit fires and climbed street poles all while emergency crews and 400 extra police officers tried to keep order around the Villanova campus.

And as was the case after the Eagles’ recent Super Bowl win, the local street poles were greased to prevent fans from climbing and hurting themselves or others. However, the effort appeared to be in vain as many daring climbers still managed to ascend above the masses.

The Philadelphia police had the street poles greased ahead of the Eagles' final two postseason games of the 2017 campaign. 

Unlike the Philadelphia PD, which used Crisco and a hydraulic fluid before the NFL title game and Super Bowl, respectively, Radner's grease is a bit of a mystery.

'I don't know what they're using but some poor guy from Villanova has to smear it all over the place,' Radnor Police Lt. Chris Flanagan told the Philadelphia Inquirer before Monday's game. 'We just don't want anybody falling, that's the biggest problem.'

There were no immediate reports of any arrests around the Villanova campus 

News helicopter video showed a small fire being set in the street with some students trying to jump over the flames. Students could be seen climbing trees and grabbing branches to add fuel to the fire. Police later began to move people away from the flames.

There was no immediate word on any arrests.

A city spokeswoman confirmed to the Philadelphia Inquirer that the city would hold a parade for the Wildcats, but details about the event have yet to be revealed. 

When the Wildcats last won a national title in 2016, the parade took place on the ensuing Friday and cost a reported $22,000 as the school paid the tab for extra police, EMS workers and post-parade sanitation.  An estimated 60,000 people attended that parade, whereas hundreds of thousands of Eagles fans swarmed the city during that procession.
