Expert gives her tips for scoring thousands of followers on Instagram

Even if you don't work in the social media industry, most people aren't immune to the pressure of having an aesthetically-pleasing feed of photos and carefully curated posts.

And whether you're trying to grow your business online or take full advantage of your personal page, content creator Rebecca Harrop has spoken to FEMAIL about how to gain thousands of followers on Instagram.

The Brisbane-based 19-year-old was helping a family friend curate her beauty Instagram, and watching the followers and 'like' count grow day by day, when she realised this might be her calling.

The Brisbane-based 19-year-old was helping a family friend curate her beauty Instagram, and watching the followers and 'like' count grow day by day, when she realised this might be her calling.

'I'd see these relatively well-known or 'Insta famous' people doing these cool things and realised I could make that work for my own page as well,' she told FEMAIL.

She now posts flatlays of products and food staples, as well as daily Instagram Stories involving polls and templates, that are contributing to her rapid gaining of followers.

And according to her, gaining thousands of followers is as easy as using all of the 'shareable' features the app has to offer.

So what does it take to become an influencer?

Always use your 30 allocated hashtags

'You can actually just Google the top trending hashtags for a particular topic and copy and paste those across or do some research into the market you're trying to break into,' Rebecca said.

For example, beauty 'grammers could use #makeup, #cosmetics, #girly, #bblogger, #igmakeup and #instagood to get more 'likes' and eyeballs on their page.

But you have to post the hashtags on your actual caption, not in the comments below, which is a common mistake.

'If you don't share the post with the hashtags immediately and together you're risking having your post not show up at the top of that list of hashtags,' she said.

'I do understand it can look a little messy to have so many hashtags but don't delete them. If your post is tracking well at the top of a hashtag and you get rid of it, you'll lose that audience.' 

Edit your photos in the same colour scheme 

Instead of chopping and changing your colours, stick to one filter or preset for all of them. 

'You don't want to be posting a really blue-toned picture next to a yellow-toned one because warm and cool tones won't go together,' she said.

'Lightroom is particularly good for nature photos or pics where you might have a gorgeous beach in the background. You can make the water look more blue, for instance,' she said.

Use your 'Highlights' on Insta Story

Instagram now allows you to save your Stories at the top of your feed under a bar called 'Highlights'.

'If you're a brand having a sale on Boxing Day you could post information about the sale on your Highlights, rather than in the main feed where it might not fit your theme,' Rebecca explained. 

Create a 'cover photo' on your Highlights that are all similar looking to give a sense of professionalism. 

Show yourself on Insta Stories - even if it's a brand page

Don't hide behind product pics and flatlays.

'It's important to show that there is a person behind these pictures, that's what drives traffic in your direction,' Rebecca said.

'I use templates to share information about myself with my followers or people can do an Instagram Live video to talk to their followers in a relaxed setting.'
