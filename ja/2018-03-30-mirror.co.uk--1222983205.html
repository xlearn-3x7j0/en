European giants 'in pole position to sign Anthony Martial'

Juventus are growing increasingly confident of signing Anthony Martial.

The Frenchman is said to be frustrated with his role at Manchester United having spent the season in and out of the side.

Martial's agent fuelled speculation earlier this month when he admitted he didn't know if he would be at Old Trafford next term.

And Tuttosport reports Juve think they are in pole position to sign Martial at the end of the campaign.

Manchester United ready to pounce for Barcelona defender Samuel Umtiti

Manchester United are ready to take advantage of the breakdown in contract talks between Samuel Umtiti and Barcelona, writes Mirror Football's David McDonnell.

Negotiations between Barca and France star Umtiti over an improved deal have stalled, with the centre-back said to want to treble his current £50,000-a-week salary.

Although Umtiti is under contract until 2021, Barca opened talks over a new deal early to try to head off interest from a host of top European clubs, including United.

Jose Mourinho is looking for a centre-back this summer to partner Eric Bailly and has Umtiti and Real Madrid's Raphael Varane, both 24, on his list of targets.

United are able to pay Umtiti the £8m-a-year salary he is demanding, one Barca are unwilling to match and which could ultimately lead to his departure this summer.

Barca would normally wait to open contract talks with Umtiti, but with his release clause £52million – a modest figure given the exorbitant fees in the current market – they have had to act now.

Nou Camp officials know they risk losing Umtiti his summer if they cannot agree new terms, with United confident they use the contract stand-off to their advantage to land the player.

Barcelona fear they will lose defender Umtiti and have drawn up a list of replacements, according to Sport.

Barcelona have not given up hope of keeping the 24-year-old at the club but have put together a contingency plan in case he heads for the exit.

Why a Saudi Arabia international is training with Manchester United for next three weeks revealed

Saudi Arabia international Mohammad Al-Sahlawi will train with Jose Mourinho's Manchester United team, it has been confirmed, writes Mirror Football's Jake Polden.

The 31-year-old striker is in training for the World Cup in Russia, and is hoping to step up his preparation at Carrington over the next three weeks.

Al-Sahlawi is a key player for his nation, and has netted a brilliant 26 goals in just 33 international appearances. But unfortunately for the forward, Jose Mourinho has no intention of actually signing him.

The news that he would be training with the Manchester United team was announced by Chairman of the General Sports Authority of Saudi Arabia.

Taking to Twitter, Turki Al-Sheikh wrote: "It was agreed with Manchester United for Mohammed Al-Sahlawi to join for a training programme for three weeks, may God benefit him."

Al-Sahlawi currently plays for Al-Nassr, but has been allowed to train with the Manchester United squad as part of a commercial agreement between the Red Devils and the sporting authorities in the Gulf state.
