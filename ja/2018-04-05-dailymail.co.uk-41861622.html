Photos captured from a helicopter showcase the world's highest peaks

Published: 03:18 EDT, 5 April 2018 | Updated: 03:18 EDT, 5 April 2018

If you've always dreamed of witnessing the world's highest mountains in all of their glory then keep scrolling now.  

Professional adventurer Jack Wheeler - who has visited every country in the world and broke world records for being the first to skydive on the North Pole and for being the youngest to climb the Matterhorn at just 14 years old - now runs expeditions around the world, with one of his most popular tours taking passengers through the Himalayas by helicopter.

The 74-year-old from California, who has an infectious thirst for exploration, told MailOnline Travel that from all of the experiences he's had, helicoptering in the Himalayas has to be one of his all time favourites with visits to the world's highest peaks intermixed with landings at remote communities on the way.

Photos from his past excursions - which traditionally run in May and November to allow for better flying conditions - capture unbeatable close-up views of mountains including Dhaulagiri (26,795 feet), Makalu (27,838 feet), Cho Oyu (26,864 feet) and Everest (29,029 feet).

Wheeler says he uses specialised helicopters and some of the world's top rescue pilots, as flying at such high altitude is extremely difficult with the low air pressure making it difficult to generate lift. All passengers must wear oxygen masks when they fly.

Due to the specialist nature of the Himalaya Helicopter Expedition trip, tickets come in at just under £16,000 per person for the eight-day tour, excluding international flights. So, if your bank balance doesn't quite run to that, buckle up for a ride from the comfort of your armchair and minus the oxygen mask...
