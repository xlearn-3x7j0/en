Latest on The Undertaker's WWE status amid WrestleMania 34 return rumours

A possible WrestleMania 34 mega-match between John Cena and The Undertaker still hangs in the balance - just days before the extravaganza.

WWE is yet to advertise the bout, after Taker once again refused to answer the challenge of Cena on this week's edition of Monday Night Raw.

The decision not to confirm whether the long-awaited showdown between two of the industry's biggest stars is one that has many fans puzzled.

The Undertaker appeared to retire and say an emotional farewell after his main event defeat to Roman Reigns at last year's WrestleMania.

Leaving his hat, gloves and iconic black cloak in the centre of the ring, many thought the 53-year-old was calling time on his illustrious career.

The seven-time world champion returned on Raw 25 in New York in January and cut a rambling promo, which left everyone none the wiser.

And by ignoring weeks of taunts from Cena, 40, fans remain in the dark as to whether the pair will clash in New Orleans, Lousiana on April 8.

It seems inconceivable that after months of speculation and rumours, the Phenom would not show his face at the Mercedes-Benz Superdome.

But he did undergo hip surgery last year and there are some observers who believe he is not ready for a singles match at the Show of Shows.

It has been suggested in some quarters that Big Evil may turn up as part of a segment intended to lead to a future confrontation with Cena.

However, that would leave the audience disappointed and Cena having to accept watching proceedings as a member of the WWE Universe.

In the pay-per-view era, not announcing a bout of this magnitude would be commercial suicide, but now it seems fans will watch Mania regardless.

That is because every year the company records a large increase in WWE Network subscribers following the Showcase of the Immortals.

WWE simply don't need to reveal whether this long awaited battle will be part of a seven-hour show with an already massively stacked card.

So the question remains, is The Undertaker really retired? Will he confront Cena as he sits at ringside watching the event with fellow fans?

Could he resurrect his American Bad Ass persona - having ditched the Lord of Darkness character - and hit the ring on a motorbike?

The man who recorded his entrance song during that phase of his career - Kid Rock - will be on hand following his WWE Hall of Fame induction.

By now WWE fans have all seen footage posted online by Taker's wife, ex-WWE women's champion Michelle McCool, of them working out together.

Surely that hasn't all been for show - there has to be an end game here, which must be some kind of encounter with the 16-time world champion.

PWInsider.com has reported that The Undertaker is scheduled for WrestleMania 34, so no matter what, he will be there in some shape or form.

That seems entirely logical - he and Big Match John must collide in some fashion on Sunday night.

But don't be surprised if the match we all want to see is held off for another year - even until WrestleMania 35!
