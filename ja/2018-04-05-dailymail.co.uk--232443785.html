Russell Simmons claims rape accuser sent nudes after alleged attacks

Russell Simmons shot down sexual assault allegations by one of his 16 accusers in court documents filed Wednesday

Russell Simmons, who has been accused of sexual misconduct by 16 women, claims one continued to sext him weeks after an alleged rape, according to legal documents filed on Wednesday.

The Def Jam Recordings co-founder, 60, said Jennifer Jarosik, 37, who came forward in January alleging Simmons raped her, once in 2011 and again in 2016, sent him nudes and romantic texts after both of the alleged attacks, according to claims in court documents, obtained by TMZ.

Days before the alleged August 2016 rape, the woman apparently wrote to him: 'love to c u soon' and 'I emailed u love' when she did not receive a reply back right away.

A few weeks after the alleged second attack, she wrote to him: 'I miss you Russell... are you OK?' and 'Sending love,' as well as 'Coming to 1 a. I want to see u OK', according to the documents. Simmons said he ignored the messages. 

Phat Farm clothing creator Simmons and self-proclaimed 'writer and yoga instructor' Jarosik have been 'casual' acquaintances for over a decade, he said.

The documents allege the woman has a 'propensity to exaggerate,' suffers 'untreated mental health issues,' and is 'unfit to properly parent' her young son.

Amid the new claims, Jarosik's attorney, Perry C. Wander, told the gossip site: 'Ms. Jarosik is a loving mother and a good parent and her parenting abilities have no relevance in this matter.'

Jarosik is suing Simmons for $5million for allegedly raping her during an interview in New York City and years later at his home in Los Angeles. Simmons and his legal team claim the large sum of money is her motivation.

Jarosik came forward to give her account of two alleged incidents in a January interview with Megyn Kelly.

'He asked to speak with me privately. So my director left the room. He aggressively came onto me, he started to kiss me, then he raped me.'

She told Kelly she tried to fight him off, but that it 'happened so quickly I just froze.'

Jarosik said she was prompted to come forward this year after hearing other women's stories about Simmons.

'The music industry and the hip-hop industry have a history of misogyny and the exploitation of women,' Jarosik's attorney Perry Wander told The Associated Press.

'It's time that they faced the music.'  

Simmons said previously in a statement that the allegations in her suit are 'absolutely untrue.'

'I look forward to having my day in court - where, unlike the court of public opinion, I will have the ability to make use of fair processes that ensure that justice will be done and that the full truth will be known,' Simmons said in a statement. 

In the lengthy statement he said that the allegations of women against him range 'from the patently untrue to the frivolous and hurtful.'

Simmons said: 'These horrific accusations have shocked me to my core and all of my relations have been consensual.'
