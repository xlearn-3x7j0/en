Stedman Bailey opens up about being shot twice and comeback to NFL 

Former Rams receiver Stedman Bailey has opened up about being shot in the head, his miraculous recovery and his arduous return back to the NFL.

'I'm ready to show the world that I can't be stopped,' the 27-year-old said, who participated in Marshall's pro day last month.

He continues to train among NFL players, including Rams running back Todd Gurley because he says he wants to be an inspiration to others, he said in an interview with the Bleacher Report.

West Virginia graduate Stedman Bailey runs the 40-yard dash during pro day in front of NFL football scouts, Thursday, March 29, 2018, three years after he was shot twice in the head

Bailey has set a high goal for himself - to land on an NFL roster and win Comeback Player of the Year honors in 2018.

'I know when I step on the field, there's a 100 percent chance I win Comeback Player of the Year. And then I'll get up on the stage and just blow you away with all that I've been through,' he said.

On November 24, 2015, Bailey was in a car with four other people, including a cousin of his and the cousin's two children, at a private residence in Miami when another vehicle arrived and someone inside it opened fire. 

Wide receiver Bailey watches from the bench in the fourth quater of a preseason NFL football game against the Cleveland Browns August 2013, in Cleveland

His cousin, Antwan Reeves, was injured after shielding his ten-year-old daughter and five-year-old son, however Bailey took two bullets to the head.

In October he had surgery to insert a plate into his skull, which 'changed the game' for his return to football, The Herald-Dispatch reported.

The plate is stronger than a skull bone and will be able to protect him against forceful hits he would take playing in the NFL.

'I never had a doubt in my mind that I’d be able to play again,' Bailey said. 

Bailey talks with media after working out for NFL scouts during Marshall University's Pro Day on Wednesday, March 7, 2018

'It kind of hurt with reports from doctors that I may not be able to walk or I may have trouble speaking again. Me, just being a positive person that I am, I just waved those reports off like, "OK, we’ll see." I set it in my mind to show them differently.'

Speaking about the tragic incident and his miraculous recovery Bailey said 'I have a divine purpose to be here. You don't just survive those shots and walk around normal.'

'I feel like we as people all have specific plans on Earth,' he continued.

Bailey said he wants to inspire others and prove to them that they can handle anything. 'I want to be the one to show people that no matter what you go through, you don't have to let other people define who you are as a person, what you can do as a person -- and I can show the world anything is possible.'

Bailey has gone as far to imagine what his first touchdown celebration would be if he gets a second chance. 'I'm taking my helmet off so everybody can see,' Bailey said of the scar that runs across his skull. 'Helmet. Off.'

Bailey catches a long pass in the closing minutes at Lambeau Field on October 11, 2015 in Green Bay, Wisconsin, a month before the shooting 

In 38 career games, including eight starts, he tallied 59 catches for 843 yards and two touchdowns, along with 46 yards and a touchdown on the ground. 

Bailey also returned a kickoff 90 yards for a touchdown in 2014. Bailey played three years for the Rams.

He was on suspension for violation of the NFL's substance abuse policy when he was shot twice in Miami in 2015.

Bailey told Dunn he's been medically cleared by his personal doctor to return to football.
