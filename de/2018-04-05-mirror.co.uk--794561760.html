Masters golfer suffers freak injury during Augusta round

Tony Finau suffered a freak injury in the Par-3 Contest at Augusta National - as he dislocated his ankle before popping it back into place.

The 28-year-old wowed the crowds by acing a hole-in-one at the 115-yard seventh during the annual contest on the eve of his Master's debut.

The American, ranked world No. 34, sprinted down the fairway in celebration but excitement turned to concern as he came to a halt, stumbled backward and rolled over his ankle.

Finau then seemed to pop it back into place himself and then limped off.

He played on for two holes following the dislocation but then attended a medical facility and received treatment after his round.

Finau, who was due to tee off at 17:43BST on Thursday is now in doubt for The Masters, as Finau’s agent told ESPN: "The X-rays were negative and Tony will have an MRI scan on Thursday morning.

Other freak accidents...

Steve Morrow

In the 1993 League Cup final against Sheffield Wednesday, the midfield bagged the winner.

As the final whistle was blown and the celebrations began, skipper Tony Adams hoisted the hero into the air and promptly dropped him.

Morrow broke his collarbone and was straight in the back of an ambulance. He missed the rest of the season - including the FA Cup final! Poor Steve …

Dave Beasant

One-time England goalkeeper Beasant was a big fella and famously made history for Wimbledon's Crazy Gang in 1988 with the first ever penalty save in an FA Cup final.

He showed less safe hands as a Chelsea player five years later when he dropped a jar of salad cream which he instinctively stuck out a foot to save it and severed a tendon in his big toe.

More of a case of mayo toes than butter fingers …

Alex Stepney

Continuing the theme of goalkeepers, the former Man United player dislocated his jaw barking out order at his defence against Birmingham.

Darius Vassell

During his days at Aston Villa, Vassell rather foolishly attempted a spot of DIY medical care. Attempting to relieve the pressure of on a swollen big toe by draining the blood, he figured it would be a great idea to drill through the toenail… with a power tool!

The result was a nasty infection and several weeks on the sidelines.

Rio Ferdinand

Who says professional footballers have too much spare time? Absolutely not!

During his spell at Leeds, England defender picked up a tendon strain in his knee against Pro Evo. Apparently, it’s because he had his leg resting on the coffee table for too long when watching TV.

I think we’ve all been there…
