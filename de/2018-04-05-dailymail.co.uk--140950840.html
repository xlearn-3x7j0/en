Woman who shoved a man onto train track charged with attempted murder

Published: 23:28 EDT, 4 April 2018 | Updated: 06:54 EDT, 5 April 2018

A woman was indicted for attempted murder on Wednesday for allegedly tring to shove a construction worker into the path of an oncoming train.

Marjorie Chambers, 57, was charged with the felony charge for the attempted killing of Stephen Ware, the New York Daily News reports. 

The push occurred at the Union Square station in Manhattan and was captured on video Friday, according to Assistant District Attorney Shanitra Waymire.

As the Brooklyn-bound Q train made its way to the station around 12.15am, police quickly grabbed Ware.

The 27-year-old had been making his way home when the random attack occurred.

'I was walking straight down the platform toward the L when someone said 'f**k you' and just pushed me onto the tracks,' Ware detailed. 'I landed on my feet and fell over. It was such a flash. I came close to the third rail.'

Chambers has two prior arrests for trespassing in 2017 and assault for when she allegedly stabbed someone with scissors in 1995. 

The assailant had her arraignment conducted via a video from the Bellevue Hospital to Manhattan Criminal Court with judge Kevin McGrath. 

Chambers was ordered held on $150,000 bond or $100,000 cash bail.

Her lawyer, Rosemary Vassallo-Vellucci, argued that the case may be 'overcharged.' 

Chamber's lawyer added that her client is a mother-of-four and also a grandmother to three.

Her next court appearance is scheduled for April 16.
