Dele Alli outperforms England legends after 100 league games

  • Dele Alli added two goals to his Premier League tally against Chelsea on Sunday
  • The Tottenham star, 21, has already played 100 games in England's top division
  • He has scored 36 goals and assisted a further 25 for Tottenham in that time 
  • A quartet of England legends could not come close to his stats in the same time

Published: 08:25 EDT, 3 April 2018 | Updated: 10:26 EDT, 3 April 2018

Tottenham star Dele Alli is vastly outperforming a dazzling array of England midfield legends after 100 Premier League games, new statistics have revealed.

Alli celebrated his 100th Premier League appearance for Tottenham with two goals in a 3-1 win over Chelsea last weekend to earn their first victory away to the Blues since 1990.

It was a standout performance from the 21-year-old, who had been left fearing for his place in the England squad ahead of this summer's World Cup after playing just 22 minutes across two games during the international break.

But not only did it deliver a timely message to Gareth Southgate, it further cemented his place as one of the Premier League's most productive attacking midfielders with 61 goal contributions (36 goals, 25 assists) to his name already.

To put those statistics in context, Alli has contributed far more than Frank Lampard (18), Steven Gerrard (24), Paul Scholes (34), and David Beckham (43) managed in their first 100 Premier League appearances.

The figures serve to suggest that Alli may be heading for similar or even greater heights than the legendary quartet, who acquired 402 England caps and 20 Premier League winners' medals between them.

Making his figures all the more impressive, 10 of Alli's goals have come against the division's big six. He has scored five against Chelsea, two against Manchester City and one against each of Liverpool, Arsenal and Manchester United.

With 36 goals in the division already, Alli is on track to mount a serious challenge to Frank Lampard's 177 goals, which makes him by far the most prolific midfield goalscorer in the Premier League era and fourth overall.

Whether he stays in England's top flight for long enough to reach it, however, is a different question. Indeed, his performances have naturally drawn interest from Real Madrid and he could follow the same path trodden by Beckham by moving to Los Blancos.
