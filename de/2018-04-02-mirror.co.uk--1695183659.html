The Masters 2018: Live stream details, tee times, odds and more for Augusta

It promises to be one of the greatest Masters of all time with so many strong contenders and fascinating stories to follow.

Tiger Woods completing his renaissance would be one of the most incredible stories in sporting history.

But competition is fierce this year with so many world class players already breaking through in 2018 with victories.

Justin Thomas, Dustin Johnson, Jon Rahm, Jason Day, Bubba Watson, Phil Mickelson, Paul Casey and Rory McIlroy have all got the 'W' this year with their games in a good place.

Don't forget the defending champion either, Sergio Garcia finally broke through to win a major at Augusta last year - can he repeat to win another Green Jacket?

When and where is The Masters?

The Masters takes place over four days, from Thursday April 5 to Sunday April 8 at the historic Augusta National Golf Club in Georgia, USA.

When will it begin on Thursday?

The tee times have yet to be released with the full field set to be finalised on April 1 following the Houston Open, but look for the first group to go out at 8am local time (1pm UK time).

Tee times

The full rundown of tee times for Thursday and Friday will appear here when they are released.

What channel is it on?

The action will be shown live on Sky Sports across all four days, while the BBC Two will have third and fourth round action live on (starting at 7:30pm UK time on the Saturday, with coverage beginning an hour earlier at 6:30pm on the Sunday).

Catch the highlights on BBC Two with Thursday's best action shown at 5:45pm on Friday and Friday's best action shown at 1:15pm on Saturday.

Can I live stream the event?

Yes, you'll be able to stream through Sky Go , through Now TV or on the iPlayer.
