Zidane refuses to guarantee Bale start against Juventus despite two-goal display

Real Madrid boss Zinedine Zidane has refused to guarantee Gareth Bale a starting berth when Los Blancos take on Juventus in the first leg of their Champions League quarter-final on Tuesday night.

The Welshman struck twice as Real saw off Las Palmas in La Liga to consolidate third spot and plunge their opponents deeper into the relegation mire.

He stepped up in the absence of Cristiano Ronaldo, who was rested with the Bianconeri in mind, but that still may not be enough to secure a place in the starting XI.

Zidane said: "I'm very pleased with his performance. You'll have to wait and see if he plays on Tuesday."

Bale was a second-half substitute in both legs of the last-16 victory over Paris Saint-Germain.

He continues to be linked with a move away from Real Madrid with his relationship with Zidane reportedly strained.

The Champions League represents Real's last chance of silverware this season, sitting 13 points adrift of Barcelona in La Liga and having crashed out of the Copa del Rey.
