Ronaldo's bicycle kick applauded by all as Ramos gets 400th booking

The Champions League returned on Tuesday night as the quarter-finals of the competition kicked off. 

The first two first-leg ties of the last eight Juventus host Real Madrid, while Bayern Munich travelled to Sevilla, who knocked out Manchester United in the previous round.

Here, Sportsmail picks out six things you may have missed during both fixtures. 

Cristiano Ronaldo celebrates at full time after Real Madrid's victory over Juventus on Tuesday

They can't help but be amazed 

Cristiano Ronaldo's second goal against Juventus on Tuesday was one of the goals of the season.

The ability to get himself into position before executing the perfect bicycle kick was the perfect way to take his tally in the Champions League to 22 this season.

And it wasn't just Ronaldo's Real Madrid team-mates and manager Zinedine Zidane who were impressed.

Cristiano Ronaldo scored with an outrageous bicycle kick against Juventus on Tuesday night

Even Ronaldo's manager, Zinedine Zidane, couldn't believe what he had just witnessd

Even the Juventus fans who had packed into the Allianz Stadium couldn't help but clap his excellent strike that made it 2-0. 

Stoke striker and Sportsmail columnist Peter Crouch also couldn't let it go without his reaction. 

'There is only a few of us who can do that,' Crouch tweeted, referencing the goal he scored for Liverpool against Galatasaray in the Champions League. 

Juventus central defender Andrea Barzagli shrugged his shoulders after Ronaldo's strike

Juventus supporters could only applaud after Ronaldo doubled his tally on the night

Ronaldo continues to break his own records 

Cristiano Ronaldo has now scored 22 goals in Champions League quarter-finals, the same total that Juventus have scored in this stage of the competition

Ronaldo is also the first player in Champions League history to score in 10 consecutive games (16 goals). 

On top of that, the Real Madrid forward has scored in each of his six appearances against Juventus (10 goals).

Ronaldo has now scored 22 goals in the Champions League quarter-finals 

Dybala getting into a bad habit

Paulo Dybala has enjoyed a fine third season at Italian champions Juventus but he does seem to have picked up a bad habit.

The game against Real Madrid on Tuesday night was his third of the season against Spanish opposition. 

It was also the second game against teams from La Liga in which he has been accused on diving. In November against Barcelona it was suggested he dived to try and win a penalty.

He appeared to do something similar against Real Madrid but this time he was booked for his troubles. It was made to look even worse when he was sent off after receiving his second yellow of the night.

Paulo Dybala was booked for diving when he threw himself to the ground in the first half

Dybala was further punished in the second half when sent off for a second bookable offence

Sergio Ramos receives his 400th booking

You know what you get when you select Sergio Ramos in a starting line-up. You'll get passion, hard work and even the occasional goal. 

What you will also have to live with, however, is the ever-growing number of yellow cards dished out to the defender. 

This time he went into the referee's notebook for a blatant push on Dybala. It was his 400th yellow. 

Next stop 500. Well, you wouldn't put it past him, would you.  

Real Madrid captain Sergio Ramos was shown his 400th yellow card on Tuesday night in Turin

Ramos was shown a needless yellow card when he pushed over Juventus forward Dybala

How did he miss? 

Champions League football is high stakes stuff. So when Pablo Sarabia found himself in a perfect position to score for Sevilla he fluffed his lines.

The ball flashed across the Bayern Munich penalty area but he failed to convert from agonisingly close range in front of an open goal.

Sarabia did atone for his error minutes later when he scored to put Sevilla ahead.

Sevilla's Pablo Sarabia was left embarrassed when he missed from close range against Bayern

Isco finally replicating national team form?

There were some raised eyebrows when Isco got the nod to start against Juventus.

The accusation has been that Isco hasn't played as well for Real Madrid as he has for Spain.  

It is little harsh given it's not often a player will score four goals in a game as he did in the 7-1 win over Argentina. 

Indeed, he went a way to silencing his critics by performing a key role between midfield and attack, having a 100 per cent pass completion record in the first half and nutmegging Douglas Costa.

Isco turned on the style for Real Madrid when he nutmegged winger Douglas Costa

Isco slipped the ball between Costa's legs before running round the other side to get it back
