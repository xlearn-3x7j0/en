Published: 06:19 EDT, 3 April 2018 | Updated: 06:21 EDT, 3 April 2018

Manchester United are aiming to re-inject life into the Stretford End by offering thousands of £15 tickets to 18-25-year olds. 

The Premier League club have been examining options to improve the atmosphere at Old Trafford following criticism from supporters and manager Jose Mourinho.

This morning they announced a price freeze on season tickets for a seventh year in a row. And at the same time, United officials revealed a plan to try and boost noise levels in what was once an iconic stand which offered ferocious backing. 

Man United will offer thousands of £15 tickets for the Stretford End to 18-25-year olds 

They have introduced a new £15 ticket in the lower tier of the Stretford End which can only be bought by those between the age of 18 and 25. They say it ‘reinforces the club’s commitment to a vibrant atmosphere while helping younger fans to watch live, affordable football’.

United also state that the series of price freezes is the equivalent of a 17 per cent saving. They are also offering half-price season tickets to Under 18s and Over 65s and a 25 per cent discount for 18 to 20-year-olds.

United manager Jose Mourinho and supporters have criticised the atmosphere at Old Trafford

‘The club enjoys unparalleled support and we recognise the sacrifice many fans make to watch the team home and away,’ said managing director Richard Arnold. 

‘Continuing the freeze in match and season tickets for next season is driven by our belief in supporting fans attending our games.

‘The introduction of reduced ticket prices for young adults in the Stretford End lower tier stems from suggestions generated by fan groups through our Fans’ Forum, aimed at building on the tradition of vibrant support.’
