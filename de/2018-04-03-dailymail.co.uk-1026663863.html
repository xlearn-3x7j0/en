Klopp claims Liverpool 'need to create own history'

  • Liverpool host Manchester City on Wednesday night in first leg of quarter-finals
  • Jurgen Klopp urged his side to create new moments that will go down in history
  • He insisted that his side 'are ready' for the all-British Champions League tie
  • Klopp confirmed that defender Joel Matip is out for the remainder of the season 

Published: 10:34 EDT, 3 April 2018 | Updated: 11:25 EDT, 3 April 2018

Liverpool manager Jurgen Klopp has urged his side to create new moments that will go down in the history of the club against Manchester City in the Champions League.

He conceded that his side 'need to be proud of our history but we need to create our own history' as he insisted 'the boys are ready' for the showdown against City.

The Reds host Pep Guardiola's side on Wednesday night in the first leg of the quarter-finals.

Klopp spoke to media on Tuesday ahead of Champions League showdown against Man City

In his pre-match press conference on Tuesday afternoon, Klopp said: 'We need to be proud of our history but we need to create our own history.

'You have to do your own things. The boys are ready.'

Liverpool head into the last-eight tie on the back of an important win against Crystal Palace on Saturday. 

However Klopp received some bad news about defender Joel Matip earlier in the day as the centre back is expected to be out for the rest of the season as he is set to have surgery on a thigh injury. 

Klopp confirmed Matip was out for the remainder of the season, he said: 'Joel is out for the rest of the season, no doubt, but he should be back for the start of pre-season.'

On other injury news he said: 'Adam (Lallana), we have to see. It could have been worse. It's a hamstring injury and he's unavailable for the next few weeks. There's a chance he could be back this season.

'Emre will be very, very close for tomorrow.

'If there's a centre half in the room - I'm open for talks!'
