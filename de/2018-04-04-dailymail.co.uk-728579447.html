AC Milan 0-0 Inter Milan: Mauro Icardi's horror miss costs away side

Inter Milan will be scratching their heads after not winning this game against their fiercest rivals. 

Their captain Mauro Icardi, will not sleep a wink tonight, having uncharacteristically missed from point blank. Twice.

The Argentina striker has been ruthless all season but failed to convert the simplest of chances in the dying moments to snatch all three points.

Mauro Icardi failed to score from close range with the goal gaping in the dying moments against rivals AC Milan

Inter forward Icardi hung his head in shame after missing a gilt-edged opportunity to open the scoring earlier in the game

AC Milan striker Patrick Cutrone overheadkicked into the corner of the goal but it was ruled out for offside 

Icardi thought he had given Inter the lead after slotting under the AC Milan goalkeeper to score in the first half

The referee consulted VAR and chalked off the goal, having deemed Icardi's foot was just offside before he scored

He had found the net during the first 45 but after consulting VAR, the referee adjudged him to have jumped the gun and chalked it off for offside. It was a close call.

AC Milan had the ball in the net themselves, through Patrick Cutrone. The Italian superbly overheadkicked into the corner but the flag had already been raised. This time the technology was not needed. 

Despite the scoreline it was not a drab match, far from it. Inter always looked dangerous but also looked terrified of making a mistake at the back which made for an entertaining affair.

But was it a Milan derby of old? No. Both these sides are clearly still going through a transitioning period but it looks to be Inter who will emerge out of the other side first.

Luciano Spalletti's side didn't quite dominate the proceedings but were certainly on top and the only side you could have argued deserved the three points.

Before the match, Inter gave their arch rivals a bouquet of flowers in honour of former England international and Milan midfielder Ray Wilkins, who died on Wednesday morning at the age of 61, before Franco Baresi - a former team-mate - paraded a shirt which had 'Wilkins 8' on the back.

Once the game kicked-off it was all Inter. They set the tone from the first whistle, keeping the home side camped out in their own half.

Alessio Romagnoli prevented them from breaking the deadlock within the opening 90 seconds when Rafinha picked up a slack clearance from Ricardo Rodriguez inside the Milan penalty area, but he could only fire straight at the Italian centre-back. 

It took 20 minutes for AC Milan to create anything of note and that was from a set-piece. Hakan Calhanoglu whipped in a delicious cross and Learnardo Bonucci flicked it on at goal. It looked destined to find the corner, but somehow Samir Handanovic sprung across his goal line to deny him. 

Despite Inter being on top and controlling possession of the ball, the two sides went into the break on level terms.

Just before the hour mark, Inter re-won the ball in midfield and started a break down the wing. Ivan Perisic beat his man and whipped in a teasing cross. Icardi met it but somehow missed with the goal gaping.

Hakan Calhanoglu and Milan Skriniar both crash to the ground as they compete for possession of the ball

A section of the home support also held up a banner in tribute of former player Ray Wilkins, who passed away this week

It was not his evening, but things did get worse. After Cutrone's goal was ruled out, Franck Kessie tried his luck on the turn but shot harmlessly over. 

Inter went close once more when Brozovic picked out Perisic after having too much time in midfield, but his driven cross to find Icardi at the far post was parried by Donnarumma into the path of Roberto Gagliardini, who blazed wide after last-ditch defending by Rodriguez.

The match was heading for a draw but there was still time for one final twist, though it did not prove to be decisive. 

Perisic again broke down the wing in the dying moments and provided a superb delivery. Icardi was on the stretch but could not get enough contact on the ball as it drifted wide.

The real winners of this were Roma and Lazio, who now will be boosted of their chances of qualifying for next season's Champions League.
