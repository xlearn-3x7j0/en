Boy, 13, who survived being stuck in a sewer for 12 hours, speaks out

The 13-year-old boy who survived 12 hours after plunging 25-feet into a Los Angeles sewer pipe has spoken out about the ordeal, saying that he prayed constantly not to die as the night wore on. 

Jesse Hernandez fell into the four-foot diameter pipe while playing atop a Los Angeles Bureau of Sanitation Structure in Griffith Park on Sunday afternoon. 

He didn't see the wooden plank that covered a drainage ditch, and when he stepped it on the board it snapped and he plunged downward - spending the next several hours floating through tunnels filled with rushing water, sewage and toxic gas. 

'I was thinking like I was gonna die and I was never gonna see my parents again,' Hernandez told CBS News.

'One of the cameras showed fingerprints on the sides of the sewer, some elbow scrapings,' assistant director at LA Sanitation Adel Hagekhalil said.

'Right away, they thought something's close by.' 

'When I was sliding down, I was making those marks because I knew they were going to come in and try to find me,' Hernandez told KTLA.

'I was just praying to God to help me and not to die. I was scared.' 

He said the tunnels were dark and quiet, adding: 'I was scared.' 

'You could just hear the water running through,' he told NBC. 'You couldn't see anything.'  

Hernandez was found 'alive and talking' early Monday morning, and he was lifted through a maintenance hatch near where the 134 Freeway goes under the 5 Freeway, LA Fire Department Captain Erik Scott said.

Crews used remote video cameras to find the boy in the maze of 6,400 feet of underground pipes that cross under freeways and run parallel to the LA River. 

Scott called the frantic search a race against the time, explaining that the 'survivability diminishes in that toxic environment.' 

One he was pulled out, Hernandez immediately asked for a cell phone so he could call his mother, telling her: 'Mom, I'm alive. Come pick me up.'  

The teen underwent  'decontamination' at the County USC Medical Center before being released to recover at his home in Huntington Park. 

His stepfather Arturo Ramirez said the boy's blood test came back clean but that they were going to keep an eye on his condition. 

'We were praying, Ramirez told KTLA. 'I was very, very happy. All we wanted to do was hug him and kiss him.'
