Australia's nightmare tour of South Africa ends in humiliating 492-run hammering

Australia's nightmare tour of South Africa has ended in yet more humiliation after they were hammered by 492 runs in the fourth and final Test in Johannesburg.

Vernon Philander took 6-21 in Australia's second innings to wrap up a 3-1 series victory for the Proteas and inflict their opponents' second heaviest defeat ever, and fourth heaviest in all Test cricket, in terms of runs.

Aiden Markram cracked 152 for the hosts in their first innings as they posted 488 all out. Half centuries from Usman Khawaja, Tim Paine and Pat Cummins helped Australia to 221 in response.

South Africa opted not to enforce the follow-on and skipper Faf Du Plessis' 120 led them to 344-6 declared in their second dig.

That left Australia needing an impossible 612 runs to win, and they were cleaned up for just 119 thanks largely to Philander, whose six wickets came for the concession of just three runs.

Morne Morkel took the final wicket on his last appearance for South Africa, and he said: "It's tough to put it into words, I'm obviously very emotional but it's been a great journey.

"I'm so grateful for all the opportunities I was given and to go out on a high - the mission was to beat the Aussies on home turf and to accomplish that is a special feeling."

The match was the last of Darren Lehmann's coaching reign after the coach stood down in wake of the ball-tampering scandal.

Results on the tour were rendered almost irrelevant when opening bat Cameron Bancroft was seen using sandpaper to alter the condition of the ball on the third day of the third Test in Cape Town.

The incident led to skipper Steve Smith and vice-captain David Warner receiving 12-month bans with Bancroft being hit with a nine-month suspension.

Australia will not play any more cricket until their limited overs tour of England in the summer.

They will play warm-up fixtures against Sussex and Middlesex before five one-day internationals against England.
