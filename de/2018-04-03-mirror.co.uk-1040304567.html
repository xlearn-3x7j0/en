Raheem Sterling forced to apologise to Usain Bolt after prank backfires

Raheem Sterling was forced to apologise to Usain Bolt after a video prank backfired.

Whilst filming with YouTuber KSI, the Manchester City man let the host send a text to whoever he wanted off his phone - and he regretted it big time.

KSI decided to take the opportunity to text world-record sprinter Usain Bolt, and his choice of question confused the Jamacian Olympian.

After Bolt's confused reply to a message asking him how many girls he had slept with, Sterling calls up to apologise in the hilarious clip.

KSI grabbed the England international's phone and asks him: "What can I say...?"

Sterling replies: "[Bolt's] calm to be fair, as long as it's not anything strange.

"[KSI's] making me nervous!"

KSI's message to the Olympian read: "How many girls have you [bleeped]? I'm interested."

Sterling, who saw the funny side, said: "Oh my days, he's gonna be like 'huh?'

"[Bolt] just replied 'what??' I have to call him to apologise.”

He then calls up the Jamaican, and says: "Sorry I'm doing a video for YouTube with KSI and one of the things was to find someone in your contact to message. And this idiot sent that.

"When he sent it I'm like nah, no way. Sorry about that."

Luckily, Bolt saw the funny side and the whole thing ended with a laugh.
