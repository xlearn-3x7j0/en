Former Man United keeper Anders Lindegaard signs new Burnley deal

  • Goalkeeper Anders Lindegaard has signed on at Burnley until June 2019
  • Lindegaard has not featured for the Clarets since joining last summer 
  • He signed from Preston but is not preferred to Nick Pope or Tom Heaton

Published: 10:23 EDT, 5 April 2018 | Updated: 10:23 EDT, 5 April 2018

Burnley goalkeeper Anders Lindegaard has signed a new contract to remain at Turf Moor for another season.

The 33-year-old has signed an extension that will see him stay with the Premier League side until at least June 2019.

Lindegaard has not featured for the Clarets since joining them last summer, with England's Nick Pope making the number one jersey his own.

A statement on Burnley's official website said: 'The former Manchester United keeper and Danish international has played a key role as part of the goalkeeping unit working under Billy Mercer at Turf Moor.

'Lindegaard has been on the bench for the past 27 Premier League games, providing back-up for Nick Pope in the absence of injured club captain Tom Heaton.

'And with Heaton closing in on a return to first-team contention and Lindegaard signed up, the Clarets' goalkeeping department remains in safe hands.'

Denmark international Lindegaard arrived in England when he joined Manchester United in 2010 and made 19 league appearances for the Old Trafford side before spells with West Brom and Preston.
