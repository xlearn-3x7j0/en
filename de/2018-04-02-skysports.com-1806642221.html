Pernilla Lindberg beats Inbee Park in ANA Inspiration play-off

By Ali Stafford

Last Updated: 02/04/18 5:24pm

Pernilla Lindberg claimed a maiden major title after edging past Inbee Park in a mammoth play-off at the ANA Inspiration in California.

The duo finished tied on 15-under in regulation play and could not be separated after four play-off holes on Sunday before darkness fell, taking the contest to a fifth day for the first time in tournament history.

The three players shared the spoils over the first three extra holes on Monday morning at Mission Hills Country Club, only for Lindberg to secure victory with a birdie at the eighth extra hole.

"I know I'm a grinder and towards the end yesterday I felt that this is mine and I could do this," Lindberg said. "I just kept fighting away."

The two players ventured to the par-four 10th for the fifth play-off hole, where Lindberg almost holed from 30 feet as the pair registered two-putt pars.

Park found the bunker off the next tee - the par-three 17th - as Lindberg found thick rough just short of the green, only for the pair to get up-and-down to sink clutch putts and extend the contest.

Lindberg attacked the green in two when the players returned to the 18th, the par-five used for the four play-off holes on Sunday, as Park elect to lay-up and pitch to 10 feet with her third shot.

The former world No 1 missed her birdie attempt to hand Lindberg the advantage, only for the Swede to lag her eagle try to six feet but push wide her following effort on her way to a three-putt par.

Both players found the green in two when they returned to the par-four 10th, where Lindberg drained a 25-footer for victory and Park failed to convert her birdie chance to extend the contest.
