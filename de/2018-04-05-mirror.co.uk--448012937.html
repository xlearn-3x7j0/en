When is the Grand National 2018? All the details on the Aintree Festival

It's the biggest race in the British horse racing calendar - the Randox Health Grand National.

And as the horse racing jump season builds pace towards a period of thrilling race meets, the main event of the campaign is almost upon us.

The handicap steeplechase, complete with over four miles of track and 30 jumps, is coming round faster than a champion racer round the bend.

With 40 horses set to compete in the iconic race, Aintree is set to be the focus for its three-day festival.

But when is the Grand National? Here's all the details you need to know...

When is it?

The Aintree Festival begins on Thursday April 12, and will be taking place till the final race on Saturday 14.

With the first race expected to kick off at around 1.40pm on the opening day, there is plenty of top quality action for race-goers to get stuck into.

But it's the festivals showpiece Grand National race which will garner the most interest.

It will be the penultimate showdown of the festival with the contest set for a likely 5.15pm slot on Saturday 14, so get that date in your diary!

Race dates and schedule

An official schedule is yet to be announced, so keep an eye out here and we'll update you as soon as possible.

But in the meantime, you can still work out what day might be best for you if you want to join in on the action.

Opening day - Grand National Thursday April 12

There are plenty of Grade One races, with the Randox Health Foxhunters' Steeple Chase and the Aintree Hurdle on the agenda.

Ladies Day - Friday April 13

Ladies Day at the Aintree Festival is not just any Friday, it's Fabulous Friday where the punters are encouraged to dress to impress.

The festival will be hosting a Style Award under the hashtag 'FabulousFriday'.

And of course there's lots of top drawer racing to whet your appetite, with the Randox Health Topham Steeple Chase and the JLT Melling Steeple Chase set to be contested.

Find out more about Ladies Day in our guide.

Grand National Day - Saturday April 14

The pinnacle day of the festival, and one of the biggest days in racing worldwide - Grand National Day.

Since 1839 the National Hunt race has been run annually, and like every year that has gone before now, it promises to be an historic day at Aintree.

The racecourse will be a packed house in anticipation of the day's highs and lows, and will hold its breath for the main event.

Four miles, 514 yards, two laps, 30 fences, but only one winner, who will it be?

Tickets

Tickets have been in high demand and are now in short supply, with the official website sold out.

Fear not though, as there are still some available on Seatwave and Stubhub for you to get your hands on.

How can I watch it?

This year the Grand National will be aired live on ITV for the second year running.

Ed Chamberlain is scheduled to present.
