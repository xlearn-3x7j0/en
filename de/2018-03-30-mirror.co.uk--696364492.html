Mourinho responds to claims he spoke to France boss Deschamps about Pogba

Jose Mourinho has denied claims that he spoke to Didier Deschamps about Paul Pogba's recent form, saying he hasn't spoken to the France boss for 14 years.

Pogba has come under the spotlight in recent weeks after struggling to hold down a place in the Manchester United side, with plenty claiming that heading off to international duty must have been a welcome break for him.

Deschamps spoke about Pogba's recent displays after France's friendlies against Colombia and Russia, against whom he scored a fine free-kick.

"This is a situation (not playing at United) that he must not appreciate because of everything he would have been able to offer," said the France boss.

"There must be numerous reasons. He cannot be happy with what he is going through with his club.

"It’s not specific to Paul. It can happen elsewhere that a player is a bit down or is going through a particular situation with his club.

"That could be Paul now, or others at other times. And I will do as I always do and talk to that player to understand better, because I don’t have all the information."

But after several reports claimed that Mourinho had spoken to Deschamps about Pogba, the United boss denied that at his press conference ahead of Saturday's meeting with Swansea.

"That’s not true," he said.

"The last time I spoke to him was when we faced each other in 2004."

That was in the Champions League final of that year, when Mourinho's Porto beat Deschamps' Monaco 3-0.

Pogba is expected to be in the squad for the visit of Swansea, with United seeking to hold onto second place in the table.
