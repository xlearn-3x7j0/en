Sergio Garcia reveals incident that almost ruled him out of Masters success

Sergio Garcia has revealed he nearly stepped on a rattlesnake just days before winning the Masters title.

The Spaniard finally won his first Major at Augusta last year when he saw off Justin Rose in a play-off.

But the Ryder Cup legend risked another sudden-death scenario the week before when he was hunting on his future father-in-law Marty Akins' ranch in Texas and his now wife Angela spotted the snake.

The world No.9 said: “I would literally have put my foot probably three inches left of it. Obviously we were wearing snake boots and everything, but you never know. If I step on it and it bites me, maybe I’m not even playing in Augusta. So I guess it was just one of those things that was meant to be.”

Angela said Garcia showed less cool than on the greens of Augusta – and she had to grab the gun and shoot the snake.

“It was pretty funny to me that eight days after he almost stepped on that rattlesnake, he won the Masters,” said the American.

The two were married last summer and Angela last month gave birth to their first daughter named Azalea.

“She said we should name our first baby something related to where you win your first Grand Slam,” Garcia said. “I hoped it wasn’t Shinnecock.”
