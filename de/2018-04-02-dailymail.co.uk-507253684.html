Chelsea Q&amp;A: What next for Antonio Conte and his struggling squad?

Chelsea’s first defeat by Tottenham since February 1990 was excruciating for a club who this time last year were cruising towards becoming champions of England.

They are now fifth and on the eve of Sunday’s clash, Antonio Conte admitted that losing would leave them all but out of the race for Champions League qualification.

So what now? The danger for Chelsea is this could be only the beginning of their troubles and Sportsmail answers your questions here.

Chelsea were handed their first defeat by Tottenham since February 1990 on Easter Sunday

What does this mean for Conte?

Defeats such as this only further force him towards the door, as if he wasn’t heading there already.

As Chris Sutton wrote for Sportsmail on Monday, this was the day Conte seemed to run out of ideas. Once Tottenham got their noses in front, there was only going to be one winner.

Even when Chelsea scored their opener through Alvaro Morata, Conte’s celebration was unusually muted. He later kicked out at a few water bottles following Dele Alli’s well-taken goal, though stood with hands in pockets for the majority.

The Italian is set to leave at the end of the season when Roman Abramovich decides, after a review of their title defence, that he has not done a good enough job. There will be no vote of confidence statement, no trying to hide their disappointment.

Losing out on the Premier League title to Pep Guardiola’s Manchester City juggernaut is one thing. Failing to qualify for Europe’s elite competition is entirely different.

This was the day Antonio Conte seemed to run out of ideas and was pushed closer to the exit

What are the dangers of missing out on a top-four finish?

Primarily, they have players who would find it embarrassing to not be competing in the Champions League. Eden Hazard, for one.

Here’s where we stand with Hazard at the moment: Chelsea want him to sign a new £300,000-a-week contract, he is continuing to stall, the club are concerned, Real Madrid’s tails are up.

Ask yourself, why has he not signed it yet? Already frustrated by being used up front rather than on the wing at times by Conte, maybe he has been waiting to see who the new manager is. Maybe he was waiting to see whether Chelsea qualify for the Champions League.

As for Thibaut Courtois, he is likewise holding off on signing a new deal with his current contract set to expire at the end of next season. Arsenal were heavily criticised for allowing themselves to get into a similar scenario with Alexis Sanchez and Mesut Ozil. Chelsea now face that themselves.

Players, including Eden Hazard, would find it embarrassing to not be in the Champions League

So could this harm Chelsea in the transfer market?

It could. Imagine you’re, say, Marco Verratti and Paris Saint-Germain, for whatever reason, are willing to listen to offers. They come flooding in. Not being able to offer Champions League football would only harm Chelsea’s chances.

Sure, you can insert a big bonus for him playing a part in getting you back into Europe, but why would he bother if he can get it guaranteed elsewhere?

Big names want to be on the big stage. Chelsea would have to convince whoever they sign that they are on their way back and this comes at a time when a squad overhaul is being called for.

With the World Cup taking up a chunk of this summer, they could have done with the Champions League in their back pocket to lure players. It ticks boxes.

This will harm Chelsea’s chances in the transfer market when their squad needs rebuilding

And in their managerial search?

Conte was always expected to leave at the end of the season anyway, but his successor would have liked the Premier League club in adequate shape at least.

Luis Enrique has continued to act as favourite while Thomas Tuchel and Carlo Ancelotti have also been mentioned. Chelsea will hope whoever takes over does not mind that they have slipped to fifth.

It is unlikely something such as this would force one of those candidates to snub an approach, however. Coaches can often pride themselves on turning things around.

Luis Enrique continues to be the favourite to take over from Conte at Stamford Bridge

What has Conte said?

Crestfallen Conte was asked whether he was worried about his immediate future following the loss to Tottenham. Chelsea are a club that traditionally act once top-four hopes fade, after all.

Conte claimed he was not concerned about losing his job in the aftermath, but did admit he was worried about why his hard work is not getting him results.

’If we are in this position, we deserve to be in this position,’ Conte said. ‘You have to ask the club, not me [about the significance of finishing in the top four].

‘I repeat: my task is to work and I know I work 24 hours a day for Chelsea, me and my staff. The commitment of the players is top.

'Despite this we stay in this position. We must be a bit worried about this.’ Interesting.

Conte claimed he was not concerned about losing his job in the aftermath of the Spurs defeat

What is needed now for Chelsea?

A rebuilding job. Conte is a good coach, no doubt about it, and he will say he is the man for the job.

Some supporters will say he has earned that right, given he won the title in his first season in England. Others will say Chelsea lacked leaders against Tottenham on Sunday and that is Conte’s fault because they sold Diego Costa and lost John Terry on his watch.

Fans are split. Conte has always insisted if you give him a 6/10 player, he will turn him into a 8/10. Give him an 8/10 player, he will turn him into a 10/10 one. That takes time and patience is a luxury rarely afforded in the Premier League.

Chelsea owner Abramovich will want an instant reaction and the club are likeliest to look elsewhere rather than extend Conte’s contract, which is now set to enter its final year.

On the plus side, playing in the Europa League could offer youngsters such as Dujon Sterling and Ethan Ampadu a chance of first-team football. In the aftermath of a defeat by Tottenham, though, the silver lining is hard to find.
