How Dustin Johnson is trying to avoid repeat of back injury at Masters 2018

World No.1 Dustin Johnson is renowned for not having much upstairs.

American writer Rick Reilly once claimed he is “so dense, light bends around him”.

And so after slipping on the wooden stairs of his rented Augusta home in his socks and hurting his back 12 months ago, Johnson has taken the step of renting a ­bungalow at the ­Masters this year.

“It’s not the same one, no,” said the 2016 US Open champion. “The other one just got bad juju so I’m not going there.”

The other half of American model and singer Paulina Gretzky had been on top of the world arriving in Georgia last year.

He became world No.1 for the first time with his win at the Genesis Open – the first of a hat-trick of victories – and was hot favourite until his fall.

Despite turning up on the Thursday to try to play, he pulled out just before his tee time.

“It was tough to watch but I couldn’t do anything else – I was still laying on the couch,” he said. “I wanted to be there playing becasue I was playing probably the best golf of my career. I didn’t want to watch it on TV, but things happen.”

Johnson took four weeks off but ­reckoned it took “a good five months” to recover from his Masters withdrawal.

The 33-year-old started his year saying he was capable of ­winning nine or 10 events – and promptly won the Sentry ­Tournament of ­Champions in Hawaii by eight shots.

“If I can play like that every week, yeah, I’m going to win a lot of times,” he said.

With huge drives and sharp wedge-play, he also has the game for Augusta, where he has finished tied-fourth and tied-sixth in his last two ­appearances.

Jon Rahm claimed catching Johnson as world No.1 was like trying to defeat “Superman”.

“I check the rankings and it’s nice to see your name on the top,” said the laid-back American. “I still don’t really ­understand them but I can read ‘No.1’.”
