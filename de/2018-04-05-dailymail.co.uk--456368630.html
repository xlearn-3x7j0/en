Woman sues Mormon Church after alleged rape by Mormon leader

McKenna Denson's suit accuses Joseph L Bishop (pictured), 85, of 'horrific sexual assault' in a secret room at the flagship center in Provo back in 1984

A woman who claims she was raped by the president of the Latter-Day Saints Missionary Training Center in Utah is suing the Mormon church. 

McKenna Denson's suit accuses Joseph L Bishop, 85, of 'horrific sexual assault' in a secret room at the flagship center in Provo back in 1984.

She also claims Bishop - who she is suing alongside the church - had been put in charge of the training facility despite there being 'red flag sexual improprieties' earlier in his career. 

Mentioned in Denson's suit is the case of a young woman serving as a missionary in Argentina and the alleged inappropriate treatment of women at Weber State University when Bishop was president, the Salt Lake Tribune reports.

Craig Vernon, Denson's attorney, said his client intends to hold a press conference later today, where the results of a polygraph test she took will be released.

A statement from Vernon's law firm read: 'Denson seeks justice for this horrific sexual assault as well as the ongoing emotional distress she suffered by repeatedly being ignored, disbelieved, shamed and blamed by the Church she loved so dearly.'

But a spokesman for the church responded only by referring to an earlier statement regarding allegations made against Bishop and the church.

'The Church has great faith in the judicial system to determine the truth of these claims,' the statement explains.

'Nevertheless, the Church takes seriously its responsibility to hold its members accountable for their conduct with respect of the laws of God and man.' 

Her case first came to the attention of many when an audio recording of a conversation between Denson and Bishop was released on the Mormon Leaks website. 

Bishop claimed in the conversation to not remember sexually assaulting her, but he did apologize and describe himself as a predator because of other misconduct.  

After that conversation, police officers said Bishop remembered taking her into a room and and asking her to 'show him her breasts' - which she reportedly did.  

The lawsuit claims he led her into a room, tried to kiss her, then pushed her onto a bed and tore her blouse and skirt.

She attempted to push Bishop away and leave but she was unable to stop him, the suit alleges. Soon after, she managed to flee, the suit claims.

Bishop then allegedly told her nobody would believe her testimony if she reported the crime, adding: 'Look at you, look at me'.   

Denson claims she told a church leader three years later but nothing happened. 

'In an apparent effort to protect the Church and the self-proclaimed sexual predator, leaders engaged in an ongoing pattern of not only failing to believe Denson, but even more disturbing, blaming and shaming Denson,' her lawyer, Vernon, wrote.

The suit also alleges Bishop, while president of the Buenos Aires North Argentina Mission in the late 1970s, confessed to misbehavior while speaking to his boss. 

Vernon also wrote in the suit, as reported by Fox13, that the case represents an 'epic institutional betrayal of trust'. 

He added: 'Bishop was called to be the President of the MTC in Provo, Utah. There, instead of having access to hundreds of young missionaries, the Church elevated him to a position of power over thousands of young women who were training to be missionaries.' 

But Bishop's family, who have previously accused Denson of making false claims, said Bishop denies the allegations.
