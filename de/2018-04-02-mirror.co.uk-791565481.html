Pardew's West Brom reign over after he failed to bring trademark fighting style

Alan Pardew's brief, controversial West Brom reign is over after he neglected to bring his trademark fighting style to Albion's relegation battle.

Pugnacious Pardew helped make a name for himself as a manager with his touchline antics and aggression.

There were infamous scrapes with Arsene Wenger when the then West Ham boss and his Arsenal counterpart had to be separated in 2006.

Then as Newcastle manager, TV cameras caught him calling Manchester City manager Manuel Pellegrini a "a f****** old c***" during a 2-0 defeat in 2014.

A record seven-game ban subsequently followed for his headbutt on Hull's David Meyler later that year.

But Pardew was unveiled with a promise of a more refined approach at West Brom in November on a contract until 2020 after pledging to have learned from mistakes.

Yet it meant he never got a strong grip on Albion's dressing room as the club's season imploded.

A return of just one League win in 18 matches made his departure inevitable from the doomed basement club.

When Tony Pulis was sacked the Baggies were a point above the drop zone.

But expectations of a bounce from a bold fresh style under Pardew never materialised.

In fact, his reign had the opposite effect as they now find themselves 10 points adrift. Pardew was hindered by injuries to creative duo Nacer Chadli and James Morrison.

While potential January messiah Daniel Sturridge has been crocked and played just 77 minutes at a cost of £50,000 every 60 seconds.

But an eighth successive Premier League loss at home to Burnley was the final straw with some fans throwing their season tickets on the pitch in disgust.

Afterwards Pardew made his latest public bid to force Albion into paying him off early when he admitted he was struggling to motivate the players.

In truth he lost them months ago as the squad didn't warm to either Pardew's tactics or confident personality.

There were moans over his call to take them on a warm weather bonding trip to Barcelona over Valentines Day in February.

The players wanted to stay behind with their families instead.

But Pardew insisted on going despite the temperatures being barely warmer than the Midlands.

The flagrant lack of respect for his authority was remarkably laid bare in Spain where club captain Jonny Evans and senior pros Gareth Barry, Jake Livermore and Boaz Myhill allegedly stole a taxi on a boozy night out.

They broke a midnight curfew to stay out until 5am just two days ahead of an FA Cup fifth round home tie with Southampton.

Albion predictably lost and haven't won since a shock 3-2 win at Liverpool in the previous round.

Pardew played 3-5-2 that day and then upset players with his subsequence preference for 4-4-2.

Chris Brunt, a Hawthorns stalwart since 2007, openly challenged Pardew about it in the dressing room after the home loss to Huddersfield in February.

Then PSG loanee and Polish international Grzegorz Krychowiak also hit out at Pardew when replaced by Sam Field, 19, against Leicester in the 59th minute last month.

Both stars subsequently apologised but the dissent at Pardew's reign was audible.

In Saturday's latest loss, it emerged from the stands too with a number of derogatory songs about the Baggies boss.

It would only have got worse this weekend at home to Swansea so putting club legend Darren Moore in caretaker charge has surely spared more ugly scenes.

New club chief Mark Jenkins , who returned in February when Chinese owner Guochuan Lai sacked chairman John Williams and CEO Martin Goodman, has promised a wide-ranging shake-up.

But there will be no escaping the club's looming relegation.

While Pardew, having waited 11 months to return to management after leaving Crystal Palace, could have an even longer wait for his next job.
