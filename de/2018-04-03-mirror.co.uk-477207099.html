Decision made on Canelo vs Golovkin rematch

Saul 'Canelo' Alvarez has pulled out of his rematch with Gennady Golovkin following his failed drugs tests.

The pair were due to meet in Las Vegas on May 5, eight months after their first fight ended in a controversial draw.

But Alvarez twice tested positive for the banned substance clenbuterol in February, throwing the fight into doubt.

The Mexican claimed he had eaten tainted meat in his homeland and expected the rematch to go ahead but the Nevada Athletic State Commission suspended him pending an investigation.

Alvarez has now withdrawn from the fight but maintains his innocence and said he expects to be cleared to face Golovkin in August or September.

He added: "I have always been a clean fighter and I always will be a clean fighter.

"I want to prove without a doubt that I have never intentionally ingested clenbuterol.

"I have nothing to hide and I want to be open and transparent through this process. I have never taken illegal substances and this is no different.

"I am sad and feel powerless that I can’t make this fight happen at this point."
