Where is Free Willy star Jason James Richter now?

This year marks the 25th anniversary of the release of Free Willy, one of the biggest kids' films of the 1990s.

But while the star Jason James Richter is still best known for his role as the young Jesse in the smash hit movie, the now 38-year-old is taking on more roles than ever - and is reportedly still worth a pretty penny.

Free Willy, which debuted back in 1993, follows the story of Jesse, a young boy who is put up for foster care and ends up working at a marine park run by the family who had taken him in. 

The big one: Jason James Richter, who played Jesse in the smash hit film Free Willy, is still in the acting business 25 years on

Then and now: Jason was launched to fame through the role in 1993 after being discovered as a youngster by a Japanese casting agency in Hawaii

There, he meets Willy, a captured Orca whose life in the park has left him unruly, unhappy and hostile towards most of the marine staff - except Jesse of course.

Jesse uncovers a plot to kill the whale as a part of an insurance scam and embarks on a mission to set Willy free, culminating in the famous moment where Willy leaps over the crying Jesse to rejoin his family in the wild.

When Jason was just three years old, his parents moved with him from Oregon to Hawaii. He was discovered by a Japanese casting agency there as a youngster and was put up for TV spots before he eventually moved to Los Angeles in 1989 to throw himself into the industry. 

Jason managed to land the role in Free Willy - his very first movie gig - after making it through a grueling audition process that saw him selected from a group of over 4,000 young actors. 

Launching: Free Willy was an incredible hit, grossing more than $150 million at the box office

Skyrocketing: Jason went on to star in two sequels for the movie and was even able to double his fee to $1 million at age just 15

Moving on: Jason also starred in Neverending Story III: Return To Fantasia

Looking on: At age 18, Jason (pictured in January) took a break from acting to pursue music

He eventually starred in two sequels to the film - doubling his fee on the second movie to $1 million at age 15 - as well as landing roles in the likes of The Neverending Story III, Cops & Robbersons and TV shows such as Sabrina the Teenage Witch and Rugrats.

In 1998 Jason stepped away from acting when he was just 18, instead turning his focus towards music. Aside from one role opposite Kate Hudson in 2001's Ricochet River would be nearly 10 years before he returned to the screen.

He dipped his toe back into the industry with 2009's Tekken and an appearance on TV show Bones, but in the last few years appears to have really ramped up his appearances.

Looking back: Eventually, Jason caught the acting bug again, dipping his toe in with some roles back in 2009

Still in the game: In the last few years, Jason (pictured in 2017's Last Rampage) has been taking on more and more roles

Familiar face: According to Metro, Jason is worth an impressive $500,000

According to IMDb, Jason has seven projects slated for 2018, including horror film Ghost in the Graveyard and thriller Driver.

Despite all these projects, Jason has still maintained a relatively low profile over the years, which he has said leads to people assuming that he has gone through the worst.

Around Free Willy's 20th anniversary, he told Daily Beast: 'There were rumors that I got a face tattoo. That I got into a car accident. That I became a drug addict. All this stuff.' 

Nothing could be further from the truth, it seems, as Metro reports that the former child actor is actually worth $500,000.
