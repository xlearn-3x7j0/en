England tour series ratings: Jonny Bairstow star turn but Alastair Cook dreadful

Despite a battling display in the Second Test there were more misses then hits in this short New Zealand tour.

Tail-ender Ish Sodhi hit a gutsy half century as New Zealand fought a defiant rearguard action on a tense final day to hold on for a draw in the second test on Tuesday and secure a first series win over England since 1999.

Winners of the first test in Auckland by an innings and 49 runs, New Zealand were 256 for eight when the umpires offered Sodhi the light, still 126 runs in arrears but with the all-important draw secure.

But the tour seemed ill-prepared with many - save a few notable exceptions - not living up to their reputation.

Here's how they rated...

Alastair Cook 1

An awful tour for England's record run scorer, but he took a couple of catches to get on the board. Owes his skipper a big one

Mark Stoneman 7

Two fifties in two games represents a reasonable return for the opener, but he seems to get stuck after raising his bat. Has to take another step forward this summer.

James Vince 7

Just the one game to show he has what it takes and a well made 76 again suggests he can live and possibly thrive at this level. Needs to cash in on his winter experience in the summer.

Joe Root 6

Two more fifties to go with his five from the Ashes were handy contributions but he knows he can do more. Growing in stature as captain.

Dawid Malan 5

A rusty series from Malan who hadn't played much cricket after the Ashes and it showed. But he finally got his feet going in Christchurch and reminded everyone of his ability.

Ben Stokes 6

A shame he couldn't bowl properly throughout the series, but showed real heart and character in adapting his game to try and save the first Test.

Jonny Bairstow 8

England's only century maker and a model behind the stumps. His efforts with the bat in Auckland though were shocking.

Stuart Broad 8

A return to form for Broad who got some just rewards for his hard work in between series' in the indoor nets at Nottingham. He's got lots more to give.

Mark Wood 4

Used as a battering ram and enforcer in Christchurch and it worked in the second innings. He is better than this though and his aim in the summer must be to get his pace and movement back.

James Anderson 7

Typically high-quality stuff from Anderson in keeping with how he's bowled over the last 12 months. He is a gem that needs looking after until he finally calls time whenever that will be.

Jack Leah 6

A very promising debut from Leach who has got terrific control and gives the ball a rip. There wasn't much assistance for him in Christchurch but there will be elsewhere.

Chris Woakes 3

A decent game in Auckland with the bat, but it is with the ball that he needs to step up and away from home his poor record is symptomatic of England's woes.

Moeen Ali 1

Has lost his way in Test cricket and could do with finding his way again with Worcestershire, but the IPL is the next stop and it is unclear when he'll be back playing Test cricket again.

Craig Overton 2

Has got all the endeavour and character to be a useful Test cricketer but on flat pitches currently lacks the skill to get the ball moving. He can improve on this.
