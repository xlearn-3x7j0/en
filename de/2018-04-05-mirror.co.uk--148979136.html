Perrie Edwards cheers on her boyfriend - but what she's wearing angers fans

Little Mix star Perrie Edwards was at Anfield to see boyfriend Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain star in Liverpool's 3-0 win over Manchester City, but what she was wearing didn't go down well.

Oxlade-Chamberlain scored a stunning goal as the Reds overwhelmed City in the first leg of the Champions League quarter-final , with his strike from distance putting them two goals ahead on a raucous Anfield night.

The star was seen singing You'll Never Walk Alone alongside friend Ellie Hemmings before the game, but - as reported by our friends at the Liverpool Echo - something wasn't quite right.

Despite looking as stylish as ever in a white long-sleeved top with a pair of cropped trousers, the style icon was turning heads for a completely different reason - her half-and-half scarf .

The 24-year-old took the controversial decision to wear a scarf bearing both the LFC and the Man City logo, and is in both clubs’ colours.

Although the video was posted to Instagram - it enraged fans so much they even took to Twitter to tell Perrie what they thought.

One user even took the time to tweet Oxlade-Chamberlain about it.

He said: “Ox tell Perrie the half-and-half scarf is a no no.”

Twitter user @Chinerz added: “Whys Perrie rocking a half-and-half scarf?”

Tino Palmer said: “Only thing that annoyed me that second half was finding out Perrie Edwards was wearing a half-and-half scarf.”

In February, merchandisers Hat Scarf or a Badge came up with a half-and-half design that was dubbed as ‘the only appropriate’ one.

The store revealed a Bill Shankly-inspired scarf that features Liverpool - and Liverpool Reserves.

The Twitter account @hatscarforabadg posted: “We’ve decided to finally give in and do a half n half scarf,... we know they are not liked but think you might like our version, #lfc#liverpool#shanks”.
