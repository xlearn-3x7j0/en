French Guineas aim for Capla Temptress

Last Updated: 02/04/18 11:40am

Capla Temptress has returned to Marco Botti and could have the French 1000 Guineas as her first major target.

Although the daughter of Lope De Vega holds an entry in the Qipco 1000 Guineas at Newmarket on May 6, the Newmarket handler feels the French equivalent at Longchamp a week later could be the more sensible option.

The Team Valor-owned filly won a Grade One for Botti at Woodbine in Canada last season, before moving to Bill Mott and finishing down the field in the Breeders' Cup Juvenile Fillies Turf at Del Mar in November.

Botti said: "She is back with us and I'm very pleased with her. She ran at the Breeders' Cup, then the owners had a meeting afterwards and decided to send her back to Europe.

"She had 40 days in quarantine after the Breeders' Cup and arrived back in Newmarket in December. She did well for us last year and I thought she was going to America for good, but thanks to Team Valor she is back.

"She will not go for a trial in the Fred Darling or Nell Gwyn. We are entered in the English Guineas and the French Guineas.

"We will see how she is mid-April and it would be silly not to consider the English Guineas, but at this stage I would say she is more likely to take her chance in the French Guineas.

"I just think statistics tend to show that is a little bit easier than the English one and it gives us a bit of extra time with her.

"She has not grown much, but she has done well physically and I think she looks stronger than last year. There is no doubt she has the ability and a great mind and that is a big plus."
