St Bart's locals are shocked over Pippa Middleton father-in-law claims

Locals on the plutocrats' playground of St Bart's (Saint Barthélemy) in the Caribbean say they were 'in shock' over the teen rape investigation surrounding hotel owner and property developer David Matthews – the father-in-law of Pippa Middleton.

But islanders have admitted to DailyMail.com, 'there is no law for these people on the island – they can do whatever they want here.'

Pippa's father-in-law was arrested at a Paris airport last week over allegations he had raped a 15-year-old girl in the late 90's. The first alleged assault is said to have taken place on the Caribbean island of St Bart's in 1998 and the second in Paris a year later.

His alleged victim, now aged 34, lives in the UK but is understood to have come forward to police in the French capital last year.

Her mother is said to be 'helping her with the case'. A source close to the enquiry said: 'The victim was just 15 at the time of the first attack, and the attacks continued.'

The source added: 'These are the attacks investigators are focusing on. A formal complaint was made to police in 2017.'

Residents and island authorities alike expressed their surprise at the scandal which now shrouded one of St Bart's most powerful investors and his iconic hotel, Eden Rock.

David Matthews had humble beginnings in Yorkshire, England as an apprentice mechanic and built his car business into a multimillion-pound fortune.

He and Jane, his second wife, bought and began renovating Eden Rock in 1995 after visiting the island and falling in love with it. They purchased the hotel in St Jean Bay from much-loved local eccentric and adventurer, Remy de Haenen and moved to the Caribbean with their four children.

One resident, from a notable family who have lived on the island for generations, told DailyMail.com: 'Local people who grew up on this island loved Remy, there was an elegance and simplicity to the place when he had it.

'But now these resorts - Eden Rock, Nikki beach, Bagatelle - they bring a lot of people to the island that causes a lot of problems, like drugs.

'Not necessarily the guests but the seasonal staff. They are worked extremely hard all day and then they party all night.

'It used to be people would smoke pot but now you see people on the streets with their eyes rolling back in their heads.

'St Bart's has changed dramatically and not necessarily for the better. It has been a bit of a relief to have some of these places missing this season after the hurricane.'

The five-star Eden Rock hotel, where rooms start at $600 a night, has become a favorite of celebrities and royals including Tom Hanks, Beyonce and Leonardo Di Caprio along with Matthews's son James and his new wife, Pippa. The rest of the Middleton clan have also been spotted at the resort.

Staff and nearby shop owners told DailyMail.com that they had not seen Matthews for several weeks.

It was expected that he would return to St Bart's, a tax haven which is his permanent residence, after he was released on bail following 48 hours in French police custody.

Statute of limitations for rape cases in France is 20 years after the victim has turned 18. The alleged victim in this case would have turned 18 in 2003. This means the case can be dealt with up until 2023.

Matthews was placed under formal investigation by a magistrate, similar to being charged with a crime in the UK. It points to 'serious or consistent' evidence of involvement and is a move towards trial but investigations can be dropped without ever going to court.

If found guilty, he faces up to 15 years in prison for the crime of 'rape of a minor by a person of authority'.

Local authorities in St Bart's went to lengths to explain that they have no part of the investigation.

The island's president, Bruno Magras (pictured right with Matthews) told DailyMail.com that he knew nothing about the alleged sexual assaults

The chief of the local Police Territoriale in the port town of Gustavia said that he found out about the alleged sexual assaults in the newspaper last week.

'I tell you, we are very surprised,' he said. 'This is someone we know on the island who has contact with the President. I can't tell you any more.

'If someone makes a report in this situation, they would do it with the Gendarmerie, not here. Maybe they have something but we don't get information,' he said.

The chief, who refused to give his name, added that there have never been any other problems with Eden Rock Hotel.

However one of Eden Rock's neighbors told a different story. 'We are forced to call the police because of the excessive noise coming from the property in the early hours,' the neighbor said.

'The staff are often partying late. At the moment, the management is good and there is respect. It has not always been the case in the past.'

The local police are not part of the investigation which is being led by the Juvenile Protection Brigade (BPM) in Paris. The BPM investigates allegations of pedophilia and child prostitution.

St Bart's is also protected by the National Gendarmerie, the police force of France, who handle serious crimes and control the island's official points of entry.

One officer told DailyMail.com he was 'very surprised' and had read about the allegations 'on Facebook'.

One local told DailyMail.com: 'It's incredibly sad people think St Bart's is only places like Eden Rock and don't get to know the other side of the island.'

Another said: 'This happened 20 years ago and we have only been here for three or four years.'

Gendarmerie officers declined to comment due to ongoing investigations. They said under French privacy laws, they were also barred from providing any information as to whether Matthews had returned to the island through an official port of entry, which they control.

The island's president, Bruno Magras, also told DailyMail.com that he knew nothing about the alleged sexual assaults. In an email response to questions, he wrote: 'I am sorry, but I am not willing to interfere in a story which I don't know nothing about.'

He earlier told DailyMail.com: 'I know Matthews as an investor on the island, that's all.

When asked if he would cooperate with a full investigation of the allegations, he said: 'We don't have any authority on justice, that's the [French] government's business.'

Magras is president of the local legislature and has ruled over St Bart's for 15 years, first as mayor when the island was part of Guadeloupe, before becoming president a decade ago when it became a 'collectivite'. He has been instrumental in St Barth's rapid development and favorable conditions for the mega-wealthy.

Matthews is being represented by Farrer & Co, the London law firm which has represented members of the Royal Family for 300 years.

A spokesman said: 'David Matthews categorically denies the allegation and unequivocally contests the untrue and scandalous accusation.'

Unlike the muted response from officials, bars, restaurants and stores on the island were alive with whispers of the news, which was being read online and shared on social media.

One bartender told DailyMail.com: 'It's very typical in St Bart's for people to know a lot of secrets for a long time and yet never talk about them. It's that kind of place.'

A lifelong resident said: 'There is no law for these people on the island – they can do whatever they want here.

'People think that St Bart's is only made up of places for the rich and famous. But for those who remain here year-round, it is a really tight-knit community.

'Most people still know each other from their school days and during Hurricane Irma, for example, local people came together, did the clean-up and repairs for each other and the island was quickly back on its feet.

'It's incredibly sad people think St Bart's is only places like Eden Rock and don't get to know the other side of the island.'
