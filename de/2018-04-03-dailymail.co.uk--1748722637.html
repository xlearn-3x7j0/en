Elon Musk begins sleeping at Tesla's Californian factory

Published: 18:39 EDT, 3 April 2018 | Updated: 18:39 EDT, 3 April 2018

Elon Musk has begun sleeping at the Tesla factory in California to take charge of the electric car maker's sluggish production.

Tesla, which was co-founded by the 46-year-old billionaire, has been struggling to hit targets for manufacture of its flagship Model 3 sedan.

The business says it is producing 2,000 of the cars per week, hailing its 'most productive quarter in history'.

But it remains short of the 2,500 per week Musk vowed to be making by the end of March.

However, he still claims the firm is on track to hit 5,000 per week by the middle of the year.

Because of this, the firm said it was not likely to need to raise debt or equity to prop up its finances this year – a claim regarded with some scepticism by analysts.

Production of the Model 3, Tesla's first mass market electric car, is crucial to the company becoming profitable. 

But after a torrid week when the business's stock was battered and its credit rating downgraded, Musk declared on Twitter: 'I'm back to sleeping at factory. Car biz is hell.'
