Hummels clears the air with Bayern target Tuchel

Mats Hummels has buried the hatchet with former manager Thomas Tuchel as Bayern Munich continue their search for Jupp Heynckes' summer replacement.

Current manager Heynckes is set to retire from management in the summer after his fourth spell with the German giants and former Borussia Dortmund manager Tuchel is on their shortlist of potential replacements. 

Tuchel departed from Bayern's Bundesliga rivals Dortmund in May last year after his relationship broke down with the club's board.

Mats Hummels (centre) revealed he met with Thomas Tuchel during the international break

Hummels, who followed in the footsteps of Robert Lewandowski and Mario Gotze by making his switch to Bayern in 2016, endured a tough relationship with his former manager which could have played a part in his eventual departure.

However, speaking to SportBild, Hummels revealed he has since attempted to build bridges with Tuchel while on international duty with Germany.

'We met during the international break in Dortmund and talked,' Hummels said. 'I can say that it was a good conversation.'

Hummels, who was part of the Bayern side that battered Dortmund 6-0 in last weekend's derby, could be hoping to butter his former manager up ahead of a potential swoop for him in the summer. 

Bayern Munich are searching for Jupp Heynckes' replacement as he prepares to retire

Current manager Heynckes has even given his seal of approval for his potential successor. 

'I think that Thomas Tuchel has the quality to train a Bayern,' Heynckes also told SportBild in March. 'He has continuously made his way up the track, in Mainz from the youth teams. Borussia Dortmund then played under Tuchel great football, with a good system.

'Tuchel became runner-up, cup winner and played attractive football; I enjoyed watching his BVB. That's why I appreciate him and consider him a very good coach.' 

Tuchel is also wanted in the Premier League with Arsenal interested should they part ways with Arsene Wenger at the end of the season.
