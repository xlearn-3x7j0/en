Man United stars arrive for training ahead of Swansea clash

Manchester United's stars arrived at the AON Training Complex on Friday ahead of their weekend clash against Swansea.

Jose Mourinho, who is normally chauffeur driven, got a lift with assistant coach Rui Faria on Friday morning.

Luke Shaw and Paul Pogba, who have both faced the Portuguese's wrath of late, were pictured entering the training ground sporting glum faces.

Jesse Lingard, Marcus Rashford, David De Gea, Romelu Lukaku and Ashley Young were also snapped driving into Carrington.

The Red Devils host the Welsh side at Old Trafford on Saturday for a rare 3pm kick off.

With no major injuries from the international break, United will be looking to further cement their place in the Premier League top four against the Swans.

However, winning will be no formality with Swansea showing good form since Carlos Carvalhal took charge.

The countrymen are good friends and studied for their UEFA Pro Licence together, while Mourinho invited Carvalhal to watch training sessions when he was in charge at Chelsea and Real Madrid.

But Swansea's Premier League visit to Manchester United on Saturday will be the first time that Carvalhal and Mourinho have been in opposite dug-outs.

"He is an inspiration, our king of football," Swansea manager Carvalhal said.

"He opened the doors to the new generation of managers to go abroad, he opened the ideas of the world on Portuguese coaches.

"In the 1960s and 1970s we had a lot of emigration in different worlds, but sometimes one guy can accelerate that idea.

"Mourinho did that because he was public in football, he showed what the modern Portugal was about.

"He is smart, talks many languages, works in many different countries and I am proud to share a nationality with him.

"You love him or hate him because of his personality and the controversy. I understand that, but I like him a lot."
