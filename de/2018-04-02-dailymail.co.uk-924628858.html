Barcelona hopeful Messi can overcome muscular issues to face Roma

  • Barcelona face Roma in the first leg of their Champions League quarter-final
  • Ernesto Valverde's side host the Serie A side at the Nou Camp on Wednesday
  • Doubts have materialised around Lionel Messi's fitness for the encounter
  • Valverde has admitted that Messi was still in pain when featured against Sevilla 

Published: 08:50 EDT, 2 April 2018 | Updated: 09:41 EDT, 2 April 2018

Barcelona are hoping Lionel Messi can overcome his muscular pains to face Roma in their upcoming Champions League quarter-final.

Having successfully seen off Chelsea - 4-1 on aggregate - in the last-16 last month, Ernesto Valverde's men host Serie A's Roma at the Nou Camp in the first leg on Wednesday night.

The La Liga leaders are vying for their first European crown since 2015 after watching bitter Rivals Real Madrid win the past two competitions.

Barcelona are waiting to discover Lionel Messi will be fit to face Roma on Wednesday night

Messi trained with Barca on Monday and has played an integral part as always in Barca's progress this season - his stoppage-time equaliser in Saturday's 2-2 draw at Sevilla testament to that - but question marks have arisen around the Argentina forward's fitness.

Prior to the Sevilla draw, Messi had sat out Argentina's international friendlies with Italy and Spain and head coach Valverde has admitted Barca's star man is still in some pain.

It would take a significant amount of pain to force Messi completely out Wednesday's tie and he is expected to at least feature on the Barcelona bench.

The Barcelona squad trained on Monday ahead the Champions League quarter-final clash

Sergio Busquets was also present at training after overcoming a minor foot injury

Elsewhere, Sergio Busquets was a welcome sight on the training ground as he trained with the first team after recovering from a foot injury that saw him sit out Barcelona's last two matches.

Roma meanwhile, are awaiting a prognosis on Radja Nainggolan's thigh injury. The Belgium international, who has started all of Roma's Champions League matches, limped off 16 minutes into Saturday's 1-1 draw at Bologna.

Roma coach Eusebio Di Francesco is doubtful Nainggolan will recover in time

'He is the `Ninja,'' Di Francesco said. 'He told me to wait and tried to run it off, but if he had to give up and leave the pitch that means there's really something wrong and the problem is serious.'  

As we approach the business end of the Europe's most elite competition, Champions League progress is just one of Valverde and his Barca players' priorities.

The La Liga leaders will have one eye on their busy schedule that sees them face three matches within the space of six days as they look to tie up the league title in the coming weeks.
