Jersey's Commonwealth Games shock beating Australia in lawn bowls

  • Jersey beat Australia 21-18 in round one of the men's triples lawn bowls event
  • The two teams find themselves in a group including Botswana, Brunei and Fiji 
  • Two wins on Friday morning would see Jersey consolidate top spot in section B 

Published: 06:41 EDT, 5 April 2018 | Updated: 06:41 EDT, 5 April 2018

Jersey pulled off one of the biggest shocks so far in the Commonwealth Games as they beat a highly-fancied Australia side 21-18 in the men's triples lawn bowls.

Australia were expected to comfortably navigate a group which features Botswana, Brunei, Fiji and Jersey.

But successive wins over Australia and Botswana on Thursday has placed Jersey in firm control as they eye unexpected success in the event. 

Jersey pulled off a shock beating Australia in the lawn bowls at the Commonwealth Games

The men's triples side beat a heavily fancied Australia team 21-18 early on Thursday morning

Malcolm de Sousa, Derek Boswell and Ross Davis form the men's triples team for a country that has a population of just over 100,000.

With Australian sport looking for any escape from the recent ball-tampering cricket scandal, the opening defeat on the Gold Coast will come as a real disappointment. 

Australia's team of Barrie Lester, Nathan Rice and Aron Sherriff will look to respond on Friday as they face two pivotal matches against Botswana and Fiji before a concluding match against Brunei on Saturday.

The men's triples lawn bowls features four sections whereby the top two sides progress to the quarter-finals which take place on April 7.
