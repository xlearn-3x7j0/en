Joshua wants to fight Wilder in UK - stats show Brit is bigger draw

After cruising past Joseph Parker to win his fourth world title, only the WBC strap eludes the all-conquering Anthony Joshua.

Deontay Wilder lies in wait and a heavyweight unification showdown is the mega-fight every boxing fan wants to see. 

Joshua is adamant the bout should be held on these shores as he is the bigger draw. Sportsmail have compared Joshua and Wilder's last three fight to see if the facts back up AJ's verdict.

Anthony Joshua has fought three times in front of enormous crowds in the last year

JOSHUA VS PARKER 

Venue: Principality Stadium

Location: Cardiff 

Attendance: 78,000 

WILDER VS ORTIZ 

Venue: Barclays Center

Location: Brooklyn, New York

Attendance: 19,000 

A packed out Principality Stadium roared Joshua on against Parker over 12 rounds. 

Perhaps the fight failed to deliver on the hype but the 78,000 stuffed inside Cardiff's brilliant venue witnessed a boxing masterclass as AJ used his jab to comfortably out-point his challenger. 

It was AJ's second bout at the Principality and having travelled around the UK since turning professional, the former Olympian has developed a loyal fanbase unrestricted by geography. 

The British fighter overcame Joseph Parker in front of 78,000 at Principality Stadium

Principality Stadium is ideally suited to big nights of boxing. The steep sided stands allow for a great viewing position, even up in the Gods. 

Wilder's epic brawl with previously undefeated Cuban Luis Ortiz was his first sell-out at Barclays Centre. The Brooklyn venue usually houses the Nets and Islanders but has become a favoured fight spot. 

Even with increased appetite to watch Wilder, now 40-0, the attendance pales in comparison with Joshua's draw.  

Deontay Wilder packed out the Barclays Center but the capacity was only 19,000

JOSHUA VS TAKAM 

Venue: Principality Stadium

Location: Cardiff

Attendance: 75,000 

WILDER VS STIVERNE

Venue: Barclays Center 

Location: Brooklyn, New York

Attendance: 10,924

Joshua's promoter Eddie Hearn mocked Wilder for 'fighting in front of 6,000 or 7,000 fans' in the wake of Joshua's triumph on Saturday night. 

He went on to add: 'Have you seen where he boxes? In some great venues, but with no-one there. Look at what Joshua is doing.'

Hearn was slightly off with the numbers but his point remains a valid one. On his first foray across the Welsh border Joshua put 75,000 bums on seats, the contrast is stark with Wilder's rematch against Bermane Stiverne. 

Wilder is the WBC champion and a unification fight against Joshua seems inevitable

Stiverne failed to land a single punch despite earning £360,000 for his work and Wilder obliterated him in the first round. It's possible that fans saw the chasm in class between them which would explain why 8,000 tickets at the Barclay's Center remained unsold.  

Joshua's clash with teak-tough Carlos Takam was televised on Showtime's boxing channel in the US but only managed to peak at 361,000 viewers, underlining the fact that he remains a relative unknown across the pond. 

JOSHUA VS KLITSHKO

Venue: Wembley Stadium

Location: London

Attendance: 90,000 

WILDER VS WASHINGTON 

Venue: Legacy Arena

Location: Birmingham, Alabama

Attendance: 12,346 

Joshua's coming-of-age fight was an unforgettable scrap with Wladimir Klitshko. Both men picked themselves up from the canvas and the drama played out in front of an incredible 90,000 spectators. 

The snowball of Joshua's popularity grew even larger as a result of the heroic performance and there's no doubt another fight at Wembley would sell out, and sell out fast. 

Joshua want to take on Wilder at the home of English football, declaring: ''All these years, British fighters have to go over to America... we're staying over here... we can do it at Wembley!'

Having fought three massive stadium fights in a year to underline his superstar status, the ball is very much in his court. 

AJ defeated Wladimir Klitschko in front of a staggering 90,000 strong crowd at Wembley

Barry Hearn believes the 28-year-old should resist the temptation to settle for home comforts and try to crack America, a market he's yet to fully tap. 

The veteran promoter said: 'I still think in the long term, if Anthony sits down and thinks it through, he may come to the decision that to be a truly global sports star he needs to go to America — not just for the money — but, in terms of the brand value of AJ, fighting in America is going to be on his horizon.' 

Wilder drew a crowd of 12,346 three fights ago when Joshua was putting on a behemoth of a show. 

The WBC champion overcame Gerald Washington in his home state of Alabama but he's hit the big time now and a unification showdown with Joshua would no doubt be in a fighting Mecca of Las Vegas or New York if he were to convince the Brit to cross the Atlantic. 

In three fights Joshua has pulled crowds of 243,000 and Wilder just 42,270. The Brit holds the aces when negotiations begin but the temptation of untold riches and celebrity in the US may yet lure him to Wilder's backyard. 

Joshua's fights have become incredible shows and tickets sell out rapidly
