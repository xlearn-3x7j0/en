Burton Albion 1-1 Middlesbrough: Assombalonga snatches late equaliser

A 90th minute equaliser by Britt Assombalonga denied Burton Albion a first home win since September and salvaged a point for Middlesbrough in their faltering promotion push.

Burton took an early lead and looked to be heading for a rare victory at the Pirelli Stadium as Tony Pulis’s side laboured to create, but Assambalonga struck with a header in the closing stages.

The result actually strengthens Boro’s position in the top six momentarily after Bristol City’s defeat but more performances like that will see the side remain in the Championship for another campaign. 

Britt Assombalonga snatched a dramatic late equaliser for Middlesbrough against Burton

 The substitute was on hand to nod home from close range to break the hearts of Burton

Marvin Sordell's strike Middlesbrough looked as though it was enough to earn the win

Sordell opened the scoring after just six minutes, slotting home comfortably from close range

But they just couldn't hold on and the Boro man was on hand to deny Burton a precious win

Victory could have proved transformative for Burton in moving off the bottom but Nigel Clough’s side had to settle for closing the gap to safety to five points with only six games left.

The Pirelli Stadium has been far from a happy home for Burton this season but Clough got an early impression this afternoon might just be a little different.

The game was only six minutes old when Lloyd Dyer escaped Martin Cranie to break down the left and slide a low cross that somehow found Sordell in the middle unmarked. Sordell swept the ball in with minimum fuss and Boro were unexpectedly chasing the match.

Sordell’s goal was Burton’s first in this ground since January, an extraordinary run stretching back 405 minutes.

From there, the pattern set in of Burton sitting back in a compact shape and challenging Boro to find a way through. The visitor’s ambition for doing that largely depended on getting the ball to Adama Traore and seeing what the whippet-speed winger could do.

In the 28th minute Troare exploded into a burst on the break that saw him slalom two Burton defenders before being fouled, possession still flowing to Stewart Downing for a shooting chance he wasted by dragging wide.

As Boro toiled, Pulis took drastic action, replacing Jonny Howson with Britt Assombalonga eight minutes before the interval with no obvious injury to the midfielder who bore a face of thunder as he walked to the dugout.

An extra striker certainly gave Burton more to think about defensively and in the 50th minute Stephen Bywater was perhaps fortunate to receive just a yellow card for handling outside the area with Traore in pursuit.

Assaombalonga wasted a good chance on the break before Patrick Bamford finally had Boro’s first shot on target in the 56th minute. But it was straight at Bywater.

Liam Boyce headed Dyer's corner wide and Mo Besic then shot too closely to Bywater. It seemed Boro would slip to defeat when Assombalonga wasted a golden opportunity by blazing over when set clear by Traore but the striker made amends with seconds left.

Traore played in Jack Harrison and though his lofted shot hit the bar the ball fell invitingly for Assombalonga to head in.
