Ray Wilkins' final TV appearance

Ray Wilkins appeared on TV just one week before his death, discussing his beloved Chelsea on Sky Sports.

It was confirmed the ex-England captain, 61, passed away on Wednesday after suffering a heart attack and a fall at his home a few days prior.

The former Blues assistant manager had been in a medically induced coma but son Ross, 36, told the Mirror Wednesday afternoon: "Dad has unfortunately passed away."

Wilkins had appeared on TV just last Tuesday, fulfilling his punditry duties for Sky Sports on their news channel's 'Daily' show.

The ex-Man United midfielder has been discussing Chelsea's chances ahead of last Sunday's clash against Spurs.

"It's a must-win against Tottenham on Sunday, absolute must-win. Tottenham are without Harry Kane, which gives Chelsea the edge," said Wilkins.

"Tottenham are in good form; I think Chelsea playing against Tottenham at Stamford Bridge will have the upper hand."

"We have got to be on the front foot. We have to make sure Tottenham suffer. Therefore, I would play Kante and Fabregas in midfield," he said.

Wilkins was also asked if Chelsea still had a chance of finishing inside the top four if they drew against Spurs, he replied: "No, I don't think there is. I think the gap's too big. The only salvation is the semi-final of the FA Cup, of course."

Wilkins leaves behind wife Jackie and two children, Ross and his sister Jade.

The family are understood to be thankful for all the messages of support the former Chelsea and Manchester United midfielder had received from around the world.

In a statement issued via St George's Hospital, Ray's family said: "It is with great sadness we announce that Raymond Colin Wilkins passed away this morning.

"We would like to thank St George’s staff for the amazing work they have done to care for our beloved Ray.

"We would also like to say thank you for the many goodwill messages we have received from Ray’s friends, colleagues, and members of the public.

"Ray leaves behind his loving wife, Jackie, daughter Jade, son Ross, and his beautiful grandchildren, Oliver, Frankie, Ava, Freddie, Jake and Archie.

"We are asking for privacy at this very difficult time."
