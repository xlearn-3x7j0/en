Jason Day finds beer cup instead of fairway as shot lands in fan drink

  • Jason Day hit a wild shot on the first hole which landed in a fan's beverage cup
  • The ex-world No 1 took it well as the fan finished his drink to give the ball back
  • Day bogeyed the first hole at the Masters before finding par on the next two

Published: 12:13 EDT, 5 April 2018 | Updated: 12:42 EDT, 5 April 2018

One fan had a moment to remember at the Masters in Augusta as a wild shot from Jason Day in round one saw the ball land directly into the spectator's beer cup. 

In a group with Bubba Watson and Henrik Stenson, former world No 1 Day bogeyed the opening hole after a unique beverage interruption.

Despite the opening disappointment, both day and the fan appeared in high spirits at the freak landing of the ball and the spectator finished his drink before handing the drenched ball back to the golfer.

Jason Day chats to a fan after his shot sees the ball land in the spectator's green beer cup

The former world No 1 was in good spirits as the fan finished his beverage in front of Day

Day bogeyed the first hole after his shot landed in the cup before going on to par the next two

The patron showed no willingness to simply throw his drink away and will have won the hearts of many fellow spectators as he downed his drink in front of a jovial Day. 

The light-hearted moment may not have been factored in to the Australian's preparation but after the first hole bogey, Day hit two pars to steady himself on the course.

Australian golfer Day remains one of the favourites to end the event wearing the iconic green jacket but he will need to find more fairways than beer cups to make that a reality. 

With an $11million (£7.9m) prize purse awaiting the winner, the players' know both a green jacket and huge prize money will turnaround fortunes this year. 

The final competitor's will take to the course at Augusta National on Sunday, April 8.
