St Mirren boss new front runner for Charlton job but Bowyer will remain for now

St Mirren boss Jack Ross has emerged as a candidate to become the next Charlton boss.

Lee Bowyer has been given the job until the end of the season but Charlton are still looking at making a permanent appointment to replace Karl Robinson.

Crawley boss Harry Kewell is also on Charlton’s wish list as an Australian consortium looks to take charge at the Valley.

But Ross is a genuine contender along with Kewell and Bowyer while he has also caught the eye of Ipswich as they look to replace Mick McCarthy at the end of the season.

Ross has become one of the brightest young British managers after the job he has done at St Mirren.

Barnsley offered Ross the job earlier this season but he opted to stay at St Mirren but Championship clubs believe he could be ready to move this summer.

Ross took over St Mirren when they were bottom of the Scottish Championship in January 2017 but he saved them from relegation before guiding them to a 12-point lead at the top of the table this season.
