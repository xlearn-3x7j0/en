Jurgen Klopp fires warning to Liverpool stars despite big win over Man City

Jurgen Klopp vowed there will be no party from Liverpool, even though they’ve “beaten the best side in the world”.

First-half goals from Mo Salah, Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain and Sadio Mane lit up one of Anfield’s greatest European nights.

But the Reds manager is adamant his team will still “have to work like hell” if they are to knock out the Premier League champions-elect.

Klopp said: “It was really, really good. We beat the best team in the world. But I don’t think about things like that.

“I have never been part of the best team in the world but I always knew it is possible to beat them. It is rare because they are so good.

“I could be very positive and answer the way you want me to, but that would only make Pep even more angry. He would only have to put the newspapers in the dressing room and say ‘look what Klopp said’.

“We have to work like hell. We are not in the next round. Why should I celebrate it? There is no party yet.”

Klopp even had an answer for those who pointed out that one Liverpool goal at City next week, would leave Guardiola’s side requiring five to go through to the semi finals. “I seem to remember we have conceded there five once already.

“I don’t think it makes more likely we are through. We have to work. You have to celebrate the party when the party starts, not four weeks before.”

Guardiola insisted City are not out of the Champions League – despite their Anfield mauling.

He said: “The result is tough, but I don’t have the feeling it’s over. I believe a lot in this team. They showed me many good things in the season.

“We have 90 minutes. We are going to try. It is so complicated because we are going to play a top, top team.”

Guardiola admitted the electric atmosphere generated by Liverpool’s fans inside Anfield was a big factor.

“Each club has their own atmosphere. We know how special Anfield is. I said to our players we must be ourselves.

“It was tough, but the last minutes of the first-half and especially the second half was good.”
