Charlie Adam: Salah wouldn't get a game at Real Madrid or Barcelona

  • Mohamed Salah has scored 37 goals in 42 games in first season at Liverpool
  • Charlie Adam claims Salah wouldn't get a game for Real Madrid or Barcelona 
  • Adam believes Liverpool can win the Premier League in the near future 

Published: 08:09 EDT, 2 April 2018 | Updated: 08:10 EDT, 2 April 2018

Former Liverpool midfielder Charlie Adam has claimed that Mohamed Salah wouldn't get a game at Real Madrid or Barcelona despite his stunning performances this season.

After joining from Roma last summer, Salah has enjoyed a sensational first campaign at Liverpool which has encompassed him scoring 37 goals, 29 of which have come in the Premier League.

However, Stoke midfielder Adam indicated that there is little chance of Salah leaving Anfield any time soon as he suggested the Egyptian wouldn't be able to command a regular starting berth for either Real or Barcelona.

Mohamed Salah has scored 37 goals in all competitions during his first season at Liverpool

Charlie Adam, now of Stoke, was a Liverpool player between 2011 and 2012

Adam told talkSPORT: 'Where's he going to go? It's difficult for him, where does he go?

'Will he get a game at Real Madrid? Will he get a game at Barcelona? 

'I don't think he would.

'I think he will stay there.'

Liverpool are currently third in the Premier League, some 18 points adrift of leaders Manchester City.

However, Adam believes that Jurgen Klopp's side are capable of winning the title in the near future if they make sufficient additions to their squad.

Salah has impressed for Liverpool but Adam feels he couldn't play for Real Madrid or Barcelona

'They are capable of winning the league, I'm firmly believe that they are,' Adam added.

'They're one or two short. They've got Naby Keita coming in over the summer, Emre Can looks like he will go.

'But if they can get another one or two in I think they can challenge next summer.'

Liverpool still have an opportunity for glory this season in the Champions League and they will turn to Salah for inspiration when they host Manchester City on Wednesday night in the eagerly-anticipated first leg of their quarter-final tie.

Salah found the net and provided an assist for Sadio Mane when Liverpool beat City 4-3 in the Premier League in January to end their opponents' unbeaten run.
