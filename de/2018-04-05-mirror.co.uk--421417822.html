All you need to know about Grand National Ladies Day - including dress code

Hats the way to do it when the Grand National festival's Ladies Day comes around.

Arguably, the horses play second fiddle to the frocks on the day when women are encouraged - no, urged - to dress to impress.

Expect dazzling dresses, sky-high stilettos, seriously thick Scouse brows and fascinators aplenty on Ladies Day as Aintree goes glam.

Tens of thousands of punters will cram into Liverpool's Aintree Racecourse for the event being billed as 'Fabulous Friday'.

But when exactly is Ladies Day, how can you get tickets and what should you wear? Here's everything you need to know.

When is Ladies Day?

It might not be a great omen, but this year Ladies Day falls on April Friday 13.

How to get tickets

If you want a ticket, you'd better be quick - many sections have sold out.

You can still buy tickets for Festival Zone - Tattersalls at £43.

Go to the official Randox Health Grand National wesbite .

What is the dress code?

There's no official dress code, but punters are encouraged to dress to impress.

Sports gear and fancy dress costumes are not permitted, but beyond that, it's fair game.

Colourful, sleek and smart dresses are usually the order of the day.

Hats are optional, but frequently worn.

A 'Style Code' inspired by the Coco Chanel quote, “Dress shabbily and they remember the dress; dress impeccably and they remember the woman” aims to inspire.

Who's on the race card?

The gates for Ladies Day open at about 10.30am.

The first race is usually scheduled for 1.40pm and the last one around at 5.15pm.

The JLT Melling Steeple Chase and the Randox Health Topham Steeple Chase will be among the races, but the official card is yet to be announced.
