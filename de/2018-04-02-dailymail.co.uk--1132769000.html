Sunderland 1-3 Sheffield Wednesday: More misery heaped on Black Cats

For three days those of a Sunderland persuasion believed that the Great Escape was on - again - a hope triggered by Friday night’s shock 4-1 win at Derby County.

Not now, not after this demoralising home defeat, a 12th of the season in front of their own fans.

The escape is on alright, they’ll be exiting the Championship in the coming weeks, becoming only the fifth team to suffer successive relegations from the top-flight to the third-tier.

Atdhe Nuhiu celebrates after rounding off a comfortable afternoon for Sheffield Wednesday

A sodden Chris Coleman celebrated in the rain at Derby but he looked like he was drowning beneath a sea of negativity and weariness on full-time here.

The Black Cats are five points from safety with six to play but they have won just one in 12 and the chances of them winning half of their remaining matches - as will probably be needed - is virtually nil.

Not that they played particularly badly here. Sunderland right-back Donald Love sized a strike from 25 yards early on and was unlucky to see his shot shave the crossbar.

They did find the target twice before half-time - both from free-kicks - but Owls goalkeeper Joe Wildsmith was equal to the efforts from Bryan Oviedo and Aiden McGeady.

It was not until early in the second half that the visitors registered an attempt on goal when Barry Bannan took aim from distance, drawing well wide.

So when the midfielder gathered possession in a similar position moments later he instead elected to cross. 

It was a wise ploy. His delivery was nodded down by Atdhe Nuhiu and Lucas Joao volleyed in the opener.

The lead lasted just 90 seconds and, from a Lynden Gooch cross, George Honeyman headed home at the far post.

But Wednesday had hit upon a winning formula, not that crossing the ball is too complicated a tactic.

On 70 minutes they were back in front when Joey Pelupessy swept over a free-kick and Tom Lees was afforded the freedom of the six-yard area to volley into the gaping net.

Five minutes later and another cross brought another goal, Adam Reach delivering from the left and Atdhe Nuhiu turning in after Lee Camp had saved from Joao’s flick.

A Sunderland penalty appeal had split the visiting goals, Gooch going down under the sliding challenge of Bannan. Replays, however, showed that the American had fallen too easily.

And that is what Sunderland have been doing all season, tumbling towards League One with little resistance.
