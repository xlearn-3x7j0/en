Ronnie O'Sullivan completes incredible snooker record

Ronnie O’Sullivan suffered a shock China Open exit – despite a brilliant 14th career 147 break.

The Rocket was hammered 6-2 by bogey opponent and world No73 Elliot Slessor at the National Sports Centre in Beijing.

The 23-year-old from Gateshead has now beaten five-time world champion and player of the season O’Sullivan in both of their career meetings.

But even in defeat O’Sullivan took centre stage with a magnificent break taking only six minutes and 54 seconds that earned him a £42,000 bonus unless equalled this week.

The 42-year-old already held the record for the number of competitive 147s before his clash with Elliot Slessor in Beijing.

However that could not take the shine off a sensational victory for Slessor, who also beat O’Sullivan 4-1 at this season’s Northern Ireland Open.

Geordie Slessor is on his second crack at the tour, almost quit the game last summer and is still battling to keep his pro status this season.

O’Sullivan said: “I thought if I am going to go out I might as well try and do something good. I didn’t think I was going to win it playing like that, so the 147 is second prize.”
