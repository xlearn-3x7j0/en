Lampard loved what Alexander Arnold did in Liverpool's win over Man City

Frank Lampard ran out of superlatives for Trent Alexander Arnold following Liverpool's 3-0 victory over Manchester City.

The Reds well and truly demolished the Citizens in the first leg of their Champions League quarter-final clash on Wednesday night, as Mohamed Salah and co. ran riot.

Liverpool are known for blowing teams away, and have battered more than one side this season, as they close in on a top-four finish in the Premier League.

But there have been question marks over how good they are defensively; questions that Alexander Arnold went some way to answering by himself in front of a packed Anfield crowd.

Ahead of the game many feared the 19-year-old would be outclassed by the Premier League's in-form winger, Leroy Sane.

And Pep Guardiola set up his team to attack down the left-flank, in order to put the youngster under pressure and carve out opportunies.

But Alexander Arnold put on one hell of a show and marked Sane out of the game, showing quality, composure, and experience beyond his years.

After the match, Chelsea legend Lampard told BT Sport: "How great to see youth take on a challenge like this. The mentality of the boy to do that, with a little bit of pressure on him because of recent performances."

But it wasn't just the defender's performance that impressed him. Lampard was equally delighted by the way the teenager riled up his team-mates and the Anfield crowd in the closing stages of the conest.

Lampard added: "Not just all that great positional play, getting close to Sane, running with him, being in that position, but that [moment] where he got his fists and he was pumped.

"A 19-year-old boy and all of a sudden he's becoming a man because when you perform like that on an occasion as big as this, it makes you feel like that [puffs chest out]."

Rio Ferdinand was in complete agreement with Lampard, and added: "Could he change his fortunes after making mistakes in the last couple of games? He went away, done his homework, and the kid came good today. He was fantastic.

"Everything about him today… he was tenacious, he was disciplined, he concentrated and he done everything right.

"Bear in mind that he was up against Sane - probably the most in-form winger in the league at the moment - and he had him in his pocket. He was fantastic in everything he done."
