Ricciardo believes he can be champion... but fuels Red Bull exit talk

Daniel Ricciardo still fully believes he can become Formula One world champion - but whether he can achieve the dream at Red Bull remains to be seen. 

The 28-year-old Australian has been a regular at the front of the grid for the last five years, with a best result in the drivers' championship of third in 2014 and 2016.

Despite the dominance of Mercedes and Lewis Hamilton last season, and the huge improvements already evident in Ferrari this campaign, Ricciardo still fancies his chances of winning the highest honour.

Speaking on the In The Pink podcast with Natalie Pinkham, Ricciardo said he feels he has the ability to become world champion, despite being a nearly man throughout his career so far.

He said: 'I do. I don't like the word expectations but I expect it from myself - what l believe l can do and from what I've learnt over the years, I expect l can deliver that.'

However Ricciardo was less sure whether he can win the title with Red Bull, who were notably slower than Mercedes and Ferrari in the opening race weekend of 2018 in Australia.

Ricciardo's contract expires after this season, and though Red Bull are keen to keep hold of both him and team mate Max Verstappen, Ferrari and Mercedes are also interested.

Ricciardo's Red Bull deal expires after this season and his future remains uncertain

He said: 'In this sport you need the tools as well. I hope to get to those tools. l don't want to say l expect to receive those tools but if l get those tools l would expect it from myself to get the job done.'

When asked directly about his plans for 2019 and beyond, Ricciardo was evasive, and said: 'Next year will be a big thing for that. I have another year with the Red Bull contract so we will see where l end up.' 

Ricciardo finished fourth in his home race at Melbourne, with Sebastian Vettel of Ferrari winning and Hamilton in second.

The F1 season continues this weekend in Bahrain, however Ricciardo is being his usual laid back self in the preparations.

He posted a picture to Instgram of him in a digger, with the caption: 'I'll be driving something a little quicker this weekend.'
