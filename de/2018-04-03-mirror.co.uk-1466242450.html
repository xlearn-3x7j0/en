Incredible number of WrestleMania weekend events revealed

WrestleMania is the flagship event in the WWE calendar - attracting tens of thousands of fans from around the globe.

This year will be no different, when WrestleMania 34 comes to the Mercedes-Benz SuperDome in New Orleans, Louisiana on April 8.

But it has also become a vital attraction for independent promotions and 'WrestleMania Weekend' has developed into a veritable feast of wrestling.

The trend first started in 2006, when the Ring of Honor promotion ran two events in Chicago on March 31 and April 1, ahead of WrestleMania 22, on April 2 that year.

Ring of Honor drew record-setting crowds for its two shows and that, in turn, encouraged other promotions to schedule events in subsequent years to coincide with WrestleMania.

This year WWE's annual showpiece returns to the location of WrestleMania 30 in 2014, when Daniel Bryan won the WWE World Heavyweight Championship.

And no fewer than FIFTY wrestling-related events are scheduled to take place in New Orleans between Thursday, April 4 and Tuesday, April 9.

In addition to WrestleMania 34, WWE will host its annual Axxess fan interaction event, the 2018 Hall of Fame ceremony, NXT TakeOver: New Orleans, Raw and SmackDown LIVE.

Piggybacking on the lure of Mania and the huge numbers of fans it attracts, top independent promotions will visit The Big Easy ahead of WWE’s showcase spectacular.

Ring of Honor (ROH) will run its annual Supercard of Honor show at the Lakefront Arena on April 7, with a mouthwatering main event, which has been months in the making.

The show will be headlined by a huge battle between New Japan Pro Wrestling superstar Kenny Omega and former WWE stalwart Cody (formerly Cody Rhodes).

To demonstrate how important the pull of Mania is to promotions like ROH, it is expected to draw a sell-out crowd of 5,700 - shattering its record of 3,500 from last year’s Supercard of Honor.

Philadelphia, Pennsylvania based EVOLVE Wrestling is an independent group affiliated to the WWE and run by a consultant to the sports entertainment giant, Gabe Sapolsky.

It will run two of the larger non-WWE events with EVOLVE 102 on April 5 featuring a main event of EVOLVE Champion, England's Zack Sabre Jr., versus former UFC competitor Matt Riddle.

The following day, again at the Pontchartrain Center, EVOLVE 103 will feature Riddle taking on Japanese powerhouse Daisuke Sekimoto, in a hugely anticipated clash.

Perhaps the most eclectic card of the weekend is Joey Janela’s Spring Break 2 show.

Janela is a young, cult favourite on the US independent scene and he will headline his own show in a match with Japanese legend, The Great Sasuke.

Sticking with unlikely pairings, the show will also see the highly-touted Austrian brawler WALTER collide with ex-WWE superstar, former Quebecer, Pierre Carl Oullet.

Not wishing to be left out, two of the most popular British promotions will also be holding events in New Orleans.

Revolution Pro Wrestling will hold its second WrestleMania weekend event on the afternoon of April 6, with a show headlined by the group’s British Heavyweight Champion, Zack Sabre Jr., defending his title against New Japan hard-hitter Tomohiro Ishii.

Fellow London-based promotion PROGRESS Wrestling also returns to WrestleMania weekend for a second time with two huge shows at noon on April 6 and 7.

The likes of WWE United Kingdom Champion Pete Dunne, former titleholder Tyler Bate and the highly promising Jinny will be among the stars taking part at the events.

Activities won’t be restricted purely to wrestling shows, however.

An Evening with Paul Heyman (presented by Scotland's Inside The Ropes) will take place on April 5, with Brock Lesnar’s advocate treating attendees with stories from his legendary career.

The following day, commentary great Jim Ross will host the imaginatively titled ‘Slobberknocker Sessions’, featuring a Q&A and meet and greet opportunity with the WWE Hall of Famer.

Perhaps the biggest late-night party of the weekend will take place on April 6, with ex-WWE Champion Kevin Nash’s Shenanigans on Bourbon Street.

Unlimited drinks, live music and a host of wrestling personalities will be on hand to ensure it will be one of the weekend’s livelier occasions.

WrestleCon will be the largest fan convention outside of the WWE’s Axxess events and will bring together dozens of famous names from wrestling’s past and present.

Ric Flair, Sting, ‘Stone Cold’ Steve Austin, Shawn Michaels and Bret Hart are just some of the many superstars who will take part in the mammoth meet and greet assembly.

Even a bar crawl – labelled ManiaCrawl – will take place on the day of WrestleMania, to get fans in high spirits ahead of the Showcase of the Immortals.

Fans are spoilt for choice if they wish to binge on live grappling and its culture - the days leading up to WrestleMania have truly become a celebration of wrestling.
