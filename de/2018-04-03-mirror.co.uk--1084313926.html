Revealed: Who knocked John Cena off top spot in the WWE T-shirt sales list

For as long as most modern day wrestling fans can remember, John Cena , a 16-time world champion, has been, as his nickname suggests, the face of the WWE .

Ever since Cena ascended to the top of the card way back in 2005, kids and adults alike have spent fortunes on his merchandise, from t-shirts and hats to wristbands and belts.

The 40-year-old has had some rivals along the way, with the likes of other polarising figures such as CM Punk, the New Day and Daniel Bryan with his 'Yes' merchandise all pushing him close.

But where those have failed, others have now succeeded as Cena finds himself off the top spot in T-shirt sales charts on the official WWE website.

According to Pro Wrestling Magazie , The Champ has seen his place as king of the ring usurped by AJ Styles, The Usos, Elias and Braun Strowman.

It shows a clear shift in who the audience are relating to right now, and if history is anything to go by, Vince McMahon and co will no doubt be listening.

Whilst it could be attributed to Cena's in-ring popularity, there are other factors to take into account, such as the designs of the shirts themselves.

The New Day have a number of fans of their merchandise merely because of the way it looks - it's trendy and cool - whilst the likes of Rusev and AJ Styles have many fans due to their wrestling ability within the squared circle.

He may not be shifting as many shirts, but if we know one thing about John Cena it's to write him off at your peril.
