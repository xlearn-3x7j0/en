Nigel Quashie shares heartwarming Ray Wilkins story in emotional tribute

Former Queens Park Rangers midfielder Nigel Quashie has shared a heartwarming story about Ray Wilkins in tribute to the late England and Chelsea legend.

Wilkins passed away on Wednesday morning, aged 61, after suffering a cardiac arrest and being placed into an induced coma.

The football community, stretching from Glasgow to Milan, has come together to pay tribute to Wilkins. Quashie, who was handed his debut at QPR by 'Butch,' has shared an emotional story about his former manager.

"I will always be proud to say how lucky I am to have been coached, managed and even meet you," the former Portsmouth and Southampton star wrote on his Instagram page.

"If it wasn’t for you I would never have started my career playing football and will never forget going to Manchester United as a YTS trainee to do all the kit and boots in the changing room for the first team squad.

"1hr 30 mins before kick off you name the team and I was in it and that’s how my debut went, I didn’t even get to think about it because all you said was your number 18, here are your boots and shin pads , just go and play, have fun and treat it like your over the park with your mates.

"My mum never had a mobile phone back then because we couldn’t afford anything like that and everything went through her work or home phone.

"I asked you after the game if I could call home to speak to her, you said use my mobile and call this number, it was my mum on the other end of the line and she said she was outside by the away team bus, I went outside to see her and asked how did you get here because I knew she couldn’t afford it, she told me Ray had got the club to pay for her train ticket to come to the game and then told me Ray got the club to give us our first mobile phone in life during the week and he wanted to surprise me knowing I was making my debut.

"I was given a chance by Ray Wilkins one of the legends of the game and to this day you are the first person I mention when it comes to football and that will never change. You will be missed so much not just in the football world but as a real human being with such class. Thanks for everything and my thoughts go to all the family. Sleep well Sir Ray Wilkins."

Another anecdote that summed up Wilkins' good nature and sense of humour has been shared by Jodie Morris from their time together at Chelsea.

Morris wrote on his Instagram: "I’d been out injured for a long time and Razor bursts into the treatment room while mid training session and says ‘come on fella let’s get you involved... You can be ref as I’ve had enough of those Herberts moaning.’

"Typical of ray to know I hated missing training, and being out a while you can feel isolated... So I came out and took his whistle and he took his watch off and put it on my wrist. So my timing was impeccable and I ref’d the game with aplomb but I’d been used to seeing Ray with Rolexs but as I was taking the watch off to give it back I noticed it was was an Omega...

"Me being an uneducated ‘young pup’ (as Ray would say) and wanting to have a bit of banter with him I cheekily said ‘Omega?? Come on Razor I expected better from you’...

"Ray with his face resembling that I'm about to take you to school look says, ‘why don’t you just have a look at the back of the timepiece before handing it here Fella’... So I flipped it over and it had a big AC Milan badge engraved with ‘FRANCO BARESI TESTIMONIAL’ and the date... ’I take it back Razor I take it back’... He just winked at me and walked off chuckling to himself... Loved him!!"
