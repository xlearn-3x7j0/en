Fire breaks out in Russian shopping centre

Some 600 - including many children - were evacuated from the Moscow shopping mall where one man died and four were injured, authorities have said.

Alexey Karpov, 45, was killed in the fire at Perseus Dlya Detei children's shopping centre on Wednesday.

Karpov, who worked at the mall, was rescued from the burning building, but died in the ambulance.

A medical emergency helicopter seen at the site of a fire at the Persei Dlya Detei [Perseus for Children] shopping mall in Moscow

The Persei shopping complex in Moscow's east is being evacuated after a fire broke out

Three men and one woman were injured.   

Twenty people had to be rescued by fire ladders as flames tore though the complex.

The blaze began on the sixth floor in the engine of a lift, according to one report.

Another said the fire started in a yarn storage room on an upper floor. 

'The victim evacuated from the burning facility has died in the ambulance. Efforts to save him have failed,' the source said.

Five firefighters were also injured.

A male eyewitness said: 'I was there with family. The guard began to run around and scream that everyone must go outside.

'There was no alarm, only a guard running in panic. Initially we thought it was a drill.

'We went outside slowly. There was not so much people there.'

He added: 'The escalators were switched off, so we went down to the first floor by stairs. All the doors were open.

'When we were already outside we saw the smoke on the highest floors. In three minutes, the first firefighters arrived.'

Two Ka-32 helicopters were being used in the rescue operation at the mall on Wednesday

A emergency services spokesman confirmed the fire has been put out. 

'The fire was given the 2nd rank of complexity, there are injured, visitors are evacuated by the rescue ladder,' said a source.

Two Ka-32 helicopters were being used in the rescue operation.

Video posted on Twitter showed massive plumes of black smoke rising from the top of the building. 

It comes after a deadly blaze at a mall in the Siberian city of Kemerovo left 64 dead, most of them children. 

Locals said a series of false alarms had been sounded at the mall in recent days following the blaze in Kemerovo.

Another source said that due to training after the tragic blaze in Kemerovo, evacuation was speedy.
