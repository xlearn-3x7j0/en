Man United's Jesse Lingard says current stars can emulate Class of '92

  • David Beckham, Paul Scholes and the Neville brothers came through together 
  • Reserve team boss Nicky Butt was also part of the club's famous Class of '92
  • Winger Jesse Lingard believes United's current crop can emulate those stars
  • He cited young talents Paul Pogba, Marcus Rashford and Scott McTominay

Published: 08:48 EDT, 30 March 2018 | Updated: 09:21 EDT, 30 March 2018

Jesse Lingard has admitted that Manchester United's famous Class of '92 are inspiring the club's current young stars to emulate their wealth of success. 

Under the tutelage of Sir Alex Ferguson, David Beckham, the Neville brothers, Paul Scholes and Nicky Butt all came through the ranks into the first team.

The promotion of homegrown youth into the first team was heralded by supporters as the Reds motored to a period of sustained success both domestically and in Europe with the treble in 1999.  

Jesse Lingard wants to emulate Manchester United's Class of '92 with his young team-mates

Lingard believes he, Paul Pogba, Marcus Rashford and Scott McTominay can take inspiration

McTominay and Rashford are two of United's youngest first team players in the current squad

'It's all about making new history but you see the team with Paul Scholes, David Beckham, Gary Neville etc and you see what they achieved and it is only right that the likes of me, Marcus and Paul try and emulate that and win trophies,' said Lingard in an interview with BBC's Football Focus. 

'They came through the ranks together, but my youth team went their separate ways so to have another home lad like Marcus in the team and with Paul having been here since 16, we just want to try and emulate what they did.' 

The young charges, including the more recent rise of McTominay, have been pivotal for United boss Jose Mourinho since he took charge and Lingard's consistently high level of performances this season have seen him strengthen his case for a World Cup place with England.  

Lingard has enjoyed his best form for club and country this season and eyes a World Cup place

With the international break concluding for England with a 1-1 draw against Italy at Wembley, United's players returned to Carrington to prepare for Saturday's visit of Swansea. 

United put four past a hapless Swans earlier this season but the South Wales outfit have become a far tougher proposition under Carlos Carvalhal.

Three points for Lingard and his team-mates at Old Trafford will further strengthen their case for a Champions League place and, should Liverpool win earlier in the day, will restore the Reds to second place.
