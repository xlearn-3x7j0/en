Man Utd misfit Shaw in line to start against Swansea, reveals Mourinho

  • Manchester United host Swansea in the Premier League on Saturday afternoon
  • Luke Shaw could make just his 13th start in all competitions for United this term
  • Ashley Young suffered a knee injury during England's 1-1 draw with Italy 

Published: 08:40 EDT, 30 March 2018 | Updated: 08:42 EDT, 30 March 2018

Luke Shaw could keep his place in Manchester United's starting line-up against Watford at Old Trafford if Ashley Young is ruled out through injury.

Jose Mourinho criticised Shaw after replacing him against Brighton at half-time a fortnight ago, adding to concerns in the player's camp that he is being treated unfairly by the United boss.

Young has established himself as United's first-choice left back but picked up a knee injury in England's draw with Italy in midweek. It means that Shaw, 22, could make only his 13th start of the season in all competitions this season against Swansea on Saturday.

Manchester United defender Luke Shaw could start against Swansea on Saturday afternoon

'If he's available Luke Shaw is always an option,' was Mourinho's rather lukewarm response to being asked about Shaw's availability on Friday. 'Ashley Young was available to play against Brighton so I started with Luke Shaw, so that's not a problem.

'Ashley Young had a problem in the last part of the game against Italy but we think there is a chance for him to recover.'

Phil Jones is fit again and Daley Blind, another option at left back, is training again after completing his recuperation from an ankle injury in Holland. However, reserve goalkeeper Sergio Romero was hurt on international duty for Argentina.

'Romero is injured, and I think he will be out for a few weeks,' added Mourinho. 'I think in the end he was lucky because it was a strong contact and will take a little bit of time to recover.

'Daley Blind came back from his treatment in Holland and started training with us in the last few days. We recovered all the other problems we had, including Phil Jones. Marcos Rojo of course played with Argentina so the picture improved a lot for us in that aspect.'
