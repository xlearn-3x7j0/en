Spieth skips ball across pond at Augusta’s hole 16 in Masters practice

  • Jordan Spieth produced an impressive effort as he warmed-up on Monday
  • World No 4's shot skipped off the water five times before it reached the green
  • The 2015 Masters champion finished tied for third at Houston Open on Sunday 

Published: 05:46 EDT, 3 April 2018 | Updated: 05:49 EDT, 3 April 2018

Jordan Spieth almost pulled off a remarkable shot by skipping the ball across the pond at Augusta's hole 16 in Masters practice.

Even still it was an impressive effort from the world No 4 as he warmed-up for the Masters Tournament on Monday. 

From the edge of the pond, Spieth's shot skipped off the water five times before it reached the green to make it an exceptional approach shot.

The crowd that turned out to watch players polish their game ahead of the first major the year certainly seemed impressed as the ball found the green.

The 2015 Masters champion finished tied for third at the Houston Open on Sunday and is currently one of the favourites to land the Masters title again.

Vijay Singh did manage to pull off the amazing shot on the 16th hole during Augusta practice back in 2009.

It was not only Spieth's audacious effort that attracted attention on Monday as thousands of fans flocked to watch Tiger Woods practice ahead of the Masters, which starts on Thursday. 

He enjoyed his long-awaited return to Augusta and did it in style as he eagled the second hole.

The 42-year-old was all smiles after he chipped one onto the green that travelled a long time before dropping into the cup to send the supporters watching into raptures.
