Ronaldo reveals if overhead kick against Juventus is best goal of his career

Cristiano Ronaldo has admitted his goal against Juventus is the best of his career.

The Real Madrid superstar scored a stunning overhead kick in the Champions League quarter-final first leg as his side took a 3-0 lead in Turin.

Ronaldo opened the scoring early on before connecting with Dani Carvajal's cross with an incredible acrobatic effort.

It was one of a number of great goals from the Portuguese great down the years with many debating if it is infact his best.

And the man himself has had his say, admitting: "It was probably the best goal of my career.

"The whole team great but of course people are now talking about my second goal - it was indeed a spectacular goal.

"I once scored one with the national team which hit the bar and bounced down over the line but the referee didn't give the goal.

"This one was a similar one, well, no, actually this one was better.

"Then there was another overhead kick for the national team, but if I had to choose one I'd choose the one from today.

"It was spectacular, I jumped very high and it's a goal that will live long in the memory. It's probably my best goal.

"As many people have said, I've been looking to do it for a while, but it depends on the circumstances of the game.

"It just came to me and I decided to give it a go. Sometimes it doesn't come off, but you always have to try. Today I tried and it went in. A great goal!

Ronaldo was applauded by the Juventus fans after the goal and admitted it will live long in his memory.

"It was one of the most beautiful moments of the night. To receive a standing ovation from a stadium with so much history, is a unique experience," he added.

"I am really happy and touched. When I was a kid, I liked Juventus and the fact that their fans have applauded me will stay with me. It is a great memory.

"To all the Italians in the stadium I just want to say: "Grazie!"
