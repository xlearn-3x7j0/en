Max Whitlock shines as England scoops six Commonwealth Games golds on day one

Max Whitlock showed the champion quality Alistair Brownlee could not as England bounced back to boss the Commonwealth Games ' day one medal table, writes Alex Spink on the Gold Coast .

Hours after Brownlee came up well short in his triathlon title defence, fellow two-time Olympic gold medalist Whitlock made no such mistake.

Britain’s greatest gymnast delivered two flawless routines to join the outstanding Nile Wilson in helping England easily retain their men’s team crown - one of six golds captured on the opening day.

“I’m really happy with floor and pommel,” said Whitlock, who won his two Olympic title disciplines with increased levels of difficulty as England triumphed by a massive 10-point margin over Canada.

“I had to wait until the last two pieces to compete but the most important thing for me was that I contributed to the team. Taking gold with the boys is always a special feeling.”

It was a feeling England had to wait until late in the day to enjoy, after the Brownlee brothers bombed in the men’s triathlon and world swimming champion Ben Proud false started in the 50m butterfly.

Alistair, the king of triathlon, was expected to spark England’s gold rush but instead finished a distant 10th, with brother Jonny seventh — it's the first time in four years that neither had made the podium in a race they both competed in.

“I’ve had a lot of luck over the years and it just ran out today,” said Alistair, who led out of the water and off the bike before breaking down on the 5km run. “I’d probably run only a handful of times in the last month or so due to a calf issue - and very fast for no more than a minute or two at a time.”

Jonny said he had “expected” his brother to break down as he did but refused to write off their chances in Saturday’s mixed relay — perhaps because Jess Learmonth is also in their team and she took silver in the women’s event.

They were not even the best of British here, with Scotland’s Marc Austin taking bronze behind South African winner Henri Schoeman.

However, after that disappointment for England cyclist Sophie Thornhill and her pilot Helen Scott began the comeback with gold in the blind and visually impaired sprint. And when swimmer Aimee Willmott followed the gymnasts by winning the 400m individual medley, England had claimed three golds in just seven minutes.

The gold rush continued in the pool with further gold medals for James Wilby, Eleanor Robinson and Thomas Hamer, leaving host nation Australia in no doubt that their arch-rivals mean business Down Under.

Four years ago, England topped the medal table for the first time since 1986. But they have never managed it in the southern hemisphere.

In the team events, Tracey Neville’s netballers thrashed Scotland 74-28, before the Scots gained revenge at basketball, beating England 78-65.

England’s hockey women opened up with a comfortable 2-0 win over South Africa.
