Boyhood club of ex-Liverpool star John Arne Riise unveil statue in his honour

Former Liverpool defender John Arne Riise has had a statue unveiled in his honour at his boyhood club Aalesund.

The 37-year-old is the most capped player in his native Norway and returned to Aalesund a year before he hung up his boots back in 2016.

As a solid full-back, Riise is best remembered for his thunderbolt of a left foot which helped Liverpool to five major honours - including the 2005 Champions League - during his seven-year stint on Merseyside.

The Norwegian also had spells with Monaco, Roma and Fulham during his career, which ended with Indian Super League club Chennaiyin F.C two years ago.

Writing on his Instagram about the unveiling of the statue, he wrote: "So honoured and proud, thank you!"

His old side Liverpool face Manchester City on Wednesday night in the quarter-finals of the Champions League, and speaking earlier in the week, Riise reckons they're very close to winning trophies in the near future.

As reported by Sky Sports , Riise said: "[Klopp] will win trophies, I am sure of it. As soon as you start winning trophies there is no way back and he is just going to keep going and going.

"The style of play now is amazing and they are doing well in the Champions League.

"Hopefully next season they can get a better start in the Premier League so they can fight from the beginning."
