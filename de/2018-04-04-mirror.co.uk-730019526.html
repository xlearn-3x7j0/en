Tommy Fleetwood on why he won't mind Masters crowd serenading him

Tommy Fleetwood is getting Big Love on the PGA Tour – and the European No.1 has urged Americans fans: Don’t Stop.

The Race To Dubai winner’s rock-star looks and beaming smile have made him a cult figure at US tournaments.

And when the world No.12 walks down the fairways with Tiger Woods, you will hear the noise Everywhere.

“The American fans are so enthusiastic and boisterous, which is great,” said the Southport pro, who finished fourth at the US Open.

“I feed off it and I love it. Considering that I am not that well-known in America, it is great to have that support. People just take to you.

“There are a lot of remarks about my hair and music. ­People tend to want to sing Fleetwood Mac songs to me which is all great. I am all for it. Long may it continue.

“I can’t really explain why. It’s the way I have been brought up by my dad – always smile at people and always be nice. And it has come across well in America.

“It has been a gradual thing as we have played over here more. And sometimes when you are playing you don’t quite realise it. When people – like my wife Clare or friends – come and support me, they say: ‘You are really popular in America, you have got a lot of people rooting for you.’

And then I think: ‘Yeah, it is great.’ It has just grown a little. And I have had some stellar groups. I have played with Mickelson and Kuchar in LA and had strong groups that people have followed.

“It has just grown from there. I have been lucky. I find it brilliant and I really enjoy it.”

Fleetwood, who has a base in Orlando, has already played five tournaments in the USA after taking up full PGA Tour membership.

“If I am not playing in a tournament I am perfectly happy getting up in the morning and getting on a buggy and going to play some golf in the sunshine with my shorts on,” he said.

The Everton fan found his Masters debut last year less fun as he missed the cut after two poor rounds of 78 and 74.

“Because of bad weather last year, two practice rounds got called off and I wasn’t prepared enough around the greens,” he recalled.

“I definitely will do better this year.”

Competing in more Majors and making his first Ryder Cup are his aims for this season after his outstanding 2017.

But he might not be as popular with the Americans in Paris. “You never know,” he laughed. “It depends how I play!”
