Man City to face Liverpool in a pre-season friendly held in New York

  • Manchester City and Liverpool will go head-to-head in New York on July 25 
  • It is likely to be held at New Jersey's 82,500-capacity MetLife Stadium
  • City are also set to have pre-season friendlies in Chicago and Miami this summer 

Published: 04:36 EDT, 30 March 2018 | Updated: 08:30 EDT, 30 March 2018

Premier League champions elect Manchester City will face Jurgen Klopp's Liverpool in New York on July 25.

Sportsmail understands Pep Guardiola's side are also set to take in Chicago and Miami during their summer tour of America.

A stay in New York will be sandwiched between the two other cities, with the friendly against Liverpool likely to be held at New Jersey's 82,500-capacity MetLife Stadium, although that is yet to be confirmed.

Manchester City and Liverpool will meet in the summer for a pre-season friendly in New York

The game, on July 25, is likely to be held at New Jersey's 82,500-capacity MetLife Stadium

Guardiola will also take in the home of City's sister club, New York City, to make use of the facilities and drop in on Patrick Vieira.

There will not be a Manchester derby, even though Jose Mourinho is seemingly heading to Miami as well. Discussions have taken place about Manchester United playing Real Madrid in Florida.

United, who are basing themselves at UCLA in Los Angeles, are also believed to be meeting Liverpool, potentially at the Big House in Michigan, which holds 107,000. 

The two rivals will have played each four times this season - twice in the league and in Europe
