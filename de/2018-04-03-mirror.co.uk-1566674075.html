Player ratings as Real Madrid proved too good for Juventus

Real Madrid proved their class with a dominant 3-0 win over Italian side Juventus in their Champions League quarter-final first leg clash in Turin.

A rematch of last season's final in Cardiff garnered the same result, with Cristiano Ronaldo once more scoring a brace as Zinedine Zidane's side continued the defence of their trophy.

Ronaldo opened the scoring after less than three minutes, poking home from close range after a clever ball from Isco.

The 33-year-old bagged his 119th Champions League goal with a sublime overhead kick just after the hour mark, before Paulo Dybala's red card ended Juve's hopes of a comeback.

Marcelo added a third as Los Blancos al but booked their spot in the semi-finals.

Here are Alex Richards' player ratings from the clash in Turin...

Juventus ratings

Gianluigi Buffon 5

Little the legendary Italian could do about any of the goals; looks destined to retire without ever getting his hands on the Champions League.

Mattia De Sciglio 5

The Italian international often found himself overrun.

Andrea Barzagli 5

Stepping in for the suspended Mehdi Benatia, the veteran defender lost Ronaldo for the opener.

Giorgio Chiellini 5

Continues to put his body on the line for the cause, but had no answer to Ronaldo.

Kwadwo Asamoah 5

The Ghanaian badly struggled at left back.

Rodrigo Bentancur 5

Unquestionably talented but often bypassed by Madrid's midfield.

Sami Khedira 7

An all-action display from the German, who tried to drag his side into the game after the early concession.

Douglas Costa 5

The mercurial Brazilian put in some excellent crosses but at other points allowed his touch to get away from him. Frustrating.

Paulo Dybala 4

Huffed and puffed but failed to make a major impression before being sent off in the 65th minute.

Alex Sandro 5

Much more at home at left-back with space to move into, rather than playing on the half turn.

Gonzalo Higuaín 6

Led the line well and linked play, but will think of what could have been after Navas' save

Subs: Mandzukic (Asamoah, 68), Matuidi (Costa, 68)

Real Madrid ratings

Keylor Navas 7

Tremendous reflex save denied Higuain in the first period and again denied the Argentine in the final moments.

Dani Carvajal 7

Solid defensively and adds drive to Madrid's attacks down the right; his cross set up Ronaldo for his second.

Raphael Varane 6

Classy and composed for the most part, but sometimes appears to find it too easy and switches off - witness Higuain's golden first-half chance.

Sergio Ramos 7

Leads by example and doesn't let those around him rest on their laurels.

Marcelo 8

Played a key role in the first, scored the third - in any other team he'd likely be playing as a creative No.10.

Luka Modric 7

Typically hard-working and understated performance from the Croatian.

Casemiro 6

Had some trouble with Dybala's movement, but largely protected his defence well and offered balance.

Toni Kroos 7

World Cup winner thrashed the crossbar from 25 yards in the first half and kept Madrid ticking over.

Isco 8

The in-form playmaker justified his selection with a sublime display at the tip of Madrid's midfield. His touch and movement were on another level to anyone else on the field.

Cristiano Ronaldo 9 MOTM

Another pair of Champions League goals - 24 in his last 14 ties - by the man for the big occasion; he remains peerless in the penalty box as his overhead kick showed. 119 Champions League goals now, my word.

Karim Benzema 5

More selfless running to stretch the Juve defence but didn't look like scoring before being replaced before the hour mark.

Subs: Lucas Vazquez 6 (Benzema, 58), Marco Asensio (Isco, 74), Mateo Kovacic (Modric, 81)
