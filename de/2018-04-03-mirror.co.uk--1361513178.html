The 9 best WrestleMania debuts as Rousey set to make her 'Show of Shows' bow

Ronda Rousey is set to join a pretty exclusive list of professional wrestlers who've made their debut at WrestleMania.

Apart from celebrity spots like Lawrence Taylor, Floyd Mayweather and Mr T. there've only been a couple who've actually made it onto the card without appearing in the ring before.

Rousey joins the likes of Sting, Fandango and Big E to name a handful of superstars to make their bow at WWE's biggest event.

Seeing as that list was far too short to compose, we've run down the nine best WrestleMania debuts of all time...

AJ Styles - WrestleMania 32

Styles kicks off the list as more of an honourable mention, as the first person to ever appear at New Japan's Wrestle Kingdom and WrestleMania in the same year.

The rumble kick-started his WWE career but his match with Chris Jericho elevated it, and despite losing, Styles never looked back.

He heads into this year's card against Shinsuke Nakamura to defend his WWE Championship, in a repeat of the epic bout they had at the aforementioned Wrestle Kingdom.

Mickie James WrestleMania 22

The Mickie James stalker storyline was probably one of the most compelling angles in Diva history.

The superfan-turned-overzealous rival story was great and culminated in a memorable WrestleMania bout.

James spent much of the match antagonising Women's Champion Trish Stratus, and defeated her using much of her own move set before finishing her off with her staple Chick Kick.

The match is remembered most by Mickie's V finger lick, which WWE wiped from footage.

Speaking of the notorious incident, she said: "I came back through the curtain and Vince was really mad. In the moment it had seemed amazing to me. I thought 'Vince will love this'.

"'Crass' was the word I got. He didn't like it at all. He told me, 'We're going to have to go back and edit that out. Do you know how much time (this will take)?'"

The Hardy Boys - WrestleMania 2000

Brothers Jeff and Matt really burst onto the scene at No Mercy a couple of months prior to WrestleMania 2000, but it was here they began to cement their legacy.

The duo were in the bout that stole the show, with their Triangle Ladder Match with Edge & Christan and the Dudley Boyz becoming one of the most epic and legendary encounters of the Attitude Era.

While they may not have come out on top, this match changed their lives and was a pioneer of the Tables, Ladders and Chairs matches that would define their careers.

Mr Kennedy - WrestleMania 23

Although Mr Kennedy didn't quite go on to have the fledgling wrestling career so many thought he would, his WrestleMania debut remains among the most memorable in WWE history.

His pre-Mania push was huge, as he received the star treatment while a microphone descended into the middle of the ring so he could announce his name.

He defeated Edge, Randy Orton, Jeff and Matt Hardy, King Booker, Finlay and CM Punk to claim the victory in the Money in the Bank Ladder match.

In the years to follow, amidst injury, Wellness Suspension and backstage politics, he found himself out of the upper-mid-card range and his eventual release in 2009 signalled the end of what promised so much.

Edge & Christian - Wrestlemania 2000

Despite already mentioning this match, it seems fair to give Edge & Christian their own berth due to the success they went on to achieve in WWE as individuals and a tag team.

The aforementioned match was actually won by the blond-haired duo, who beat The Hardys and The Dudleys in a truly epic clash.

It won the Match of the Year 2000 before all three would also go on to claim the same prize the year after for the Tables, Ladders and Charis match at WrestleMania X-seven.

While a stable, Edge & Christan won seven Tag Team Championships. They'd go on to have incredibly successful individual careers in WWE, with Edge a seven-time World Heavyweight champ and Christian a two time holder of the belt.

Kane - WrestleMania XIV

When Kane burst onto the scene as the Big Red Monster, it finally found a fitting character for Glen Jacobs who had done the rounds as a host of other gimmicks including Isaac Yankem and the Fake Diesel, which failed to take off.

His WrestleMania bow was against his on-screen brother, Undertaker, and despite his undefeated streak starting seven-years prior to 14, Kane gave him his first real test.

It was arguably the finest performance he ever gave in the ring, and it took Taker three tombstones to finally put under his little bro.

Kane is undoubtedly one of the great big men who lasted the test of time once the Attitude Era came to an end.

John Cena - WrestleMania XX

Would you argue against anyone who says John Cena is up there with WrestleMania's biggest stars of all time?

His longevity and kayfabe success mean he's a bonafide WWE legend - and it all began at Mania 20.

Cena arrived on the scene as a genuine star, gathering steam as a fan favourite and his match against Big Show for the US Championship helped elevate him to stardom.

Having hit Show with two 500lbs 'FU's', Cena took the title from a veteran - something that many before him had done on the road to success.

From 21 through to 29, Cena would find himself in the main event or battling for a title as he became Vince McMahon’s chosen one.

Undertaker - WrestleMania VII

The man's legacy speaks for itself and it all began with a fairly innocuous match way back in 1991 at WrestleMania 7.

Taker went over Jimmy Snuka in a match which lasted just four minutes and 20 seconds, and even though WWE didn't know what was about to happen over the next two decades, his performances suggest they had a clue.

Eight months after the Snuka win, Taker would beat Hulk Hogan for the WWF Championship at Survivor Series as he began to cement himself as a legend within the company.

Taker's Mania streak sits at 23-2, notoriously losing to Brock Lesnar at WrestleMania 30 and Roman Reigns at 33, but having overcome the likes of Kane, Ric Flair, Triple H, Batista and Shawn Michaels to name but a few, there's no denying a feat of this kind and one we'll never see again.

Brock Lesnar - WrestleMania 19

Brock Lesnar was big, strong and downright frightening - his allure so strong that his first Mania match was a main event, world title bout.

His match against Kurt Angle was one of the greatest main events in Mania history. Lesnar going for a shooting star press will live long in the memories of anyone who saw it.

Even though he missed - and truly it could have ended his career there and then - Lesnar got back on his feet and hit the Olympian with an F-5 to win the match and the title.

Lesnar remains much of a Marmite figure with fans of WWE, and even though he's likely main-eventing again this year against Roman Reigns, he almost threw it all away.
