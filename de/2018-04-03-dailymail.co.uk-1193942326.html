Seven-foot alligator given police escort off a highway in Florida

  • Jacksonville Sheriff's Office dispatched an officer to the spot on Interstate 295
  • He found a seven-foot alligator lounging happily on one side of the highway
  • The officer drove the reptile into the median barrier and out of harm's way 

Published: 09:57 EDT, 3 April 2018 | Updated: 09:59 EDT, 3 April 2018

A seven-foot alligator brought a busy highway to a standstill in Florida before being given a police escort to the other side.

Jacksonville Sheriff's Office responded to a call about a wandering alligator on the northbound side of Interstate 295 Sunday.

When an officer arrived at the spot, he found the animal attempting to traverse the road.

Amusing dash cam footage captured by a passing driver shows authorities guiding the seven-foot long reptile.

'My wife and I were driving on highway Interstate 295 when we came across a 'gator in the middle of the highway,' said the passing driver.

Using what appears to be an umbrella, the officer drives the reptile across the highway, carefully to keep out of range of its jaws.

The alligator eventually took refuge on the median barrier of Interstate 295

Aided by a member of the public, the officer is able to encourage the animal over the road.

The alligator appears to in no hurry, leisurely crawling across the tarmac.

And when it reaches the median barrier it decides to take a rest.
