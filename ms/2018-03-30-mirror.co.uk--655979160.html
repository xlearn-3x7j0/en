The position Arsenal will prioritise making a new signing this summer - revealed

Arsenal have made signing a central midfielder their transfer priority this summer amid uncertainty over Jack Wilshere's future.

Wilshere is out of contract in the summer and is not happy with the deal on the table.

He is now considering his options and Arsenal are eyeing reinforcements in the middle of the park, reports the Telegraph.

Aaron Ramsey is also yet to extend his contract while there are doubts over Santi Cazorla's career.

Arsenal are also reportedly looking to strengthen in central defence this summer.

Arsenal handed big injury boost ahead of Sunday's showdown against Stoke

Jack Wilshere is fit to face Stoke on Sunday after Arsenal insisted there was no bust-up with England over his fitness, writes Mirror Football's John Cross.

Wilshere did not travel to Holland with England and came back early for treatment because of a knee injury amid reports that Arsenal were unhappy with the Football Association.

But Arsenal No2 Steve Bould, standing in for Arsene Wenger because of a sore throat, insisted that there was no issue with the way Wilshere had been handled.

Bould said: “Not that I’m aware of. I don’t know where the issues or the reports are coming from to be honest with you. Jack had a bit of a knee problem I think, but he’s come back and he’s fine. As far as I’m aware, there’s no issue.

“Jack trained yesterday so he’s OK. Everybody else has come back as far as we’re aware. We’ll find out a bit more this morning, but Jack was OK yesterday.”

Bould confirmed fit-again Alexandre Lacazette was back in training but was unsure if he could be back to face Stoke at the Emirates on Sunday.

Bould added: “That, I don’t know. He’s trained. He trained yesterday but whether he’ll be involved on the weekend or not, I’m not entirely sure.”

Arsene Wenger 'hungrier than ever' as Arsenal boss attempts to overcome massive financial hurdle

Arsene Wenger has got more “hunger” than ever to succeed as Arsenal boss - but is facing a major financial hurdle this summer, writes Mirror Football's John Cross.

Wenger, 68, is under major pressure to save the club’s season by winning the Europa League and missing out on a place in the Champions League for a second year running could have big consequences.

Arsenal’s estimated annual wage bill of £225m is higher than Liverpool, comparable with Chelsea but not as big as the Manchester clubs and yet they could have to make tough financial decisions this summer.

It leaves Arsenal facing a pivotal summer as Wenger has one year left on his current deal, his future is under the spotlight and they are also looking to shake-up the squad.

But Wenger’s assistant Steve Bould insists the Frenchman is as determined as ever. “He’s remarkable,” said Bould.

“Behind closed doors I’ve never known someone who’s as hungry and determined to win football games. It’s as big as I’ve ever seen, yes, for sure.

“I admire him immensely. He takes unbelievable stick off an awful lot of people and he’s just one of the great managers. It’s a pleasure for me to sit next to him.”
