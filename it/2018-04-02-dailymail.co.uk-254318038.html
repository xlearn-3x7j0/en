Chelsea and Spurs fans clash outside La Reserve Hotel on Fulham Road

  • Police caught in the middle of clashes between Tottenham and Chelsea fans 
  • The two sets of fans clashed outside of Stamford Bridge after Sunday's game
  • Bottles and traffic cones were thrown before police horses were deployed
  • Tensions grew after Spurs won at Stamford Bridge for first time since 1990

Published: 19:38 EDT, 1 April 2018 | Updated: 05:09 EDT, 2 April 2018

Hundreds of Tottenham and Chelsea fans had to be separated by police as tension grew following Spurs' 3-1 win at Stamford Bridge on Sunday.

The large majority of Spurs supporters were being escorted away from the stadium when the ugly scenes unfolded which saw bottles of beer thrown as well as a traffic cone.

Only a handful of police officers were originally on hand to try and calm down the incident before the exchanges between the two sets of fans became heated.

Chelsea and Tottenham fans clash outside of La Reserve Hotel on Fulham Road on Sunday

Several law enforcement officers kept the rival supporters apart as the Spurs contingent loudly sang 'We are Tottenham' on Fulham Road.

One fan even begins to climb onto a car in an attempt to reach the goading crowd in the frantic scenes outside the hotel.

One eyewitness, Joe Forrester, filmed the commotion from La Reserve Hotel which is a stone's throw from Chelsea's stadium.

Fans jumped on top of cars while other supporters threw a traffic cone and beer bottles

He told Sportsmail: 'Chelsea fans all came running out of the hotel bar when Spurs fans came past and it went from there. 

'I filmed from the roof of La Reserve Hotel on Fulham Road and it all kicked off.'

Missiles struck the parked cars outside of the hotel before police horses were deployed to control the crowd.

Shortly afterwards, the police received more back-up to end the trouble which began around 6pm on Sunday evening.

Mauricio Pochettino's side ended a 28-year streak of not winning at Stamford Bridge after goals from Christian Eriksen and Dele Alli proved to be too much for Antonio Conte's side.
