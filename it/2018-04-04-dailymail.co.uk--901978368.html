These fitness fanatics are proof the scales DO lie!

Weight loss experts often against using the scales to chart your progress and these pictures seem to prove it's sage advice. 

Fitness fanatics from around the world have been sharing their amazing body transformations on Instagram under the hashtag #screwthescales.

And aside from their fantastic figures, the most incredible thing about the snaps is that they weight exactly the same or even slightly more now than they did when they were unfit and not eating healthily. 

Fitness blooger Brittney Elizabeth from Arizona, who has lost 95lbs, advised followers to stop letting the scale determine your success. 

She shared side-by-side photos, revealing that she weighed 145lbs in both. 

However, in one snap she had a bulging tummy and in the other she's showing off her abs in tight-fitting leggings.

Veronica Leea from Madrid said that her figure proves that body transformation has nothing to do with 'losing weight'. 'So please STOP being obsessed about a number!' she said

Meanwhile Veronica Leea from Madrid revealed how the most frequent question she's asked if 'how do I lose weight?'. 

'Honestly if you look at these pictures the weight isn't the one that showcases the difference,' she explained. 'So please STOP being obsessed about a number! It doesn't define you at all.'

Wellness coach Nicole Raczynski said that at 5'2" and weighing 150lbs people would probably think she's overweight if they hadn't seen her. 

'We need to erase the stigma of the scale,' she explained. 

Professor of health psychology at the University of Surrey Jane Ogden has previously carried out research that shows how weighing yourself can damage how you feel about your body and may even make you put on weight by encouraging comfort eating when the number on the dial is not quite as expected.
