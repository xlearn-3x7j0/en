Fireman Matt Parziale happily climbing the ladder

After locking up a spot for the Masters, most golfers are in the mood to celebrate. After punching his ticket for Augusta, Matt Parziale embarked on a five-hour drive home, followed by four hours sleep before punching in for his 24 hour shift as a firefighter.

Amateurs have long been a part of the Masters story, of course, a nod in the direction of the tournament founder and the greatest amateur of all, Bobby Jones. But these days they're usually young wannabes taking up their last perk for winning a top amateur event before joining the pro ranks.

They're rarely real amateurs holding down real jobs like Parziale.

Matt Parziale in his firefighter kit - he is taking on the Augusta course as 31-year-old amateur

Matt Parziale is a firefighter from Brockton Massachusetts  and will be the latest amateur to take on Augusta when he plays in the Masters

Matt Parziale (right) will play the Masters at Augusta National as an amateur this week

And what a job. Like his father before him, Parziale, 31, works out of one of the busiest fire stations in America, midway between Boston and Fall River, Massachusetts. 

'My dad actually just retired in November, and he worked 32 years,' Matt told The Boston Globe. 'He never missed any hockey game I had so I figured it was a good schedule for me to play competitive golf. Being around the guys growing up, I always enjoyed going to the station and spending time with them. And I knew most of them before I got on, and it's such a great group of guys. We have such a good time.'

He previously tried to make it as a pro a decade ago but struggled. 

'I had a blast, but I had no money,' he said. 'I saw my dad spend his whole life loving his job and I decided to follow in his footsteps.' Regaining his amateur status, he won the US Mid-Amateur Championship last summer to earn a place in the field at not only the Masters but also the United States Open at Shinnecock Hills in June.

On Monday came his real celebration - a practice round with none other than Rory McIlroy, with his dad on the bag as caddy.

The 31-year-old enjoyed a practice round with former world No 1 golfer Rory McIlroy

'I've always wanted to play golf with Rory and he didn't disappoint either as a golfer or as a man,' said Parziale.

His blue-collar hometown of Brockton is known for producing two of the great boxers of all time in Rocky Marciano and Marvin Hagler. This week, in Ladder 1, they're embracing a gentler sport, and the heart-warming story of one of their own.
