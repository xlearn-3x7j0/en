England claim their first two gold medals of Commonwealth Games

  • England have claimed their first gold medals of the Commonwealth Games
  • Max Whitlock, Nile Wilson, Courtney Tulloch, James Hall and Dominick Cunningham took gold in the men's team gymnastics event
  • Swimmer Aimee Willmott then won the 400m individual medley on day one
  • Sophie Thornhill and her pilot Helen Scott took gold in visually impaired cycling sprint race 

Published: 06:02 EDT, 5 April 2018 | Updated: 06:23 EDT, 5 April 2018

Max Whitlock led England to their first gold medal of the Gold Coast Commonwealth Games with victory in the men's gymnastics team event in Coomera.

The double Olympic champion top-scored with his floor and pommel routines to clinch the win alongside team-mates Nile Wilson, Courtney Tulloch, James Hall and Dominick Cunningham.

England's total of 258.950 left them more than 10 points clear of Canada in second place, with Scotland narrowly beaten into the bronze medal position.

At around the same time on the opening day, England's Aimee Willmott claimed gold in the women's 400 metres individual medley to deny Scotland's Hannah Miley a third successive title.

Willmott finished in four minutes 34.90 seconds as Miley, who won gold at Delhi 2010 and Glasgow 2014, finished 0.26secs behind.

Bronze went to Australia's Blair Evans in 4:38.23.

In the velodrome, England's Sophie Thornhill and pilot Helen Scott won gold in the blind and visually impaired (B&VI) sprint. 

Also on the opening day of action on the Gold Coast, England's Jessica Learmouth took silver in the women's triathlon and swimmer James Guy took bronze in the men's 400m freestyle. 

However there was disappointment for the Brownlee brothers Alistair and Jonny as they finished below the podium positions in the men's triathlon.
