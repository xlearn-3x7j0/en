Wolves ball boy keeps his job following online petition from fans

A Wolves ball boy, who shoved and made a finger gesture towards Hull assistant Andy Crosby on Tuesday night, has avoided the sack following an online petition.

Tom Aitchison, 16, snatched the ball from the Hull dugout as they attempt to wind down the clock in the 2-2 draw at Molineux.

The timewasting tactics could not prevent Oskar Buur Rasmussen from netting a late equaliser to snatch a point for the Championship leaders.

Wolves ball boy Tom Aitchison took action against timewasting from the Hull City bench

He took the ball from assistant Andy Crosby to hand the ball to a Hull player to take a throw-in

He then made a finger gesture towards the Hull dugout as play re-started late in the game

The incident occured near the end of the game, as Crosby looked to keep hold of the ball in the technical area.

The teenage ball boy was filmed snatching the ball out of his hands and racing down the touchline to hand it to an opposition player for a Hull throw-in.

As he went to take a seat he appeared to aim a finger gesture towards the away dugout.

He was then sent from the pitchside but cheered by home fans as made his way to the stands.

Amid fears he would be sacked an online petition was launched and signed by 3,600 people, but the club confirmed he would be keeping his job.

Petition organiser, Nathan Kerr, told The Sun: 'A lot of fans were outraged. As far as we are concerned a ball boy is there to retrieve the ball and they were holding it to waste time. It's out of order.

'I wasn't close enough to see but many fans have said he was gesturing 'No 1' as we are top of the league.

'I saw him go for the ball and someone fell over, not sure if he just lost his balance.

'The kid is a hero and was just showing some passion. The response has been amazing.

Wolves managing director Laurie Dalrymple tweeted on Wednesday: 'To confirm, there have been no staff dismissals following last night's game.

'All ball crew were invited to report for duty for our next home game and reminded of their role and responsibilities.'
