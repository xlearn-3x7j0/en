Trump claims CNN only hires his political haters

Donald Trump vented at CNN on Tuesday, claiming the network is unwilling to hire journalists who might support his agenda.

'Check out the fact that you can’t get a job at ratings challenged @CNN unless you state that you are totally anti-Trump?' the president tweeted.

'Little Jeff Zuker, whose job is in jeopardy, is not having much fun lately. They should clean up and strengthen CNN and get back to honest reporting!' he added, misspelling the name of CNN president Jeff Zucker.

Network talent immediately jumped to defend their objectivity – including CNN media reporter Brian Stelter, who erupted during a morning broadcast.

President Donald Trump ramped up his longstanding war with CNN on Tuesday

It's unclear where Trump got the idea that CNN has an ideological litmus test for hiring, but he claimed as much in a tweet

The Pew Research Center found that CNN, like MSNBC and the legacy broadcast networks' news divisions, all serve audiences that are generally more liberal than America as a whole

'He's on Twitter this morning saying that it's a fact that CNN only hires people if they're anti-Trump,' Stelter marveled. 'Now, we all around this table know there's no litmus test. There's no test like that. That's crazy talk.'

'It's crazy talk, but the president called it a fact. He called it a fact. [It] makes me want to go back to bed, guys.'

CNN's public relations team tweeted: 'The personal political beliefs of CNN's employees are of no interest to us. Their pursuit of the truth is our only concern. Also, Jeff's last name is spelled Z-U-C-K-E-R.'

CNN White House reporter Jeff Zeleny tweeted that his network was '[n]ot anti-Trump. Not pro-Trump. That's not our job.'

Trump also launched a Twitter broadside against television news in general, writing that 'Fake News Networks' including CNN, NBC, ABC & CBS 'have done so much dishonest reporting that they should only be allowed to get awards for fiction!'

A 2014 Pew Research Center study of media outlets found that ABC News, CBS News, NBC News, CNN and MSNBC all serve audiences that are ideologically to the left of the average American.

CNN was rated as more liberal than all the others, with the exception of MSNBC. The Fox News Channel was the only cable news outlet whose audience is more conservative than the average American.

In January the White House and the Republican National Committee issued 'Fake News Awards' to media outlets that had driven up the president's blood pressure dring hs first year in office.

CNN took home more 'falsies' than any other outlet.

The network's chief White House correspondent, Jim Acosta, drew condemnation from the political right on Monday after he peppered Trump with questions about immigration as the president greeted children during the annual Easter Egg Roll event.

Journalists mostly defended Acosta for making the most of an opportunity. But Trump's re-election campaign manager Brad Parscale channeled his boss instead.

Trump re-election campaign manager Brad Parscale wrote that CNN White House reporter Jim Acosta should have his credentials revoked for peppering the president with immigration questions during the White House Easter Egg Roll

'I think the White House should pull [Acosta's] credentials because he yelled questions, as he continues to do at inappropriate times, while the President was coloring books with children. Disrespectful and would have never been allowed previously,' he wrote on Twitter.

Trump's jab about 'ratings challenged' CNN is backed by recent Nielsen numbers, as reported by TVNewser. 

Among the ten cable news programs with the highest number of viewers overall, seven are seen on Fox News and the other three are on MSNBC.

Including only adult viewers between the ages of 25 and 54 – what broadcast executives aggressively chase as 'the demo' – CNN's 'Anderson Cooper 360' made the list in tenth place.

The network, however, offered a positive spin on Tuesday.

'As for "challenged," CNN just finished its second highest rated first quarter in the past nine years,' its PR team wrote on Twitter.

They also highlighted a Monmouth University poll, released Monday, that found 48 per cent of Americans trust CNN more than Trump, compared with just 35 per cent who say the opposite.

CNN's public relations department insisted its ratings are on the rise, but none of its programs were in the list of the 10 most-watched cable news shows in February

Among Republicans, however, those numbers are flipped upside down. Fully 75 per cent of GOP voters say Trump is more trustworthy than CNN, with just 12 per cent disagreeing.

Zucker appears to be making his stand based on the ideological leanings of Fox News.

He claimed last month during an industry conference that the ratings leader 'is really state-run TV,' according to The Hollywood Reporter. 

'It is a pure propaganda machine and I think does incredible disservice to this country.'

But despite the dismal numbers, Zucker insisted the previous three years have been among CNN's best in history.

'We've had a very good run,' he said. 'We made CNN very much relevant again.'
