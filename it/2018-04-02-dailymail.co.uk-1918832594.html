Holloway vs Nurmagomedov at UFC 223 - winner likely to face McGregor

Max Holloway has rescued UFC 223's main event by stepping up on short notice to replace Tony Ferguson who suffered a freak injury to his knee. 

The highly anticipated lightweight title fight between Ferguson and Khabib Nurmagomedov was shelved after 'El Cucuy' tore a ligament while fulfilling media obligations six days from their 155-lbs showdown.

Holloway is the current featherweight champion and has agreed to step up a division for his shot at becoming a two-weight champion. 

Max Holloway agreed to step up at short notice and take on Khabib Nurmagomedov

With Conor McGregor previously holding both belts, the Irishman seems destined to face the winner in a massive fight later this year. 

Although many called for the 'Notorious' to enter the octagon at short notice and battle Nurmagomedov, UFC president Dana White insisted September is a more likely date for McGregor's return. 

'I don't think Conor McGregor could make the weight on six days' notice. That's a tough weight cut for him.

Dana White believes Conor McGregor could face the winner in September 

'And Conor is not the guy I want to throw in as a late replacement fighter. He's a guy I want to make a fight with and have the right amount of time to promote the fight.

'I think Conor could return in September, and it could be against one of these guys,' he told ESPN. 

Ferguson is out of the equation and loses his interim lightweight belt, a crushing blow for a man riding a 10-fight win streak. Three prospective fights between the standout lightweights have now been cancelled. 

But his loss could be Holloway's gain and understandably White was full of praise for the Hawaiian for rescuing the showpiece in New York. 

'He's a stud. The guy hasn't lost a fight since 2013, and that was a decision to Conor McGregor,' White added. 

'He's the type of guy that steps up for opportunities like this. And when you think about it, if he wins this fight, other than McGregor, he will be the only guy to hold two belts at the same time.'

Nurmagomedov is yet to be beaten and is a huge favourite to win the lightweight title 

Holloway has won 12 consecutive fights and the 26-year-old could shoot to an unprecedented level of stardom by beating the so far untouchable Nurmagomedov. 

The fearsome wrestler is a heavy favourite with the bookies and should fancy his chances against a much lighter opponent. 

The UFC continue to be plagued by late withdrawals but fortunately on this occasion pay-per-view sales shouldn't take too much of a hit given Holloway's champion status and McGregor's involvement in both title pictures producing a fascinating back-drop to Saturday night.
