Real Madrid 'unsure on bid for Chelsea star Eden Hazard'

  • Real Madrid reassessing their interest in Chelsea forward Eden Hazard
  • Hazard's poor form and an asking price of over £100m is causing some concern
  • Chelsea are hoping to tie Hazard down to a new £300,000-a-week deal son 

Published: 02:28 EDT, 4 April 2018 | Updated: 03:52 EDT, 4 April 2018

Real Madrid are weighing up whether to drop their plans to make a summer bid for Chelsea star Eden Hazard and are yet to make any form of contact regarding the move, according to reports.

The Belgium international has been on Real Madrid's radar for several years but the continued dominance of Cristiano Ronaldo has diminished their need for a left-sided forward. 

That could change this summer although the Evening Standard reports that as of yet there has been no advances made towards either Chelsea or the player's entourage regarding a move.

Eden Hazard has emerged as a potential summer target for Real Madrid this summer

Real Madrid are under pressure to spend big after failing to challenge Barcelona in La Liga and a marquee signing is likely to arrive in one form or another after the World Cup this summer.

However, it now looks unlikely to be Hazard with his poor form starting to cause concern among Bernabeu officials. 

The 27-year-old has fallen from his high perch as Antonio Conte's men, like Real Madrid, failed to mount a respectable title defence. 

Chelsea will be unwilling to part with the jewel in their crown and are ready to demand a transfer fee well in excess of £100million to fall in line with the transfers of Neymar, Kylian Mbappe and Ousmane Dembele. 

The 27-year-old will lead Belgium to the World Cup in Russia this summer as captain 

Real Madrid worry they will stall the progression of Marco Asensio (left) by signing Hazard

There is also a belief that signing Hazard could stall the progress of Marco Asensio, a young Spaniard that Real Madrid have exceedingly high hopes for. Not only does the Spaniard come with a homegrown tag, he is also five years Hazard's junior.

Chelsea will continue their attempts to tie Hazard down to a new contract, although they face an almighty battle to convince him if, as expected, they drop out of the Champions League and into the Europa League.

They are ready to make him their highest paid player with a £300,000-a-week deal, increasing his wage from £200,000-a-week. But dropping down to Europe's second tier competition leaves them vulnerable to potential suitors.
