Ronaldo says he was 'touched' by standing ovation he received in Turin

  • Cristiano Ronaldo has thanked Juventus fans for standing ovation he received
  • The Real Madrid forward scored a spectacular bicycle kick in their 3-0 win
  • The Juventus Stadium crowd rose to their feet to applaud the effort on Tuesday
  • Ronaldo reveals he was 'touched' by the gesture and says it's a 'great memory' 

Published: 06:35 EDT, 5 April 2018 | Updated: 08:13 EDT, 5 April 2018

Cristiano Ronaldo has thanked the Juventus supporters for applauding his spectacular overhead kick against them in the Champions League.

The Real Madrid talisman reveals it was a 'great memory' for him and has even admitted to liking the Italian champions when he was younger.

Ronaldo doubled Real's lead in the first leg of their quarter-final clash with a stunning bicycle kick to bring all four stands of the Juventus Stadium to their feet.

Cristiano Ronaldo reveals he was 'touched' by standing ovation he received by Juventus fans

Ronaldo brought the Juventus Stadium to its feet after scoring a spectacular overhead kick

The Portuguese forward seem taken aback and appeared very gracious after he finished celebrating with his team-mates.

When asked about his reaction to the standing ovation, Ronaldo told Real Madrid's official YouTube channel: 'It was one of the most beautiful moments of the night.

'To receive an ovation from the stadium with so much history, is a unique experience.

'I am really happy and touched. When I was a kid, I liked Juventus and the fact that their fans have applauded me will stay with me. It's a great memory. 

'To all of the Italians in the stadium I just want to say: "Grazie!"' 

The 33-year-old appeared gracious after receiving the ovation and thanked the Juventus fans

Ronaldo, who has now scored 39 goals in 36 games in all competitions this season, has labelled the spectacular finish as 'probably the best goal' of his career.

He said: 'People are now talking about my second goal. It was indeed a spectacular goal.

'It was probably the best goal of my career. I want to thank the whole team, we were great today (Tuesday).'

Zinedine Zidane's side take a commanding 3-0 lead back to the Bernabeu for next week's second leg.
