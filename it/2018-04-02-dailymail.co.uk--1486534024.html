Rory McIlroy enjoys early practice round at Augusta ahead of Masters

Rory McIlroy got an early feel for Augusta on Monday as he looks to finally complete his career Grand Slam.

McIlroy, who goes into the Masters in strong form, was pictured polishing up his game ahead of the start of the first major of the year on Thursday.

The Northern Irishman enjoyed an early practice round in the sun with his caddie Harry Diamond three days before the start of the eagerly-anticipated tournament.

The 28-year-old played alongside American Wesley Bryan and English amateur Harry Ellis

McIlroy was seen playing alongside American Wesley Bryan and English amateur Harry Ellis during his round as he adapted to the pristine surroundings.

McIlroy, among the early arrivals in Georgia, will fancy his chances at Augusta this week and goes into the tournament as one of the strong favourites.

The 28-year-old picked up his first victory since September 2016 when he won the Arnold Palmer Invitational just last month.

And speaking ahead of Thursday's start, McIlroy, who has won the British Open, US Open and US PGA Championship, admits he is prepared to 'p*** a few people off' in order to finally win the Masters following his heartbreak back in 2011.

McIlroy has admitted he will have a smaller entourage with him during the Masters

'I think everyone feels the same about the Masters as it's the one tournament everyone wants to go to,' McIlroy said. 'But this year I'm trying to cut that back and not have such a big entourage around me and not too many people there. 

'I basically want to try and treat it like a normal week instead of having seven people in the house and renting another house for 10 people. 

'It becomes quite a production at times and I just want to have it chilled and quiet. It might piss a few people off but, if it helps me win the Masters, I don't mind that.' 

Meanwhile, Justin Rose, who missed out on the Masters title last year following a play-off with Sergio Garcia, has also arrived in Augusta early.

The Englishman took to Instagram in the early hours of Monday morning to post an artistic photo of Augusta Clubhouse.
