West Ham midfielder Michail Antonio ruled out for remainder of season

  • Michail Antonio's season is over after he suffered a hamstring injury on Saturday
  • The 28-year-old limped off in tears before his team-mates secured a vital win
  • Manuel Lanzini is set to be back in the West Ham squad for Chelsea this Sunday

Published: 05:37 EDT, 2 April 2018 | Updated: 05:46 EDT, 2 April 2018

Michail Antonio’s hamstring injury has ruled him out for the rest of the season but Manuel Lanzini is set to be back in the West Ham squad for Chelsea this Sunday.

Antonio was visibly upset after being forced off only nine minutes into their relegation six-pointer against Southampton at the London Stadium on Saturday.

The 28-year-old, whose season has been disrupted by injuries, limped off in tears before his team-mates secured a vital Premier League win over the Saints.

ANTONIO'S INJURIES

Injuries this season: 

Type: Rib bruise

Type: Calf Problems 

Length of time out: Dec 23, 2017 - Feb 2, 2018 (41 days)

Type: Hamstring injury

Length of time out: March 31, 2018 - remainder of season

Antonio’s injury will come as a blow to West Ham but Lanzini’s return following a knock is a boost. The Argentine is set to face Chelsea at Stamford Bridge this weekend.

Three of the Hammers’ next four games are against Chelsea, Arsenal and Manchester City and David Moyes has been frustrated by a lack of options in the squad. His bench for the game against Southampton raised eyebrows for its lack of experience.

West Ham currently sit 14th in the table – five points above the Premier League’s bottom three.

Antonio’s injury is just his latest issue this season. A groin problem kept him out for more than a month in the winter after being ruled out in the Autumn with damaged ribs.
