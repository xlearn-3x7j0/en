Alabama dog owners charged with negligent homicide woman is killed

The owners of a group of dogs that mauled to death a 24-year-old Alabama woman in the town of Section have been charged with criminally negligent homicide.

Brian Keith Graden, 43, and Melody Ann Graden, 45, were both booked into Jackson County Jail on Tuesday evening. 

The couple have also been charged with four counts of failure to immunize a pet for rabies, jail records show.    

The Gradens owned the five dogs that fatally attacked Emily Colvin on 7 December. She died in front of her Section home while another woman was injured.

Brian Keith Graden, 43, and Melody Ann Graden, 45 are to stand trial for the fatal dog attack at a later date. They have been charged with criminally negligent homicide

The couple have been indicted by a Jackson County grand jury, a Jackson County Sheriff's Office spokesman said.

A friend of Colvin's told WAAY 31 she had complained about the dogs running loose in the area before. 

A judge recently ordered four of the dogs to be euthanized - a fifth was killed by police on the day of the attack.  

Colvin's death prompted Alabama lawmakers to pass a bill this year that can impose felony charges on owners of dogs that seriously injure or killed someone, al.com reported. 

The Gradens  have not been charged under the news law because it was passed after Colvin's death.

She was killed just days after another north Alabama woman was killed in a dog attack. 

In that case, Tracy Patterson Cornelius, 46, was killed in Marshall County. The owners of the dogs in that attack have been charged with manslaughter, a felony.
