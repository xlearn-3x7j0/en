He'll be back! Arnie returns in new PPI advert

Published: 18:37 EDT, 3 April 2018 | Updated: 18:37 EDT, 3 April 2018

Trolley dolly: Arnold Schwarzenegger in the new PPI advert

Arnold Schwarzenegger’s robotic head is starring in an ad urging families to reclaim mis-sold payment protection insurance.

The publicity campaign, run by the City watchdog, features the head of Schwarzenegger, who played The Terminator in the eponymous films and is an ex-governor of California, driving around on tank tracks.

In the latest advert the head appears from an old woman’s shopping trolley. He urges passengers at a bus stop to claim for PPI compensation ahead of a deadline in August next year.

The Financial Conduct Authority is also launching radio adverts today as part of its marketing efforts.

The campaign features an impersonator rather than the star himself and is being funded with £42million from the big banks responsible for the mis-selling scandal.

Schwarzenegger, 70, had emergency heart surgery last week. Yesterday he tweeted that he was on the mend.
