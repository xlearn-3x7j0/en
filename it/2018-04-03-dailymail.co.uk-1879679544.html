13 states under winter storm advisories

The wet and cold beginning to spring continues today as more than a dozen states were placed under weather warnings. 

A day after New York City was hit with its heaviest April snowfall in decades, another unseasonable snow storm has struck the Midwest dumping heavy snow from the Dakotas into Iowa and Minnesota on Tuesday morning.

The cold blast will see temperatures will plummet to freezing over the next couple of days.

Meanwhile, Ohio has been struck with a heavy deluge and the weather system appears to be moving east.

Ohio, Indiana, Kentucky, Tennessee, Arkansas and Mississippi are bracing for strong winds, tornadoes, and hail today, ABC News reports.

Snow will continue to fall in the north, from Iowa and Wisconsin, to Michigan which could receive up to a foot of snow. 

The storm will hit the East Coast by Wednesday afternoon, stretching as far south as Florida and as north as Maine, bringing powerful winds and hail.

And it's not the end of the bad weather. Another storm appears to be brewing which will hit the Midwest on Friday and spread into the Northeast on Saturday morning.

National Weather Service meteorologist David Stark blamed multiple cold fronts from Canada for the late snowfalls. 

'It's not unusual to see snow in early April,' Stark told the New York Daily News. 'You're not going to see it every April, but it's not unprecedented.'  

AccuWeather meteorologist Tom Kines also warned that this might not be the end of the late snow.
