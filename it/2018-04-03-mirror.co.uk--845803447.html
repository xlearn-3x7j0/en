'Ronaldo will be more bothered about miss than incredible goal'

Rio Ferdinand believes Cristiano Ronaldo will be thinking about his miss against Juventus - rather than his incredible acrobatic goal.

The Real Madrid superstar scored twice to give his side a 3-0 lead in the Champions League quarter-final first leg.

After opening the scoring early on, Ronaldo scored a stunning overhead kick to put Real in pole position to reach the last four.

His goal sparked wild celebrations among Real's players and fans while the home support even applauded the Portuguese.

Ronaldo should have had his hat-trick late on but fired over from Lucas Vasquez's cross.

And former United teammate Ferdinand suggested that miss will be on Ronaldo's mind, rather than his superb strike.

"He has turned himself into a box player. He is about end product," he said on BT Sport.

"I bet you he is thinking more about his miss!

"He will be slapping himself in the face in the mirror after the game. Zidane must be devastated!"

Frank Lampard added: "The cross doesn't need to have too much on it. The greatness is, that will be on his mind."

Ferdinand lavished praise on Ronaldo, who took his tally in the competition to 119 with the brace.

"In his relentless pursuit of greatness, he produces an absolute moment of brilliance," he added.

"The athleticism, look how high he is off the floor, it is a joke."
