Garcia to dish up wife Angela's cake at Masters Champions Dinner

  • Every year the reigning Masters champion decides on the menu for dinner 
  • Champions come together to eat usually traditional dishes of winner's homeland
  • Sergio Garcia posted menu on Twitter and wrote about his excitement
  • He has chosen traditional lobster rice, international salad and Tres Leches cake 

Published: 11:16 EDT, 3 April 2018 | Updated: 11:17 EDT, 3 April 2018

Sergio Garcia served up four brilliant rounds of golf to win the Masters last year and now he's dishing up a treat at the annual Champions dinner. 

The Spaniard revealed his menu in a Twitter post, unsurprisingly taking inspiration from his homeland. 

To start, the former winners will tuck into some 'international salads', which are inspired by past champions from around the world.  

Sergio Garcia posted this picture and is looking forward to hosting the Champions Dinner

The Spaniard is dishing up traditional Spanish Lobster Rice for his main course

The main is where Garcia has put his stamp on the dinner, opting for 'Arroz Caldoso de Bogavante' which is traditional Spanish Lobster Rice.   

And his wife Angela, who celebrated Garcia's triumph with him at Augusta last year, is baking up Tres Leches Cake which is a sponge dessert soaked in milk. 

Garcia also posted a photo of past Masters champions on Twitter, writing: 'So excited to host my first Champions Dinner at the Masters. Can't wait to sit around and listen to all of those great champions!'

To wash down the grub, Garcia has chosen a white wine from vines between 60 and 80 years old, a bottle of which sells for £34.98. For those who prefer red, a 2014 Pinea Tempranillo will be available.

Angela Garcia will be serving up some Tres Leches Cake for dessert at the dinner

The Masters is close to Garcia's heart having won his first major on the legendary course. He even named his daughter Azalea after the 13th hole as it played a critical part in his success last year. 

Augusta National is steeped in tradition and the Champions Dinner is a key part of that, one the 38-year-old is clearly relishing. 

After feeding the former champions, Garcia's attention will turn to the golf itself and his defence of the iconic green jacket.  

Garcia won the first major of his career after beating Justin Rose in a play-off
