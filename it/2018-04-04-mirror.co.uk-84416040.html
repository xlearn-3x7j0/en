Liverpool squad arrives at Melwood - including star who hasn't played all season

Liverpool stars arrived at training ahead of Wednesday night's Champions League quarter-final clash with Manchester City - who took a morning session at Everton's Goodison Park.

The Reds welcome the Premier League champions-elect to Anfield hoping to record a positive result before next week's Etihad tie.

A host of players were pictured arriving at the 54,000-seater stadium - six hours before kick off - after a busy day so far by all accounts.

Once they leave Anfield, they'll be heading to the team hotel before going back to the stadium later on with the club asking supporters to show their passion pre-match "in a friendly, respectful and considerate manner", with reports fans are going to line the streets with flares and fireworks rife.

Sadio Mane, Mo Salah, Roberto Firmino, Alex Oxlade Chamberlain and Jordan Henderson were all snapped in their pre-match attire heading into the ground.

They were followed by boss Jurgen Klopp who will be looking for a repeat of the win over City earlier in the campaign - the runaway leaders only loss in the Premier League this season.

Earlier in the day, Salah and Firmino were pictured en route to the light session at the club's Melwood training ground.

Nathaniel Clyne was also pictured, and he'll have to be on top form should Liverpool keep out Pep Guardiola's side, with the Reds amidst a defensive crisis.

Joel Matip , Ragnar Klavan, Joe Gomez and potential centre-back Emre Can are all out of the game, leaving Virgil van Dijk and Dejan Lovren as Jurgen Klopp's only fit senior centre-backs.

And the German knows it'll be tough to keep free-scoring City at bay, but plans to attack from the off to achieve the right result.

“Yet we are difficult to defend against, too," he said ahead of the game. "You give us the chance to play one pass and it is very difficult to stop the next one. We either miss the chance or we score.

“If you are West Brom, you cannot be brave against City because you cannot attack them high or they run through you like a warm knife through butter.

"You have no chance. But we can. You need to have a plan for your players and then it is a mix of quality and ­character. We all have to use the skills of the players.”

Manchester City stars also touched down on Merseyside and they'll train at Reds' rivals Everton's Goodison Park ahead of the showdown.

Former Liverpool man Raheem Sterling will no doubt face a hostile reaction at Anfield as he has done ever since his £50million move back in 2015.

Kevin De Bruyne and Leroy Sane will also be hoping to continue their rich veins of form, with the diminutive Belgian no doubt key to any sort of City victory.

Sane is enjoying his second season in Manchester having notched 13 goals and 15 assists in all competitions thus far for the Citizens.
