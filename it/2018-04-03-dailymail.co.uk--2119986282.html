Craig Gordon to make early comeback for Celtic against Dundee

Craig Gordon will make his Celtic first-team comeback weeks earlier than expected against Dundee on Wednesday night.

On-loan Scott Bain is ineligible to face his parent club in the rearranged Premiership clash, clearing the way for Gordon to defy concerns that his latest knee injury was season-ending.

Confirming Gordon is back in time for the title run-in and the Scottish Cup semi-final clash with Rangers, Rodgers said: 'Craig is fine and should be available for tomorrow.

'He's back before his target date and he's worked very hard, looked good in training and we'll just make sure there are no after-effects.

'Obviously, Scott can't play tomorrow, so if all's well with Craig then he'll start.' While acknowledging that many feared the worst when Gordon was injured against Hibs on January 27, Rodgers was always confident the 35-year-old would return to full fitness.

'It was a tough one for Craig at the time, a sore one, especially with his previous injury.

'He's been down and had a couple of reviews during his rehabilitation so it's all been progressing very well and he looks quick and strong again and ready to compete now.

'There's always a fright when you get injured and you're out for a period of time, but he got a great confidence booster a couple of years ago when he came back and trained. And the number of games he's played has given him great confidence.

'The injury happened and he's come back really strong now.' Leigh Griffiths, Tom Rogic, Stuart Armstrong and Patrick Roberts have all returned from lengthy injuries in recent weeks, with Kieran Tierney scheduled to restart training on Thursday.

Boss Brendan Rodgers is pleased to have key players available at thids stage of the season

Pleased to have key players available at an important stage of the season, Rodgers added: 'Some aren't totally match ready but the availability is very important. They're training well. The group is very competitive every day.

'Everyone is fighting for their position and that's probably what we've missed this season because we've been unfortunate with the number of injuries we've had, whereas last year we had hardly any.

'That makes the competition greater on a daily basis and then you have to be at your best to play. We saw that last weekend with the speed and energy and creativity in the team. If the starting XI is not quite working we have good options for change.'
