Harry Kane fires warning to rivals after returning from injury for Tottenham

Harry Kane has warned Tottenham’s top-four rivals that he is fitter and fresher than ever as the season reaches its climax.

The 35-goal marksman was back for Sunday’s 3-1 win at Chelsea - earlier than expected after just three weeks out with an ankle injury.

His return is a huge boost for Spurs with seven Premier League games to go and an FA Cup semi-final at Wembley on April 21.

Kane said: “Whenever something bad happens in football or life, you have to find the positives.

“For me, it was a chance to get a couple of weeks’ break, refresh the other muscles, and get ready for the business end of the season.

“That’s how I looked at it. I didn’t know how quickly I would be back, but I went away with one of the physios, did some rehab and I knew last week it was looking good and I would be able to step it up. Thankfully I was ready for the game.

“The swelling went down quickly, and whenever I get injured I prepare right and make sure I do everything I can to get the swelling down and get back on the pitch.

“Hopefully this time it was quicker than last time. I do the right things off the pitch, and I think that’s a big help.

“The injury was pretty much exactly the same time as last year, funnily enough. I was feeling strong last year and that’s what I’ll try to do this year.

“As a team, we’re in a great position for Champions League football, and we’ve just got to continue that.

“We’ve got big games coming up, so we’ve got to look forward to that.”

Kane has also warned Mohamed Salah that he is ready to push the Liverpool winger to the wire for the Golden Boot.

In Kane’s absence Salah has pulled five goals clear in the race to become the Premier League’s top scorer.

The Egyptian now has 29 top flight goals compared to Kane’s 24.

But Kane, who saw off Romelu Lukaku last season, is relishing his toughest battle yet.

He said: “Salah’s done fantastic, he’s a great great player. Yeah, it’s competition. I had the same last year.

“For me, I’ve got to focus on my own game and my own team, and we’ll see what happens come the end of the season.

“He’s obviously got a bit of a head start now, and we’ll see what happens. I can’t control what he does, so I’ve just got to make sure I prepare well for games and just be ready.”
