Photos reveal how Hurricane Irma helped carve a new 100 acre island

Scientists have discovered a 100-acre island off the coast of Georgia which was carved by last autumn's deadly Hurricane Irma.

The furious storm ripped through the Atlantic last September, leaving a trail of destruction behind.

And recently-recorded drone footage reveals that the strong winds - which reached up to 185mph - helped shift a sand bar to create a new isolated piece of land which has been christened Little Blackbeard Island.

This Google Earth photo shows how the island looked before the deadly storm ripped through the Atlantic last September, with a narrow strip of sand connecting it to another land mass

Researchers at the University of Georgia told FOX 13 News that the relentless gales blew out a strip of narrow land that once extended south from Blackbeard Island toward Sapelo Island.

In turn, a small island was formed but it is only now that aerial photos have shown this new land mass.

Dr Marguerite Madden, who is a professor at the University of Georgia and director of the Center for Geospatial Research, explained: 'The areas are very dynamic because it’s sandy.

'It’s not unusual. Storms can cause dramatic changes in a short amount of time.'

An aerial photo shows how the connecting strip now appears submerged by the Atlantic Ocean

Photographs taken by Dr Madden and one of her colleagues show that Little Blackbeard Island has sweeping sand beaches with a grassy outcrop and woodland area in its central region.

The scientists accidentally discovered it while researching vegetation in the area.

Previously, as shown by Google Earth images taken before Hurricane Irma hit, the sandy outcrop was connected to Blackbeard Island by a thin strip of land.

Fred Hay, who helps manage Sapelo Island for the state Department of Natural Resources’ Wildlife Resources Division Geologists, told Savannah Now that this 'bridge' of land had been eroding for decades but Irma accelerated the process.

The connecting strip now appears submerged by the Atlantic Ocean.

Blackbeard Island and Sapelo Island are both state-protected pieces of land and run as a nature and forest preserves.
