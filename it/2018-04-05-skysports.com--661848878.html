Andre Fabre eyes Craven meeting at Newmarket for Le Brivido

Last Updated: 05/04/18 5:06pm

Royal Ascot winner Le Brivido could return to Britain to make his long-awaited comeback in the Abernant Stakes at Newmarket on April 19.

Andre Fabre's charge won the Jersey Stakes last summer, but has not been seen in competitive action since after missing the rest of the season through injury.

The French trainer had identified the Prix Edmond Blanc at Saint-Cloud on Easter Monday as a likely comeback target, but decided against running Le Brivido due to the prevailing testing conditions and is now eyeing an outing at the Craven meeting.

The Coolmore team recently bought into the colt and earlier this week he received an entry in next month's Lockinge Stakes at Newbury.

Fabre said: "He wasn't ready to run in that sort of ground at Saint-Cloud.

"The next race for him (in France) is not until May 1, so the Abernant Stakes at Newmarket is a possible race for him.

"I am very happy with him."

Another Fabre inmate who could cross the Channel this spring is the exciting filly Wind Chimes.

The Coolmore-owned daughter of Mastercraftsman is a general 10-1 shot for the 1000 Guineas at Newmarket on May 6, having won each of her two starts as a juvenile at Chantilly.

"She was supposed to run at Deauville on Monday, but I think I might wait and run her in the Prix de la Grotte at Longchamp (April 15)," Fabre added.

"We will see how she runs. That (1000 Guineas) is possible."

Rabdan made a big impression when opening his account at the second attempt at Saint-Cloud and was put in the Investec Derby at Epsom at the second entry stage.

However, Fabre warns it would be dangerous to get carried away with the son of Frankel's visually striking performance.

He said: "It is hard to take the form seriously in those conditions, I think. I would be delighted if it was true, but we will see.

"He could run in the Prix Greffulhe (Saint-Cloud, May 8). That will tell us more."

Fabre was out of luck with his four runners on Dubai World Cup night at Meydan.

Cloth Of Stars fared best of the quartet, finishing third in the Dubai Sheema Classic despite pulling fiercely for his head for much of the mile-and-a-half journey.

"He got upset in the gates and pulled very hard during the race," said the trainer.

"He ran OK and has come home fine and is in all the big races.

"If everything is good he could go for the Prix Ganay at Longchamp (April 29)."

Talismanic failed to fire in the World Cup itself.

Fabre said: "I think it was a lack of experience in this type of race (that was the problem)."
