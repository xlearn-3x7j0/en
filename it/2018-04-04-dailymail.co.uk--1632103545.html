This plus-size male model wants to promote self-love and acceptance

A male plus-size model refuses body shamers stop him from pursuing his fashion dreams - and is proving that by showing off his 405lbs physique in a series of sensuous photos.

Arcadio Del Valle, 30, from Boston, Massachusetts, said he used to let his XXXXXL frame hold him back from exploring his personal style but has now gotten 9,765 followers on Instagram that love his body for what it is.  

And in a series of provocative photos, shared to his Instagram, the male model can be seen posing in a variety of racy outfits, while proudly putting on a show.

In one photo, Arcadio wears a black crop top, pants and jacket while making a sensual facial expression and in another picture, Arcadio poses on top of a bed while wearing a T-shirt and light pink shorts. 

But Arcadio wasn't always the confident model he is today.  

In an interview with Them Arcadio said that his biggest struggle when first starting out in the fashion industry was preparing to be the biggest in body size at auditions and knowing he had to prove himself ten times more because of that.

At the moment, he said he's not signed to an agency but he's working as hard as possible to make that happen.    

Surreal: He said the experience was 'surreal' and that his heart was pounding out of his chest the entire time

He said: 'I know there are people looking for someone like me to represent myself and my look. The plus-size industry doesn't always mean body positivity industry, or an inclusive industry.'

And that was never more prevalent that the time he arrived at at a show and though they requested him to walk, they had nothing for him to wear.

He said: 'None of it fit. I had to improvise and do the best I could.'  

And to make sure situations like this don't happen to other plus-size models, he said that 'we've got to keep talking and keep pursuing opportunities that will break those boundaries'. 

'We've got to be the ones who push for change with our voices and actions by way of our social platforms. If we stay quiet, the industry will never change. We're slowly making progress,' he told Them. 

 Finding his humility: Arcadio said that it has been 'humbling to find this confidence in myself'

 Work with it: He wants other people to embrace their flaws and 'work with it' 

Confident: Arcadio said he has walked for another plus-size fashion label Brandon Kyle, which he has transformed his confidence

He added that growing up in a world where media urges people to look a certain way, be a certain weight or fit into a mold become engraved in people's minds - and he knows the feeling.  

'I wasn't happy with myself growing up and in my early twenties.'

'But when I saw people like plus-sized model Tess Holliday loving their bodies and embracing fashion, it inspired me to start doing the same.'

He also said that he was inspired by Zach Mike, who came out as the first plus-size male model and to be signed with the major agency, IMG.

Two years ago, Arcadio walked the runway at New York Fashion Week for plus-size streetwear brand VOLARE and since then, he has walked in three major runway shows and another plus-size fashion label Brandon Kyle, which he said has transformed his confidence.   

Arcadio, who works full-time as a staff assistant, then began sharing photos of his outfits and style on Instagram, which got him a lot of positive feedback.

Working hard: At the moment, Arcadio is not signed to an agency but he is working hard to change that

Empowering message: His message is that 'if you look a certain way or are a certain size, you can still love fashion and do anything you want'

But despite the positive feedback, he still gets attacked by trolls who leave nasty messages about his weight.

'They criticize my body shape and size and the fact that I wear makeup. They say I shouldn't expose so much of my body because I'm big.'

'They say I am promoting obesity and that being unhealthy is acceptable and that is not the case at all.'

He said his whole message is that even if 'you look a certain way or are a certain size, you can still love fashion and you can do anything you want. It's been humbling to find this confidence in myself.'

'All I want to do is to promote self-love and acceptance as you are, regardless your size, gender or disability.'

'My point is for you to take those flaws and use it to help others embrace it and work with it.'

'In my ideal world high-end brands from Gucci to Louis Vuitton, and high street stores like Old Navy and Gap would make clothes for people my size and finally include us in fashion.'

'There is a market out there if they just open their eyes. Bigger guys love fashion too. I just want everyone to be able to express themselves.'
