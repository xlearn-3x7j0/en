Georgia woman admits posting video depicting school threat

Police in Georgia have arrested a 55-year-old YouTube knitting instructor after she allegedly posted an expletive-laden video online that depicted a shooting threat at a Savannah elementary school.

The video, titled 'Shooting Godley Station School with Machine Gun Adventures Street View,' showed a street view video of the school grounds while machine gun fire played in the background. 

Teresa Richardson, who admitted to creating the video as an expression of her right to free speech, can be seen in the video giving the middle finger. 

YouTube star jailed: Teresa Richardson, 55, a popular YouTube crocheting instructor, has been accused of posting  a video that depicted a shooting threat at a Gerogia elementary school.

The video showed a street view of the school grounds at Godley Station Elementary School in Savannah (pictured) while machine gun fire played in the background

The video also featured obscene language aimed at teachers and students at the school.

After an investigation, officers arrested Richardson on a charge of dissemination of acts related to terroristic threats, which makes it illegal for anyone to use a computer network to disseminate any terroristic threat. 

'We take these threats seriously, and as soon as we learned about them, we immediately assigned investigators to investigate these threats,' said Savannah Police Chief Mark Revenew. 'Anyone who makes threats to our schools and the potential safety of them will be investigated thoroughly and dealt with appropriately.'

The video appears to have been a departure for Richardson, who is best known for making instructional YouTube videos teaching her followers to knit and crochet.

Richardson, who goes by the moniker 'Crochet Geek,' has more than 369,000 subscribes on YouTube and has uploaded nearly 7,300 videos that have drawn 119million views since 2006.

She sells her handmade products online and writes a blog about crocheting. In 2011, she was featured in a Forbes Magazine multi-part series focusing on some of YouTube's most successful entrepreneurs to date.

The story revealed that Richardson a the time was a divorced mother of two grown sons, both of whom have followed their father into military service. It appears that She has since remarried. 

Following her arrest on Friday, the 55-year-old crochet tutor has been freed.

Richardson's YouTube channel has been dormant for the past three days, but she has been using Twitter to rail against perceived freedom of speech violations, along with describing the cars that drive by her window, listing all the electronic devices that in her opinion are 'Read by Artificial intelligence' and touting the merits of CGI technology. 

In the immediate aftermath of her release from jail, Richardson fired off a string of tweets addressing her legal troubles.

'My civil rights have been violated, since I was arrested for posting videos online, on youtube, similar to gaming videos,' she wrote. 'I posted a video on youtube using keywords based on popular topics. creating a screen recording similar to gaming videos. I'm currently not using the words because social media is being used to collect data on private citizens thru youtube & facebook.'

In another angry tweet, she lamented: 'I'm no Rosa Parks, but I understand the mission with Freedom of Speech, & protecting American citizens from surveillance related to Social Media. WE should be able to speak freely on Social Media without fear, or do we truly have FREEDOM of SPEECH & first amendment rights?!'
