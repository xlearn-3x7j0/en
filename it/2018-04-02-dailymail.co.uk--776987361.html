Alan Pardew leaves West Brom after winning just one league game

  • Alan Pardew took charge of West Brom in November following Tony Pulis exit
  • But ex-Crystal Palace boss has been unable to steer West Brom clear of trouble
  • Pardew mananged just one victory in 18 Premier League matches at the club
  • First-team coach Darren Moore has taken temporary charge at the Hawthorns

Published: 06:39 EDT, 2 April 2018 | Updated: 06:44 EDT, 2 April 2018

Alan Pardew's disastrous spell as West Brom manager has come to an end after the club announced his departure on Monday morning. 

Pardew was named as Tony Pulis' successor in November but was unable to steer West Brom clear of trouble, winning just one of his 18 league games in charge.

First-team coach Darren Moore has taken temporary charge with the club bottom of the table and 10 points from safety with only six matches remaining.
