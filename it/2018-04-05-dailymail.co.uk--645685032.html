Australian cricket will be holding its breath until Warner accepts ban

  • COMMENT: David Warner has yet to publicly accept his Cricket Australia ban
  • Both Steve Smith and Cameron Bancroft will not appeal their sanctions
  • They can begin the healing process, but Warner's silence has authorities nervous

Published: 20:04 EDT, 4 April 2018 | Updated: 20:04 EDT, 4 April 2018

Australian cricket will be holding its breath until David Warner follows the example set by Steve Smith and Cameron Bancroft and accepts the ban imposed by Cricket Australia for his part in the cheating controversy.

While Smith and Bancroft can now begin their own healing process, Warner's silence will leave the authorities nervous - just as his scripted press conference did on Saturday.

Two questions above all hang in the air. Was this the first time Australia had tampered with the ball? And did only three players know about the sandpaper plan? 

David Warner has yet to follow Steve Smith and Cameron Bancroft in accepting his ban

Asked both during his press conference, Warner hid behind repetitive versions of his own remorse. He was offered the chance to ease concerns, and failed to take it.

The worst-case scenario for the Australian game is that Warner, sensing he is being cut adrift, will decide to take others down with him. 

At best, he may simply challenge a ruling which has made clear he is no longer regarded as leadership material.

Either way, Australian cricket will not be able to move on until their former vice-captain has explained his position. The fallout from the scandal is not over.

Smith has revealed on Twitter that he won't be appealing his one-year cricket ban

Bancroft received a nine-month ban following a Cricket Australia investigation
