The 7 worst Premier League managerial reigns ever

Oh, Alan.

West Brom's 2-1 defeat at home to Burnley on Saturday was the last straw for the club's much-criticised owners, who acted swiftly and sacked manager Alan Pardew after a disastrous four months in the job.

Pardew leaves the Baggies rock bottom of the table, 10 points from safety and with just six games left.

So they're down, basically, and plenty have wondered just why they didn't wield the axe sooner following 12 defeats in Pardew's 18 league games as manager.

It was a shocking run of form, and one that can only be 'bettered' by six full-time bosses in Premier League history when it comes to the least amount of points won per game.

Although they did achieve better results at other times, between them these seven stints brought just four Premier League wins and 36 points.

Remember these shockers?

7. Alan Pardew (West Brom, 2017-18) - 0.44 points per game

P18, W1, D5, L12 - Points: 8

Appointed in November to replace a Tony Pulis who had apparently lost his mojo, Pardew only oversaw one league victory - against Brighton in January - as his side slipped towards certain relegation.

6. Billy Davies (Derby, 2007) - 0.43 points per game

P14, W1, D3, L10 - Points: 6

After achieving promotion against the odds, Davies steered Derby into the 2007/08 season, where they promptly went off the rails.

He was sacked after 14 games, of which they lost 10, but on the plus side he was in charge for their one victory of the season against Newcastle.

5. Ian Holloway (Crystal Palace, 2013) - 0.38 points per game

P8, W1, D0, L7 - Points: 3

After another unexpected promotion, 'Ollie' astonishingly signed 18 players to prepare for life in the Premier League, but he only oversaw them in eight matches.

Palace lost seven of them, with their only win coming at home to a Sunderland side who would soon sack Paolo Di Canio.

4. Terry Connor (Wolves, 2012) - 0.31 points per game

P13, W0, D4, L9 - Points: 4

An unlucky 13 for Connor, who was given the Wolves reins at the tail end of the 2011/12 season after the soon-to-be-seen Mick McCarthy - for whom Connor remains a faithful No. 2 - got the boot.

On the plus side he did get four draws, but they lost the other nine and Wolves went down.

3. Mick McCarthy (Sunderland, 2003 & 2005-06) - 0.27 points per game

P37, W2, D4, L31 - Points: 10

Now Big Mick is a fine boss, but he was to emphatically find out that Sunderland and the Premier League just don't mix over two spells in the division.

Appointed at the tail end of 2002/03 with the club on the way to relegation, he'd return to the top-flight with them in 2005, but won just twice in 37 games.

2. Paul Jewell (Derby, 2007-08) - 0.21 points per game

P24, W0, D5, L19 - Points: 5

Taking over from Billy Davies, Jewell wasn't able to turn around that shipwreck that was Derby County's 2007/08, in which they set the Premier League record low points tally of 11.

Jewell got five of those, drawing with Newcastle, Manchester City, Birmingham, Sunderland and Fulham.

1. Frank De Boer (Crystal Palace, 2017) - 0 points per game

P4, W0, D0, L4 - Points: 0

Yes, it was only four games, but De Boer's spell at Crystal Palace at the start of this season takes the prize for incompetence, with the Dutchman sacked after failing to score a goal or get a point.

The axe fell - probably a bit early - after losses to Huddersfield (3-0), Liverpool (1-0), Swansea (2-0) and Burnley (1-0), making De Boer an unwanted footnote in Premier League history.
