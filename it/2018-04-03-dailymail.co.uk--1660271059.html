Tottenham chief Levy's pay packet up £3.2m to £6m over last year

Tottenham chairman Daniel Levy has become highest paid chairman in the Premier League after his annual pay packet soared to £6million as the club benefited from record revenues.

Levy’s gross salary went up by £3.2m, according to the club’s official accounts, as he hailed an 'historic period for the club'.

His total £6m wage equates to £115,000 per week - more than Harry Kane, who earns a basic salary of £110,000-a-week.  

Chairman Daniel Levy saw his annual pay packet increase from £2.8m to £6m over the last year

The club made £306.3m in total revenue last season after the club competed in the Champions League for the first time since 2011. 

The return is an increase of almost £100m from the 2016 revenue of £209.8m, with the club also seeing a rise in television, sponsorship and corporate hospitality, and merchandising income.

It resulted in profits for the year of £41.2m, up from £33m in 2015-16.

The latest set of figures cover 2016-17, Tottenham's final season at White Hart Lane

Levy said: 'Extraordinary levels of financial and human resource have seen significant progress on capital projects, with schemes underway at both the new stadium site and our Training Centre.

'As custodians of the Club we are ever-conscious of the need to ensure the future stability of the Club whilst managing its growth.

'We are in an historic period for the Club and there is a growing sense of excitement. There will, however, be many challenges in the coming months as we near the latter stages of the construction of the new stadium and its opening.

'The drive and determination to deliver our best across all areas of the Club, together with the unity and support of all involved, will, I believe, see us meet those challenges.'

Spurs are set to move into their new 61,559-capacity stadium at the start of next season

Revenue from television and media went up from £94.8m to £149.8m as the record Premier League broadcasting deal kicked in.

Sponsorship and corporate hospitality revenue increased from £48.8m to £54.7m, with merchandising revenue going up £2m to £14m.

Tottenham are expected to benefit from increased revenues again once they move into their new 62,000-seater stadium this summer.
