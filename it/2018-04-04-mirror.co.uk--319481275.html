Barcelona vs Roma live score and goal updates - Champions League latest

Barcelona have not won the Champions League since their 2015 success in Berlin against Juventus.

The runaway La Liga leaders are favourites to win this year's competition - but must beat Italian opposition first.

Step forward Roma, who conquered Shakhtar Donetsk on away goals to reach the last eight.

The Serie A giants are the underdogs heading to the Nou Camp but will be determined to stay in the tie before the return leg at the Stadio Olimpico.

So, will Lionel Messi and Co run riot - or will the 'Lionel Messi of goalkeepers' Alisson keep the hosts at bay?

Fans in the UK who are BT Sport subscribers can watch a live stream of the action via the broadcaster's digital app.

But you can follow all the drama as it happens at the Nou Camp right here with Mirror Football.

15:34

Early Team News - Roma

AS Roma’s attacking midfielder Cengiz Under will miss Wednesday’s Champions League game at Barcelona because of injury, but Radja Nainggolan will make the trip.

Under, whose five league goals this season have all come since the winter break, suffered a thigh injury while he was with Turkey during the international week.

Nainggolan limped off during the first half of Roma’s 1-1 draw at Bologna on Saturday, but Roma said the combative Belgian had been included in the 21-man squad for the quarter-final first leg.

15:30

Early team news - Barcelona

Sergio Busquets is back in Barca training ahead of the clash having missed games against Athletic Bilbao and Sevilla with a broken foot.

He is likely to return straight into the starting XI, despite Ivan Rakitic impressing as deputy.

Lucas Digne is Barca’s only star definitely missing the clash at the Nou Camp.

15:29

Valverde hoping to be too much for Roma

Barcelona coach Ernesto Valverde is hoping the combination of Lionel Messi’s expected return and a passionate Nou Camp crowd will be enough to overpower Roma in the quarter-finals of the Champions League.

Barca host the first leg of the last-eight tie on Wednesday and are likely to have their star man back in the starting XI following hamstring trouble.

Messi suffered a niggle on international duty with Argentina and began on the bench for Saturday’s visit to Sevilla.

Even that could not stop him locating the spotlight, grabbing an 89th-minute equaliser after stepping off the bench at 2-0 down.

“Messi is unique in the world. Unique in the history of football,” Valverde told a press conference.
