Cardiff captain mocks size of Derby player's manhood in shocking taxi rant

Cardiff captain Sean Morrison has been filmed mocking the size of one Derby County player's manhood and branding others "s***" and "an embarrassment" in an expletive-filled rant.

The two sides, both of whom remain in the promotion picture in the Championship, are due to meet at the iPro Stadium on April 24.

Ahead of the match, Morrison appears to have launched a foul-mouthed tirade at Derby counterpart Richard Keogh and teammates Alex Pearce and Jason Shackell, who is now on loan at Millwall.

The clip that has emerged on Twitter shows Morrison, seemingly in the back of a taxi, making the offensive slurs.

"Alright Richard, Richie. Just want to say mate, you are a mug," he said - seemingly aimed at Keogh.

"'Give me the ball, give me the ball, I really want the ball' - then you just run as fast as you can 10 yards then just cut inside to the other centre-half because you are s*** at football.

"Pearcey is up your a***. Alex Pearce is so far up your a*** it is an embarrasment.

"Shackell as well, he doesn't show his d***. Little insecure c***. Little tiny d***, little tiny willy.

"And you are all scared of playing us. See you in a couple of weeks you bell."

Cardiff are currently second in the Championship and Derby fifth.

The clash later this month is likely to be a season-defining encounter.

It was originally scheduled for last month before being postponed because of heavy snow.

However, there was some controversy with Cardiff boss Neil Warnock brandishing the decision a disgrace before suggesting it was due to Derby having another of injury problems in their squad.

After the original postponement, Warnock said: "We got to the ground and the car park was clear, the pitch was perfect. Absolutely disgraceful really.

"All week I have listened to comments from Derby, they have all been negative, and that's before the snow came down. They have been talking about the injury situation. Things like that should be looked into."

Derby boss Gary Rowett hit back at Warnock, joking he didn't know the Cardiff chief was a "health and safety" officer.
