Watch as Jack Nicklaus' 15-year-old grandson hits hole-in-one at Augusta

Jack Nicklaus hailed a "special" day at Augusta after witnessing his 15-year-old grandson hit his first ever hole-in-one at The Masters Par 3 competition.

The traditional curtain raiser for the first major of the year at Augusta was won by 68-year-old Tom Watson, but it was a non-professional, at the other end of his golfing life, who took all the headlines.

Gary Nicklaus, 15, one of the Golden Bear's 22 grandchildren was caddying for his 78-year-old grandfather.

The 18-time major winner allowed his grandson to step away from the bag and hit the shot of a lifetime - topping, Nicklaus said, any moment he had experienced before at Augusta.

Quite the statement, given his six Green Jackets during his illustrious career.

Nicklaus tweeted: "With all due respect to The Masters, allow me to put my 6 Green Jackets in the closet for a moment and say that I don’t know if I have had a more special day on a golf course.

"To have your grandson make his first hole-in-one on this stage.... WOW! #Family #memoryofalifetime."

The tournament begins on Thursday with the field wide open and Sergio Garcia returning as champion after capturing his first major last year.

Tiger Woods fever has taken golf over again over the last few weeks and Rory McIlroy is primed to complete the career Grand Slam if he can clinch a win this week.
