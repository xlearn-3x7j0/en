EFL announce Championship Player of the Year contenders and Team of the Year

The EFL has announced the contenders for the 2017/18 end-of-season awards.

The three-man shortlists have been revealed for the Player of the Season prizes in the Championship, League One and League Two.

All 72 managers were invited to vote for their players of the season, which compiled each division's Team of the Year.

Cardiff boss Neil Warnock, whose side are second in the Championship, has scooped the Manager of the Year prize, with Shrewsbury's Paul Hurst and John Coleman of Accrington Stanley winning the awards in Leagues One and Two respectively.

Here are the key awards and shortlists listed by the EFL ahead of the ceremony on Sunday April 15.
