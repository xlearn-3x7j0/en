Barbara Bush gives a rare insight into her 72 year marriage

Former First Lady Barbara Bush has shared intimate details about her relationship with George H.W. Bush in a romantic essay penned for her alma mater's magazine. 

Writing for Smith College's Alumnae Quarterly magazine, the 92-year-old who has been married to the former POTUS for seven decades revealed she's 'still old and still in love with the man I married 72 years ago.'

Bush dropped out of Smith in 1944 and married two weeks later, however the school awarded her an honorary degree in 1989.

As reported by The Boston Globe, the former FLOTUS joined nine other women from the class of 1947 to provide updates for the magazine.

A full-page photograph of Mrs Bush, sitting in a garden in Kennebunkport, Maine, also appears in the publication.

It accompanies a quote which reads: 'I have had great medical care and more operations than you would believe. I'm not sure God will recognize me; I have so many new body parts!'

She also praised her husband, adding: 'Also, George Bush has given me the world. He is the best — thoughtful and loving.' 

The couple served as President and First Lady from January 1989 until January 1993. 

Although she didn't mention her five children or 17 grandchildren by name, the mom and grandmother shared that she is 'proud' that all of her children are working and 'serving others in their own way.'

Long marriages run in the family - eldest son George W has been married to his wife Laura since 1977, second son Jeb has been married to his wife Columba since 1974, and youngest son Marvin and his wife Margaret walked down the aisle in 1981. 

Bush has sent occasional updates to the alumnae publication, over the years, the first going back to 1953 when it reported that her second son, Jeb, had been born. 

The publication listed him as John as he was baptized John Ellis Bush.

And in November 1972, the native New Yorker gave an insight into the family's life in New York City, where she lived with her husband while he served as US ambassador to the United Nations.

'The family of five children find living at the Waldorf rather formal after their life in Texas but they enjoy taking other embassy children to the ball games, have "Americanized" the apt. by hanging only US artists, serving local wines and buying only American-made clothing,' she wrote. 

In 2015, she penned another note, this time hinting at her 93-year-old husband's ailing health and medical troubles.

'My precious George has been in the hospital and out, thank God. We have the best five children in the world — all giving and caring individuals,' she wrote. 

'Being almost 90 is tough, as many of you know, but with a loving, close family, almost anything can be survived.'

Part of the 'Seven Sisters', Smith is a private, independent women's liberal arts college located in Northampton, Massachusetts and counts the author Sylvia Plath, activist and writer Gloria Steinem and Yolanda King, daughter of Martin Luther King as alumnae. 

Former First Lady Nancy Reagan also attended the college, graduating in 1943.
