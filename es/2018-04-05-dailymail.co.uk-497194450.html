Boy, 6, is hit by falling street sign outside Penn Station in New York

A six-year-old boy has been hospitalized after he was struck by a falling sign near Penn Station on Wednesday afternoon.

He was hit while on the corner of West 32nd Street and Seventh Avenue at around 4.20pm, according to police.

The boy was knocked unconscious but eventually came to and was rushed to Bellevue Hospital where he was reported to be in serious but stable condition, reported NBC 4.

Authorities have not revealed what caused the sign to fall but, according to Weather Underground, wind gusts of 40mph were recorded at the time in the area. 

'It was very, very windy,' Andrew Smails, a vendor, told the news station. 'I just saw the sign come blowing down, and I saw the child get hit and knocked to the ground.'

'The flags were really, really going mad and flapping,' Andrew Southern, of Jersey City, who was across the street when the sign fell, told the New York Daily News. 

'It was really, really windy. And people stopped to help him, which was good, I thought.'

Video taken of the scene and shared on the Citizen App showed at least two ambulances and a fire truck outside Penn Station as a crowd consisting of emergency responders and pedestrians surrounded the boy.
