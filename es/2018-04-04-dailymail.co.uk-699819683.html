LA Galaxy technical director: Ibrahimovic rejected £70m China deal

Zlatan Ibrahimovic turned down a deal worth almost £70million-per-year from Chinese Super League clubs to play for LA Galaxy, the club's technical director Jovan Kirovski has revealed.

The Swede joined the MLS side at the end of March on a free-transfer after he and former employers Manchester United agreed to terminate his contract three months early.

Shortly after his move, it was revealed that the 36-year-old had accepted a 95 per cent pay cut to join the Galaxy but, according to Sports Illustrated, he also turned down a move to China which would have seen his salary dramatically increased to $100million-per-year (£69m).

Zlatan Ibrahimovic made an instant impact on his LA Galaxy debut by scoring two goals

Kirovski, who also played for United and the Galaxy during his 21-year playing career, explained that the hardest part of negotiating a deal was explaining to Ibra's super-agent, Mino Raiola, why his client would have to accept a contract worth $1.5million-per-year (£850,000).

He said: 'When he signed officially was two weeks ago. Then you can breathe again.' 

Ibrahimovic, whose contract at Old Trafford was worth approximately £19million-a-year, was forced to take such a large pay cut after he was declared an MLS TAM (Target Allocation Money) player.

TAM PLAYERS 

A TAM (Target Allocation Money) player is someone who has been brought an MLS club whose previous wage package exceeds the league's Maximum Salary Budget Charge.

TAM funds are distributed to MLS clubs who can use the funds for a number of different reasons, including: 

  • To sign or re-sign an existing player
  • Converting a designated player to non-designated by buying down their salary budget charge
  • Using up to $200,000 to sign new, homegrown players to their first MLS contract

A TAM player is someone that has been bought by an MLS club, whose previous salary package exceeded the maximum wage restrictions put in place by the league.

This means that due to Ibrahimovic's lofty salary package at United, the Galaxy are only able to offer him the maximum of $1.5m-per-year.

And it was money well spent clearly, after the Galaxy were rescued by their new star man when Ibrahimovic came off the bench on his debut to score two late goals and secure an unlikely 4-3 victory.

Trailing by three goals at their StubHub Centre home to Los Angeles FC, Galaxy had pulled to goals back before Ibrahimovic announced himself with a wonder-goal almost immediately after being introduced, before heading in a stoppage-time winner.

'I'm still numb,' Kirovski said. 'Especially for us. With LAFC coming, and being down 3-0, it's like: What? It was an emotional roller coaster. This was special.

'He shows up and walks the walk. It's incredible.'
