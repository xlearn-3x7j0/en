Zlatan Ibrahimovic's Man United departure was sad, says Jose Mourinho

Jose Mourinho has paid tribute to Zlatan Ibrahimovic after the Swedish star cut short his contract at Manchester United to join LA Galaxy.

The move to Major League Soccer effectively brings down the curtain on Ibrahimovic's top-flight career after spells with Ajax, Barcelona, Paris Saint-Germain and both Milan clubs.

The 36-year-old scored 28 goals in his first season at Old Trafford, helping United win the Europa League, League Cup and Community Shield, but never truly recovered from a ruptured cruciate ligament in his right knee.

Mourinho was speaking on Friday ahead of United's Premier League clash with Swansea

Ibrahimovic was given a hero's welcome as he touched down in Los Angeles on Thursday night

The Swedish star landed at the airport late on Thursday after signing for LA Galaxy 

Ibrahimovic took time out to speak with the LA Galaxy fans and pose for pictures with them

He will now begin his contract with the club and could make his MLS debut this Saturday

Ibrahimovic made only seven appearances after returning to action in November, and his move to the Galaxy had been anticipated for some time.

Mourinho said: 'For me, it's always sad when the big players go in the direction of the end. I remember, for example, (Luis) Figo's last match with me at Inter, and I always remember tha as one of my saddest moments because I always feel very sorry when the big ones finish or go to the last period of their careers.

'Zlatan's goodbye moment was more in that direction. He's a huge, big player that top football, European football, is losing now and forever because he will not be back to this level of football.

'But fantastic for him in this period. It's a fantastic way not to go from being a player to being a former player, this period with Galaxy. These couple of years will be very good for him and American football.

'What he can do – teach, attract, his personality, passion for football he's always had since his recovery – was an example of what a professional should be is more the MLS and US as a football country is going to get, what more is Zlatan is going to get, will be just a way for him to enjoy the last couple of years of his football.'
