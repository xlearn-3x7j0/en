Kevin De Bruyne thinks he is the Premier League Player of the Year

Manchester City's Kevin De Bruyne believes he would be a worthy recipient of the Player of the Year award but knows he has hot competition from Liverpool's Mohamed Salah.

City's Belgium midfielder has looked nailed on to pick up individual honours from the Professional Footballers' Association and Football Writers' Association as he drives Pep Guardiola's team towards the Premier League title with some incredible football.

But Salah refuses to step away from the contest and his winning goal for Liverpool at Crystal Palace on Saturday took his individual tally to 29 Premier League goals.

Kevin De Bruyne has been a leading contender for the Premier League Player of the Year

On Wednesday at Anfield, the two will come face to face when Liverpool host City in the Champions League quarter-final first leg and De Bruyne said: 'Well, if I get it, it would be nice, I think, for the team and for me.

'In one way I believe I deserve it, because I have been very consistent in my form. I am very happy with myself, I am very pleased with how I have played and I didn't expect to be this good this season to be honest.

'There is almost no game where my standard has dropped, so I have been very constant. To maintain the level in the amount of games I have played, the amount of minutes, I am very pleased with that.'

De Bruyne knows that he has a worthy challenger in Salah, the Egypt forward who arrived at Liverpool last summer from Roma for £34million.

'You cannot compare, it is impossible,' De Bruyne said with a smile. 'Everybody has his own opinion. It depends on what people are looking for in a player.

'Salah has been on a remarkable goalscoring run and he plays very well in an inside forward type of role. This season he has been incredible, so to compare us, I don't know.

'With the statistics, how are we going to compare ourselves? It's totally different.

'I played at the weekend like a defensive midfielder, so how you can compare me to an inside forward? I have no idea.

'But Salah has been outstanding, his record speaks for itself. It's just the way it goes. Luckily I don't have to make the decision.'

City go to Anfield on Wednesday knowing that Liverpool are the only team to have beaten them in the league.

Much has been made of the atmosphere at Anfield on European nights but De Bruyne doubts that will be a factor. 'I don't think so,' he said. 'Obviously they have their style of play and to play there is tough.

'But apart from those 10 minutes when we conceded goals last time, we really did ok. It was not the best game we have played, but I don't think there were lots of periods when they were in control.

'Those 10 minutes, they changed the whole face of the game. If they score, you know it is going to get a bit more heated than if we score! It's just the way football goes.

'You saw it against Everton at the weekend. They have a good crowd but if you are three goals ahead, then it is really difficult for them to get into it. Let's hope we can do the same at Anfield.

'Liverpool play the full press, so it will probably be very different from some of our recent games.

'It's the Champions League, the quarter-final, it is all or nothing. You have two games, so hopefully we will go there, do our thing and have a good game.'

City go straight from tomorrow night's game into Saturday's home match against Manchester United. If they win that they will be crowned Premier League champions.

'Well, these are the games you play the whole season for, eh?' said De Bruyne.

'You try to do well the first six, seven, eight months to be where we are now. We are going to relish this position.'
