Boy, 5, killed brother, 7, after finding gun while looking for candy

A seven-year-old boy has been shot dead by his five-year-old brother who allegedly found his father's handgun while scouring the family's St Louis home in search of Easter candy.

Police on Monday identified the victim in Saturday's shooting as Jermon Perry. They say they're still looking into the circumstance surrounding the boy's death, and no charges have been filed.

A preliminary investigation shows that at around 2.15pm on Saturday, Jermon, his twin brother and their younger sibling were upstairs in their home in the 3100 block of California Avenue, while adults including their parents, were downstairs.

Police believe Perry's five-year-old brother retrieved a loaded gun and pulled the trigger, firing a round into Jermon's head while the child was playing a video game in his bedroom.  

Erica Jones, a family spokeswoman, told the St. Louis Post-Dispatch when the boys' mother, 37-year-old Michelle Lawson, heard the shot, she ran upstairs and found Jermon, known to his family as 'MonMon,' bleeding in a a chair.

He was rushed to a hospital but doctors were unable to save him and he was pronounced dead at around 10pm, reported KMOV.

Erica Jones, a family friend, said the younger boy found his father's gun in a drawer while looking for candy, and did not know it was real

Twin: Jermon, known as 'MonMon,' was struck by a bullet in the head while playing a video game in his room, where his twin brother was also present

Jones said relatives believe the younger brother was looking for candy and didn't know the difference between a toy and real gun. 

The loaded firearm belonged to the boys' father, 39-year-old Jerico Perry, who has a conceal carry permit. The gun was being stored in a holster in an unlocked dresser drawer.

'He never thought the kids would get to the gun,' Jones said.

Jones, whose own daughter was killed in a drive-by shooting in 2015 and left behind a young child, told the paper Jermon's siblings do not understand that he has died and are still waiting for him to come home from the hospital.

On top of losing her seven-year-old son, Michelle Lawson is battling breast cancer for a second time: she had a double mastectomy in 2014 but the illness has since returned.

Jones has launched a GoFundMe campaign to help Lawson and Perry with their son's funeral expenses, writing in the description that the first-grader 'was a charmer who loved life & family he was always giving love to the women of the Family & playing with his twin brother & youngest brother.'
