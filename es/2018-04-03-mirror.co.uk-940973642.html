All you need to know about Ronda Rousey including net worth and WWE plans

Ronda Rousey is set to bolster her net worth after signing a huge deal with WWE.

The former UFC champion will officially sign her contract with the Raw brand at the Elimination Chamber pay-per-view on Sunday night.

After making her debut appearance at the Royal Rumble, she will likely find out her opponent for Wrestlemania when she puts pen to paper on the deal.

After spending a career as the face of MMA's most popular brand, Rousey has amassed a multi-million pound empire.

Here is all you need to know about sport entertainment's newest star...

What is Rousey's net worth?

After earning millions upon millions of pounds in the UFC Octagon, Rousey has a net worth of approximately $12million, according to Celebrity Net Worth.

She took home $3million from one fight alone, when she made her long-awaited comeback to face Amanda Nunes at UFC 207 in December 2016.

What is Rousey's WWE salary?

It is not yet known how much money Rousey is earning from WWE.

However, as she will take her place as one of the company's marquee names, she can be guaranteed a multi-million pound deal.

Forbes claim that John Cena, who has been one of WWE's top names for well over a decade, earns around $8m-a-year.

Brock Lesnar, another former UFC star, has been claimed to take home 12m after bonuses.

Dolph Ziggler, who has been a member of WWE for 13-years, earns $1.5million.

Who will Rousey face at Wrestlemania?

While a match has not yet been confirmed, there has been speculation as to what sort of a match Rousey could take part in.

According to Justin Barrasso of Sports Illustrated Rousey is currently working on a stunning superstar mixed tag team match at WWE's showcase event in April.

Rousey has listed The Rock, Kurt Angle, Shane McMahon or Seth Rollins as her order of preference for the star studded battle.

The Rock is unlikely to be available due to his film contracts preventing him from taking on work or activities which would include contact or a high-risk of injury.

Therefore Kurt Angle is set to team up with newbie Rousey.

The pair are expected to fight Stephanie McMahon, who stared down Rousey during her appearance at the Royal Rumble, and Triple H.

What has Rousey said about joining WWE?

Rousey told ESPN: “This is my life now. First priority on my timeline for the next several years. This is not a smash-and-grab; this is not a publicity stunt.

“When I first met with Triple H, I told him, ‘There are other things I can do with my time that’ll make way more money, but I won’t enjoy nearly as much.'

“It’s funny — it’s kind of like acting, in that it was something I always wanted to do but I never thought was in the cards for me.

“And now that I realize I really do have this opportunity, I feel like my 6-year-old self would totally kick my ass if I didn’t take it.”
