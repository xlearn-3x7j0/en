What time is Manchester United vs Swansea? Team news, betting and more

Manchester United will return from the international break looking to consolidate second place in the Premier League table.

The Red Devils have won seven out of their last nine games but face the prospect of arch rivals Manchester City winning the title in the derby next week, no matter what the result at Old Trafford tomorrow.

Even with a game in hand the club are just two points ahead of Liverpool, and with Chelsea facing Tottenham Hotspur at Stamford Bridge, will no doubt understand the significance of the game.

Swansea City meanwhile sit 14th, just three points outside the relegation zone in what is becoming one of the tightest races to stay up in years.

Yet under Carlos Carvalhal the Swans have lost just twice, while big wins over Liverpool and Arsenal will bring hope to fans come the final few months of the season.

Where and when is the game?

The game is on Saturday, March 31 at Old Trafford- kick off is at 3:00pm.

What channel is the game on?

The match isn’t live on TV but you can follow all the action at Mirror Football.

Team news and squads

Manchester United's Marouane Fellaini is set to start in place of Scott McTominay.

Paul Pogba could also feature after netting for France in an international friendly - with Ashley Young expected to shake off a knock he got on England duty.

Swansea have no fresh injury worries. Andy King and Andre Ayew are both expected to return to the starting line-up after being ineligible for the FA Cup defeat to Tottenham two weeks ago.

Jordan Ayew serves the second game of a three-match ban, while Renato Sanches (hamstring) and Angel Rangel (groin) remain out.

Provisional squad: Fabianski, Naughton, Van der Hoorn, Fernandez, Mawson, Olsson, Ki, Carroll, King, Clucas, A Ayew, Nordfeldt, Roberts, Bartley, Britton, Dyer, Narsingh, Routledge, Abraham, James, Byers.
