Kellyanne Conway bashes author's claim she's Trump's no.1 press leaker

Kellyanne Conway is denying that she's a prolific leaker, a bombshell claim made Sunday by author Ronald Kessler, whose new book about the Trump White House will drop a new bomb on Washington on Tuesday.

And she threw a sharp elbow at former Trump strategist Steve Bannon in the process, suggesting he was a loose-lipped provocateur while he served in the administration.

Kessler said on CNN that 'if you wonder why there are so many leaks out of the White House, one reason is Kellyanne Conway is the number one leaker.'

But Conway said history will eventually show others at 1600 Pennsylvania Avenue filled reporters' heads with tales of intrigue during Trump's first year in office.

Kellyanne Conway pushed back Monday against an author's claim that she has been the number one leaker' in the Trump White House

Ronald Kessler, whose new book on the Trump White House comes out on Tuesday, said Sunday on CNN that Conway has openly trash-talked Ivanka Trump and Jared Kushner

Conway threw suspicion on Steve Bannon, the president's former strategist, saying his public statements slamming the political media meant no one would ever suspect him of leaking

'We learned a long time ago around here that people who say, "Enemy of the people! Opposition party. Fake news! Biased media!" – they can talk to the media all day long because nobody would suspect them,' she said on 'Fox & Friends.'

Bannon, who left the White House last August, famously labeled America's political media 'the opposition party.'

Kessler's latest book examines President Trump's presidency through a mostly friendly set of eyes

But it was President Trump himself who branded the White House press corps 'the enemy of the American people,' catching no end of grief over it, more than a year ago.

And Trump has tweeted and spoken about 'fake news' more times than all but the most bored reporters would care to count. 

Kessler claimed on Sunday that Conway trash-talked Ivanka Trump and Jared Kushner during one conversation with him, 'saying that they leak against Steve Bannon.'

'And I know that White House aides have seen texts that she has sent to other journalists dissing her colleagues, leaking material,' the author added.

But Conway insisted Monday that she has 'a great working relationship and a great relationship with Jared and Ivanka. I had dinner with them recently at their house.' 

Conway told Fox correspondent Abby Huntsman that her phone lit up on Sunday with texts and emails saying: 'Wow, I feel so left out. You never tell me anything.'

'Leakers get great press,' she cautioned. 'And one day, Abby, I will have my say. That will be very, very fascinating.' 

President Trump, Conway said on the Fox News Channel, knows 'who the leakers and the liars are, and have been'

Kessler (left) told CNN that Conway said the most 'mean, cutting and obviously untrue things about Reince' Priebus, when she thought she and the author were speaking off-the-record

Kessler's book is titled 'The Trump White House: Changing the Rules of the Game.'

In addition to portraying Ivanka and her husband as Conway's gossip targets, he panned their decision-making skills.

The power-couple 'would push the most disastrous and foolish decisions of Trump's presidency ... They had no understanding of the basic fundamentals of how government works, how a campaign works, how politics works,' he wrote.

'Most of all, they had no understanding of the political consequences of their actions.'

Asked on CNN for examples, Kessler pointed to the May 2017 firing of FBI Director James Comey, which prompted Deputy Attorney General Rod Rosenstein to appoint Special Counsel Robert Mueller to handle the Russia probe. 

'Jared said, "Well, Democrats will love that. This is a political move",' Kessler explained. 

'In addition, they pushed hiring Anthony Scaramucci,' he said. 'That had to be the most absurd hire in the history of the White House.'

Kessler spoke much more highly of first lady Melania Trump's political prowess. 

'Her judgment is spectacular,' he gushed, explaining to Tapper that she was the individual who got Trump to run for president. 

'He was sort of pussy-footing around about running and she said, "You have to declare your candidacy",' Kessler said. 'And that's what happened and he went up in the polls.'
