Sexual harassment classes introduced for children after complaints

A North Bay school district has introduced sexual harassment classes after receiving complaints from teachers regarding their students.

Starting April, schools in the Tamalpais Union High School District in Marin County, California will make sure sexual harassment classes are mandatory for students, according to CBS San Francisco. 

Jessica Crabtree, a teacher at Redwood High School said: 'When we look on the national scene and we wonder how the Harvey Weinsteins are made — we're making them.'  

Superintendent David Yoshihara announced that students will take sexual harassment classes after receiving several complaints from teachers

Eva Rieder who works at the Tamalpais Union High School District, said: 'I have been sexually harassed, stalked, intimidated, threatened, bullied and defamed by male students.'

Superintendent David Yoshihara announced the new curriculum at Tuesday night's school board meeting after receiving several complaints from teachers. 

'They need to know. And I think it's our job to help them know,' he said.

Yoshihara elaborated and said students will learn the definition of sexual misconduct and also how they can report harassment to the district. 

Senior students will learn about the topic of consent: 'Informed consent is essentially what it means to say ‘no’ and essentially letting the other party know when you have consent,' he said.

One of the students at Redwood High School, Kevin Gann, said he was keen to learn more: 'I think it can be a good idea if they do it in the right way.' 

'I’m open to almost anything. If people tell me about it, want me to do it, I might go,' he said.

His classmate Russell Camden agreed.

'It’s important because it’s happening to teachers and you can’t feel safe in a learning environment or a teaching environment if you’re getting harassed by the people you are supposed to teach,' he said. 

The district has also added a Title IX coordinator due to the allegations. 

Title IX became law in the 70s and prohibits discrimination on the basis of sex in federally-funded education programs.
