The start of the season may not have gone to plan for Lewis Hamilton but the world champion made a colourful arrival ahead of Sunday's Bahrain Grand Prix. 

After the bitter disappointment of losing the opening race of the season in Australia, the Brit arrived at the Sakhir Circuit wearing a purple baseball cap and a multi-coloured shirt.

Carrying his smart phone in hand the Mercedes driver cut a relaxed figure as he strolled through the paddock, with many other F1 stars also landing in the desert.

Hamilton was expected to win in Melbourne but an error on strategy allowed title rival Sebastian Vettel to steal in and claim victory at Albert Park.

'It feels like a dark cloud but it's not,' Hamilton told reporters after finishing second.

'We have a great car, we are still the world champions and with a couple of adjustments we can win the next race.'

Vettel appears not to have stopped smiling in the 10 days that have since passed as the Ferrari star arrived in high spirits for the second race of the season.

Another driver with reason to be in a good mood is Fernando Alonso following his fifth place for McLaren in Australia.

The Woking team, now on Renault power, are hoping for a return near the front of the grid on a regular basis following a horror three years in partnership with Honda.

Like Hamilton, Alonso also looked relaxed as he arrived at the circuit with girlfriend and model Linda Morselli.

The Sakhir action will see the drivers race under the floodlights amid the dark of the desert night, with the grand prix set to get underway at 4.10pm BST.
