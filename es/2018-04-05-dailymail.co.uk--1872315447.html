Bones of Louise Pietrewicz discovered 51 years after she vanished

The skeleton of Louise Pietrewicz, missing for 51 years, has been found buried under a house that belonged to her boyfriend. 

The skeleton of a woman missing for 51 years has been found buried under a house that belonged to her boyfriend.

Louise Pietrewicz suddenly vanished from the small town of Southold on Long Island on October 6, 1966, after she left her abusive husband.

Detectives discovered the bones wrapped in a burlap bag buried seven feet below the basement of the 17th-century home last month.

DNA from the 38-year-old's brother and daughter matched with the bones, confirming they were hers.

The house at the time she disappeared belonged to Southold policeman William Boken, who lived there with his wife and children.

He and Ms Pietrewicz were having an affair, though both their partners knew about it. 

Boken died in 1982, and the whereabouts of Ms Pietrewicz remained a mystery for decades until earlier this year when the case was reopened.

The basement was examined and parts of it were dug up in 2013 with no results, but police were this time armed with a ground-penetrating sonar. 

The Office of the Suffolk County Medical Examiner was yet to determine a cause of death.

The breakthrough in the case came after the local Suffolk Times ran an in-depth eight-part investigative series in the paper and produced a three-part documentary shedding new light on the missing person case, both titled Gone.

The publicity generated by the reporting and the film prompted the local police to reopen the investigation.

Boken's ex-wife Judith Terry then told detectives a body was buried in a burlap bag in the basement of the four-bedroom, two-bathroom home.

Ms Pietrewicz's family including her brother Leo Jasinski and 63-year-old only daughter Sandy Blampied were overcome by the news her body was found.

'I choked up. I have never forgotten Louise and I never will. She was my sister. It is over now,' Mr Jasinski told the Suffolk Times.

Ms Blampied said she never gave up hope to find her beloved mother and always suspected Ms Terry knew something and would be the one to unlock the mystery.  

'I just broke down. I just broke down and cried. It is so hard to believe what has happened. I still can't believe it. This was my mother,' she said.

Ms Pietrewicz had recently left her husband of 16 years, a prosperous farmer named Albin Pietrewicz, who was said to have been physically and verbally abusive.

She relocated with her 11-year-old daughter Sandy to her family's farm in Sagaponack, New York. 

Shortly before she vanished, Ms Pietrewicz bought two plane tickets for herself and her daughter so they could travel to Florida.

A day before her last sighting on October 5, Ms Pietrewicz emptied her bank account containing just over $1,770 and closed it.

Louise's sister Josephine last saw her riding away from Sagaponack in Boken's car, acting as if she did not want to be seen by anyone.

The next day, Boken resigned from the local police force after taking a three-day sick leave.

Ms Pietrewicz's purse was recovered a week later on a shoulder of Route 25. Its contents included a World War II savings bond, the woman's Social Security card and a postcard bearing the name of a doctor in Glen Cove.

Police investigated the incident as a missing person case, not as a possible homicide, and it got no attention from the local press at the time.

Following Ms Pietrewicz's disappearance, detectives interviewed her estranged husband, who said he was humiliated by her infidelity and did not care what about her whereabouts, or whether she was alive or dead.

A year later, Louise's lover Boken was arrested on suspicion of abusing his wife Judith and was committed to a psychiatric hospital.

At one point, he allegedly threatened his wife that he would bury her in their basement 'with that other b****.' He was never prosecuted in connection to Pietrewicz's disappearance, having been officially declared mentally ill, and died in New York City in 1982. 

Albin Pietrewicz divorced his missing wife in 1974 and remarried two years later. He died in 2000.
