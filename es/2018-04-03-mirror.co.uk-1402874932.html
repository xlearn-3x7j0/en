Arsenal star reveals nasty wound ahead of crucial CSKA Moscow clash

Alexandre Lacazette took to his Instagram account to reveal a nasty wound suffered on his first game back from injury.

The Frenchman has been sidelined for the last two-months with a knee injury, a long-term problem which he suffered after the 1-0 defeat to Tottenham in February.

He returned for the 3-0 win over Stoke on Sunday, netting the third goal from the penalty spot after Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang's double had given Arsenal a comfortable win.

However, it came as a cost as he posted a picture of a tasty looking gash on his leg, writing: "Welcome back Laca," with a laughing emoji.

It was only his second goal of 2018 after that spell on the sidelines, with Lacazette chasing a spot in France's World Cup squad.

The 26-year-old was recently replaced by Sevilla striker Wissam Ben Yedder in Didier Deschamp’s lastest team and he'll be targeting an injury-free run for the remainder of the season.

Lacazette thanked Gunners fans for their reception after the match, saying: "What a feeling to be back at home. Thanks to all the fans for the support. We will need you until the end."

His timely return will please Arsenal fans and their number nine will likely be given the nod in Thursday's crunch Europa League tie with CSKA Moscow with Aubameyang cup-tied.
