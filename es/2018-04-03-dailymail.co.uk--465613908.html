AC Milan 'eyeing summer moves for Arturo Vidal and Memphis Depay' 

  • AC Milan are being monitored by UEFA following a £200million spending spree
  • But the Italian giants are reportedly eyeing more big names this summer
  • Arturo Vidal and Memphis Depay have been linked with a move to the San Siro

Published: 06:34 EDT, 3 April 2018 | Updated: 06:35 EDT, 3 April 2018

AC Milan are reportedly eyeing a double summer swoop for Arturo Vidal and Memphis Depay – provided they don't fall foul of UEFA's Financial Fair Play regulations.

Milan are currently being monitored by European's governing body after spending more than £200million on new players over the summer.

But according to Italian paper Tuttosport, they are still in the market for big names as they look to regain their place at Europe's top table.

AC Milan are reportedly interested in a summer move for Bayern Munich's Arturo Vidal

The San Siro club are also believed to be keen to sign Lyon and Holland star Memphis Depay 

Bayern Munich midfielder Vidal has been linked with a move for Manchester United. But Milan are reportedly confident the Chile international will ask to leave Bavaria this summer. 

With only one year left on his current deal, the midfielder's transfer fee should be within reach for Milan, though FFFP sanctions could render his wages unaffordable. 

Milan could also face a battle with United for Lyon forward Depay. Jose Mourinho's side have a buy-back option on the Dutchman, who enjoyed a disappointing spell at Old Trafford before reviving his career at Lyon.

Vidal, who has been linked with Manchester United, trains ahead of Bayern's clash with Sevilla

Depay previously labelled Milan 'a great club and a great team with a great history'. But any hopes he has of a move to the San Siro could be curtailed by the club's financial issues.

Amid concerns over Milan's club's spending spree — and their ability to repay loans to an American hedge fund — UEFA rejected the club's voluntary agreement to restructure their finances earlier this season.

As a result, the club face FFFP sanctions and have been pursuing a number of free transfers, with Pepe Reina and Ki Sung-yueng among the reported targets for the Italian giants.
