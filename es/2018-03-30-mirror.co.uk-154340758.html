Anthony Joshua and Joseph Parker weigh in live for heavyweight fight

Joshua is softer than Parker, claims Kiwi's promoter

Joseph Parker’s promoter has taunted Anthony Joshua , claiming he is “softer” than the Kiwi he faces on Saturday because he’s had it easy in his career.

David Higgins says Britain’s world heavyweight champ has had the red-carpet treatment since joining the GB Boxing programme as an amateur in 2010.

Joshua was fully funded with lottery money before he turned pro in 2013, whereas Parker received no state help.

“New Zealand doesn’t have a boxing heritage and there’s no government funding of boxing, so we’ve done it on the smell of an oily rag,” said Higgins.

“Joseph didn’t win any medals. He started with nothing and no name and we have scrapped, hustled and fought our way to the top of the world and that gives us real confidence and an in-built strength.

“Joshua, on the other hand... credit to him for turning his life around, but British boxing gives their fighters a lot of support, a lot of sponsorship. Then he won Olympic gold, and it’s all easy.

“Therefore I think he may be softer.”
