The Day King Was Shot: 26 Times Articles That Told the Story

The bullets that mortally wounded the Rev. Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. in Memphis were fired a little after 6 p.m. Central Standard Time on April 4, 1968. The first news bulletin flashed across television screens about an hour later.

The New York Times newsroom had only hours to rework a newspaper that would be printed later that night, and 26 articles that Friday would mention Dr. King.

That first draft of history is rendered below in those articles. You can click on any headline to see the article as it appeared in the late city edition of the paper on April 5, 1968.

The Front Page

Under the banner headline “Martin Luther King Is Slain in Memphis; A White Is Suspected; Johnson Urges Clam,” The Times ran six front-page articles about Dr. King.

Unrest Begins

In addition to the reports above from Harlem and Brooklyn, the article from Washington reported on a clothing store being looted. And The Times reported that the major television networks broke into regularly scheduled programming with bulletins about Dr. King’s death around 8:19 p.m. Eastern and showed live video footage of the demonstrations and rioting starting to occur in Memphis, Washington and New York.
