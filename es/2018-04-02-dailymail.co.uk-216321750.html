Where it all went horribly wrong for Alan Pardew at West Brom

In the end, the only surprise was the timing. West Bromwich Albion and Alan Pardew were always going to part, but the club had resolved to maintain the status quo until the summer.

A quite awful first-half in the defeat to Burnley altered that approach, and the board finally accepted keeping Pardew was doing neither him nor them any favours.

Nevertheless, it seems odd to make the change now with six games left and survival a long-since departed hope. Done earlier, after defeat to Huddersfield say, and who knows?

Alan Pardew left West Brom as manager by mutual consent on Monday morning

It was clear at that stage the relationship between Pardew and his players had seriously broken down and would not produce a positive culmination to the campaign.

The dressing room after that loss saw heated scenes, with Chris Brunt criticising the tactics employed and also the effort of certain teammates. Pardew delivered a response before Claudio Yacob also lended his voice to the argument, with John Carver then stepping in as peacemaker.

Senior players were not buying into Pardew’s methods, upset at his approach to younger teammates, while at the same time the manager was alarmed at a lack of togetherness.

Pardew's disastrous spell as West Brom manager included just one Premier League victory

Publicly, Pardew eased sense of a rift but privately he was worried at a group offering excuses and not action. Some were said to be leaving the training ground after sessions with unseemly haste.

West Brom had appointed Pardew in anticipation of an instant bounce but his first game ended 0-0 against Crystal Palace and it never got better from there.

There are some who argue the players subconsciously eased off after the departure of Tony Pulis, then there are those who think Pardew’s personality and that of the majority of the squad was destined to produce clashes.

The departure of assistant head coach Ben Garner, who was popular among players and led certain pre-match meetings, should also not be underestimated.

West Brom players cut dejected figures following their defeat by Burnley at the weekend 

Pardew was certainly denied much chance to inject the team with new faces of his choosing. Last summer’s spending meant financial regulations pinched tightly and although West Brom pushed the limit on wages to bring in Daniel Sturridge, the Liverpool striker was Pardew’s only major January addition.

In hindsight, that move has proved a disaster, costing the club £50,000 for each of Sturridge’s 78 minutes on the field. Grzegorz Krychowiak has been another £110,000-per-week miss.

Sturridge, out with a hamstring injury, has only started one game – at home to Southampton – and that was a selection that saw players question Pardew because he dropped the in-form Jay Rodriguez.

The Barcelona trip will go down as the motif for this season. Nobody could have predicted four senior players allegedly stealing a taxi during an early morning visit to McDonald’s and Gareth Barry, Jonny Evans, Jake Livermore, and Boaz Myhill badly let down the club.

But there had been reservations beforehand about going abroad in the slender gap between a Monday night match at Chelsea and a Saturday FA Cup tie against Southampton.

The desired team spirit was unlikely to find fertile ground in Spain when many wished to spend Valentine’s Day with their partners. Some also suggest Pardew letting his hair down too filtered through to a group who under Pulis, would be coached to and from the evening meal when on a club tour.

Pardew was brought in as manager to save West Brom from relegation to the Championship

The Baggies are bottom of the Premier League table on 20 points after 32 games played

Barry’s visit to a student nightclub before Saturday’s game was a bad call in the circumstances and another reminder that Pardew’s authority has been eroded.

It was in the week of the Barcelona trip that chairman John Williams and chief executive Martin Goodman paid with their jobs for the decision to sack Pulis, who owner Gouchuan Lai now believes might have kept the club up.

Certainly Pardew’s record of eight points from 18 matches is excruciating and by remaining in charge he would have only damaged his reputation further. At his best he is a progressive coach and compelling communicator but proved a poor fit at the Hawthorns.

PARDEW'S WEST BROM REIGN IN NUMBERS 

21 - games in charge of the club, in all competitions - 18 in the Premier League and three in the FA Cup.

1 - league win, 2-0 against Brighton in January. It is their only league win since August 19.

2 - Pardew's FA Cup wins with the club exceed his tally in the league after Albion saw off Exeter and, remarkably, Liverpool before losing to Southampton.

8 - points for West Brom in 18 league games under Pardew, compared to 12 from 14 before he took over.

4 - games taken for Albion to score their first goal under Pardew, Gareth Barry's 77th-minute strike in December's loss to Manchester United.

4 - Salomon Rondon finishes as West Brom's leading goalscorer in league games under Pardew. He and Jay Rodriguez have six apiece in all competitions.

50% - half of the team's points this season, 10, came in their first eight games - and seven, or over a third, in their first three.
