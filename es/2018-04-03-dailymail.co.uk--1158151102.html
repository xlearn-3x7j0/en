Why using shampoo or conditioner to shave your legs is not a good idea

Ask any woman what she spends the most time doing in the shower and the answer will usually be shaving. 

But to cut down on time, some ladies will naturally lean towards using their regular shampoo, conditioner or shower gel as a shaving cream.

Not only are they already in your shower but it's cheaper to use something for two uses than it is to buy two different products.

But according to dermatologist Dr. Anita Sturnham, it's worth purchasing them separately.

'Shaving has a naturally exfoliating mechanism of action, so you need to use products that are designed to work in synergy with your razor and improve glide, reduce friction, and the risk of irritation and cuts,' she told Popsugar. 

'These formulations will coat the hairs, making them thicker and more difficult to shave.'

Pros and cons of shaving

Pros

There are so many razors available to suit different skin types that hair removal is now quick, inexpensive and efficient. There are even portable on the go razors that do not need shaving gels making hair removal so simple. 

Shaving cuts the hair at the surface of the skin so it does not distort the hair follicle like waxing or leave the skin prone to ingrowing hairs. Suitable for all skin types, budgets and hair and is pain free. The good news is that you don't need a professional to perform shaving so you can DIY at home.

Cons 

Because shaving does not remove the hair from the hair root it needs to be performed daily for the skin to feel silky smooth and hair free. As the hair grows back with a blunt tip, it can feel stubblier if you leave the hair to grow.  

'It can clog your razors, which ultimately means using your razor more often for results that aren't as smooth,' she said.

'Advanced shaving tools now allow the choice to incorporate technology such as flexible razor heads, ergonomically designed handles, moisture glide ribbons, multiple blades and in-built combs to perfectly match the right razor to your skin type. 

'This allows for silky smooth hair free skin without the shaving rashes, nicks and cuts of yester year.' 

Other options, like waxing and laser, offer more long-term solutions to the hair problem but tend to come with more pain and a higher price tag.
