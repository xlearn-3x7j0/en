Baby orangutan is washed in a sink at an animal care centre 

  • Baby orangutan seemed relaxed while it was being washed with a bowel of water
  • However, like any baby, it started to squirm when some seemed to go in its eyes
  • Adorable footage was filmed at an animal care centre in Queenstown, Indonesia

Published: 08:12 EDT, 5 April 2018 | Updated: 08:14 EDT, 5 April 2018

This is the adorable moment a baby orangutan holds onto a woman's hand as it enjoys a bath in a sink.

The cute animal looks completely relaxed while holding onto the carer and staring into space.

The footage was filmed in Queenstown, Indonesia at an animal care centre.

The orangutan can be seen clutching the hand and arm of the woman washing it, while another films the heartwarming moment. 

The video starts with the orangutan sitting calmly while water drenches its head and shoulders. 

After a few seconds, it notices the camera and stares into it, while the woman recording the video can be heard cooing in excitement.

The adorable baby orangutan could be seen peering at the camera as it enjoyed a bath in an animal care centre

The orangutan is being washed in a sink, with water splashed all over it.

Although it appears to be relaxed initially, it cringes and turns its head to the side as more and more water is used.

It seems like the baby orangutan has had enough as it tries to stretch away from the water bowl and push the carer's arm back. 

Although it looked as if it was enjoying the bath at first, it cringed and moved its head away when too much water got into its eyes

After being drenched in the water, it is lifted off the sink and taken away by the carer, dripping wet.

It is presumably heading off for a thorough and vigorous rub down to dry off.  

The video was recorded in February and shows how the adorable primate seems to love a nice bath - although like any baby, it seems to panic when the water gets a bit too much.
