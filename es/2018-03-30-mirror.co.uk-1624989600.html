What channel is Chelsea vs Tottenham? All the details you need...

It could be the match to decide which clubs make the Champions League spots come the end of the season.

Just five points separate these London rivals, with Tottenham Hotspur above their long time foes.

But the last time they won at Stamford Bridge was in 1990, when a pint of milk cost 30p.

Mauricio Pochettino will be hopeful Harry Kane makes a shock return but will be wary of rushing him back too quickly - with Spurs still set for a crucial run-in.

Chelsea will feel they have to win this to have any realistic chance of playing alongside Europe’s elite next season.

The Blues could find it sticky going after losing three out of their last five.

Spurs on the other hand have only lost five games all year, and although The Blues won at Wembley back at the start of the season, they'll be confident of a result to consolidate their advantage.

Here's all you need to know about the game...

When and where is the game?

The game is on Sunday, April 1 at Stamford Bridge- kick off is at 4pm.

What channel is the game on?

The game is live on Sky Sports Main Event and Sky Sports Football - coverage for both starts at 3.30pm.

Can I stream the game live?

Yes, the game is available to stream live on mobile, tablet or desktop via Sky Go for Sky Sports paying subscribers, or on Now TV.

Mirror Football will also be covering the game via Live Blog.
