‘If you don’t want a car and want an ashtray, this is for you’

A Queensland man is attempting to sell a beaten-up 90s car that has no registration with an honest twist on a buy and sell Facebook ad. 

The car is detailed as a '1990 something Mitsubishi Mirage' was posted on Facebook and selling for $550. 

Sean Connors posted the ad for his sisters 'ashtray mirage' on Sunday after she bought a new car. 

Dubbed the 'ashtray mirage' this car was posted on a buy and sell page in a refreshing take to selling the second-hand car

The car which seller Sean Connors described as 'something you wanna drive into a tree,' is scratched up and 'has damage on probably every panel and a little bit of rust here and there'

Mr Connors admitted the car 'has damage on probably every panel and a little bit of rust here and there', joking it was 'something you wanna drive into a tree [sic].'

Mr Connors told Daily Mail Australia he was selling his 18-year-old sister's car because it was no longer needed by his family.

'It's taking up space out the front and it's not straight enough to clean up so it's easier for me to sell it off dirt cheap,' he said. 

The hilarious ad is brutally honest about the condition of his 18-year-old sister's car 

The 21-year-old said he wrote the description because he didn't want to gloss over the details of the car.

'People always have hidden problems with selling cars, I'm 100 per cent honest about everything to sell it,' he said. 

He said: 'It's a good car underneath it's just ugly on top, if you're a smart person you could probably sell it more for parts and make a bit of money on it.'

The 21-year-old said the car is 'a good car underneath it's just ugly on top'and would be a good buy for someone who would sell the parts

Sean confirmed he was hopeful the car would sell for the asking price but said he is happy to negotiate

The 1999 Mitsubishi comes with 'countless free rollies and filters' and '19 lighters' according to the Facebook ad. 

Sean also said the car has 'no airbags' and 'four pretty well fresh as f*** tyres'. 

'Come down and buy my car today and I give you a good deal, $50 off if you can skid it into 3rd gear at the top of my hill,' he joked in the ad. 

Sean confirmed he was hopeful the car would sell for the asking price but said he is happy to negotiate. 

'I might get $200 which I will be happy with but you gotta start high and say you're negotiable to sell a car,' he said.
