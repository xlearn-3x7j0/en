WWE WrestleMania 34 matches as full card is confirmed

WWE's annual extravaganza WrestleMania will be held at the Mercedes-Benz Superdome in New Orleans, Louisiana on April 8.

Every year the U.S. sports entertainment giant somehow manages to serve up an even bigger spectacle and WrestleMania 34 should be no different.

However, while nothing in the world of wrestling measures up to the grandeur of the Showcase of the Immortals, its the action in the ring fans love the most.

Roman Reigns earned the right to challenge Raw's WWE Universal Champion Brock Lesnar in the main event by triumphing inside the Elimination Chamber.

So what about the rest of the card? Mirror Sport's Neil Docking look at the rumoured bouts that will grace the Grandest Stage of Them All.

Singles match for the WWE Universal Championship

Brock Lesnar (c) (with Paul Heyman) vs. Roman Reigns

A rematch three years in the making will see Roman Reigns try to defeat Brock Lesnar after a failed attempt in the main event of WrestleMania 31.

That night Reigns missed out on the WWE World Heavyweight Championship thanks to Seth Rollins cashing in his Money in the Bank contract.

Rollins pinned his former team mate in The Shield and left him and Brock cursing his opportunism in a shocking end to the event in March 2015.

Reigns set up this match with a triumph in the Elimination Chamber and then called Lesnar out for failing to show up on Raw, claiming he was protected by Vince McMahon, which earned him a storyline suspension from the WWE chairman.

Brock replied by appearing and pummelling Reigns two weeks in a row - the first brutal attack when his challenger was handcuffed and helpless.

Singles match for the WWE Championship

AJ Styles (c) vs. Shinsuke Nakamura

Royal Rumble victor Shinsuke Nakamura declared his intention to capture the WWE Championship from SmackDown LIVE's top man AJ Styles back in January.

In contrast to the lukewarm response to the polarising Reigns facing the seemingly UFC-bound Lesnar, fans cannot wait to see this showdown.

Nakamura and Styles produced stunning matches during their time competing at New Japan Pro Wrestling and this could be an all-time mat classic.

Styles ensured it would take place by overcoming John Cena, Kevin Owens, Sami Zayn, Dolph Ziggler and Baron Corbin in a six pack challenge at Fastlane.

Everyone is expecting a classic and according to Styles, not even losing a leg would keep him from making this bout the real 'main event'.

Tag team match

Ronda Rousey and Kurt Angle vs. Stephanie McMahon and Triple H

When Ronda Rousey appeared at the end of the Royal Rumble, fans immediately started guessing who she would face in her professional wrestling debut.

The former UFC Bantamweight Champion stared down Asuka plus Raw Women's Champion Alexa Bliss and SmackDown Women's Champion Charlotte Flair.

Instead Ronda will clash with Raw's commissioner Stephanie McMahon and her husband Triple H, who she had an altercation with alongside The Rock at WrestleMania 31.

That was all but confirmed when Rousey put HHH through a table during her contract signing at Elimination Chamber, after Kurt Angle revealed the pair were using her.

When The Game sucker-punched Raw's general manager in revenge, the Olympic Gold Medallist decided he would partner Rousey in a headline grabbing bout, and the following week she even got her hands on Stephanie.

Singles match

John Cena vs. The Undertaker

John Cena all but ended months of speculation when he called out The Undertaker for a massive WrestleMania collision.

Between them, Cena and The Deadman have headlined no less than nine Manias, but they have never clashed at the Grandest Spectacle in Sports Entertainment.

The 16-time world champion said the match was "impossible", presumably because Taker is meant to be retired after his WrestleMania 33 defeat to Roman Reigns. However, he has since goaded Taker into dropping his ego and trying his best to give him a Tombstone at the Show of Shows.

This one has not been confirmed yet - but everything points towards the modern face of WWE squaring off with the legendary Phenom in an all-time dream match many fans thought would never happen.

Singles match for the WWE Raw Women's Championship

Alexa Bliss (c) vs. Nia Jax

The winner of the first women's Royal Rumble match was the undefeated former NXT Champion, Asuka, who is based on Raw.

Asuka ended months of speculation by challenging SmackDown's Queen, Charlotte Flair, for her women's title at Fastlane.

A battle with Bliss had appeared a bit of a mismatch, as she has been running scared from Asuka since losing to her on Raw back in January.

She will instead face her longtime best friend, Nia Jax, after it was revealed she had been using the Samoan powerhouse and bullying her.

Nia heard Alexa bodyshaming her backstage in a catty exchange with Mickie James and is now out for revenge over The Goddess.

Singles match for the WWE SmackDown Women's Championship

Charlotte Flair (c) vs. Asuka

Nia Jax lost to Asuka at the Elimination Chamber and missed out on an opportunity to make her BFF Alexa Bliss defend her title in a triple threat.

That cleared the way for Asuka to compete one on one with Bliss and be crowned the top woman on Monday nights, next month in Louisiana.

But the Raw roster now also includes Ronda Rousey and WWE clearly wishes to avoid having two all-conquering badasses on the same brand.

Asuka will instead face wrestling royalty Charlotte Flair, who seems to be the one woman with a realistic chance of ending her undefeated streak.

This will mark the first time Mania has featured multiple women's singles matches since WrestleMania 22 - although one of those was a Playboy Pillow Fight!

Triple threat match for the WWE Intercontinental Championship

The Miz (c) (with Bo Dallas and Curtis Axel) vs. Seth Rollins vs. Finn Balor

The Miz is enjoying his eighth reign as Intercontinental Champion, a feat bettered only by the great Chris Jericho.

But it could come to an end in a big triple threat match at WrestleMania 34, when he faces two of WWE's top tier talents.

The 'Must See Superstar' will be challenged by two former world champions, company favourites Seth Rollins and Finn Balor.

Balor hasn't held gold since injury meant he was forced to drop the Universal Championship he won at SummerSlam 2016... from Rollins.

Tag team match

Kevin Owens and Sami Zayn versus Daniel Bryan and Shane McMahon

SmackDown LIVE commissioner Shane McMahon has warred with Kevin Owens since July, when KO blamed him for losing his US title to AJ Styles.

He again pointed the finger at Shane when he lost a rematch at SummerSlam with his boss as special guest referee, then insulted his family.

After headbutting Vince McMahon, Owens faced Shane in Hell in a Cell, surviving thanks to the help of his longtime sparring partner Sami Zayn.

They sabotaged Team SmackDown at Survivor Series and became embroiled in a dispute between Shane and his general manager Daniel Bryan.

With Bryan supporting the pair and Shane determined to fire them, some thought the two authority figures could even come to blows.

At Fastlane, Shane cost both Owens and Zayn the chance to become WWE Champion and made a match between the pair of antagonists.

But everything changed when they gave him a brutal beatdown and a week later, Bryan announced his return to the ring, only to suffer the same fate at their hands. Now Shane is recovering from a serious illness in hospital, but still expected to partner Daniel against the duo.

Tag team match for the WWE Raw Tag Team Championship

Sheamus and Cesaro (c) vs. Braun Strowman and Elias

Sheamus and Cesaro have not only adopted the name The Bar but undeniably set it for tag team wrestling over the last year.

The hard-hitting Europeans went from being rivals and unlikely tag team partners to one of the best duos anywhere in the world today.

After dispatching Titus Worldwide once again on Raw, they pointed out there was barely anyone left for them to face.

Former NXT standouts The Revival immediately spring to mind as a pairing capable of producing magic against the veteran champions.

But now 'The Monster Among Men' Braun Strowman has won the chance to face Sheamus and Cesaro. But who (if anyone) will be his partner?

It is suspected that man will be the Sinister Songsmith Elias, a recent rival of Strowman, who is without a spot on the Mania card.

Fatal four-way match for the United States Championship

Randy Orton (c) vs. Bobby Roode vs. Jinder Mahal vs Rusev

Bobby Roode's United States Championship reign didn't quite get off the ground on the recently underwhelming blue brand.

He struggled to get over as a face following the strange decision not to push him as a heel on his main roster debut.

But he wasn't the only one floundering on Tuesday nights, with the likes of Randy Orton and Jinder Mahal also lacking direction.

At Fastlane, Orton captured the title from Roode in a cracking bout, marking the first time he had won the US Championship.

Post-match, the new Grand Slam Champion was attacked by both Mahal and Mahal, surely paving the way for another triple threat match.

But WrestleMania will in fact be Rusev Day as the incredibly popular Bulgarian Brute was added to the mix. Rusev Machka!

Triple threat tag team match for the WWE SmackDown Tag Team Championship

The Usos (c) vs. The New Day vs. The Bludgeon Brothers

The only duo who enjoyed a more impressive 2017 than The Bar were SmackDown's Tag Team Champions Jimmy and Jey Uso.

The twin brothers worked the feud of the year with The New Day and produced a series of fantastic battles with Big E, Kofi Kingston and Xavier Woods.

They went at it one more time at Fastlane, but The Usos emerged with the gold still around their waists ready for a WrestleMania humdinger.

That was thanks to The Bludgeon Brothers, who ensured the bout ended in a no contest by destroying the two factions in a brutal beatdown.

A showdown involving all three teams has now been confirmed, which will hopefully be the first time The Usos will be on the main card at Mania.

Tournament final match for the vacant WWE Cruiserweight Championship

Cedric Alexander vs. Mustafa Ali

The final of the WWE Cruiserweight Championship tournament has been revealed as 205 Live looks to crown a new king on the kick-off show for Mania.

As the new general manager of the brand, England's Drake Maverick, aka Rockstar Spud, has overseen a revival in the show's fortunes.

Now Cedric Alexander will face Mustafa Ali, as the two friends compete for the right to be the next lord of the cruiserweights.

Alexander and Ali are both fan favourites who can wow between the ropes. Will Cedric's spectacular Lumbar Check or Mustafa's beautiful imploding 450° splash win the day?

Andre the Giant Memorial Battle Royal Match

The Andre The Giant Memorial Battle Royal was first held at WrestleMania 30 and has since been won by Cesaro, The Big Show, Baron Corbin and Mojo Rawley.

The four victors have enjoyed mixed success but it's a good way to cram a lot of talent otherwise left off the WrestleMania card into some kind of encounter.

HBO's Andre The Giant documentary is set to be released two days after this year's WrestleMania, which means a lot of focus could be put on the match.

Confirmed so far are Dash Wilder and Scott Dawson of The Revival, Baron Corbin, Mojo Rawley, Tye Dillinger, Dolph Ziggler and Breezango allies Tyler Breeze and Fandango. Oh and 'Woken' Matt Hardy - so surely Jeff Hardy aka Brother Nero will be involved too, right?

Women's WrestleMania Battle Royal Match

Originally the Fabulous Moolah Memorial Battle Royal was scheduled for WrestleMania 34 to honour the WWE Hall of Famer, and was introduced to serve as a female counterpart to its male version, the Andre the Giant Memorial Battle Royal.

However, WWE changed the name to the WrestleMania Women's Battle Royal due to the backlash from fans and critics regarding Moolah's controversial past.

Open to any female wrestler from Raw, SmackDown, and WWE's developmental brand NXT, Ms. Money in the Bank Carmella became the first to announce her participation on the March 13 episode of SmackDown.

She has since been joined by Sasha Banks, Becky Lynch, Liv Morgan, Naomi, Natalya, Ruby Riott, Sarah Logan, Bayley and Lana.
