Sterling signing new Manchester City deal is 'so important': Guardiola

  • Manchester City's Raheem Sterling has enjoyed his best ever season this term 
  • It is thought talks over fresh terms with City will begin in the summer 
  • European giants Real Madrid have been linked with the England international
  • City take on Everton at Goodison Park in the Premier League on Saturday 

Published: 09:21 EDT, 30 March 2018 | Updated: 11:46 EDT, 30 March 2018

Pep Guardiola claimed 'it's so important' Raheem Sterling signs a new long-term contract at Manchester City.

Sterling has enjoyed his best ever season this term and looks set to be an integral figure for Gareth Southgate at the World Cup.

It's thought discussions over fresh terms will begin in the summer, with Real Madrid also linked with the forward.

Raheem Sterling has been in fantastic form for Premier League leaders Manchester City

The England international has attracted interest from European giants Real Madrid

Asked if talks had already started, Guardiola said: 'No. I'm not the guy for contracts. No doubt it's so important.

'The deals are the deals, the agents, maybe other clubs are interested in him. He knows how important he is for us.'

John Stones will miss out on a return to Everton on Saturday after suffering a concussion during England's draw against Italy.

The central defender was withdrawn with 17 minutes remaining at Wembley earlier in the week and is ruled out.

City manager Pep Guardiola insists Sterling is an 'important' player for the Etihad outfit

'He feels really good but the rules mean seven days off,' Guardiola added. 'That's why he's not available.

'It (the title) is not already done. We need three games, or two if we win the next two. We're in a good position.

'We've been three weeks without playing games and it's just been two days back. I don't know how they're going to react tomorrow. Hopefully the same level but I don't know.'
