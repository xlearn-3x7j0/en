What Lionel Messi said in dressing room after Spain 6-1 Argentina

Lionel Messi headed to the Argentina dressing room after Spain battered them 6-1 in an international friendly on Tuesday.

The Barcelona forward played no part in the 2018 World Cup warm-up match, due to fatigue, but watched his nation from the stands.

Diego Costa opened the scoring for Spain in just the 12th minute before Isco bagged a hat-trick, and Thiago Alcantara and Iago Aspas netted a goal apiece.

Manchester City centre-back Nicolas Otamendi did score a consolation goal just before half time, when Spain were leading 2-0. But unfortunately for Argentina it remained exactly that, a consolation.

Messi was filmed leaving the stands after witnessing the horror show and heading down to the Argentina changing room.

Once there he spoke to his team-mates, saying: "We come through this together. United," according to Diario Ole.

A source told the publication that the players responded well to Messi's impromptu pep talk, saying: "What Leo did? No, really good, really good. Honestly, we needed it."

Former Chelsea striker Costa spoke out about Messi's influence on this Argentina team after the game, and brought attention to his importance.

"You can see that when Messi is not around it's something else," said Costa.

"To have Messi on the pitch changes many things. You can see that when he is not on the pitch, they are missing their leader.

"Argentinians know that Messi is Messi. I hear that people criticise [him], but Messi deserves preferential treatment because he is the best.

"A player like Messi cannot be criticised. You have to give thanks to god that he exists."
