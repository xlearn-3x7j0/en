Chelsea step up Massimiliano Allegri chase to replace Antonio Conte

Chelsea have stepped up their interest in Massimiliano Allegri in the belief the Juventus manager could be tempted by a switch to Stamford Bridge.

Allegri has two more years to run on a £10million-a-year contract at Juventus but there has been contact at an intermediary level.

He is on course to win the Italian title yet again but is becoming frustrated by an inability to compete with Europe's strongest clubs in the Champions League.

Chelsea have stepped up their interest in Juventus manager Massimiliano Allegri 

Juventus were thrashed 3-0 at home by Real Madrid in the first leg of a quarter-final tie on Tuesday.

The result has reinforced his belief that the Italian champions are making up very little ground on the teams at the very top despite finishing as runners-up in 2015 and 2017.

When Juve met Real in last year's final in Cardiff they were comfortably beaten 4-1.

'They are one of the best,' said Allegri after the game on Tuesday. 'In Cardiff they played well and here they played better.'

Current Chelsea manager Antonio Conte is expected to leave the club at the end of the season

Juventus knocked-out Tottenham at Wembley in the last-16 but after surrendering a two-goal lead to draw 2-2 in the first-leg the manager became worked up about unrealistic expectation levels in Turin.

'Tottenham and Juventus can hope to go through and qualify but compared to Bayern and Barcelona and Real Madrid, we are slightly below,' said Allegri. 'We can't forget this. Next year will be even worse.

'We can keep on talking hot air but next year will be even more challenging. Consider what has happened in the last few years, Real Madrid and Barcelona always win the Champions League.'

Real Madrid have won the trophy in three of the last four years. Barcelona and Real have won six of the last nine and are in the hunt to win it again.

Allegri is growing frustrated at Juventus' inability to challenge with the European elite

They have dominated for a decade, during which time other winners have been Bayern (2013), Chelsea (2012) and Inter (2010).

Antonio Conte thought a move to Chelsea would satisfy his ambitions to challenge for the Champions League, although, ultimately, he has been disappointed.

Conte is set to leave this summer having railed against what he perceives to be a lack of support in the transfer market and an inability to compete with Manchester City and United when it comes to salaries for the very best players.

At Stamford Bridge, they see it differently. They point to millions spent during his time on players such as Alvaro Morata and Tiemoue Bakayoko.

They point to quality players acquired and released too soon, such as Kevin De Bruyne, Mo Salah and Romelu Lukaku.

Allegri was interested in the Chelsea job when Conte was chosen in 2014 and few in Italy would be surprised if he were to move abroad after winning a fourth Serie A title, which would be seven in a row for Juventus.

He had to watch on as his side fell to a 3-0 loss to Real Madrid in the Champions League 

At Stamford Bridge, however, he remains only one of the options.

Luis Enrique is the other popular candidate and is planning a return to coaching after a year out following his exit from Barcelona.

Enrique's demands are high, however, both in terms of his personal salary and backroom staff.

Equally, these top-tier coaches have at least one eye on the shifting landscape and other very attractive job opportunities which are threatening to open up at Arsenal, Paris Saint-Germain and Bayern.

Perhaps at Real Madrid, too, very soon.

Chelsea's problem, after their nosedive under Conte, is that they might not be able to offer Champions League football, never mind a team capable of challenging for it.
