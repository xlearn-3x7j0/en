Manchester United stars arrive for training ahead of Swansea clash

With the international break now over, it was business as usual for Manchester United as they gear up for the final part of the season.

The Red Devils continue their quest of trying to finish second in the Premier League on Saturday when they host relegation-threatened Swansea.

And preparations for the clash gathered pace on Friday morning as United's playing and coaching staff arrived for training.

Jose Mourinho looked in a relaxed mood as he arrived to take Manchester United training

Mourinho was driven to the club's Carrington base by assistant manager Rui Faria

Old Trafford boss Jose Mourinho looked in a relaxed mood as he arrived at the club's Carrington base.

The Portuguese was driven to training by assistant manager Rui Faria.

As revealed exclusively by Sportsmail's Chris Wheeler on Thursday night, Mourinho's relationship with Luke Shaw has taken a turn for the worse.

Shaw challenged Mourinho over the way he has been treating him during a heated dressing-room row at Old Trafford a fortnight ago. 

An angry Shaw reacted to Mourinho's decision to replace him at half-time in the FA Cup win over Brighton on March 17 by saying: 'Why are you always picking on me?'

Mourinho responded by telling Shaw that he was not defending well enough and Brighton were having too much freedom down United's left flank.

The exchange could be heard outside the home dressing-room as both Shaw and Mourinho raised their voices before second-half substitute Ashley Young came on for his team-mate.

Despite the ongoing friction, Shaw was present for training on Friday - providing a wave on his way into the Aon Training Complex.

Another player who has fallen out with Mourinho this season, Paul Pogba - fresh from starring in France's friendly win over Russia - also provided a wave upon entering the site.

England internationals Jesse Lingard, Marcus Rashford and Ashley Young were spotted, as was Belgium striker Romelu Lukaku.

Integral trio David de Gea, Nemanja Matic and Antonio Valencia also drove into training - as did the likes of Juan Mata, Chris Smalling, Phil Jones, Matteo Darmian and Victor Lindelof.
