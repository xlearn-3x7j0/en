T-Mobile Arena offering fans refund for Canelo vs Golovkin

The long-awaited rematch between Gennady Golovkin and Saul 'Canelo' Alvarez is in jeopardy after it was revealed the T-Mobile Arena are offering refunds on tickets bought for the middleweight megafight.

Golovkin and Canelo were due to meet in Sin City on May 5, eight months after their first clash ended in a controversial draw.

But the rematch has been in doubt ever since Canelo tested positive for the banned substance clenbuterol, adverse results he blamed on meat contamination.

The T-Mobile Arena in Las Vegas is offering refunds on Canelo Alvarez vs Gennady Golovkin

Canelo tested positive for the banned substance clenbuterol in the build-up to the rematch 

'GGG' is set to defend his WBA, WBC and IBF middleweight titles against the Mexican

The Mexican is currently under provisional suspension by the Nevada State Athletic Commission, who are conducting an investigation in the doping saga.

Canelo will face a hearing on April 10, where the authorities will decide whether the fight will go ahead as planned. 

But ESPN are reporting that MGM resorts, which owns the T-Mobile Arena, are offering refunds on tickets for the bout – such is the probability that it will be cancelled.

'If a fan requests a refund for the Canelo-GGG 2 fight they may receive one, in full, at the original point of purchase,' an MGM Resorts International spokesman told ESPN.

The Mexican last month tested positive for clenbuterol while going through his training camp in his native Guadajalara.

He has since returned a number of negative tests but still faces the possibility of a lengthy ban, should a five-person NSAC panel reject his excuse.

Canelo blamed the results on contaminated beef, a common problem for athletes in Mexico – where the substance is often given to cattle.

Clenbuterol, which is banned for farming use in the United States, helps reduce fat and increase lean muscle mass.
