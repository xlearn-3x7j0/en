Cardiff captain Sean Morrison slags off Derby players in sweary rant

Cardiff captain Sean Morrison has been filmed mocking the size of a Derby player's manhood in an expletive-filled rant.

The Bluebirds, currently second in the Championship table and on course for automatic promotion to the Premier League, meet Derby at the iPro Stadium on April 24.

And Morrison has stoked the fires ahead of the clash by taking aim at his Derby counterpart Richard Keogh, defender Alex Pearce and Jason Shackell, who is currently out on loan at Millwall.

Morrison also suggests Derby, currently in a play-off position in the Championship, are 'scared of playing us' following the game's controversial postponement because of snow last month.

The clip, apparently leaked from a private players' WhatsApp group, appears to show Morrison travelling in the back of a taxi when he launches into the diatribe. 

It is believed the rant was intended as a humorous exchange that wasn't meant to be leaked. 

'Alright Richard, Richie. Just want to say mate, you are a mug,' he says, seemingly taking aim at Keogh.

'Give me the ball, give me the ball, I really want the ball - then you just run as fast as you can 10 yards then just cut inside to the other centre-half because you are s*** at football.

'Pearcey is up your a***. Alex Pearce is so far up your a*** it is an embarrassment.

'Shackell as well, he doesn't show his d***. Little insecure c***. Little tiny d***, little tiny willy.

'And you are all scared of playing us. See you in a couple of weeks you b***.'

The April 24 clash between Cardiff and Derby looks set to be crucial in the promotion race

The match was scheduled originally for Sunday March 18 but postponed because of snow around Derby's stadium, a decision Cardiff manager Neil Warnock described as 'disgraceful'.

Warnock also hinted that Derby's injury crisis at that time was a factor in getting the match called off.

Derby manager Gary Rowett hit back at Warnock, joking that he didn't know he was a 'health and safety officer'.

Cardiff currently have a five-point cushion to Fulham in third place, with the rearranged Derby clash a game in hand.

However, with matches against leaders Wolves and fourth-placed Aston Villa to come next, the picture could have changed by the time they take on Derby.
