Squirrel is brought back to life with CPR after electrocuting itself

  • Video shows electrocuted squirrel brought back to life by young men
  • Unidentified persons performer heart massage for several minutes
  • The footage shows them working for several minutes until it breathes again

Published: 07:16 EDT, 5 April 2018 | Updated: 10:23 EDT, 5 April 2018

A group of young men became everyday heroes when they saved the life of a squirrel in Colombia.

The red rodent reportedly accidentally electrocuted itself on the powerlines in Cartagena, a city on Colombia’s Caribbean coast.

It was spotted by several unidentified persons, who picked it up and started trying to revive the rodent.  

Not all heroes wear capes: An unidentified person can be seen giving an electrocuted squirrel heart massage to revive it

Video footage of their Samaritan actions have since emerged online, and been shared on social media.

The video shows the squirrel lying on the seat of a moped, seemingly immovable.

One of the young men is gently tapping on the squirrel's chest in an attempt to restart its heart.

Not giving up, they keep working on the little rodent's heart for several minutes, until it finally starts breathing again.

Hang in there, buddy: After several minutes, the rodent starts to breathe, and can be seen panting, wide-eyed with fear, as the person out of shot strokes it on the back

Up we go! Finally, the rodent scrambles off the moped-seat and jumps down and runs off

Back at it: The video was reportedly shot in Cartagena, a city on Colombia’s Caribbean coast

At first, the animal can be seen lying on its side, wide-eyed and panting - evidently frihhtened.

But the people in the video keep stroking its back and soon it jumps up and off the bike.

According to the person who uploaded the video, the squirrel 'ran back to the streets and went back to its home'.
