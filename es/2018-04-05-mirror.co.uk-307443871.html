Gary Neville explains why Liverpool have no chance of winning title next season

Gary Neville doesn't think Liverpool will be challenging for the title next season, despite their 3-0 demolition job of Manchester City on Wednesday night.

Jurgen Klopp's men outclassed the Premier League leaders - who could win the title against Manchester United on the weekend - in the first leg of their Champions League quarter-final clash.

In the first half the Reds blew the visitors away with their expansive attacking play, while in the second they defended well and stifled every Manchester City effort.

This season Pep Guardiola's swashbuckling Citizens have been running away with the league since the campaign got underway back in August.

And domestically, Liverpool have been the only team to cause them serious problems, and take maximum points off them.

But despite the Reds' impressive performance on Wednesday night, Neville still doesn't think they will be challenging for the title next season.

"Don't think so no whilst they are in the Champions League," wrote Neville on Twitter, in response to someone asking whether Liverpool could provide Manchester City with serious competition next season.

"The way they play is difficult to replicate three times a week and their squad isn't strong enough beyond first XI to withstand injuries and lots of games."

He concluded the tweet "I hope", written in bracketts.
