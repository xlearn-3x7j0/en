The Hollywood moment filmed at Anthony Joshua fight that nobody noticed

Boxing fans who packed into the Principality Stadium to watch Anthony Joshua beat Joseph Parker unwittingly found themselves cast as film extras on Saturday night.

Almost 80,000 supporters packed into the Cardiff venue to see Joshua taken the 12-round distance for the first time in his professional career.

But before the main event got underway, fans were asked to react to several fighters' names in order to generate crowd noise for the upcoming film Creed 2.

The sequel is the latest installment in the Rocky franchise and will see Adonis Creed - son of Apollo - fight Ivan Drago's son in a bid to avenge the death of his father.

Before Joshua and Parker took to the ring on Saturday night, the crowd was asked to react to the British star's name, as well as ring legend Sugar Ray Leonard and Joshua's fellow heavyweight world champion Deontay Wilder.

The legendary Rocky music "Eye of the Tiger" was also played before David Price walked out for this KO defeat by Alexander Povetkin.

Producers of the first Creed movie filmed shots at Everton's Goodison Park in 2016 when Tony Bellew - who starred in the film - was crowned cruiserweight world champion when he KO'd Ilunga Makabu.

The film is due to be released on November 21 in America.
