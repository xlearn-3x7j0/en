Parents reveal the most embarrassing things their children have said

They say that kids say the funniest things, but sometimes children let slip something their parents really wished they'd kept to themselves.

In a hilarious segment on ITV's This Morning on Wednesday, parents were asked to reveal the most embarrassing things their children had said about them on the show's Facebook page. 

Many confessions - read out by presenters Rylan Clark-Neal and Emma Willis -  involved alcohol, including the child who said they had to help their drunk mother out of a bush she'd fallen in.

Some children got very personal, including the ones who informed complete strangers that their mothers were on their periods.

Many parents were mortified when their children told teachers and even people they'd never met that they enjoy a drink.

One commented: 'In an assembly about kindness my six-year-old was desperate to tell everyone what the definition of kindness was to her. Her definition was getting my mummy a glass of wine from the kitchen.'

'My daughter had to use the word absolute in a sentence as part of her homework so she wrote my mum loves Absolut vodka,' another commented.

A third said: 'I knew things had to change when my 7 year old daughter wrote in her school book, "all we have in our fridge is dog food & wine."'

Many children embarrassed their parents by talking about their drinking habits

Others made very private remarks about their mortified parents to people they barely knew.

'My daughter was practising her spellings and the words she had to use was always, I was moritifed on parents evening when I read she had written ''My mum uses Always Ultra,''' one said.

Another commented: 'After an emergency c section with my youngest I had to have a catheter fitted for 10 days and my 4 year old told his teacher ''my mummy just sits on the settee and wees''.'

'I was standing in a hardware shop with my then 3 yr old daughter. It was full of old men, she decided to pull my skirt up and say to the old men ''look at my mummies new Minnie mouse knickers [sic],''' a third said. 

Others gave very personal details about their parents to complete strangers, including stories about their sex lives and periods
