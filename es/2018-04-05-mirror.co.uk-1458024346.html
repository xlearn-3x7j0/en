First amputee jockey to ride race over the Grand National fences

Guy Disney is set to make history next week when he aims to become the first amputee jockey to ride a race over the famous Grand National fences at Aintree.

The former army captain rides with a prosthetic lower right leg after the vehicle in which he was travelling in Afghanistan was hit by a rocket-propelled grenade in July 2009.

Disney became the first amputee jockey to win a race under rules in Britain when gaining victory aboard Rathlin Rose in last year's Royal Artillery Gold Cup at Sandown, and the pair teamed up for a repeat success in the same race in February.

Disney's intended ride in the Randox Health Foxhunters' Chase comes after he assembled a syndicate to buy Gallery Exhibition, trained by Grand National-winning trainer Kim Bailey.

Speaking to The Jockey Club's Love The Jumps podcast, Bailey said: "It will be an extraordinary feat.

"I saw Guy when he was in hospital, in Selly Oak, so I've known the story from day one. When he was lying in hospital, all he wanted to do was ride in a race and get back in the saddle and that was his ultimate dream.

"We all thought he had no chance really. And he then rode in a charity race and finished second.

"Since then he's moved on to prove himself under rules and in point-to-points. The hours he's spent with Yogi Breisner and others trying to help him to get back in the saddle and ride and get the BHA (British Horseracing Authority) to agree to let him ride, have been extraordinary."

Gallery Exhibition has been bought with the specific aim of running in the Aintree race for amateurs on April 12 and is a horse Disney already knows well.

Bailey added: "He has been here countless times schooling over my fences. Every time he came here he ended up riding Gallery Exhibition, who was a horse owned by four people beforehand.

"So he has known the horse for a long time, come here and schooled him again recently, and then the horse has come up for sale because one of the owners died, so the partnership was dissolved.

"Guy very quickly got his Somerset Racing team together to buy the horse so that he could ride him in the Foxhunters."

Unlike his jockey, Gallery Exhibition has raced over the Grand National fences previously, coming fifth in the 2016 Topham Chase.

Gallery Exhibition and Disney teamed up for the first time on course when finishing third at Musselburgh on February 3.

Aintree will be supporting the military on Grand National Thursday by providing hundreds of tickets to military veterans and specifically from the combat wounded community.
