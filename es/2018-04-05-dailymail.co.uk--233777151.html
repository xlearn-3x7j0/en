Brooklyn father found out his daughter died on Facebook

A Brooklyn father discovered his young daughter died on Facebook, two days after the child's stepdad allegedly abused her before she took her last breath.

Three-year-old Bella Edwards was pronounced dead on Monday. Police say the child appeared to have been both physically and sexually abused.

Her furious and upset biological father, Kellon Edwards, 35, revealed his ex-partner and mother to Bella, Shamika Gonzalez, failed to inform him about the horrific incident and he found out via Facebook on Wednesday, he told the New York Post.

'I didn't hear anything from [Gonzalez] until 7am today,' Edwards said, after his mother saw a Facebook post and first informed him.

'Nobody told me anything and when she did reach out, she told me she was "sorry".'

Mark Jenkins, 32, has been charged with murder and assault of the little girl, who he had been babysitting - along with her infant brother - at the time their mother was away.

The ex-convict, from Rockaway Park, was alone with the two children for about eight hours that day, Queens District Attorney Richard A. Brown said in a press conference.

'This is a terribly disturbing case. The victim here is an innocent little girl, whose body revealed traumatic abuse injuries,' Brown said.

NYPD Chief of Detectives Robert Boyce believes the child bled to death internally after being brutally beaten near her abdomen.

Edwards told the Post he never met Bella's stepdad, and that his former partner and mom to Bella met the alleged killer at an Applebee’s, where they both worked.

'I’ve never had a conversation with him, which seems weird to me that this man never even had a conversation with the child’s father and wanted to take care of her,' Edwards said.

Gonzalez discovered her daughter unconscious on a bed when she returned to their Rockaway Park residence later on Monday.
