6 greatest Champions League turnarounds as Man City face vs Liverpool

Manchester City have a mountain to climb after Liverpool humbled Pep Guardiola's men at Anfield, scored three and kept them out at the other end.

Mo Salah, Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain and Sadio Mane all scored in a thrilling opening half-hour that blew City away.

What was more impressive, and surprising, was their game management from that point onwards, keeping City out and ensuring no away goal was taken to provide encouragement heading back to the Etihad.

Tie over, right? Well, the Blues will have to believe otherwise and there are a few occasions in the past when the hosts have rallied to flip the script.

Here we remember six of the best - can City create history next week to add to the greatest comebacks?

Barcelona 6 PSG 1 (Barcelona win 6-5 on agg) - March 2017

First leg: PSG 4-0 Barcelona

Second leg: Barcelona 6-1 PSG

After being humbled 4-0 in the first leg in Paris, few gave Barcelona a chance of overturning such a big deficit in the second leg at the Nou Camp but in Luis Enrique's final season as head coach that is exactly what Lionel Messi and Co pulled off.

Barca surged ahead through Luis Suarez, an own goal from Layvin Kurzawa and a Messi penalty only for Edinson Cavani to hit back for PSG.

All looked lost for the Spanish giants but Neymar scored after 88 minutes and with an injury-time penalty before Sergi Roberto struck the final blow five minutes into stoppage time.

Chelsea vs Napoli, 2012

First leg: Napoli 3-1 Chelsea

Second leg: Chelsea 4-1 Napoli (aet)

It was destiny in 2012 after Andre Villas-Boas' Blues were humbled 3-1 in the cauldron of the San Paolo in Naples.

Roman Abramovich pulled the trigger and Roberto Di Matteo stepped in, sparking an incredible comeback with Didier Drogba, John Terry and Frank Lampard scoring at Stamford Bridge to force extra-time.

Branislav Ivanovic found himself unmarked on the penalty spot inside the Napoli penalty area in extra-time and duly finishes with aplomb, crashing home the winner - and the rest was history.

Deportivo 4 AC Milan 0 (Deportivo win 5-4 on agg) - April 2004

First leg: AC Milan 4-1 Deportivo

Second leg: Deportivo 4-0 AC Milan

No eyebrows were raised when Serie A giants Milan thumped Deportivo 4-1 in the first leg at San Siro, with superstars Kaka, Andriy Shevchenko and Andrea Pirlo living up to their star billing.

But three goals in a remarkable first half at Riazor, sparked by Walter Pandiani's opener, turned the tie around.

The Rossoneri were hanging on until the 76th minute, when Fran sent Deportivo through.

Monaco 3 Real Madrid 1 (Monaco progress on away goals after 5-5 agg score) - April 2004

First leg: Real Madrid 4-2 Monaco

Second leg: Monaco 3-1 Real Madrid

Real Madrid should have read the small print when they sent Fernando Morientes to Monaco on loan in 2003.

Annoyingly, he scored against them in the first leg of this quarter-final tie, but they won 4-2.

A healthy lead established, Los Blancos headed for Monte Carlo full of confidence, only for Morientes to once again stick the knife into his employers, with Ludovic Giuly's brace sealing their fate.

Barcelona 4 AC Milan 0 (Barcelona win 4-2 on agg) - March 2013

First leg: AC Milan 2-0 Barcelona

Second leg: Barcelona 4-0 AC Milan

Milan, by 2013 no longer the all-conquering side of old, had scored two unanswered goals in the first leg at San Siro.

Never before had a team recovered from 2-0 down without an away goal.

But there was no reckoning with the magic of Messi, as his brace preceded goals from David Villa and Jordi Alba.

Man United 3 Olympiacos 0 (United win 3-2 on agg) - March 2014

First leg: Olympiacos 2-0 Man United

Second leg: Man United 3-0 Olympiacos

Patience with Sir Alex Ferguson's successor David Moyes was already wearing thin among the Old Trafford faithful.

A 2-0 defeat to Greek side Olympiacos a month earlier did little to aid the Scot.

But Robin van Persie gave Moyes a little longer in the hotseat by scoring a hat-trick in Manchester to steer United into the quarter-finals, a stage at which Bayern Munich proved too strong for them.
