Ian Poulter celebrates winning the Houston Open in style

Ian Poulter claimed his first US strokeplay title and the last place at the Masters after winning a play-off at the Houston Open on Sunday. 

The Englishman took the title and qualified for the first major of the year in dramatic fashion and so celebrated in style. 

Poulter, who usually pulls out something special for Europe at the Ryder Cup, posted on his Instagram story his pure delight after winning the tournament.

With a beer in his hand in a car, he posted a video of him shouting 'yes' and said: 'Cheers everyone.

'Oh my God, Houston Open, amazing, we're off to Augusta baby, see you later.' 

His story continued with a video of himself and Henrik Stenson in the latter's private jet taking off for Orlando.

With more loud cheering, Poulter said: 'Orlando baby and then we're going to Augusta on Tuesday with the H. Yes!'

Poulter also posted a picture on Instagram of the pair together next to Stenson's jet with the caption: 'Thank you @henrikstenson for waiting for me @netjets you are the best ... back home in Orlando ready to rest tomorrow and head to @themasters on Tuesday.'

After making it to Orlando Poulter posted another video on his story on the jet, saying: 'We are back in Orlando, just touched down.

'My good friend Henrik decided to wait for me, thank you Henrik and he's going to drive me home as well.'

On his return home Poulter posted a video of balloons outside and said: 'Now that is a lovely, lovely, lovely return home, some balloons. God love them.'

To complete his celebrations he said: 'It's midnight, I'm sitting on the sofa, I'm catching up with everyone and I'm having a bit of cold pizza.

'Thanks for all the nice messages guys, really can't thank you enough.

'It's been amazing.'

After taking a bite of the pizza he signed off by saying: 'See you in Augusta.'  

Beau Hossler had a one-shot lead on the 18th hole but he missed a birdie putt, while Poulter made a clutch putt on the 72nd hole to force a play-off.

In the decider the 23-year-old American found a bunker and then chipped into the water as the Englishman took the title and qualified for the Masters with a par.  

Last season the 42-year old thought he had lost his card in America before being handed a reprieve after a recalculation.

The win was his first in six years but Poulter is now fully focused on getting back to his best and earning a place at the Ryder Cup. 

Poulter told a press conference streamed by the PGA Tour: 'It's tough when you're down, when you can't see the light at the end of the tunnel, when everything seems to be going wrong, it's hard, it gets you down.

'But it's not the first time I've gone through some roller coasters. That's as low as I've ever been, that's as far down the world rankings as I've been, questioning whether you've got a Tour card or not - it isn't very good.

'It's not very good for your mental strength. It's not very good for your psyche, but to reassess, to reform the team, Paul Dunkley, my agent, has done an incredible job... simplifying my life to get me back on track, it's been amazing. The journey continues.

'I've had 19 good years on tour and I guess I've got another couple coming. There's life in the old dog yet.'
