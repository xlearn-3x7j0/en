Paul Lambert says Stoke have 'great chance' to avoid relegation

Last Updated: 05/04/18 3:17pm

Stoke boss Paul Lambert says his players must stand up and be counted if they are to win their battle against relegation from the Premier League.

The struggling Potters are second from bottom of the table on 27 points, and three points from safety, with just six games left to save themselves from the drop.

Next up for Stoke is a home fixture against Tottenham on Saturday, but Lambert claims his team have the self-belief to cause an upset against Mauricio Pochettino's side.

Lambert said: "Since I've been here I haven't had to pick the team up because of how well they have played. We just haven't got the results our performances have deserved.

"The lads have the belief we can do it and that's important to me. We need everyone to stand up and if we do that then we have a great chance.

"I always look to win the next game (against Spurs), I don't target particular fixtures.

"You can win any game you go into."

The Stoke boss revealed that he has injury worries over defender Kurt Zouma and forward Mame Biram Diouf, but expects the duo to be fit for the clash with Spurs at the bet365 Stadium.

He added: "We are keeping an eye on both Kurt Zouma and Mame Diouf who we hope to have back for the weekend, but we will just have to wait and see.

"Zouma trained this morning and Diouf has a kick from Arsenal."

Eric Maxim Choupo-Moting is ruled out with a groin injury, and Greek defender Kostas Stafylidis is also missing after suffering a broken rib on international duty.
