How Young Lions have fared for their clubs since U20 World Cup glory

Almost 10 months have passed since England’s Under-20 team won the World Cup in South Korea.

Not since Sir Geoff Hurst’s hat-trick in 1966 had a Three Lions side won an international title, but Dominic Calvert-Lewin’s first half goal against Venezuela was enough to seal the trophy.

The Young Lions had progressed through a group including South Korea, Argentina and Guinea, before edging past Costa Rica and Mexico, then Italy in the semi-finals.

The victory meant Paul Simpson’s team made history as the first England youth team to win the competition, after semi-final defeats in 1981 and 1993.

But how have those who started that final progressed this season? Is it likely that any of them will make the England first team in years to come?

Here, we've run the rule over each of the XI who started the final, with an honourable mention for the substitutes on the day, too.

Goalkeeper- Freddie Woodman

From South Korea to Scotland. After saving a penalty from Venezuela’s Adalberto Penaranda in the final, Woodman now currently finds himself at Aberdeen.

In the months leading up to the final, Newcastle had sent Woodman out on loan, with Rob Elliot and Karl Darlow ahead of him in the pecking order.

He was sent to Kilmarnock, where he played 14 times.

On Transfer Deadline Day at the end of January, he joined Aberdeen.

Woodman has also stepped up a level on the international stage and has had two caps for the England Under-21 side.

Right-back- Jonjoe Kenny

Kenny, who joined Everton at the age of nine, made his first team debut on the final day of the 2015/16 season, coming on as a substitute against Norwich City.

But he had to wait until earlier this season to be given a proper run in the first team.

David Unsworth, the Toffees' under-23 team manager, replaced the axed Ronald Koeman on a caretaker basis and immediately installed Kenny in the team ahead of Cuco Martina.

He kept his place after Sam Allardyce's appointment and has played 20 times in all competitions so far this season, contributing to several clean sheets.

With Seamus Coleman back after serious injury, he finds himself on the sidelines again, but can consider this campaign his breakthrough season.

Centre-back- Fikayo Tomori

Chelsea and loan deals go hand in hand. The club is famous for the size of its loan system and Tomori is no different to others gaining experience at other clubs.

Tomori joined the Blues at under-eight level but has made just one appearance for the club in the 13 years he has been there.

Last year, he spent time under Chris Houghton at Brighton and Hove Albion and is now learning under the stewardship of Nigel Adkins at Hull City, where he's made 21 apperances.

Centre-back- Jake Clarke-Salter

Another Chelsea youth product, another loanee. Clarke-Salter joined the Blues in 2006, spending his youth career with the Blues, bar a year at Sutton United in 2007/08.

Since January, he's been on-loan at Sunderland, where he's made eight appearances. Earlier this month, he was sent off in consecutive games for the Mackems.

In 21 appearances for Chelsea, he’s scored one goal.

His only time on the pitch for his parent club came in a 5-1 demolition of Nottingham Forest in the Carabao Cup.

Left-back- Kyle Walker-Peters

Walker-Peters has spent all of his career at Tottenham Hotspur, signing for the club in 2013.

In 2015, he made his senior debut in the post-season tour of Malaysia and Australia, but continued to play for the under-23 side, performing consistently well.

He made his competitive debut for Tottenham at the start of this season, earning a man of the match award in a 2-0 win away to Newcastle United.

In February, he scored his first goal for Spurs in a 6–1 win over Rochdale in the FA Cup. He has Serge Aurier and Kieran Trippier ahead of him in the Tottenham team.

Centre-midfield - Lewis Cook

Last week Cook’s grandad picked up a £17,000 cheque after the Bournemouth midfielder made his senior England debut before his 26th birthday.

The Under-20 captain has really pushed on since last summer's tournament, becoming a regular starter in the Cherries first team.

Cook, signed by Bournemouth from Leeds in 2016, was rewarded with a call-up to Gareth Southgate's squad for the pre-World Cup friendlies this month.

Centre-midfield - Josh Onomah

Born in Enfield, just a few miles from Tottenham’s new training ground, Onomah joined Spurs' academy system in 2015.

He was first called up to the senior team in 2015, making his debut against Burnley in the FA Cup third round.

Like several other youth products at Spurs, Onomah has been loaned out, travelling north to Aston Villa.

He’s had a successful season for Steve Bruce's team, nailing down a place in the engine room and scoring three goals.

Right-midfield - Kieran Dowell

A gifted player, Dowell was signed by Everton’s academy at the age of seven.

At the beginning of this season, he was sent out on loan to Nottingham Forest.

During the first half of the season, Dowell starred for Forest, netting a stunning hat trick in a 4-3 win over Hull.

His form has tailed off since Mark Warburton was replaced by Aitor Karanka, but he can consider the season a success, with 10 goals and five assists to hisname.

Left-midfield - Ademola Lookman

Much to Everton fans' dismay, Lookman, a fast, tricky winger, joined German side RB Leipzig on loan in January.

He reportedly rejected moves to several English sides, choosing instead to try his luck in the Budesliga.

Lookman's debut was something of a dream as he scored a crucial late winner against Borussia Mönchengladbach.

He's yet to add to his tally.

Centre-forward - Dominic Solanke

It’s unusual to find a player of such a young age who's played for two of England’s biggest clubs, but Solanke is a rare talent.

The player of the tournament at last year's World Cup, Solanke scored four goals as England romped to victory in South Korea.

Struggling to force his way into the first team at Chelsea, he opted to sign for Liverpool last summer when his contract with the Blues ended.

He's made 17 appearances for the Reds, but has yet to find the net. He was, however, given his full England debut in November in a friendly against Brazil. One for the future.

Centre forward- Dominic Calvert-Lewin

The hero of the final.

Calvert-Lewin’s 35th minute strike, seizing on a rebound off Venezuela keeper Wuilker Farinez, which proved the difference and created history.

So it’s perhaps no surprise that the striker has become a regular fixture in the Everton team, notching five goals in 36 appearances.

Loan moves Stalybridge Celtic and Northampton Town have surely aided Calvert-Lewin’s development, who has already found the back of the net twice for the England under-21s.

Substitutes

Sheyi Ojo

Back in August, winger Ojo joined Fulham from Liverpool on a season-long loan.

He's made 20 appearances for the Cottagers, scoring four goals.

Ainsley Maitland-Niles

One of the few positives at Arsenal this season, Maitland-Niles, a midfielder by trade, earned praise for a string of performances at left-back, deputising for the injured Nacho Monreal.

He's been rewarded with four caps for the England under-21 side.
