Manny Pacquiao to fight Lucas Matthysse for WBA title

  • Manny Pacquiao will step back into the ring at the tender age of 39 this summer
  • Lucas Matthysse beat Thailand's Tewa Kiram to win the vacant belt in January
  • Pacquiao last fought in a unanimous points loss to Australian Jeff Horn last July

Published: 10:49 EDT, 3 April 2018 | Updated: 11:09 EDT, 3 April 2018

Manny Pacquiao will fight Argentine welterweight champion Lucas Matthysse for the World Boxing Association (WBA) crown in Kuala Lumpur in July, promoter Oscar de la Hoya said.

'Signed, sealed, and delivered: Proud to officially announce that WBA welterweight world champion @MatthysseLucas will put his title on the line against @mannypacquiao in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia on Saturday night July 14 (US time),' De la Hoya said on Twitter.

Matthysse beat Thailand's Tewa Kiram to win the vacant WBA welterweight belt in January and take his record to 38-4-0.

Manny Pacquiao will step back into the ring to fight for the WBA welterweight title belt

Promoter Oscar De La Hoya made the announcement on his official social media account

Pacquiao, 39, last fought in a unanimous points loss to Australian Jeff Horn in July last year. He is ranked second in the division and has an overall record of 57-6-2. 

The Filipino, who will hold training camps in his hometown, General Santos City, and Manila, admitted last month that he will be the underdog against his Argentine foe.

'I knew, this is going to be a tough fight. Matthysse is also a knockout artist. But I'm excited to fight and be a world champion again. So, expect me to train hard for this fight,' he added. 

'I'm the underdog in this fight but I'm used to it. It serves as a big motivation for me to train and fight hard to win the crown.'
