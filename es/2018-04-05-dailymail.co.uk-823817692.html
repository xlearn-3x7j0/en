Tottenham start installing seats to new 62,000-seater stadium

  • Tottenham hope to have their new stadium completed in time for next season
  • The 62,000-seater stadium will cost Spurs in the region of £850million
  • Spurs have begun installing the stadium's navy blue seats into the stands 

Published: 10:25 EDT, 4 April 2018 | Updated: 06:18 EDT, 5 April 2018

Tottenham's new stadium is beginning to take shape as the club reveal the start of the seat-installation process.

Spurs are due to move into their new 62,000-seater stadium, reported to cost around £850million to complete, at the start of next season and have spent there transitional season using Wembley as a temporary home. 

And as they make steady progress with the main structure, the North London club have begun the lengthy process of installing the seats for their new home. 

Seats are being installed into the stands of Tottenham's new 62,000-seater stadium

'The navy blue seats continue to be installed within the stadium bowl. #SpursNewStadium,' Spurs wrote on Instagram. 

The Stadium itself, which chairman Daniel Levy has explained will be known as 'The Tottenham Hotspur Stadium' until a suitable sponsor is chosen, includes features such asLED lighting on its outer shell which will see it glow blue for evening matches.

Spurs are hoping to have their £850million stadium finished before the start of next season 

As well as this, a retractable pitch will accommodate NFL fixtures as part of a 10-year agreement - the first of which will take place on October 14 when the Seattle Seahawks play the Oakland Raiders.

As expected, watching Spurs at their new stadium won't be cheap either, with the least-expensive season ticket price being £795 for adults and the most expensive requiring a vast investment to the sum of £1,995 - the costliest anywhere in the Premier League. 

Much is still to be done though and with their deadline for completion fast approaching, Levy revealed they may require their first hand-full of Premier League fixtures to be played away from home.
