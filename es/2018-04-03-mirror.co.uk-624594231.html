Live stream information, team news, odds and more for Sevilla vs Bayern Munich

Sevilla will attempt to draw Bayern Munich out of their comfort zone and take the game to the Germans according to Sevilla coach Vincenzo Montella.

Sevilla have surprisingly reached the European Cup quarter-finals for the first time since 1958 but while they are clearly the underdogs against the five-times champions, they are undefeated at home against German clubs in 11 matches.

"Bayern are accustomed to scoring a lot of goals, to having the ball. We have to do the same so we make them do things they're not used to,"

The Spanish side were runners up in their group to Liverpool, but finished above Spartak Moscow and Maribor stunning Manchester United to reach the last eight.

Bundesliga leaders Bayern also came second in the group stage, PSG tipping them to the top spot, with Celtic and Anderlecht getting eliminated, before they thrashed Besiktas 8-1 on aggregate.

Where and when is the game?

Sevilla host Bayern Munich at the Ramon Sanchez Pizjuan Stadium on Tuesday 3rd April. Kick off is 19:45 BST.

Early team news

Sevilla

The La Liga side will be without Ever Banega who is suspended, while Sebastien Corchia is out with a groin injury.

Bayern Munich

Bayern Munich have included Arturo Vidal and Juan Bernat in their squad for the first leg of the Champions League quarter-final at Sevilla on Tuesday after the pair passed late fitness tests.

Midfielder Vidal and fullback Bernat both missed Saturday's 6-0 demolition of Borussia Dortmund with minor injuries as the Bavarians moved to within one win of a sixth straight league title with six fixtures still remaining.
