McIlroy and Garcia enjoy final practice as they prepare for Masters

Rory McIlroy stepped up his preparations for the start of this year's Masters as he enjoyed one final practice round at Augusta National on Wednesday.

With the par 3 competition due to start later that afternoon, those fighting to take the green jacket off the back off defending champion Sergio Garcia had one last chance to get to know the famous Augusta course before the Masters kicks off on Thursday.

McIlroy, who has been drawn in a trio with 2013 champion Adam Scott and Spaniard Jon Rahm for the opening round on Thursday, is seeking to complete the career grand slam with a victory at Augusta.

The Norther Irishman, currently ranked World No 7, will be one of the last groups to tee off at 1.38pm (6.38pm BST) as he bids to improve on his best Masters effort of fourth back in 2015. 

McIlroy finished tied for seventh at last year's tournament as he watched Spaniard Sergio Garcia march to victory and his first major championship when he edged past Justin Rose in a famous play-off.

Garcia, fresh from hosting the champions dinner on Tuesday, was in action in front of a hefty Augusta crowd as he prepares for his upcoming defence.

With a field that includes a resurgent Tiger Woods, 2015 winner Jordan Spieth, US PGA champion Justin Thomas and World No 1 Dustin Johnson, there will be plenty of competition for both Garcia and McIlroy. 

Woods's first Masters appearance since finishing tied for 17th in 2015 may divert some of the limelight from Garcia when he tees off at 10.42am (3.42pm BST) alongside Marc Leishman and Tommy Fleetwood.

Woods has been tipped as a favourite after his impressive start to the season after his comeback, but one golfing legend in Jack Nicklaus believes McIlroy is the man to beat.

Six-time Masters winner Nicklaus, nicknamed the Golden Bear during his playing days, feels McIlroy should be top of the betting market following his impressive victory in the Arnold Palmer Invitational last month.

'I talked to him this weekend and saw a few things and he is really swinging well, the best I've ever seen him swing,' Nicklaus said. 'He obviously putted very well at Bay Hill so he's going to be tough to beat.

'If you picked anybody he'd be the number one to pick and rightly so. He's probably playing better than anyone else.'

McIlroy admits he feels the pressure of attempting to join Nicklaus, Woods, Gary Player, Ben Hogan and Gene Sarazen as the only players to have won all four major titles.

But Nicklaus believes the Masters is the easiest one to win due to the limited field and type of game which suits the Augusta course.

'You play it every year, you can prepare for it easier, once you play the greens you should know them,' Nicklaus told the Golf Channel. 'If you're not a long, high-ball player you can eliminate half the field before you even start.

'No tournament is easy to win, don't misunderstand me, but of the majors a top player has a better shot here than any other place.'
