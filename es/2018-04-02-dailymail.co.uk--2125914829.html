City stars pull up to training ahead of showdown with Liverpool

  • Manchester City face Liverpool in Champions League quarter-final first leg 
  • Pep Guardiola's side are enjoying remarkable season but face tricky task  
  • City stars arrived for training on overcast day in variety of luxury cars

Published: 09:44 EDT, 2 April 2018 | Updated: 09:57 EDT, 2 April 2018

Manchester City's stars arrived at training ahead of their crucial Champions League quarter-final first leg against Liverpool. 

Pep Guardiola's side cruised into training driving a variety of supercars with most looking serious, though Raheem Sterling cracked a grin as he pulled up in his Mercedes. 

Kevin De Bruyne and Gabriel Jesus both wore their hoods up, with the Brazilian offering a thumbs up to cameras following their arrival.  

Raheem Sterling grinned as he pulled up to Manchester City training on Monday

Kevin de Bruyne has been in incredible form for Manchester City so far this season

The Belgian will no doubt have a huge role to play against Liverpool on Wednesday night

It's no wonder Sterling is smiling, fresh from a goal in the 3-1 win against Everton and impressive displays as a No 10 for England. 

The former Liverpool man will be desperate to make a statement on Wednesday and continue his fine form at former stomping ground Anfield.

Ederson's bright red Ferrari stood out among the various monochrome motors and the goalkeeper will be vital to City's aspirations of progressing in Europe.  

Perhaps there was a more serious look to the City stars as they recognise the tough task ahead of them. 

Ederson stood out in his red Ferrari among the monochrome cars arriving 

Gabriel Jesus offered a thumbs up on a cloudy day in Manchester as his cruised in

Nicolas Otamendi drove his black Rolls Royce in but his face was almost obscured

The Reds boast a history littered with famous European nights and despite trailing the league-leaders by 18 points, may fancy their chances over two legs.

Guardiola spoke of Jurgen Klopp's front three with glowing praise and is not underestimating the task at hand for his players.  

'It's not just him (Mo Salah). It's Sadio Mane and Roberto Firmino, all three. They are almost unstoppable,' he said.

'The way Liverpool play is so complicated for us. We know that. They are so quick, they are so good and it is tough, but it is a quarter-final of the Champions League so we cannot expect it is going to be easy.' 

Sergio Aguero was not involved against Everton as Jesus started at Goodison Park 

Kyle Walker played the full 90 minutes for City in their 3-1 victory over Everton
