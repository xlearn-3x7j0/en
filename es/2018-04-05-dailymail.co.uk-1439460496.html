Jack Nicklaus cries tears of joy after 15-year-old grandson aces Par 3

The Masters officially starts on Thursday, but Jack Nicklaus's 15-year-old grandson produced the week's first highlight by filling in for his legendary grandfather during the Par 3 Contest and sinking his first hole-in-one. 

'I can't talk,' the 78-year-old, six-time Masters champion said after seeing his grandson ace the ninth hole. 'Got a few tears.'

Gary Nicklaus Jr. was caddying for his grandfather, who previously offered him the chance to step in during the Par 3 Contest and even predicted the outcome.

'I said I think you're going to make a hole-in-one,' a tearful Nicklaus told NBC Sports.  

Nicklaus Jr. aced the ninth hole by shooting over a water hazard and rolling the ball from the back of the green into the hole.

'I was thinking "no way" and then it looked like it stopped for a second an inch behind the hole,' Nicklaus Jr. said, adding, 'I didn't celebrate at first because I didn't think I saw it.'

And as if it wasn't enough to share the moment with his grandfather, Nicklaus Jr. hit the hole-in-one in the presence of former Masters champions Tom Watson, who won Wednesday's Par 3 Contest, and Gary Player. 

Player even added to the moment by repeatedly saying, 'You're going to like that,' as the ball inched towards the pin. 

'This is probably my favorite week of the year,' said Nicklaus Jr., who is known as 'GT.' 'This is a special tournament. Golf is a sport that I love. And for that to happen is just unbelievable.'

A tearful Nicklaus, who won 18 majors during his career, rated his grandson's ace the number one moment of his career. 

'We talked about three days ago and said 'GT do you want to hit the ball on nine?' and he said "sure,"' Nicklaus explained. 'I said, "well I think you're going to make a hole-in-one." It's his first.'

'You always want the best for your kids and your grandkids and to watch a grandkid come along and knock in a hole-in-one on the biggest venue in the world…. wow. I mean what a day.'

The legendary 68-year-old Tom Watson, who has won eight major championships and retired from the professional circuit in 2016, nabbed first in the contest with a six-under-par 21. 

Watson, who previously scored the top spot in the Par 3 Contest in 1982, is now the oldest winner in the tournament's history.

'After I birdied the first four holes, I thought it would be really neat to win the Par 3,' he said. 'I won it a long time ago and of course I didn't win the tournament.

'I'm not playing the tournament this year, so I said let's go for it.'  

Since its inception in 1960, no Par 3 Contest winner has ever gone on to win in the Masters the same year, which has caused many golfers to view first place as a bit of a jinx.

Most golfers don't even keep track of their score, instead choosing to enjoy the day with their family before the real competition begins.

The event allows both tournament participants and noncompeting past champions to hit the green, which in this case consists of just nine holes in the Augusta National grounds.

There have been 94 holes-in-one through the years, including a whopping nine in just 2016 alone. 

American Tony Finau helped this year's count with his own hole-in-one, but the PGA golfer managed to injure his ankle in the process. 

Finau had already scored the ace when he began running across the green in excitement, only to hurt his ankle. Although he kept playing in the contest, he appeared to be limping after the impromptu celebration. 

But there were plenty of sweet instances sprinkled throughout the day, such as UK golfer Danny Willet helping his son putt or American Charley Hoffman giving his daughter a high five.

One especially touching moment was delivered by none other than Justin Thomas. 

The 24-year-old American golfer, who is ranked second in the PGA tour, no doubt made one young fan's life when he pulled a boy out of the crowd and had him complete a hole on the green.

After the boy finished the shot, Thomas autographed the ball and gave it to him to take home and cherish forever.

The chase for the green jacket begins on Thursday, and this year's competition has been hotly anticipated with the return of Tiger Woods for the first time since 2015. 

'When Tiger walked onto the range today, you can tell there's an anticipation and an excitement from the crowd to watch him compete again,' English golfer Justin Rose told the Associated Press on Sunday.

Many are hoping to see Woods compete with current golfing stars like Jordan Spieth, Rickie Fowler, and Rory McIlroy, who is trying for a career Gland Slam.

The Masters is the one missing competition keeping McIlroy, 28, from becoming the sixth golfer to have ever won all four of golf's majors during his career.

Woods, Nicklaus, along with Gene Sarazen, Ben Hogan, and Gary Player, have all earned the Grand Slam.

'I'm an avid fan of the history of the game, and I know a win here and what that would mean and where that would put me in history alongside some of the greatest that have ever played this game,' McIlroy told ESPN. 

'And that would mean an awful lot to me.'
