MailOnline Fantasy Football team of the week: Kasper Schmeichel shines

It proved a lucrative weekend for the London sides as they took six places in this week's MailOnline Fantasy Football team of the week.

In gameweek 30, braces from Tottenham's Dele Alli, Arsenal's Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang and West Ham's Marko Arnautovic all ensured double figure points returns.  

But the top scoring star this time around came in between the sticks as Leicester City stopper Kasper Schmeichel saved a penalty and saw out a clean sheet in the Foxes 2-0 win over Brighton.  

Tottenham and Man City run the midfield in the MailOnline Fantasy Football team of the week

GK: Kasper Schmiechel - Leicester - 1 game, 2 saves, 1 pen save, 1 clean sheet = 13 points 

Claude Puel will have thanked his lucky stars that Schmeichel was in majestic form on Saturday. 

With big clubs circling, the Dane did no harm to his growing reputation.

Leicester owe their clean sheet at Brighton to the decisive penalty save by Kasper Schmeichel

DF: Ben Chilwell - Leicester - 1 game, 1 assist, 1 clean sheet = 10 points

As Christian Fuchs led the way during the title-winning campaign, it makes it even more impressive that Chilwell managed to usurp him. 

Provides the Foxes with a genuine threat from wide areas as shown by his assist at the weekend. 

DF: Shkodran Mustafi - Arsenal - 1 game, 1 clean sheet = 7 points

Arsenal found themselves under pressure for the opening 30 minutes on Sunday as relegation-threatened Stoke sought a crucial three points.

But the Gunners rear-guard held firm and a large part of that was due to the calmness and composure shown by German Mustafi.  

As Stoke wilted, Arsenal remained disciplined as Shkodran Mustafi ensured a clean sheet

DF: Arthur Masuaku - West Ham - 1 game, 1 assist, 1 clean sheet = 10 points

With his spitting incident at Wigan resigned to history for Masuaku, he produced a performance fans had become accustomed to at the London Stadium.

One of two Hammers to hit double figures after the battering of Southampton.  

MF: Dele Alli - Tottenham - 1 game, 2 goals = 14 points

Heading into the game at Stamford Bridge, many questioned whether Alli had a place for England this summer.

Well, he answered anything Three Lions boss Gareth Southgate may have wanted to ask him with a stunning brace to defeat Chelsea in a 3-1 win. Clinical. 

Dele Alli provided the magic moment for Tottenham as they came from behind to beat Chelsea

MF: Christian Eriksen - Tottenham - 1 game, 1 goal = 8 points

Having fallen behind to an Alvaro Morata header, Tottenham were in need of a moment that could spark them in to life. Step forward Christian Eriksen. 

The Danish playmaker took aim from 30 yards with a phenomenal effort that had dip and swerve to make it 1-1. Chelsea collapsed thereafter.  

MF: David Silva - Man City - 1 game, 2 assists = 8 points

Manchester City have an array of weapons in their arsenal; Kevin De Bruyne, Leroy Sane, Raheem Sterling and Sergio Aguero all take plenty of plaudits.

But David Silva, arguably, makes them tick as proven by his two assists in the rout at Goodison Park on Saturday.  

David Silva laid on two goals as Manchester City showed their class in the win over Everton

MF: Leroy Sane - Man City - 1 game, 1 goal = 8 points

The 22-year-old wide player has a scary amount of potential to become a world beater for Pep Guardiola's side. 

A stunning strike opened the scoring and he is having a fine season. Serious contender to win PFA young player of the year.  

FWD: Marko Arnautovic - West Ham - 1 game, 2 goals = 12 points

After the unsavoury scenes in the home defeat to Burnley, West Ham needed a player to step up and become a cult hero.

Arnautovic proved untouchable against a feeble Saints backline and joined Masuaku in the team of the week having netted twice.  

Pressure was building on West Ham but a brace from Marko Arnautovic secured three points

FWD: Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang - Arsenal - 1 game, 2 goals = 12 points

Had he not given the Gunners second penalty to substitute Alexandre Lacazette, the Gabon striker would have taken top spot in this week's team.

A converted penalty was followed by a penalty box finish as the January signing put the game beyond the Potters.

FWD: Alexis Sanchez - Man United - 1 game, 1 goal, 1 assist = 10 points

It has been a tough start to life at Manchester United for Sanchez but against Swansea he produced one of his best performances in a Reds shirt.

A goal was followed by an assist and with his old side Arsenal still yet to visit Old Trafford, he would love to be firing on all cylinders until the season's end.  

Alexis Sanchez showed what it meant as he scored for Manchester United at home to Swansea
