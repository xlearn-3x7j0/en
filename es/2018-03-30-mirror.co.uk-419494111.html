Lingard makes huge Class of '92 claim

Jesse Lingard has revealed how Manchester United's famed Class of '92 are inspiring the next generation of Old Trafford stars to create new history.

David Beckham, Paul Scholes, Nicky Butt and the Neville brothers all came through the United ranks at the same time and helped the Reds to a sustained period of domestic dominance under Sir Alex Ferguson.

Lingard, Marcus Rashford, Paul Pogba and Scott McTominay have followed a similar route through the Old Trafford youth system and are using the achievements of their predecessors to help take United back to the top.

"It's all about making new history but you see the team with Paul Scholes, David Beckham, Gary Neville etc and you see what they achieved and it is only right that the likes of me, Marcus and Paul try and emulate that and win trophies,” said Lingard.

"They came through the ranks together, but my youth team went their separate ways so to have another home lad like Marcus in the team and with Paul having been here since 16, we just want to try and emulate what they did."

Lingard has emerged as a key player for United this season, after beginning the campaign on the bench and having to be patient to earn his chance.

But the 25-year-old attacking midfielder has proved his worth with a series of impressive displays and has also made himself a certainty for England's World Cup squad in Russia this summer.

"I did well on the pre-season tour but then I wasn't playing in the first few months of the season," Lingard said.

"It's always hard, but within those periods you have to try and make an impact and you always have to be ready. I was patient and I knew eventually I would get my chance.

"It's the start now. I'm comfortable in myself and my surroundings, so that makes me play stress-free and enjoy being on the ball.

“Consistency, goals and assists - that gets you confidence and puts the thought in the manager's head that he can't drop you.

"Sometimes games may not go the way you plan it and sometimes you have to do the dirty, gritty stuff well - you have to tackle, you have to run.

“I learned that when I was young and it's a good feeling to work hard for the team, to get the ball back."

Never was that trait more evident than in England's 1-1 draw with Italy on Tuesday, when Lingard won the ball in the build-up to Jamie Vardy's opener and took a quick free-kick to set it up.

Lingard also scored his first England goal on his ninth cap, as Gareth Southgate's side beat Holland 1-0 away to continue their progress ahead of this summer's eagerly-awaited tournament.

"I've had to work hard to get in the starting line-up for England,” said Lingard, speaking to BBC's Football Focus. “Now all I can do is carry on, work harder and see where it gets me.

"Playing for England is always a big honour anyway and to score my first goal was amazing. It is a massive moment in my career.

"We have a lot of games to go for United and that's the most important thing at the moment. I have to do my job here and then see what happens [with England]."
