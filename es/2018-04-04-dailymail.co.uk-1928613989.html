Trevor Bayliss challenges next generation of Test batsman to step up

Trevor Bayliss has told the next generation of Test batsmen to stand up and score the runs that would earn them the opportunity to try to solve England’s perennial problems at the top of the order.

The England coach admitted after their seventh Test of the winter became their seventh without a win that he was no nearer solving the batting problems that continue to undermine his team at the highest level.

And while Bayliss said it is ‘more than likely’ Mark Stoneman and James Vince have just done enough to keep their places in the problem opening and No 3 spots against Pakistan next month. he left the door ajar.

Trevor Bayliss and England have struggled with problems at the top of the batting order

‘The same questions are still there but I’m not sure there are better players out of the team at the moment,’ said Bayliss after England failed to force a win on the last day of the second Test here. 

‘So who do we replace them with? There are some young players on the cusp but I guess this is a call-out to the guys in county cricket. The first few matches of the season are very important.’

England want Haseeb Hameed to score the runs for Lancashire that would earn him another chance at the top of the order while Middlesex’s Nick Gubbins will come into consideration once he has recovered from a hamstring injury.

Lower down, Liam Livingstone impressed here without getting a chance in the two Tests while, intriguingly, Bayliss threw in another name as a contender for a middle order slot this summer.

Bayliss said James Vince (pictured) is likely to keep his spot for the Pakistan Tests next month

‘The other one I’ll add in who’s impressed in the nets is Ben Foakes,’ said the coach of a man whose role here has ostensibly been as back-up keeper. ‘He’s batting very well so there’s a couple of guys in this squad who can hopefully put a bit of pressure on and make the selectors job difficult which is what we want.’

The inclusion of Foakes as a specialist batsman would certainly be an interesting call because he is so good with the gloves that his director of cricket at Surrey Alec Stewart already calls him the best keeper in the world.

But Jonny Bairstow would almost certainly keep the gloves even if Foakes joined him in the England side because he has worked so hard to turn himself into a keeper of genuine Test class and he is desperate to retain his dual role.

One batsman who looks sure to line up against Pakistan at Lord’s on May 24 in the first of two Tests is Alastair Cook even though he again has to prove he retains the qualities that have made him England’s record Test runscorer.

Mark Stoneman is also set to keep his position as opening batsman for Pakistan at Lord's

Cook averaged just five against New Zealand to give him his worst record statistically in any of his 45 Test series but he is still one of the few England batsmen capable of big hundreds and Bayliss retains faith in him.

‘It’s dangerous to write off someone with 12,000 Test runs,’ said Bayliss. ‘He’s desperately disappointed he hasn’t contributed more here but speaking to him he’s still got a hunger for the team, he still wants to do well and he still thinks he’s got a role to play. Going forward we will need his experience so hopefully it’s not too long before he is back in the groove.’

There was encouragement for England in the bowling here even if they are still finding it as hard as ever to take 20 wickets to win a Test overseas. Stuart Broad genuinely looks back to his best after the work he has put into his action while Jimmy Anderson remains as good as ever after an outstanding winter.

Mark Wood returned to Test cricket in Christchurch by showing he can still have a role as ‘an enforcer’ used in short bursts overseas while Jack Leach made a good impression and needs to be persevered with, along with Mason Crane, before England play on the spin friendly pitches of Sri Lanka next winter.

That means Moeen Ali, superlative last summer, could now be facing an extended spell out of the side, particularly as he is now heading to the IPL rather than playing any first-class cricket ahead of the visit of Pakistan.

What England really need is to fast-track a young bowler of genuine pace into the Test picture and work with him specifically on away conditions but whether the self-interested domestic game will allow that remains to be seen.

At home Chris Woakes remains a force and will be back this summer while Ben Stokes remains crucial to the balance of the side and once he is fit again to bowl without a sore back it will make a huge difference to England.

For now Bayliss, who repeated his commitment to coach in all three formats for the remaining 18 months of his contract, knows that, probably for the first time, England’s Test side are a long way behind their 50-over team.

‘In the ODIs we’ve got about 16 players trying to force their way into the team but in the Test side we’ve got three or four guys still trying to make their way and prove they are good enough to play at this level,’ added Bayliss. ‘It will be an interesting chat before we play Pakistan.’
