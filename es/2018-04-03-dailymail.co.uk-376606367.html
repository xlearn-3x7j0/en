Welcome to the suburb for millennials with super-fast WiFi

A new housing development is attracting millennials in their droves by offering affordable housing to young couples struggling to get on the property ladder - and some of the fastest Internet speeds in southern California.

New Haven Ontario Ranch, a sprawling estate 35 miles from Los Angeles, offers houses from $200,000 to $500,000 and WiFi speeds of 1,000 megabits per second - meaning it only takes six seconds to download a whole movie.

And when they are not surfing the Internet, residents can enjoy a selection of pools, parks and leisure centers, which are being developed as the site grows.

Sam Shwetz, 25, moved to New Haven with his wife, Sydney, a university events assistant, after he left the Army in 2016.

Leaving behind Orange County, where the median house price is around $714,500, they bought a 1,900-square-foot property in New Haven for a more affordable $445,000.

Shwetz, a 25-year-old property manager, said he and Sydney now pay some $100 more per month for their mortgage than they previously paid in rent but maintain their decision to buy was the right one.

He told Business Insider: 'It's a trade-off — there are not a lot of amenities yet, but there are plans to do it.

'In my mind, that's why housing prices are cheap, and as they build up, housing values will go up.'

The Shwetzes are part of an increasingly lucrative market for developers, given that millennials now make up 36% of all homebuyers.

Defined as people born between 1981 and 1996, millennials have a median income is $88,200, meaning most are forced to move to the suburbs to avoid a house of their own.

The developers of New Haven are keen to tap into this market, with plans to expand the site to around 162,000 residents across 47,000 homes within a decade.

It will eventually sprawl across 1,000 acres and include several schools. 

And in a further nod to the family ambitions of young couples, the developers have already installed a 'super' playground with a zipline and performance stage. 

There is also an outdoor big screen and bar alongside picnic areas, a fire pit and a BBQ pavilion.  

Despite New Haven’s out of town location, it is less than five miles from a Metrolink station and near several major freeways.
