AC Milan pay tribute to former player Ray Wilkins ahead of Inter clash

AC Milan legend Franco Baresi held up a shirt bearing the name of Ray Wilkins prior to the Milan derby following the former Rossoneri midfielder's tragic death.

Wilkins passed away on Wednesday aged 61 after suffering a heart attack last week.

Milan are taking on Inter at the San Siro and Baresi laid a wreath behind the goal in honour of the ex-Chelsea and Manchester United star.

Fans also held up a banner holding a message loosely translated as: "Goodbye Ray - AC Milan legend."

Wilkins swapped Old Trafford for Milan in 1984 and played for three years in Italy before switching to Paris Saint-Germain.

His playing career started at Stamford Bridge before moving to United in 1979.

From PSG he headed for Glasgow and signed for Rangers in 1987. After two years he was back in London for two spells at QPR either side of a short stint at Crystal Palace.

He also represented Wycombe, Hibs, Millwall and Leyton Orient before hanging up his boots in 1997, as well as earning 84 caps for England.

Wilkins managed QPR and Fulham and later the Jordanian international team as well as spells as a No.2 at Chelsea, Watford, Millwall, England Under-21s, Fulham and Aston Villa.
