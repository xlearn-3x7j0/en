Bayern Munich stars prepare for Champions League tie against Sevilla

  • Bayern Munich face Sevilla on Tuesday in their Champions League quarter-final
  • The Bavarians will aim to build commanding lead ahead of next week's return leg
  • Arturo Vidal and Juan Bernat have both shaken off injuries to take place in squad

Published: 08:46 EDT, 3 April 2018 | Updated: 08:46 EDT, 3 April 2018

Bayern Munich took a stroll around Seville as they stepped up preparations for their Champions League quarter-final first leg against the La Liga side.

The pair face each other at the Sanchez Pizjuan on Tuesday evening ahead of next week's second leg in Germany.

And Bayern, who are runaway leaders in the Bundesliga, have taken a strong squad to Spain in the hope of coming away with a crucial lead. 

Bayern Munich coach Jupp Heynckes led his players on a morning stroll in a rainy Seville

Franck Ribery (left) was among the star names to travel to Spain for the all-important clash

Joshua Kimmich (left) has also been named in the squad for the game at the Sanchez Pizjuan

Supporters of the German club gathered in rainy conditions to take photos of the team as they left their hotel, with coach Jupp Heynckes leading the squad on their morning walk.

The likes of Franck Ribery, James Rodriguez and Joshua Kimmich were also in attendance as Bayern braved the weather to take in some of the sites in Seville.

Heynckes was given a boost on Monday when key duo Arturo Vidal and Juan Bernat were passed fit for the game.

Midfielder Arturo Vidal has given Bayern a boost by proving his fitness in training

Vidal (left) joined Juan Bernat in shaking off injury to travel to southern Spain for the match

Both players took part in an evening training session at the Sanchez Pizjuan and could take their places in a close-to-full-strength lineup.

But Manuel Neuer's continued absence means second-choice goalkeeper Sven Ulreich is expected to start between the sticks.

Bayern head into the clash off the back of a resounding 6-0 victory against fierce rivals Borussia Dortmund. 

Heynckes' men hold a 17-point lead at the top of the Bundesliga and have lost just once in all competitions since the turn of the year.
