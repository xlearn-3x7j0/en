The sporting deaths of 2018

From football trailblazers to professional boxers, the world of sport has lost some iconic names in 2018.

Popular figures like Jimmy Armfield, Cyrille Regis and Ray Wilkins have all lost their lives through illness.

American sports stars, including NBA basketball and NFL American football players, have been on the end of tragic incidents in the United States.

There has also been sad passings in motorsport and refereeing.

Here, Mirror Sport remember the other famous faces from the sporting world who have lost their lives in 2018.

Paul Alcock

Died 30 January, aged 64

The former Premier League referee lost his battle with caner in January.

Alcock was perhaps most famed for his altercation with Paulo Di Canio, during the charismatic Italian's time at Sheffield Wednesday.

Di Canio's firm, two-handed shove to the chest of Alcock sent him tumbling to the ground in dramatic fashion, but proved to be a turning point in the FA's bid to stop the abuse of officials.

A shopping centre manager in Maidstone, Kent, he couple his day job with becoming one of the best referees in the country.

Jimmy Armfield

Died 22 January, aged 82

England legend Armfield sadly lost his battle against cancer in January.

The ex-Blackpool great - who played 43 times for his country - was 82.

The right-back was all set for a place in England's 1966 World Cup side but injured his toe in a warm up match, with Fulham's George Cohen taking his place.

A one-club man, he made 627 appearances for Blackpool in total, scoring six times.

He then went on to manage Bolton Wanderers and Leeds United before becoming a respected pundit.

Davide Astori

Died 4 March, aged 31

Fiorentina announced that captain Davide Astori has died at the age of 31 in March.

The Italian international, with 14 caps to his name, was found dead at the La di Moret hotel in Udine, ahead of the Viola's meeting with Udinese.

Astori, who started his career at AC Milan, enjoyed a 12-year career in Serie A.

He spent six years with Cagliari, making 174 appearances for the Sardinians. In 2014 he joined Roma on loan, before joining Fiorentina the following year.

He was made captain for La Viola by Stefano Pioli earlier in the 2017/18 and had made 27 appearances during the campaign, scoring once.

Sir Roger Bannister

Died 4 March, aged 88

Sir Roger Bannister, the first athlete to run a sub-four minute mile, died aged 88 in Oxford.

A statement released on behalf of Sir Roger's family said: "Sir Roger Bannister, died peacefully in Oxford on 3rd March 2018, aged 88, surrounded by his family who were as loved by him, as he was loved by them.

"He banked his treasure in the hearts of his friends."

Rasual Butler

Died 31 January, aged 38

Former NBA star Butler died alongside his American Idol popstar wife in a car accident.

Butler, 38, played 14 seasons in the NBA with eight franchises after starting at Miami Heat in 2002.

He reportedly lost control of his Range Rover at approximately during the early hours of the morning in Studio City, California, hit a parking meter and flipped the car.

Leah LaBelle, 31, was Butler's wife and an R&B singer who appeared on "American Idol" in 2004.

Chris Edwards

Died 31 March, aged 41

Former English, British and Commonwealth boxing champion Edwards died after suffering a fatal heart attack.

The 41-year-old was a hero of his native Stoke-on-Trent and was known as the Fenton flyweight.

Edwards was crowned English champion in 2006 after beating Dale Robinson, before winning the super-flyweight title in 2007 against Jamie McDonnell.

He then added the Commonwealth title to his honours when he beat Wayne Bloy in a flyweight bout in 2009, also winning the British title in the process.

Juan Carlos Garcia

Died 9 January, aged 29

Former Wigan Athletic defender Garcia died after a battle with leukaemia.

The Latics confirmed the Honduras international, who played for the club between 2013 and 2016, died in his homeland overnight, surrounded by his family.

When Garcia's Wigan contract ended in 2016, he was granted permission to remain in the UK to continue his treatment, with support from the club and the PFA.

He returned to Honduras in late 2017.

Dan Gurney

Died 14 January, aged 86

The winner of four F1 Grand Prixs, American racer Gurney died from complications of pneumonia.

Across 11 years in the sport, Gurney raced for the Ferrari, BRM, Porsche, Lotus, Brabham, Eagle and McLaren teams.

He became the first-ever drive in F1 to wear a full face helmet in Grand Prix racing when he donned the now-customary lid at the German GP in 1968.

Died 8 March, aged 47

Tributes poured in from the motorsport community following the death of popular broadcaster Henry Hope-Frost.

The journalist and presenter, 47, tragically passed away following a motorbike accident.

The incident happened on the A286 in Surrey when Hope-Frost's bike crashed into a car and Hope-Frost was declared dead at the scene.

He is survived by his wife Charlotte and three young sons.

Rene Houseman

Died 22 March, aged 64

World Cup-winning Argentina striker Rene Houseman passed away after a long illness.

Nicknamed 'Loco' he played in both the 1974 and 1978 competitions and was known for his dribbling, speed and power.

In all he played 55 times for Argentina - scoring 13 times - while also appearing for Huracan, River Plate and Colo-Colo.

Houseman played primarily on the right wing during a 14-year playing career.

Edwin Jackson

Died 4 February, aged 26

Indianapolis Colts footballer Jackson was killed by a suspected drink driver.

He was one of two people killed in the early hours of this morning on Interstate 70.

Jackson and another man, Jeffrey Monroe, were stood near a parked up vehicle when they were both struck by a black Ford F-150 pickup truck on the emergency shoulder.

Liam Miller

Died 10 February, aged 36

Former Manchester United and Republic of Ireland midfielder Miller died aged 36 after a battle with pancreatic cancer.

The 21-cap international was diagnosed with the condition in November and returned home to Cork from the US where he had been playing.

Miller came through the youth ranks at Celtic and helped them to the Scottish title in 2003-04 before moving to Manchester United.

He went on to make 22 appearances for the Red Devils in all competitions, scoring two goals during his time at Old Trafford.

Miller spent time on loan at Leeds before moving to Sunderland, where he helped the Black Cats to the 2006-07 Championship title.

John Molyneux

Died 7 March, aged 87

Former Liverpool defender John Molyneux has died in March.

Right-back Molyneux, who represented England at youth level, made 249 appearances for the Anfield club, scoring three goals between 1955 and 1962.

In a message from their official Twitter account, the club said: "We're deeply saddened by the passing of former player John Molyneux.

"The thoughts of everybody at #LFC are with John’s family and friends at this sad time."

Keith Murdoch

Died Friday 30 March, aged 74

The All Black prop at the centre of one of the most remarkable and mysterious rugby stories ever told died at the age of 74.

Keith Murdock's death was confirmed by New Zealand Rugby on Friday.

His amazing story began nearly 46 years after a fateful night in Cardiff that changed his life forever, report WalesOnline.

In December 1972, the giant prop became the first All Black to be sent home from a tour following an incident in Cardiff.

Cyrille Regis

Died 15 January, aged 59

Regis, the West Brom and Coventry legend, died aged 59 after suffering a heart attack.

The former striker, a pioneer for black footballers when he played alongside Laurie Cunningham and Brendon Batson at The Hawthorns, died on Sunday night.

Regis started his career at the Hawthorns, going on to make 241 appearances for the Baggies, scoring 81 goals.

In the late 1970s, the trio of Regis, Cunningham and Batson - who were nicknamed the Three Degrees by their then-manager Ron Atkinson - were subjected to racist abuse.

The Celebration, a statue in their honour, was unveiled at West Bromwich in 2014.

Scott Westgarth

Died 25 January, aged 31

Professional boxer Westgarth tragically passed away after collapsing following a fight in Doncaster in February.

The 31-year-old was taken ill after winning a brutal fight on points against Dec Spellman, in which both men were knocked down to the canvas.

Westgarth completed his post-fight interviews before stumbling to the back, where paramedics were called to his aid and he was taken to hospital.

Ray Wilkins

Died 4 April, aged 61

Former Chelsea captain Wilkins passed away following a cardiac arrest at his home.

Wilkins, who also played for Manchester United, was taken to hospital where he was placed in an induced coma, but he died several days later.

Wilkins had carried out media duties on talkSPORT and Sky Sports - he is a regular on both platforms - shortly before suffering the cardiac arrest.

A winner of 84 England caps, he became the fourth player sent off while playing for his country when he was dismissed during the 1986 World Cup finals in Mexico in a game against Morocco.

Wilkins - known in the game by his childhood nickname 'Butch' - played for 12 sides and was on the coaching staff of several more, but is commonly associated with Chelsea.
