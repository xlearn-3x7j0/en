Riders disqualified from race for cycling under closing level crossing

  • Several riders disqualified for cycling under closing railway crossing
  • Incident occurred during the Scheldeprijs in Belgium on Wednesday
  • UCI race rules say that riders must stop at a railway crossing before it closes
  • Footage posted online shows moment the large group of riders flouted the rules 

Published: 12:13 EDT, 4 April 2018 | Updated: 12:13 EDT, 4 April 2018

Team Sky's Ian Stannard was one of a number of riders disqualified from the Scheldeprijs on Wednesday after cycling under a closing railway crossing.

Footage posted on social media showed Stannard and other members of the second peloton ignoring a closing barrier in Krabbendijke, Belgium.

The riders were subsequently caught up by race commissaries on motorbikes and disqualified.

Union Cycliste Internationalen (UCI) race rules dictate that cyclists must stop at a railway crossing before it closes.

Riders were disqualified from the Scheldeprijs for cycling under a closing level crossing

Footage showed the second peloton breaking UCI rules by traversing the railway crossing

Officials on motorcycles caught up with the group, who were subsequently disqualified

Several riders argued with race officials about the disqualification while others cycled on

But despite being repeatedly signalled to stop, several riders continued the race, while others could be seen arguing with officials.

Along with Chelmsford-born Stannard, other high-profile cyclists reportedly disqualified include Arnaud Démare of Groupama-FDJ, Tony Martin from Katusha-Alpecin and LottoNL-Jumbo's Dylan Groenewegen.

Defending champion and pre-race favourite Marcel Kittel was part of the first peloton and avoided the incident completely.

Those disqualified may also face further sanctions, such as suspension or fines. 

The race was won by 21-year-old Fabio Jakobsen of Quick-Step Floors ahead of Bora–Hansgrohe's Pascal Ackermann and Team Sky's Chris Lawless.
