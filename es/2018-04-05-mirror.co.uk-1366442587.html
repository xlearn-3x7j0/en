Manager hints at what he'll say to Martial amid Juventus links

France boss Didier Deschamps says he will give Anthony Martial his opinion amid reports the 22-year-old could leave Manchester United and sign for Juventus.

The Red Devils winger, whose contract will expire at the end of next season, is reportedly growing frustrated at Old Trafford at his inability to cement a regular spot in Jose Mourinho's first team.

The Portuguese has also lost his patience in the Frenchman, who United signed for £36million potentially rising to £58million, back in 2015, and has previously criticised his languid style.

Martial recently linked up with his France team-mates and featured in two World Cup warm-up friendlies ahead of the finals, which kick-off in a few months time.

And his international boss, Deschamps says that while he won't advise Martial on what to do in his situation, he will offer his opinion.

"The players know it would be better if issues in the market were resolved as soon as possible, but I know that won't be the case," Deschamps told Tuttosport.

"Martial has a contract with United and the club don't want to sell him. We'll see if he's looked at, by Juve or anyone else.

"I don't give advice, I give my opinion. In the end, it's up to the players to decide."
