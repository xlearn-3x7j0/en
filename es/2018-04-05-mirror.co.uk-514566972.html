'Peter Reid was a classy, silky footballer but he had a real competitive edge'

Former Everton and England Midfielder Peter Reid has paid tribute to his former England teammate Ray Wilkins :

He was a classy, silky footballer but, believe me, he had a real competitive edge.

I was fortunate enough to get into the England squad with him, an absolute gentleman, a real diamond and a fantastic footballer.

He was such a good footballer, a really good person and we ended up playing together at Queens Park Rangers for a short time.

We were pretty successful there together as well.

He was always a great pal, always great company and, like everyone else has been saying in football, he will be very sadly missed. What a character.

Ray was such a charmer - and his little phrases would make you laugh.

Everyone was called “fella” and he had a little quip for every occasion.

After his playing career, he was equally popular in the media because he was always polite, had time for everyone no matter who it was.

He loved life. I think it’s well documented that Ray had a few problems health wise but you’d never guess from his personality, his demeanour and the way he treated people. He was just a gent.

We went to the World Cup together in Mexico in 1986 and, if you remember, he got sent off against Morocco and I got in the side with him and Bryan Robson both out.

He was disappointed but he was brilliant in the dressing room and, for me personally, I effectively took his place because he then got left out and he couldn’t have been more supportive.

Of course he was disappointed but he hid it, you’d never know because there he was supporting the team, willing us to get a result against Poland and that sums him up.

He was all for the squad and all for the country.

Win, lose or draw with Ray Wilkins, he always had one thing about him - and that was called class.
