If you ever dreamed of a treehouse as a child then this unique property for sale in Devon could be for you. 

The £800,000 unusual family home is clad in timber and is set on a hillside surrounded by trees and stunning views of the English countryside.

With its treetop setting, six bedrooms and designer interior, it has the feel of a luxury treehouse that fits perfectly into its stunning Totnes location.

It's child's play! The wood-clad six-bedroom property in Devon resembles a luxury treehouse with stunning views

If you fancy spending some time sat out among the treetops, there is also a separate decked terrace that is large enough for seating and a dining area

The property has gone on the market for sale for £799,000. 

It was originally designed in 1984 by the award-winning architect Roger Hawkins and has been updated by the current owner, who is also an architect. 

Fitting in with the treehouse theme, the construction is based on a Douglas Fir frame covered in Douglas Fir cladding, with plenty of windows that make the most of the extensive views of the countryside.

The property sits on a third of an acre and there is a balcony overlooking the rear garden

It has far-reaching views of the countryside near Totnes, on the south coast of Britain, and isn't far from the beach

The unique property is clad in timber and has plenty of windows to make the most of the views out over the countryside

The property is called Atrium House, in recognition of the glass-roof above the entrance hall and staircase.

Buying agent Henry Pryor said: 'Don't let the trendy north London set see this or they will be camping out across Devon hoping for a chance to see one of the most inspiring homes currently for sale.'

He added: 'The stunning views and the extensive use of timber together with the fantastic setting all conspire to make even the most hardened urbanite sell up in a heart beat.

'The current owners have managed to take the original concept and keep it fresh and relevant. The accommodation may not be infant-friendly but for Lost Boys aged seven and up this home would be one long adventure. '

The staircase leads to the main living area on the first floor of the house, including an open-plan sitting and dining room, along with a kitchen.

This area has solid oak flooring with under floor heating and sliding glass doors that open onto a decked terrace at the back of the property.

There are also two bedrooms on this level - one of which is currently used as a study. The other is the main bedroom, which has its own dressing room and en suite bathroom. 

The remaining bedrooms are on the ground floor, where there is also a self-contained annexe with its own entrance.

This part of the house has been rented out by previous owners, with Totnes being a popular market town for visitors during the summer.

Totnes is part of an area of outstanding beauty, with Dartmoor only 10 miles to the north and beaches a similar distance to the south.

Good transport links are available via the town's mainline train station, which runs services to London Paddington.
