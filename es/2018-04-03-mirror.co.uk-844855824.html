Newcastle's Benitez wanted for return to La Liga next season

Rafa Benitez is wanted by Real Sociedad.

The Basque club, currently just 15th in Spain's 20-team top flight but safe from relegation, hope to convince the Newcastle boss to come home and lead them up the table.

Their interest is likely to strengthen Benitez’s hand this summer as he pushes for the finances to make his Magpies more competitive in the Premier League.

The former Liverpool and Chelsea chief has already started manoeuvring for transfer cash, insisting that the acquisition of players to plug gaps in his squad during the winter window is the reason behind their improved form since the turn of the year.

Newcastle signed winger Kenedy from Chelsea on loan in January and his composure, goals and assists have been key as they have lost just two of their 10 league games in 2018.

Keeper Martin Dubravka also arrived from Sparta Prague as a loanee and has been superb — he is yet to concede a goal at St James’ Park in three appearances.

Benitez also wanted a striker.

Feyenoord's Nicolai Jorgensen was beyond them, but they ended up borrowing Islam Slimani from Leicester — who made his debut after injury as a sub in Saturday's six-pointer win over Huddersfield.

The 57-year-old Spaniard says he hasn’t got to spend huge money to get an uplift, but does expect his plan to be followed.

He said: “It’s not always a question of money. That doesn’t always make the difference when it comes to signing the players you need. In January, when we were talking, we said we need a goalkeeper, a winger and a striker.

"That is what we did, and you can see the difference. It is about doing the right things at the right time.”

Once Newcastle — now seven point clear of third-bottom Southampton with seven games to go — have guaranteed their Premier League status for next season, Benitez will plan his summer recruitment.

Jorgensen is expected to remain a target to land quickly, for £18m, when the window reopens. Dubravka’s loan deal is set to be made permanent for £4m, with Benitez praising his impact.

He added of the Slovakian: “It is not just that he makes saves, it is because he talks to the defenders and he organises the defence. That is crucial for any team.”
