Shocking video captures the moment two manholes explode in Manhattan

Two manholes exploded in the Upper East Side in Manhattan, New York on Monday morning.

The underground blasts sent two 100-pound manhole covers flying into the air nearly hitting several pedestrians.

One of the manholes landed outside the doors of Thep Thai Restaurant on East 75th Street and Second Avenue.

The second one occurred just across on the corner of 76th Street.

At least two people witnessed the explosion and attempted to block the thick smoke and explosion with their umbrellas.

'There was a loud bang on 75th Street and then after about 15 to 20 minutes, there was another loud bang in the corner of 76th Street,' said Ryan O'Neill, who was sitting in a nearby Starbucks.

'Two loud bangs and quite a bit of smoke came up after that,' NBC New York reported.

The FDNY and a representative from Con Ed immediately responded to the scene and deduced that the street salt that had accumulated on the ground was likely the cause.

They said it is possible the salt had damaged the underground electrical wires, which in turn caused the explosions.

'Usually the snow, the run off, the melt, the salt, it corrodes the wires in the manholes and causes an electrical problem down there,' FDNY Battalion Chief Mark Rosenbaum said, according to CBS New York.

Two minor injuries were reported and the FDNY made sure to check carbon monoxide levels in all the nearby buildings.

Con Edison warned people on Monday saying the rapidly melted snow could lead to more electrical fires underground. 

'Salt and snow melting and going into manholes, it decays the rubberized coatings on the electrical conduits and starts fires,' said Joe Schiralli, an FDNY deputy chief. 'It happens every winter.'
