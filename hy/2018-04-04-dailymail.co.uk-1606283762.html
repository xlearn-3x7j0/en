Liverpool fans set off flares to welcome Manchester City to Anfield

Liverpool and boss Jurgen Klopp both issued apologies to Manchester City after the visitors' team bus was damaged by missiles thrown as it arrived at Anfield for the Champions League match.

Liverpool fans threw bottles, rocks and fireworks at the Manchester City coach as it arrived at Anfield. A number of red flares were also let off, making visibility difficult as supporters lined the streets outside the stadium.

The front windscreen and the driver's window were smashed and City were forced to call for a replacement to transport them back to Manchester after the game.

Liverpool boss Jurgen Klopp admitted it had cast a shadow over the tie and he apologised to the City players and staff.

He said: 'Absolutely, I really don't understand. There was a lot of talks before. We tried everything to avoid a situation like that. 

'When I came here we had to take another way as well, and then you thought OK, that's already because last time it didn't work too well.

'When we came through it was obviously very positive. But we didn't know when we are on the bus that this had happened before because City were in front of us. 

'So yes, for Liverpool FC I have to say sorry.'

City boss Pep Guardiola was thankful that there were no injuries.

The City team bus was given a police escort around the streets of Liverpool on Wednesday 

A Liverpool statement read: 'The club condemns in the strongest possible terms the scenes which preceded our Champions League quarter-final, which resulted in damage being inflicted on the Manchester City team bus during their arrival at Anfield.

'We apologise unreservedly to Pep Guardiola, his players, staff and officials caught up in the incident.

'The behaviour of a number of individuals was completely unacceptable and the club will co-operate fully with the authorities to identify those responsible.

'The priority now is to establish the facts and offer Manchester City whatever support is necessary.'

City arrived first but their bus became a target for a few missiles as it slowed to navigate the corner of Arkles Lane and Anfield Road, where most of the fans were gathered.

Liverpool arrived to a raucous welcome moments later.

Manchester City were forced to find another bus to take them home from Wednesday's Champions League showdown.   

Liverpool fans set off flares and smoke bombs as they created a hostile atmosphere outside of Anfield to set the tone for Wednesday night's Champions League battle with Manchester City.

Supporters gathered more than two-and-a-half hours before kick off in the evening's quarter-final first leg to ensure they were in place to greet the arrival of the team buses amid a backdrop of red mist.

Lining the streets around Anfield has become common practice at Liverpool, especially on European nights, and fans were determined to maintain the tradition despite the club issuing a statement urging them to remain 'friendly and respectful.

Merseyside Police had revealed that the team coaches would not take their usual route past the King Harry pub 'due to building works and to ensure the safety of fans'. Instead the team coaches will instead travel along Arkles Lane, passing The Arkles pub before turning right onto Anfield Road.

The teams were sure to receive a raucous reception as Anfield Road was 'rammed' by 5.45pm, two hours before kick-off.

And the City team bus was greeted by a chorus of boos as it snaked through the smoked-filled streets. Beer cans and stones were thrown at the windscreen.  

The door window of the bus was smashed, and there was damage to the rest of it. There was also smoke filling into the bus from the flares

Liverpool legend Steven Gerrard, who was at Anfield to cover the match for BT Sport, condemned fans  for 'overstepping the mark'.

He said: You don't want that – people overstepping the mark and smashing buses up on the way. I'm all for a hostile reception and putting pressure on opposition teams, I think we all like that as part of European football. But no, we don't like to see that.

Frank Lampard: It's difficult to control everybody. We were watching out the window and it was great, we loved the moment seeing that. 

'Obviously the huge majority of the fans are there to create this atmosphere, but there can be one or two that take it too far.

Rio Ferdinand: There is a level you don't want to go beyond, and that is bottles and stuff smashing against windows. 

The rowdy scenes came about despite an appeal by police.

A group of Liverpool fans had planned a hostile reception for City players, which led the Manchester club to express 'serious concerns' to police.

Pep Guardiola had plenty of footballing concerns to occupy his mind as he prepared to take on Jurgen Klopp for the 13th time in his managerial career.

Klopp had defeated Guardiola six times in their first 12 meetings, including when Liverpool last played City — beating them 4-3 at Anfield in January.

Speaking in the build-up to Wednesday's game, Guardiola said: 'I would love to think that we are going to do something in this competition.

'You have to feel you are favourites. You have to feel you can do it. If you don't, you've got no chance.'
