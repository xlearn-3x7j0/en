Who is Conor Masterson? Liverpool youngster drafted in amid defensive crisis

Irish teenager Conor Masterson trained with Liverpool's first-team on Tuesday as the Reds experience a defensive crisis ahead of Wednesday's Champions League quarter-final against Manchester City.

The 19-year-old Dubliner was named in the Reds' European squad at the beginning of the season, and could now find himself on the bench as the only available centre-back cover against Pep Guardiola's men.

Joel Matip , Ragnar Klavan, Joe Gomez and potential centre-back Emre Can are all out of the game, leaving Virgil van Dijk and Dejan Lovren as Reds manager Jurgen Klopp's only fit senior centre-backs.

"Joel (Matip) is out for the rest of the season, no doubt about that. It's very serious," said Klopp on Tuesday.

"Emre (Can) will at least be very close for tomorrow - but it doesn't look too good. Joe Gomez is not in for two or three weeks.

"Ragnar Klavan is unavailable. If there is a centre-half in the room I am open for talks!"

But it seems as though that centre-half could be Masterson, who has been a regular for the Under-23 side this season.

Masterson reportedly turned down Manchester United and Chelsea to sign for Liverpool aged 13 from his local Dublin club Lucan United, who received €1m when he turned 16.

Named the Football Association of Ireland's International Player of the Year for 2015, he was an unused substitute when a young Liverpool side drew 2-2 at Exeter in the FA Cup in January 2016, his only first-team involvement to date.

He was named as captain of the Under-18s for the 2016/17 season when Trent Alexander-Arnold moved up to the first team, and he's been a regular in Reds youth sides at centre-back ever since.

Masterson was also a key part of Steven Gerrard's Under-19s side that reached the quarter-finals of the UEFA Youth League this season, but he missed a penalty in a shootout defeat to Manchester City's kids.

Now he could be involved in the squad to face their senior team.
