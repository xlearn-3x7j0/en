Murder suspect, 41, is shot dead by officers in Tampa

  • Tampa police spotted the murder suspect from Georgia in a white Chevy Impala
  • He led officers on a chase for several minutes, firing at them a number of times
  • Police said he tried to carjack a member of the public and fired at officers again
  • Officers fired back, killing the 41-year-old man, who has not been identified 

Published: 07:50 EDT, 2 April 2018 | Updated: 08:31 EDT, 2 April 2018

A murder suspect was shot dead by police after taking on officers in a gun battle.

The chase began after the man, with a murder warrant from Georgia, was seen driving a white Chevy Impala in Tampa, Florida, shortly after 6pm on Sunday.

Police said the suspect led officers on a chase for several minutes, firing at them a number of times through the streets.

They said he tried to carjack a member of the public and fired at officers again.

Officers fired back, killing the 41-year-old man at E. 28th Avenue and N 15th Street.

They, along with troopers from the Florida Highway Patrol attempted CPR on the man, who has not been identified.

No officers were hurt during the chase.
