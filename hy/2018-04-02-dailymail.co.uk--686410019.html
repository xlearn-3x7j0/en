Sergio Garcia attends Drive, Chip and Putt Championship at Augusta

Sergio Garcia has touched down in Augusta ahead of what will be another thrilling week at the Masters.

But before beginning his preparations, the defending champion was one of many stars who attended the Drive, Chip and Putt Championship to give the next golfing generation a taste of Augusta National and memories that will last forever.

The Spaniard, who won his first major at the Masters last year after an epic tussle with Justin Rose, took time out of his day to greet the young golf hopefuls, as well as hand out trophies at the event that became tradition five years ago.

Garcia, donning the famous green jacket, appeared in a very relaxed mood ahead of the start of first major of the year on Thursday as he watched the children show off their talents on the driving range, the chipping area and the 18th green.

One of the standout moments of the day was when one young girl was asked to perform her best Garcia impersonation from his victory last year. And she duly obliged, crouching before clenching her fists and pumping both arms.

After enjoying some downtime ahead of a busy week, the 38-year-old later went out for a practice round with his father, Victor, but not before a big Spanish celebration.

He enjoyed dining with those closest to him, including his parents, his wife's parents, two-time Masters champion Jose Maria Olazabal and Jon Rahm, who hopes to become the fourth Spaniard to win the green jacket, along with Seve Ballesteros.

However, Garcia wasn't the only Masters champion in attendance during the family day.

The likes of Gary Player, Nick Faldo and Trevor Immelman all made their way down to watch the golfing hopefuls in action on the weekend.

Just like Garcia, they were also pictured handing out awards and posing for photos, ensuring the youngsters will enjoy a day they will never forget.   

The mood is always light the day before Masters week officially begins but with just three days left until the tournament, the world's best will now certainly be getting down to business.
