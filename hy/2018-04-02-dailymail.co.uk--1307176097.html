BUMBLE AT THE TEST: England might just have found spinner in Leach

New Zealand need a record run chase of 382 to win after day four of the second Test in Christchurch.

England declared on 352 to add to their first innings score of 307, while New Zealand's opening batsmen put on 42 before the close of play leaving them needing 340 runs to win, and England seeking all 10 wickets.

Sportsmail's DAVID LLOYD gives his views from Christchurch on the last few days...

New Zealand's Tom Latham plays at the ball as he and Jeet Raval put on 42 before close of play

The morning session showed where England are as a Test team. They weren’t sure where to go and were hesitant, not helped by New Zealand slowing it down completely with, at one stage, five overs in 30 minutes. England didn’t grasp the game and that meant at lunch-time they were in limbo.

So frustrating yet again for England to see four batsmen get pretty 50s in the second innings and nobody go on to three figures. The coaches will be infuriated by that but the players will be angry with themselves. They’ve done all the hard work and it has to be nothing more than lapses in concentration.

Time-wasting as a law of the game is now non-existent. I’ve said it that many times I’m fed up with it but Tests just meander along with 12th men coming and going with gloves, bats, helmets and all-sorts. Fifteen overs an hour really shouldn’t be any sort of problem at all but nobody seems bothered.

England declared on 352 to add to their first innings score of 307 to set a target of 382 runs

Our New Zealand broadcast colleagues are infatuated with the Barmy Army and they wanted me to go over to them before play to get a couple of renditions going. I got a Sweet Caroline out of them but sadly Billy ‘the Trumpet’ Cooper, known here as Tommy, hadn’t arrived so we had to put out own beat to it.

I’m coming to the end of this trip and I still haven’t found a beer. They have a lot of craft beers here but they are zoom juice! I asked for a nice pale ale in a bottle in one bar and they gave me one that was highly recommended. Looked at the label and it was 7.5 per cent. It would start a jet engine, that.

England might just have found a spinner. Jack Leach is exactly that, an out and out left-arm spinner. He’s been handy with the bat but his business is spinning the ball and he’s been accurate, bowled at a good pace and set his own fields. When he gets on something with a bit of help he will be a real asset.

England might just have found a spinner in Jack Leach and he's been handy with the bat

It’s been inspiring to be back in Christchurch seeing the development that is now going on after that terrible earthquake seven years ago. It will take a long time and they reckon it could be another 10 years before the city is completely finished but it will look fabulous. As usual in New Zealand, the people in the garden city have been absolutely special. When can I come again?
