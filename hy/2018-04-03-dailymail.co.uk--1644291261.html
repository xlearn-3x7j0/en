Wolves 2-2 Hull: Oskar Burr nets late equaliser to salvage point

There were murmurs of confusion around Molineux when Oskar Buur Rasmussen was sent on for his Championship debut as a late replacement for Diogo Jota.

Thirteen minutes later Rasmussen sparked a roar as his pinpoint header salvaged a point for Wolves just as Hull anticipated an unlikely victory.

Nuno Espirito Santo’s side were far from their fluid best, consistently giving away possession, and Hull led before a 20-year-old Rasmussen marked only his second Wolves game with an important goal in the 83rd minute.

Oskar Buur came off the bench to secure a point with a header seven minutes from time

David Meyler had made it 1-1 after Wolves had taken the lead by converting from the spot

Diogo Jota gave the Championship leaders an early advantage also from the penalty spot

The Wolves midfielder dragged himself up off the floor to score, having won the penalty

So Wolves maintain a sense of momentum as promotion edges ever closer, although they will have to play better than this when a Premier League side.

Nuno was unable to select Ruben Neves or Matt Doherty after their red cards at Middlesbrough but Jota returned after a two-match absence to operate as a false nine.

It was somewhat harsh on Benik Afobe, who dropped to the bench despite scoring four goals in five games, but within 20 minutes the switch worked.

A silky smooth move involving Helder Costa and Ivan Cavaleiro saw Jota burst into the box down the left and cut back too adroitly for Michael Hector, who left out a leg that the Wolves forward promptly fell over.

The Hull bench erupted in fury when referee Darren England pointed to the spot but it was foolish by Hector and Jota rose to convert the penalty for his 15th goal of a marvellous season.

Wolves wanted another penalty when Allan McGregor tackled Jota and the Hull goalkeeper had to react quickly to thwart Costa’s follow-up as he lay on the floor.

McGregor was keeping Hull in it, having earlier produced a fine save to tip over a powerful Romain Saiss header from Barry Douglas’s free-kick. A neat set-piece of their own might have brought Hull an equaliser but David Meyler badly sliced his shot.

But as half-time approached a suicidal pass by Costa allowed Meyler in and Roderick Miranda felt he had no choice but to hook an arm and bring him down. Meyler duly levelled from the spot. As far as auditions go for a regular place in the Wolves team, it was fluffed lines from Miranda, starting his first Championship game since October 28

Nuno responded at the interval by sending on Afobe for Miranda and repositioning Cavaleiro at wing-back but the errors kept coming. Ruddy saved with his legs when one Wolves mistake let in Ola Aina for a shot, and the Hull winger then struck another attempt wide.

In the 70th minute Hector headed a superb chance from Kamil Grosicki’s free-kick against the post but eight minutes later Ryan Bennett did the job for the visitors. Grosicki drilled a low cross and Bennett, trying to clear, sent the all into his own net.

Wolves responded when Barry Douglas found space on the left and Rasmussen connected sweetly.
