Robert Griffin III signs with the Baltimore Ravens

Robert Griffin III is back in the NFL after signing a one-year contract with the Baltimore Ravens.

The quarterback, 28, will serve as a veteran back-up to starter Joe Flacco following a whole year out of the sport after he was released by the Cleveland Browns in March.

The Ravens signaled their interest in Griffin last summer when Flacco's back injury was causing concerns but ultimately decided against signing him.

Head Coach John Harbaugh told a press conference: 'I'm very excited about it, the coaches are excited about it.

'Robert came in, had a really good workout, looked good throwing the ball, and was in very good shape. Obviously, his personality, he's a confident guy.

'He's a very talented quarterback who has had a lot of success in this league early on.'

Griffin responded to the signing by tweeting his new team's mantra, #playlikearaven.

The injury-plagued veteran impressed coaches during a workout at the Ravens' training centre last week.

The two sides came to a contract agreement on Tuesday and Griffin is expected to officially sign next week.

Griffin entered the NFL as a hugely hyped talent in 2012 after winning the Heisman Trophy with Baylor a year previously.

He started 15 games with the Washington Redskins, tossing 20 touchdowns to five interceptions.

Griffin scrambles for the Browns against the San Diego Chargers in the first half of an NFL game on December 24, 2016

But next season he sustained a knee sprain during a game against the Ravens, and came off for Kirk Cousins.

Griffin came back for the playoffs but injured his knee again, and then had a poor 2013 season which saw Cousins take over during the last three games.

Seven games into 2014 Cousins took over the job full-time and one year later Griffin was released.

He left the Redskins for the Browns in 2016 but was soon struck down by a shoulder injury and left the team in March a year later.

Griffin married Estonian heptathlete Grete Šadeiko, 24, last year at a lavish ceremony in Miami attended by more than 100 friends.

They became engaged in May and welcomed a daughter, Gloria, in July.

Griffin also has daughter Reese Ann, aged two, whom he shares with his first wife, college sweetheart Rebecca Liddicoat. They split in 2016.
