Photographs capture the 'perfect imperfections' of injured rescue dogs

A pet-loving photographer has captured the 'perfect imperfections' of injured rescue dogs to showcase their resilience. 

Enamoured by their ability to overcome overwhelming adversity, lifelong animal lover and professional photographer Alex Cearns hoped to capture the undeniable spirit of injured pooches.

Ms Cearns is hoping to show that the world the beauty of rescue dogs and why they should never be loved any less than 'regular' dogs.

Compiling her favourite snaps over the past eight years into a heart-warming gallery, she has released a book, titled 'Perfect Imperfection', sharing the stories behind the portraits.

Ms Cearns, 43, owner of Houndstooth Studios in Perth, Australia, said: 'As a child, my only companions were my dogs and the other animals on my farm.

'The tenacity of animals to overcome adversity never ceases to amaze me.

'They make the most out of life and from them I've learnt so much about seeing the positives in every situation and never giving up.
