Jose Mourinho's son Zuca shares dugout with father and United coaches

Jose Mourinho's son Zuca shared the dugout with his father and the Manchester United coaches in the club's 2-0 win over Swansea on Saturday.

Zuca was not the only addition to the home bench at Old Trafford at the weekend as Michael Carrick got a taste of life off the pitch as a coach during the league game.

Dressed in a United tracksuit with his initials on, Zuca was pictured walking behind Carrick and Mourinho ahead of the Premier League clash.

Zuca, a goalkeeper, was on the books of Fulham having signed a two-year academy deal at the age of 16 but he parted company with the Cottagers by mutual consent a year early.

The 18-year-old has previously played in the youth teams of Chelsea and Real Madrid when they were managed by his father.

The youngster, who's full name is Jose Mario, has a close relationship with his father and was on the touchline for United's Europa League final last season.

The pair embraced after the final whistle during the celebrations as the Old Trafford club lifted the trophy at the expense of Ajax.

The United boss alluded to the fact that Zuca could join his coaching staff last year in a TV documentary aired in Portugal.

'One day, after a period of a few bad results, he comes up with some analysis,' he said.

'Obviously suffering because Dad wasn't doing well, we had lost to Watford and Man City [in September 2016] I think. And he came with the stats of the team, I showed them to my assistants and I told them, be careful as one day this boy will take one of your places.

'I compare him with me at his age, where I would try do the same sort of things for my dad, of course with different technology methods, but analysis from a kid now aged 17, but 16 then.'
