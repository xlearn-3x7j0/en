Celtic 0-0 Dundee: Brendan Rodgers' title-chasing side held

Suddenly Dundee's decision to turn down £2million for Steven Caulker begins to make sense.

One of three impregnable centre-halves alongside Genseric Kusunga and Josh Meekings, the former England international policed a thin blue line here. 

This was his best game for Dundee on a night when Celtic spent the 90 minutes running into dead ends. Trying to thread the ball through the eye of a needle; tying themselves in knots of frustration.

This was Celtic's fifth 0-0 draw of the season in all competitions and their sixth home draw in the SPFL Premiership. Remarkably, four of the five blanks have come at Celtic Park against Rosenborg, Rangers, St Johnstone and a Dens Park side battling for their Premiership lives. 

Make no mistake, Neil McCann's team were worth a valuable point here. In the first of two visits to Glasgow this week they extended their lead over Ross County and bottom club Partick Thistle to five points before travelling to Ibrox on Saturday.

Admitting the retention of Caulker was a factor in Dundee's display manager Neil McCann said: 'I think it is a testament to the club where Steven is.

'For him to say to me when I told him about the offer that he wasn't interested, that he had a job to do here, was fantastic news for the club.

 'It was great for me that he wants to be part of it and I think he was part of a real brilliant team performance. I think he is steadily getting better all the time.

'We have not seen even near the best of Steven yet. He is going to continue to get better and better. It was a towering performance."

Celtic entered this game needing three wins for the title. Ten points clear with six to play, that state of play hasn't changed a great deal.

Few doubt the Champion will make it to seven in a row in the end. Yet nights like this are a reminder that it's been less straight forward at times than it should have been. By the end of a strangely demotivated and sloppy display, both teams ended the night with what the result they deserved.

In an unusual role reversal, Dundee keeper Scott Bain was ineligible to play for Celtic under the terms of his loan. Craig Gordon's marking his 200th appearance for the club with his 100th clean sheet. Yet it was, in the end, a slightly joyless experience. A dour and soulless night at the office. 

McGowan attempts to retain possession for Dundee while under pressure from Tom Rogic

The Scotland keeper, at least, was beyond reproach in his first game since January 27. Gordon produced a stunning fingertip save from a dipping Simon Murray long-range drive after Celtic had crafted the first chance in three minutes when Moussa Dembele's slick one two with James Forrest forced Elliot Parish to block with his legs.

Thereafter the home team found it a little harder to find space. When they did their play was over-elaborate, the shooting wild and wayward. Celtic started brightly enough and went collectively downhill.

James Forrest forced Parish into a save in 26 minutes, skinning Glen Kamara and Kevin Holt as he cut in off the right touchline, a low shot pushed wide by the Dundee keeper. From the corner Celtic forged another chance, Boyata glancing Stuart Armstrong's corner towards Scott Sinclair at the back post. Unprepared, the winger couldn't adjust his body fast enough, the ball skimming off his head and over the crossbar.

Celtic had another opening when Mikael Lustig's over-lapping run created a low cut-back for Stuart Armstrong, the midfielder sweeping his shot high over the crossbar.

Slowly, Dundee took confidence from the stalemate.

A slack Jack Hendry pass against his former team placed Celtic in a dangerous position on the halfway line. 

A cynical foul by Scott Brown subsequently poleaxed Roarie Deacon as the attacker threatened a clear run. 

There’s been plenty talk in recent weeks about the stick Celtic’s captain takes from opponents. When he has to, he knows how to give some back. 

After lengthy treatment, Deacon picked himself up and came close to silencing the home crowd when he struck the woodwork in the dying moments of the first half.

Scott Brown enters into a challenge with Dundee's Paul McGowan

In contrast with Dundee’s no-nonsense approach to defending, Hendry and Boyata showed moments of hesitation.

A meat-and-drink long ball through the middle caused panic, Deacon racing through the centre of the home defence and threatening a clear shot on goal until Boyata found the pace to throw out a leg and tackle.

As Dundee supporters howled in vain for a penalty, the ball appeared to ricochet off the shin of the Dundee player. It skidded low past Gordon before bouncing safely off the base of the upright. Celtic Park heaved a sigh of relief.

McCann’s men weren’t the only team to count their blessings at making it to half-time unscathed.

The lethargy over Celtic Park was obvious and, as the game passed the hour point, it was proving hard to lift.

Armstrong’s low right-foot long-range drive was turned round the post by a scrambling Parish. The Dundee keeper then gratefully fell low to clutch an ineffectual prod towards goal by Sinclair.

Paul McGowan controls the ball for Dundee under pressure from Brown

Celtic were going nowhere fast, Dundee packing 11 men behind the ball and dealing comfortably with a succession of hopeful attacks by employing a high defensive line and catching the home team offside time and again.

Parkhead boss Brendan Rodgers lost patience in 65 minutes, making a rare triple substitution. It was hard to argue with any of them.

Leigh Griffiths replaced Dembele, Olivier Ntcham came on for Tom Rogic and an out-of-touch Sinclair made way for Odsonne Edouard.

Celtic's Scott Sinclair tussles for the ball with Dundee defender Steven Caulker

Edouard’s selfishness was to cost Celtic a few chances, yet the young Frenchman came close to satisfying the craving for a breakthrough nine minutes from time when his back-post effort was deflected wide of goal from ten yards.

In the dying moments of the game, Celtic peppered the Dundee goal.

A floated Griffiths free-kick into a crowded area was nodded inches wide of goal by Hendry and the howls of anguish betrayed a blunt acceptance that, for the fifth time this season, they had featured in a scoreless draw. 

The statisticians will find the last time it happened. It might take some time.
