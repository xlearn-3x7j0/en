Paris Saint-Germain 'join Arsenal and Tottenham' in race for Brazilian starlet

Arsenal's pursuit of Bordeaux starlet Malcom has become more difficult after Paris Saint-Germain entered the race for the 21-year-old.

That is according to French outlet France Football, who say the Ligue 1 leaders have identified the Brazilian as a target when the transfer window opens again at the end of the season.

Malcom is likely to command a fee in excess £35million this summer after impressively breaking on to the scene with his current club.

The Gunners are keen to take him to north London but have a battle on their hands from a clutch of other top European sides.

Mkhitaryan eyeing back-to-back triumphs

Henrikh Mkhitaryan is confident Arsenal can replicate his Europa League win with Manchester United last season to qualify for the Champions League through the back door.

The Armenian, who won UEFA's No2 club competition with United before a January switch to the Gunners, is set to start Thursday’s quarter-final first leg against CSKA Moscow at the Emirates.

But Mkhitaryan, who scored the Reds' clincher in a 2-0 final victory over Ajax, dismissed suggestions Arsenal — who currently sit sixth, the position United finished in last season — will need his know-how of what it takes to win the Europa League.

He said: “I think this team already has a big experience of playing in the Europa League and the Champions League.

“So the only thing we can do is stick together to win the first game. It’s very important to get a good result before the second leg. Obviously we’re going to try to do everything to go as far as we can.”

Wenger on CSKA plans

Arsene Wenger hopes political tension between the UK and Russia will not affect Arsenal's Europa League tie with CSKA Moscow, but admits "nobody knows really what is going on".

The quarter-final clash follows the poisoning of Russian spy Sergei Skripal and his daughter with a nerve agent in Salisbury in February.

It also comes amid calls led by Boris Johnson for England to boycott the 2018 World Cup finals, which will be held in the country in the summer.

Wenger was speaking to the press on Wednesday ahead of the Gunners playing host to the Moscow-based outfit on Thursday night.
