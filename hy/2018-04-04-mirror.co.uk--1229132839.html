Saunders willing to face Golovkin - but there's a catch

Billy Joe Saunders is willing to step in at short notice to face fellow world champion Gennady Golovkin - but not until June.

Golovkin is looking for an opponent after Saul 'Canelo' Alvarez withdrew from their May 5 rematch following two failed drugs tests.

Irishman Spike O'Sullivan is the current frontrunner to face the fearsome Kazakh in Las Vegas, but Saunders is also willing to face the WBA, IBF and WBC champion.

Saunders, who holds the one remaining belt Golovkin desires, is currently nursing a hand injury and will not be fit until June.

His promoter Frank Warren wants Golovkin to travel to London for a summer showdown at a football stadium.

"Although Billy Joe can't recover in time for May 5 he could go as early as the first or second Saturday in June," he told the Daily Mail .

"I am making a huge offer for Gennady to come to a football ground in London. Otherwise we are just as happy to go to Las Vegas, where Golovkin is keen to headline for the first time in his career.

"The T-Mobile Area in Vegas are already refunding the high-price tickets for Canelo-Golovkin. Their problem now is that the date coincides with a Mexican national holiday and most of those expensive seats have been taken by the thousands who come across the southern border to celebrate.

"It might be wiser for Gennady to take a slightly smaller arena in June and fill it with Billy Joe, who really believes he has a great chance of winning that fight."

Saunders is due to defend his title against Martin Murray on June 23 but that fight would be shelved if Golovkin accepts Warren's offer.
