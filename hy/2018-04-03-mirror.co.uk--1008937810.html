Manchester United 'set to make star second best paid player' in club's history

David De Gea is set to snub Real Madrid's interest again and sign a new five-year contract at Manchester United.

Shot stopper De Gea has been a long term target for the La Liga giants, and came within a faulty fax machine of moving to the Bernabeu in 2015.

But United are poised to make the Spain international their second highest paid player, behind Alexis Sanchez, with a £350,000-a-week deal, according to the Sun.

His current deal runs out at the end of this season, although there is an option for another year.

De Gea has been the Red Devils' star man this season with a series of supernatural saves keeping them on top in the battle for second place.

Manchester United's Jose Mourinho takes aim at Liverpool and Tottenham as he closes in on second place

They may be no match for local rivals and ­soon-to-be-crowned champions Manchester City, but Jose Mourinho insists his side remain the best of the rest, writes Mirror Football's David McDonnell.

While City are a class apart, Mourinho is angered his Manchester United team do not receive the same plaudits reserved for Liverpool and ­Tottenham, both of whom trail them in the table.

Set for their highest finish since Sir Alex Ferguson walked off into the sunset with a 20th title in 2013, Mourinho went on the attack after consolidating second spot with a 2-0 win over Swansea.

Asked if his side had it within them to beat City at the Etihad next Saturday and delay their coronation, Mourinho dismissed the significance of the encounter and launched another impassioned defence of United’s progress under him.

“When is that?” he said, ­feigning disinterest in the ­eagerly-awaited derby clash.

“Next week? I’m not thinking yet about it.

“Manchester City is not important for me. What is important for me is that since the moment we left first position and went to ­second, we stayed there for the whole of the season.

“We deserved to be second, in spite of what you can say, the criticisms you make. You all say the third, fourth, fifth, sixth (placed teams) are better than us, but they’re not, because we have more points than them.

“We’re going to fight every match. Seven to go — we’re going to fight to try to finish second.

“We played very well, moved the ball well, we pressed them high, they were always far from our goal. We played very well.

“I think we have 10 more points than last season, ­something like that [Eight more than after 31 games in 2016-17]. We have more goals scored, less goals conceded, we finished sixth last season, so we’re going to try to finish second, so the season is not as bad as you try to make it out to be.”

Manchester United pre-season tour 2018 confirmed as Jose Mourinho's men face off against MLS and Mexican sides

Manchester United have announced their first fixtures for this summer's pre-season tour.

Jose Mourinho will take his side Stateside once again, where they will compete in the International Champions Cup against some of Europe's biggest teams.

But as well as games against more local opposition, United will face off against opponents from the MLS and Mexico.

They will travel to the Phoenix University Stadium on July 19 to take on Club America.

United then head to the Levi's Stadium in Santa Clara to face the San Jose Earthquakes on July 22. They took on Real Madrid in the same venue last summer.

The International Champions Cup fixtures are expected to be confirmed in the next week, with United set to face off against Liverpool in the world's second largest stadium.

Over 107,000 fans can fit into the Michigan Stadium - also known as 'The Big House'.

It will be United's second trip to the eye-catching venue, which was filled to capacity when they played Real Madrid there four years ago.
