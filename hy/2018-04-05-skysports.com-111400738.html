David Warner accepts 12-month suspension from Cricket Australia for ball-tampering

Last Updated: 05/04/18 6:50am

David Warner has confirmed he will not appeal against the sanctions imposed on him by Cricket Australia for his role in the recent ball-tampering scandal against South Africa.

Former Australia vice-captain Warner was suspended for 12 months for his role in using sandpaper to alter the condition of the ball during the third Test against the Proteas in Cape Town.

"I have today let Cricket Australia know that I fully accept the sanctions imposed on me," Warner wrote on Twitter on Thursday.

"I am truly sorry for my actions and will now do everything I can to be a better person, teammate and role model."
