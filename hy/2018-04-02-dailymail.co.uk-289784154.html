Rep Darren Soto said his wife 'drank too much' and argued with family

The wife of a member of Congress was arrested on Easter Sunday in Florida after she stopped using medications for depression pursuant to her doctor's orders and 'she drank too much' alcohol.

Amanda Soto, 33, who is married to Democratic US Representative Darren Soto, 40, was charged with disorderly intoxication on Sunday by the Orange County Sheriff's Office.

'She deeply regrets her actions and takes full responsibility for them,' according to a statement released by her husband's office.

Amanda Soto was released from custody early on Monday.

'My wife, Amanda, has for years suffered from depression and been under medical care. In accordance with her treatment plan and under her doctor’s supervisor, she recently stopped using her medications. [Sunday], she drank too much and reached an argumentative state with a family member, which led to [her] arrest,' the statement read.

'Amanda and her physician will be reviewing her mental health treatment immediately.'

The statute she is accused of violating reads: 'No person in the state shall be intoxicated and endanger the safety of another person or property, and no person in the state shall be intoxicated or drink any alcoholic beverage in a public place or in or upon any public conveyance and cause a public disturbance.' 

The disorderly intoxication charge is a second degree misdemeanor in the state of Florida, meaning it's punishable by up to one year in county jail.

Rep. Soto represents the 9th District for the state of Florida in the US House of Representative.

That district includes all of Osceola and parts of Orange and Polk counties.

Soto was elected to the House in 2016 and began his term in 2017, after nearly 10 years of collective service in both the Florida state House and Senate.
