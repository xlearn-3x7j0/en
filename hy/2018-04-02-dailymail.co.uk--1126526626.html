Heynckes insists Real Madrid have 'no chance' of getting Lewandowski

  • Jupp Heynckes says Real Madrid have no chance of buying Robert Lewandowski 
  • Bayern Munich boss dismissed talk of a move in a press-conference on Monday
  • The German club trained ahead of Champions League quarter-final with Sevilla
  • The Bundesliga champions made their final preparations before Tuesday's game 

Published: 14:00 EDT, 2 April 2018 | Updated: 14:00 EDT, 2 April 2018

Bayern Munich manager Jupp Heynckes has closed the door on a Robert Lewandowski exit, insisting that Real Madrid have 'no chance' of signing the striker.

The Poland international has been linked with a move to the Spanish capital at the end of the season but Bayern's head coach has dismissed talk of a move.

'[Chairman] Karl-Heinz Rummenigge has already said it: with Robert, Real Madrid have no chance at all,' he told a news conference.

The 72-year-old remained coy over Franck Ribery's and Arjen Robben's futures at the club with their contracts expiring in the summer.

Heynckes said: ' I have my opinion on Ribery and Robben. But I will hold back here with my recommendations,' he said.

'The club will know what they are and will decide in due course.'

The Bayern Munich boss was talking ahead of his side's Champions League quarter-final first-leg clash with Sevilla on Monday.

The German told reporters he 'only wanted a win' against his Spanish opponents on Tuesday so that can take a positive scoreline back to the Allianz Arena.

He said: 'I'm not looking for a particular scoreline, I want a win. We mustn't go into the match with a particular result in mind, we should go all out to win the game. A goalless draw is deceptive, as Manchester United found out.'

As for team news, Heynckes added: 'Vidal has trained again and I'm assuming he'll be available tomorrow. Juan Bernat is also fit again.'
