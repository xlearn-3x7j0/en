Augusta National Will Host a Women’s Golf Tournament Next Year

AUGUSTA, Ga. — Augusta National Golf Club, which did not admit its first female members until 2012, announced on Wednesday that it would host part of a national women’s amateur championship next year in the week before hosting the Masters.

“This is a dream come true,” said the retired star Annika Sorenstam, who attended the announcement. “It will be an exciting carrot for these young amateurs.”

Beginning next year, the 54-hole event will feature an international field of 72 elite players with the first two 18-hole rounds taking place at the Champions Retreat Golf Club in Augusta. The final round will be at the 85-year-old Augusta National, which has never hosted an amateur tournament.

Sixteen years ago, the club received national scrutiny over its membership policies after Martha Burk, then the head of the National Council of Women’s Organizations, wrote a private letter to the club’s chairman at the time, William Johnson, questioning the exclusion of women.

Johnson answered Burk with a sharply worded news release that said, in part: “There may well come a day when women will be invited to join our membership, but that timetable will be ours, and not at the point of a bayonet.”

A firestorm ensued that included extensive news media coverage and a small protest led by Burk outside the gates of Augusta National during the 2003 Masters.

Fred Ridley, who became Augusta National’s chairman last year, announced plans for the women’s tournament on the eve of this year’s Masters.

“We believe this new event will have a significant and long-lasting impact on the future of the women’s game,” Ridley said.

The event will be called the Augusta National Women’s Amateur Championship, and the final round will be on Saturday, Apr. 6, 2019, after the field is pared to players with the top 30 results.

The tournament is expected to be televised nationally, and daily tickets will also be made available through a random selection process. Tickets to the Masters, which has been contested since 1934, are among the most sought after in American sports.

Ridley said the women’s tournament would be an extension of Masters week, which in recent years has included a youth drive, chip and putt competition on the Sunday before the Masters.

The new Augusta amateur event could represent a scheduling conflict for the L.P.G.A.

The ANA Inspiration, the first major of the year on the women’s calendar, is held the week before the Masters. Ridley said Wednesday that he had spoken to L.P.G.A. Commissioner Mike Whan and that Whan had agreed that the Augusta event was a “big win for women’s golf.”
