Liverpool run riot as red-hot Anfield atmosphere sees Man City cave in

The history is only half written, but this night will go down in Anfield folklore.

The night when Pep Guardiola’s all conquering Manchester City crumbled as their Champions League campaign went up in a cloud of red smoke.

Liverpool were too good, too fast, too dangerous and their famous old stadium came alive again to revive memories of past European glories and inspire Jurgen Klopp’s new generation of heroes.

It was high octane stuff, Liverpool’s tempo, midfield energy and attacking prowess was too much for City as suddenly Guardiola’s Premier League champions-elect were made to look very ordinary.

Mo Salah, Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain and Sadio Mane scored in a breathtaking first half as every fan there to witness the demolition was left looking at each other in a state of utter disbelief.

Make no mistake, this was a reminder of the magic of one of football’s great European cathedrals because the red-hot atmosphere and deafening noise intimidated City. In fact, City buckled and caved in.

Liverpool’s fans lay in wait for the City team bus, showering it with bottles, coins and red flares as it arrived. When they got into the stadium, City were given no peace either as Liverpool tore into them while the away end fell silent amid the high drama on the pitch.

Klopp had claimed this was a night when Liverpool could write a new chapter of history, make their own mark alongside the five European Cups of the past.

Even though the job is only half done and the final is still a long way in the distance, this victory will give Klopp’s men reason to believe they can go all the way. And if they do rule Europe again then this might just prove to be their most significant result.

After all, Klopp hailed City as the best team in Europe and Liverpool simply blew them away in what was a fabulous advert for the Premier League and definitely one of the best all English knock out ties.

The strange thing was that City actually started quite well, Kevin De Bruyne looked to be running the show as he picked passes in midfield and Liverpool were on the back foot.

But if you needed a deciding argument as to who should be player of the year then maybe Salah provided it with a single moment of magic after 12 minutes. De Bruyne sparkled but Salah was decisive.

James Milner played the ball forward down the right, Salah broke forward and released Roberto Firmino but City keeper Ederson made a brave block, Kyle Walker failed to clear and the ball broke loose in the six yard box.

You could almost feel the Kop end behind the goal suck the ball towards the net as Firmino’s lay-off set up Salah to smash home into the roof of the net after just 12 minutes.

Anfield came alive, the roof came off as suddenly the much lauded atmosphere and noise was deafening. That was Salah’s 38th goal in all competitions this season, an unbelievable season from an unbelievable player.

Eight minutes later, it got even worse for City. Vincent Kompany fouled Firmino, German referee Felix Brych played an excellent advantage and Oxlade-Chamberlain hit a 20 yard screamer which flew past Ederson. And to think people questioned why he left Arsenal.

City had no answer, no reply and no clue. Guardiola looked stumped on the touchline, unable to make any changes or tactical tweaks to stem the tide.

After 31 minutes, Salah turned provider. Salah saw his shot blocked, went back to being winger as he stood up an inch perfect cross to the back post and Mane raced in to power home a close range header.

Incredibly, Guardiola did not make any half time changes but City did come out for the second half desperately looking for a precious away goal to give themselves a lifeline.

Leroy Sane came alive on City’s left, suddenly finding space and twice went close while Liverpool lost some of their momentum as Salah hobbled off injured after 52 minutes.

City threw on Raheem Sterling, surprisingly left out of the starting line-up, and he received a predictably hot reception after his acrimonious departure from Liverpool three years ago.

Suddenly it became a very different Liverpool performance, every player in red battling and scrapping to shut down the visitors who got frustrated as they were unable to find a way through.

It was a night which belonged to Liverpool and Klopp, his remarkable hold over Guardiola now winning seven of their 13 encounters and this will go down as arguably the most memorable of all.
