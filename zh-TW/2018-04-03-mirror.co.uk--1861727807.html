Manchester United reveal plans to boost atmosphere at Old Trafford

Manchester United have revealed plans to boost the atmosphere at Old Trafford after a series of complaints from boss Jose Mourinho.

The Portuguese tactician has been critical of the crowd on several occasions during his tenure at the helm of the club - and now United are planning to back him.

On Tuesday morning they announced a price freeze on season tickets for the seventh campaign in a row -at the same time revealed the plans to get Old Trafford rocking.

The Red Devils plan to amp up the noise levels in the Stretford end by offering thousands of cut-price tickets to 18-25-year-olds.

Priced at just £15, the tickets will be available in the lower tier with the club saying it "reinforces their commitment to a vibrant atmosphere while helping younger fans to watch live, affordable football".

United are further offering half-price season tickets to Under 18s and Over 65s and a 25 per cent discount for 18 to 20-year-olds.

"The club enjoys unparalleled support and we recognise the sacrifice many fans make to watch the team home and away,"said managing director Richard Arnold.

"Continuing the freeze in match and season tickets for next season is driven by our belief in supporting fans attending our games.

"The introduction of reduced ticket prices for young adults in the Stretford End lower tier stems from suggestions generated by fan groups through our Fans’ Forum, aimed at building on the tradition of vibrant support."
