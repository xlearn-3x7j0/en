Coach brother of two-time taekwondo gold medalist Steven Lopez banned

The older brother and coach of two-time Olympic taekwondo champion Steven Lopez has been banned from the sport for sexual misconduct, including involving a minor. 

Jean Lopez was barred by the U.S. Center for SafeSport on Tuesday after officials examined allegations from several women and concluded they showed a 'recurrent pattern' of wrongdoing.

The allegations date back to 1997 and were originally investigated by USA Taekwondo before being handed to SafeSport when it was set up in March 2017.

The investigation into Lopez sprung from an article in USA Today, which reported sexual misconduct claims made against the coach by three women. One of these women filed a complaint with US Taekwondo in 2006. 

SafeSport examined this complaint alongside those of several other women and announced its decision to rule Jean Lopez as 'permanently ineligible' on Wednesday morning, but noted this was not yet final and could be appealed. 

The organization said: 'Given the number of incidents reported over a span of several years and by multiple reporting parties, most of whom have no reasonable motive to fabricate an allegation – much less multiple, distinct incidents – of misconduct, the totality of the circumstances clearly shows a recurrent pattern of behavior on the part of Jean.

'Both individually and taken in their totality, the reported incidents either constitute non-consensual sexual misconduct and/or are contrary to long-standing community standards regarding appropriate interactions between team members.'

Jean Lopez has always denied the allegations and Dailymail.com has contacted him for comment.  

Separate allegations against Steven Lopez were reported in June and handed over to SafeSport. 

Steven, who has won five world championship titles, denies the allegations and the status of his case is unclear.

Both Steven (left, in the 2016 Rio Games) and Jean Lopez, were allowed to participate in the 2016 Olympics despite an ongoing investigation into sexual misconduct

In May, an FBI agent interviewed former Team USA athlete Mandy Meloon, accused Jean of assaulting her,USA Today reported.

Meloon said a SafeSport investigator was still trying to arrange an interview with Steven.  

Another athlete, Heidi Gilbert, came forward with accusations that Jean drugged and assaulted her.

She responded to the news Jean had been banned by saying: 'It just took way too long. But I am relieved. And very excited that no girl is ever going to have to deal with him again.'

Two other women, who spoke on the condition of anonymity, made similar allegations against the two brothers.

The USA Taekwondo probe into their alleged misconduct was launched two years ago - meaning both Steven and Jean were allowed to represent the US at the Rio Olympics despite the allegations. 

Mandy Meloon (left) and Heidi Gilbert have both made claims of sexual misconduct against the Lopez brothers

Meloon, who was released from prison last year for assaulting a sheriff's deputy in 2015, made shocking claims against both brothers during an FBI interview in May last year.

When Meloon was just 16, Jean went to her room during a 1997 World Cup event in Cairo and inserted his finger in her vagina, she claimed. 

Meloon reported the incident in a complaint she filed against him in 2006, but USA Taekwondo found her allegations 'weren't credible'. Jean also denied assaulting Meloon.

But Meloon claimed USA Taekwondo told her to sign a contract to prevent her from being alone with Jean without investigating the matter.

Gilbert, who trained with the Lopezes, claimed she was assaulted by Jean at the 2002 Pan American Championships in Ecuador.

When his younger sister Diana stepped out of the hotel room leaving her alone with Jean, he closed the door and allegedly started simulating intercourse while they were both clothed, Gilbert claims.

When they traveled to Germany the next year, Jean handed her a drink during a party to celebrate the end of the world championships, Gilbert said.

She said she was conscious, but unable to move or speak within 15 minutes.

According to Gilbert, Jean took her back to the hotel, where he choked and slapped her, inserted his finger in her vagina, performed oral sex on her, and rubbed his penis between her legs after he tried but failed to remove her pants.

'I could feel everything that was going on. I was pretty out of it, but I definitely knew. I couldn't move, I couldn't respond,' Gilbert told USA Today.

But Jean said: 'I've never been inappropriate with Heidi. I can't say anything negative about her. She was my athlete. I was her coach, but I've never been inappropriate with her.' 

Another woman, who had been on the junior national team, said Steven Lopez raped her while she was drugged and unconscious.

A fourth woman accused Jean Lopez of raping her while the two had a consensual relationship in her 20s.

When the allegations were reported in 2017, Jean said he had not been contacted by USA Taekwondo, Safesport or law enforcement about them.  

The Olympic movement has been criticised for its handling of sexual abuse cases following revelations of widespread abuse by USA Gymnastics physician Larry Nassar.
