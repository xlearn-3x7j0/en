Barcelona scouted major transfer target during England's victory over Holland

Barcelona scouts were present in Amsterdam last week as they were keeping an eye on a transfer target they've been watching for some time.

Matthijs De Ligt, 18 has been impressing for Ajax this season and Barca officials have now watched him in his last three games.

Barcelona technical secretary, Robert Fernandez, was present in Amsterdam for his team's 4-1 win over Heerenveen in which De Ligt scored the first goal of the rout.

Pepe Serer watched him play England whilst Urbano Ortega was sent to watch Portugal-Netherlands, with both trusted aides of Fernandez.

De Light has made over 50 appearances for Ajax and was the youngest player to make his Holland debut since 1931 back in March 2012 at 17-years-old.

His form has got a lot of European giants talking, with Barca favourite for his signature and national team-mate and Liverpool centre-half Virgil van Dijk says he can play for whoever he wants.

Speaking to De Telegraaf , he said: "Matthijs De Ligt is an excellent player, already mature for 18 years old.

"'Good enough is old enough', they often say. Of course I do not know what options he has [for a transfer], but when I see how he plays for Holland, it does not matter if he leaves the Eredivise now or in two years, that's up to him."
