Brendan Rodgers hails Craig Gordon after his 100th Celtic clean sheet

Brendan Rodgers saluted comeback keeper Craig Gordon for achieving the 100th clean sheet of his Celtic career on an otherwise frustrating night for the Parkhead side.

Returning from injury to play his 200th game for the club, the Scotland No1 made his first start since January 27 in a goalless draw with disciplined visitors Dundee.

Gordon's personal landmark was the only highlight for Rodgers on a night when he bemoaned his team's lack of a cutting edge.

'It's a great statistic for Craig, considering a few years ago he thought he was maybe done,' said the Celtic boss.

'It was great to have him back. He's back early and looked like he'd never been away. He was very composed and it's great for him.

'He was very alert. He's worked very hard in his rehabilitation. He's a fit guy, he really looks after his body and has a good eye on the game.

'There were no nerves, he picked his passes in the right moments. What he had to do, he did very well.'

Celtic's fourth home scoreless draw of the season - their sixth home draw in the league - leaves them ten points clear of second place Aberdeen with six games to play.

Kept at bay by a superb defensive display from Neil McCann's side, a philosophical Rodgers added: 'My whole reflection on the game is frustration that we didn't have that cutting edge.

'I thought a lot of our play was very good. We worked the ball through the thirds really well, got into lots of really good areas.

'It was a mix of us not having that final bit of quality in the final third to make the breakthrough and some good defending by Dundee. Their three central defenders blocked spaces, were very committed and made it difficult for us.

'So we are disappointed we couldn't make that breakthrough, because if we got it early on it would have opened up even more for us.

'We've seen it here a lot of times, teams hang in there if we don't make that breakthrough.

'But, listen, there are six games to go and we are ten points clear. It's a clean sheet, there was some good football. It was just that little bit of cutting edge we lacked.'

Dark Blues boss McCann was delighted with an unexpected point in this rearranged fixture, extending his side's lead over Ross County and Partick Thistle to five points.

'I'm very proud of my team,' he said. 'Celtic are the top team in the country by a stretch and I couldn't be happier. To a man my players were brilliant.

'We could have even stolen it at the end when Mark O'Hara fired a great ball across the face of goal.

'When we play with that level of intensity we are a tough team to break down and we're also a threat going forward.'
