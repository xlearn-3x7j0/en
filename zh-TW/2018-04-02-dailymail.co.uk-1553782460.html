Sheffield United v Cardiff to go ahead as snow claims several fixtures

Cardiff's trip to Sheffield United could still go ahead despite the cancellation of numerous sporting fixtures across the country due to snowfall.

The Blades have opened their box office, club shop and reception despite originally closing them on Monday morning due to adverse weather conditions and have announced the game is set to go ahead despite the challenges they face.

On Monday, United's official Twitter account announced that they had re-opened the previously closed facilities, writing: 'Reception Superstore and Box Office now open! Game on tonight as planned.'

Neil Warnock is hoping his Cardiff side are able to play their match against Sheffield United

Warnock was left raging last month as Cardiff's trip to Derby was called off

Cardiff manager Neil Warnock will breathe a sigh of relief as he feared a second match cancellation in little-over two weeks after Derby had their clash in March postponed due to 'unsafe conditions'. 

It was met by a raging Warnock, who slammed the 'scandalous' decision made only four hours before kick-off. He said on Sky Sports: 'Obviously there is snow but I think it's a disgrace quite frankly.

'You look at the game two weeks ago here against Fulham and if you look on the website all the Derby fans are saying it was ten times worse. As you see there is nothing here, the car parks are clear, all the shops are open.'

There is, however, still a chance that cancellation could occur, which would further delay Warnock's season as his Cardiff side continue their push for Premier League promotion: A victory would move them 10 points clear of third-placed Fulham.

On Monday Fixtures all across the country fell victim to the adverse weather conditions

The Blades' under 23 side have already had their game with Colchester, scheduled for the same day, cancelled as fixtures across the country - with numerous Football League games at risk - are set to fall victim to another bout of snow.

Barnsley's trip to Nottingham Forest became the first Championship game to be postponed due to a water-logged pitch, with six other second-tier fixtures in danger of facing the same fate.

Bradford's League One fixture against Walsall is one of the many to be affected and has been postponed.

A 10am inspection had to be pushed back with referee Carl Boyeson stuck in traffic on the M62 and when he finally arrived at the Northern Commercial Stadium he found the pitch was unplayable.

Boyeson also noted the wet weather is forecast to continue throughout Easter Monday. 

Elsewhere, Wimbledon's trip to Doncaster in the third-tier was also called off, closely followed by Scunthorpe versus Plymouth, due to similar circumstances.

Unseasonal Easter weather has also played havoc with the racing fixture list.

This afternoon's meetings at Chepstow, Fakenham, Market Rasen, Huntingdon, Redcar and Plumpton were all washed out while Tuesday's programme has also been badly hit.

THE RIDICULOUS WORLD OF NON-LEAGUE NORTHWICH

With the extreme weather conditions causing havoc across the UK, the woes of many football clubs will not come close to that of non-league 1874 Northwich.

The North West Counties Premier Division side were one of the many professional and non-professional teams to see their fixture cancelle due to a water-logged pitch. 

However, it has added one more game to a ridiculous end-of-season run-in that will see Northwich play 19 games in a month.

And if there luck couldn't get any worse, the location for their next fixture - at Barnoldswick Town FC - doesn't make very good viewing for the players and coaching staff of Northwich.

With their season to come to its a long-awaited conclusion which, should they complete their over-packed schedule, will see them play 77 matches within a solitary season, it's fair to say a lengthy summer break is due for Northwich.
