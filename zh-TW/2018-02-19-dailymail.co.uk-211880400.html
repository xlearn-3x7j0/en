AC Milan goalkeeper Gianluigi Donnarumma has the best future career prospects of any teenager in Europe based on his appearances over the past two years.

The CIES Football Observatory have published a report on the most promising footballers under the age of 20 and Donnarumma has come out on top ahead of Toulouse goalkeeper Alban Lafont and PSG striker Kylian Mbappe.

CIES' research analyses the domestic league experience cumulated during the last two years and gives players a star rating as an indicator of how likely they are to establish themselves in the very highest level of European football.

Gianluigi Donnarumma has been ranked as the most promising teenager in European football

Donnarumma is first, Lafont second and Mbappe third while Borussia Dortmund's 19-year-old American winger Christian Pulisic is ranked fourth. 

Everton midfielder Tom Davies is the highest-ranked English player in seventh while Ryan Sessegnon's performances for Championship side Fulham earn him 10th spot.

Leeds' Ronaldo Vieira is 20th while Manchester United defender Timothy Fosu-Mensah, on loan at Crystal Palace, is ranked 32nd. 

Manchester City's Brazilian midfielder Douglas Luiz, on loan at Girona, comes 36th and Liverpool defender Trent Alexander-Arnold (41) is the final domestic-based player in the top 50.

Donnarumma has established himself as Milan's first-choice goalkeeper since breaking into the Rossoneri's XI at the age of just 16 in October 2015.

Not 19 until later this month, Donnarumma's performances have attracted interest from Real Madrid and the 6ft 5ins goalkeeper is seen as the natural heir to Gianluigi Buffon's Italy No 1 jersey.  

Fellow stopper Lafont became the youngest-ever goalkeeper to start a Ligue 1 game when he made his Toulouse debut in November 2015, a month after Donnarumma's debut for AC Milan. 

The keeper was born in Burkina Faso but has represented France's youth teams and kept 11 clean sheets in 38 Ligue 1 appearances last year for a middling Toulouse side.

Mbappe has netted 15 times in his first 29 appearances for the Parisians and has since played 10 times for the France national team.

HOW THE CIES FOOTBALL OBSERVATORY RANKING WORKS 

CIES Football Observatory analyses the domestic league experience of Under-20 players based on their senior appearances over the past two seasons. 

Minutes played by each player is weighted according to the sporting level of the leagues they have featured in as well as results achieved by their clubs. 

The top-10 are all awarded a five-star rating, an indicator that those players should be able to establish themselves in the very best European teams. 

There are 18 four-star rated players - which indicates that they have a concrete possibility to spend the majority of their career playing in the most competitive European domestic leagues. 

The capital experience methodology allows scouts to compare players across each league from an objective basis - based on their experience.
