A commercial flight from Bangladesh crashed at the main airport in Kathmandu, Nepal, on Monday afternoon, an airline official said.

The flight, operated by US-Bangla Airlines, took off from Dhaka and crashed upon arrival at Tribhuvan International Airport, said Kamrul Islam, general manager of marketing support for the airline.

“Further details of the crash are still awaited,” he said.

The flight swerved off the runway during landing and crashed into a soccer field about 2:20 p.m., The Kathmandu Post reported, citing an airport spokesman. There was no word yet on casualties.

The airplane caught fire after the crash, the newspaper said. Images on social media showed heavy black smoke rising from the airport.

The airline’s posted schedule said Flight 211, a Bombardier Dash 8 from Dhaka, was scheduled to land at 2:15 p.m. The plane, a twin-engine turboprop, can carry as many as 78 passengers.

US-Bangla Airlines began operations in 2014, and its route between Dhaka and Kathmandu was its first international one, said the Center for Aviation. The airline is a subsidiary of the US-Bangla Group, a joint American-Bangladeshi company.
