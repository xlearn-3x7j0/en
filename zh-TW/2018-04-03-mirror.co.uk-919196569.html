Sane reacts to Liverpool fans' plans to frighten Man City players

Leroy Sane is relishing the chance to play in a red-hot atmosphere at Anfield on Wednesday evening.

Home supporters are planning to line the streets of Liverpool to give the visitors a red-hot reception on their journey to the ground, reports the Manchester Evening News.

A poster has been circulated around social media attempting to organise fans for a "coach greeting" which would stretch a quarter of a mile from Anfield.

However, Sane declared it will be 'fun' to play in what is expected to be a fervent home crowd.

The 22-year-old Germany international was in marvellous form at Goodison Park, and while he is expecting a stiffer test on the other side of Stanley Park in the first leg of the quarter-final, he says the much-vaunted Merseyside atmosphere will not be a factor.

Even at his tender age, the flying Blues winger says he has experienced some seething football cauldrons – and reckons few can touch the Schalke vs Dortmund derby matches in Germany.

He played his first Ruhr derby as a 19-year-old in front of 80,000 people, and was also impressed by the noise generated by Celtic fans in last season's 3-3 City draw in Glasgow.

But Anfield, where he scored a superb opening goal in the 4-3 defeat in January, holds no fears for him: “It's a really good stadium and the atmosphere is really good, but I don't think we'll bother too much about the noise.

“We always try to play our games and will be focused enough.

“For me, it makes it a lot of fun to play when the atmosphere is really loud.

“Even if it is coming from the other team, it pushes you a bit more. Everyone is really excited about the game.

“For the best atmospheres, I remember the derby between Schalke and Dortmund, and playing at Celtic last season, which was amazing.”
