Boss of Prudential’s fund management arm paid more than £3m last year

Published: 16:50 EDT, 1 April 2018 | Updated: 07:33 EDT, 2 April 2018

The boss of Prudential’s fund management arm was paid more than £3m last year, making her one of the FTSE 100’s best-paid female executives.

Anne Richards, the chief executive of M&G Investments, took over from predecessor Michael McLintock in 2016.

High earner: Anne Richards, the chief executive of M&G Investments, took over from predecessor Michael McLintock in 2016

Other executives who benefited from the Pru pay bonanza include the group’s chief executive, Mike Wells. He pocketed £23.6m over two years – including nearly £2m for mortgage interest payments and other costs for his home in London.

A spokesman for Prudential said: ‘This year Prudential has reported record profits, successfully delivered on tough long-term financial objectives, lifted the dividend by 8 per cent and continued to produce strong returns for customers.’

Richards’ pay package puts her among the top flight of women executives at UK blue chip firms. There are just a handful of female FTSE chief executives, who earn an average of £2.6m.

Among them are ITV’s new boss Carolyn McCall, who took home £757,000 in her final year as chief executive of Easyjet, and Emma Walmsley, who earned nearly £5m in her first nine months as boss of drug maker Glaxosmithkline.
