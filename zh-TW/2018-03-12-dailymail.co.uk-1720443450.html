People see vegetarian meals as guilt-free and less fatty than meat-based dishes, but that's not always the case.

Although meatless options served at fast food restaurants may contain more fiber and vitamins from vegetables, they can have just as much fat and calories as their counterparts.

As meat-free diets are becoming increasingly more popular in the US, many restaurant chains are adjusting their menus to satisfy this customer base.

Speaking with Daily Mail Online, experts explain whether people sacrifice nutrition for convenience when they eat vegetarian fast food meals.

Meatless options like a black bean burger (pictured) may contain more fat and calories than traditional vegetarian meals due to additives that enhance flavor

Many vegetarian fast food meals contain more fat than meat-based dishes 

Registered dietitian Paul Salter told Daily Mail Online that while vegetarian fast food may contain less fat than meat-based meals, they are also high in calories and sodium.

'When you think of a vegetarian diet, your mind goes to fruits and vegetables,' said Salter, founder of Fit In Your Dress, told Daily Mail Online. 'But fast food restaurants don't have the money, space or capacity to store rich produce.'

As a result, many of the vegetable-based meals offered at fast food restaurants are loaded with tons of preservatives and additives to enhance flavor - and to make up for the loss of meat.

'Tofu is a healthy option,' he said. 'It's a great source of protein, but when you add things to it like cheese to bring up the flavor... the calories add up.' 

For instance, a veggie burrito bowl at Chipotle containing brown rice, black beans, fajita vegetables and romaine lettuce contains just 370 calories, 65 grams of carbohydrates and 555 milligrams of sodium.

When guacamole is added, those numbers jump to 600 calories. This is way more than the calories contained in a single patty hamburger and cheeseburger offered at Shake Shack.

This meat-free bowl also contain 925 milligrams of sodium compared to the 327 and 666 milligrams in a single patty hamburger and cheeseburger offered at Shake Shack, respectively.

Furthermore, a 'Shroom Burger at Shake Shack - a vegetarian sandwich that substitutes a meat patty with breaded and deep-fried cheese-stuffed portobello mushroom - contains more calories, fat, sodium, carbohydrates and sugars than a single hamburger and a single cheeseburger served at that fast food restaurant.

The meat-free meal contains 490 calories compared to a single patty hamburger, which contains 385 calories and a single patty cheese burger which contains 460 calories. 

However, the 'Shroom burger contains seven grams of fiber compared to the other meat burgers which each contains just three grams of the nutrient. 

Meanwhile, a veggie bowl at Chipotle offers 17 grams of fiber, more than a  'Shroom burger, a single patty hamburger and cheeseburger offered at Shake Shack.

Salter said that although vegetarian fast foods are more nutritionally dense, they are high in fat compared to traditional vegetarian meal.

A veggie burrito bowl at Chipotle contains 29.5 grams of total fat, while a meat-free burrito bowl containing -brown rice, black beans, hot salsa, sour cream cheese and lettuce contain just seven grams of fat, according to MyFitnessPal.

Chemicals found in these foods may cause people to overeat 

Clinical nutritionist Dr David Friedman, author of Food Sanity: How to Eat in a World of Fads and Fiction, told Daily Mail Online that there are a lot of vegetarian fast food meals may contain obesogens, chemicals that can lead to obesity.  

How to eat healthy vegetarian fast food, according to a nutritionist 

1) Pick the right beans

For people looking for meatless options at big taco chains, clinical nutritionist Dr David Friedman suggests opting for black beans as opposed to refried beans.

2) Pick the right tortilla

People should choose a soft taco shell instead of a crispy shell that has been fried.

3) Cut the dairy

Dr Friedman recommends people limit their use of sour cream or cheese.

4) The ideal Subway sandwich

Six-inch sandwiches contain fewer calories than foot-longs.  Dr Friedman also recommends one or two slices of lower-fat cheese and adding extra vegetable toppings.

 5) Choose the right dressing

Substitute cheese, mayo and special sauces with lemon and salsa.

6) Beware of packaging

People should ask that their vegetarian sandwiches be wrapped in a paper towel as opposed to regular fast food packaging to avoid chemicals that contribute to weight gain.

'Plant-based meals aren't the get out of jail free card,' said Dr Friedman. 'I know a lot of vegans who are overweight and that's because they consume a lot of the chemicals placed in fast food.'

For instance, many fast food wrappers and packaging contain per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAs), grease-resistant substances that prevent food grease from soaking through the box and paper packaging used to wrap it.

However, Dr Friedman say these chemicals cause a hormonal imbalance that lead to weight gain.

In fact, research published in PLOS Medicine earlier this year revealed people with higher levels of PFAS in their bloodstream were more likely to gain weight, especially women. The study found that people with elevated levels of this chemical burned fewer calories while doing normal daily activities.

Dr Friedman said these chemicals also muffle leptin, a hormone that decreases hunger, while increasing ghrelin, a hormone that makes people hungry.

'That's why many people supersize their meals at these restaurants, even if they're vegan,' he said. 

How to pick the right veggie fast food meal

Salter said fast foods places like Panera Bread, Chipotle and Subway contain healthier vegetarian options largely because of the way they are stored.

He said Chipotle has the capacity to store fresh food longer than other fast food restaurants and most of their foods are cooked on demand. Which means they are less likely to contain a lot of preservatives.  

Dr Friedman, whose favorite dish from Panera Bread is the Modern Greek quinoa salad without feta cheese, said the fast food chain is very strict and straightforward when it comes to ingredients.

Overall, when it comes to vegetarian fast food meals, Dr Friedman urges people to not be so trusting and to 'dig deeper' to find out 'what's hidden' in their food.

'It's like having the word "fat-free" on a lot of foods that are making people fat,' Dr Friedman. 'Like fat-free ice cream and chocolate chip cookies may not contain fat, but they contain sugar which makes people fat.'
