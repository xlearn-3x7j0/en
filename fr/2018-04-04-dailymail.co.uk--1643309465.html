Eoin Morgan's first-class return with Middlesex delayed by injury

  • England one-day captain Eoin Morgan has suffered a small fracture in his thumb
  • The break will delay the 31-year-old's return to first-class cricket with Middlesex
  • Morgan will be out for at least four weeks according to a Middlesex spokesman

Published: 15:34 EDT, 3 April 2018 | Updated: 20:20 EDT, 3 April 2018

Eoin Morgan's return to first-class cricket after a near three-year absence has been delayed due to a fractured thumb.

England's limited-overs captain declared himself available for the first two months of the 2018 County Championship after being snubbed in January's Indian Premier League auction.

The 31-year-old has not played a four-day match since making a pair against Somerset at Merchant Taylor's School in July 2015.

Eoin Morgan has suffered a fracture in his thumb, delaying his first-class return with Middlesex

But he now faces up to six weeks on the sidelines after incurring the break at a net session at the same ground last Thursday.

'Eoin was hit on the left thumb and x-rays revealed that he suffered a small fracture. He will sadly be out of action for at least four weeks,' said a Middlesex spokesman.

With Morgan expected to be around until the first week of June — when England's one-day team meet up for a one-off match against Scotland followed by a five-match series versus Australia — Middlesex resisted the chance to sign an overseas player for the start of 2018.

But following the Irishman's setback, the unavailability of new captain David Malan for the first two rounds of Championship cricket at England's behest and the absence this month of England Lions batsman Nick Gubbins due to a hamstring strain, they reviewed that decision over the Easter weekend and brought in Australia all-rounder Hilton Cartwright on Monday.
