Arjen Robben was ready to join Man United after Alex Ferguson meet

  • Arjen Robben took tour of Manchester United before eventually joining Chelsea
  • The Dutchman says he was sold on a move after dinner with Sir Alex Ferguson
  • But there was no contact from Old Trafford after he returned to PSV Eindhoven

Published: 08:27 EDT, 4 April 2018 | Updated: 11:03 EDT, 4 April 2018

Arjen Robben has revealed he was ready to sign for Manchester United after he was wooed by then-manager Sir Alex Ferguson over dinner before eventually joining Chelsea.

The Dutchman was one of Europe's hottest prospects back in 2004 and was wanted by a host of elite clubs. Ferguson, however, stole a march on other suitors by inviting him to Manchester.

Robben says he was given a tour of Manchester United's facilities and was ready to sign a deal, but claims they then failed to make a concrete offer. Tired of waiting, the winger jumped at the chance to join Jose Mourinho's Chelsea.

Arjen Robben has revealed his career could have taken a very different path back in 2004

Robben, pictured playing for PSV in 2004, met with Manchester United's Alex Ferguson

Speaking in the May issue of FourFourTwo magazine, he reveals: 'I had a very good conversation with [Ferguson] over dinner in Manchester and we spoke about football and life. I also went and had a good look around the training ground and everything was good.

'But after I went back to PSV nothing happened. There was no real contact and the deal didn't happen. PSV were also negotiating with Chelsea at that time, so maybe they offered PSV more money? I don't really know.

'I spoke to Chelsea and I liked their plans. We had one meeting and everything was done pretty quickly. Had Manchester United offered me a deal straight after I met them, I would have signed there, but it didn't happen and I've got no regrets.'

After Manchester United failed to make an offer, Robben opted to join big-spending Chelsea

The Dutchman would win back-to-back Premier League titles under Jose Mourinho

Robben, who was 20-years-old when he joined Chelsea, went onto win back-to-back Premier League titles at Stamford Bridge before adding a La Liga at Real Madrid before settling at Bayern Munich, where he remains today.

He has won six Bundesliga titles (soon to be seven) and one Champions League during his time at the Allianz Arena and boasts and impressive record of 137 goals in 284 appearances for the German club.

The 34-year-old also has 97 caps and 34 goals for Holland.
