Sunderland 1-3 Sheffield Wednesday: More misery heaped on Black Cats

For three days over Easter weekend they believed that the Great Escape was on, again. Friday night's shock 4-1 victory at Derby was the source of Sunderland's hope.

But not now, not after this demoralising home defeat leaves them five points from safety with six to play. 

The escape is on alright, they'll be exiting the Championship in the coming weeks, becoming only the fifth team to suffer back-to-back relegations from the top-flight to the third-tier.

Atdhe Nuhiu celebrates after rounding off a comfortable afternoon for Sheffield Wednesday

A sodden Chris Coleman celebrated in the rain at Derby but he looked like he was drowning beneath a sea of weariness when he came to reflect here, even if he did try to apply a positive spin.

'Nothing is done until it is mathematically impossible,' he declared, although the Welshman was lacking his usual zest.

'We can go to Leeds on Saturday and win, I'm sure of that. One big result can change the landscape, we can't give up, we have to keep pushing.'

Coleman took his players to the Caterpillar manufacturing plant last week, but this club have been digging holes for years now and League One football will be the culmination of mismanagement at every level, a culture of incompetence the making of absent owner Ellis Short.

Perhaps the manager should have used that free afternoon to work on defending crosses. It took Sheffield Wednesday an hour to discover that Sunderland are incapable of doing so and, three deliveries later, they had scored three times.

'When you are fragile and have been hit over the head for so long there is that vulnerability,' added Coleman, who must have felt like clashing some heads together himself such was the ease with which the visitors got their goals.

Barry Bannan's delivery just before the hour was not particularly venomous but still it stung Sunderland and Atdhe Nuhiu rose unopposed to nod down for Lucas Joao to volley home.

The hosts were actually level within 90 seconds when George Honeyman headed in from a Lynden Gooch centre, Wednesday's defenders doing their best impression of Sunderland's hapless backline.

But the Owls knew that cross equalled goal and so it proved when captain Tom Lees was afforded the freedom of the six-yard area to volley in from Joey Pelupessy's set-piece delivery.

Five minutes later and same again, Adam Reach centring from the left with Nuhiu turning in after Lee Camp had saved from Joao's flick.

A Sunderland penalty appeal had split the goals, Gooch going down under the sliding challenge of Bannan.

And Coleman later fumed: 'There was a defining moment when we should have had a pen and they go down to 10 men (Bannan was on a yellow). Everyone in the stadium saw that and thought the same thing - penalty.'

Replays, however, suggested that the American winger had fallen a little too easily.

But that is what Sunderland have been doing all season, tumbling towards League One with little resistance.
