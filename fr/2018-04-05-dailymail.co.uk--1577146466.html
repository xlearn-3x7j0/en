Juventus make Atletico's Antoine Griezmann top summer transfer target

  • Juventus have made Antoine Griezmann their top summer transfer target 
  • The Atletico Madrid forward has been heavily linked with a move to Barcelona
  • Manchester United also retain an interest in the talented 27-year-old attacker 
  • The Serie A leaders want to add firepower to Paulo Dybala and Gonzalo Higuain 

Published: 06:04 EDT, 5 April 2018 | Updated: 08:19 EDT, 5 April 2018

Juventus have made Atletico Madrid forward Antoine Griezmann their No 1 summer transfer target. 

According to Corriere dello Sport, via Football Italia, the Bianconeri want to add greater firepower to their front line to ease the burden on Paulo Dybala and Gonzalo Higuain.

The France international is set to leave Madrid at the end of the season, with La Liga rivals Barcelona in pole position to secure his signature.

Antoine Griezmann has become Juventus' No 1 transfer target, according to the Italian media

The Atletico Madrid forward is viewed as one of the biggest talents across European football

The Bianconeri want Griezmann to bolster their attack of Gonzalo Higuain and Paulo Dybala

Juventus were forced back to the drawing board after their 3-0 home defeat to Real Madrid in the Champions League quart-final first leg on Tuesday.

Dybala, who has found himself linked with a move to Atletico, was sent off in the encounter as Cristiano Ronaldo exposed the attacking quality gap between the two sides.

Griezmann is currently targeting Europa League glory as Atletico face Portuguese side Sporting Lisbon in the competition's quarter-finals.

Barcelona appear in pole position for his signature with Manchester United also interested

Griezmann is viewed as one of Europe's brightest talents and will find himself at the centre of a tussle for his signature should he make the move away from Diego Simeone's side.

And while he considers Manchester United midfielder Paul Pogba as one of his closest friends, it would appear Old Trafford is an unlikely destination for the French forward.

According to The Sun, the 27-year-old was one of Jose Mourinho's top summer targets as he looks to build a side capable of challenging soon-to-be champions Manchester City but he has 'snubbed' the chance to head to the Premier League.
