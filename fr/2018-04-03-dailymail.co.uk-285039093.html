Sevilla 'asked Messi to go easy' before he netted late equaliser

  • Lionel Messi netted an 89th-minute equaliser for Barcelona against Sevilla
  • Ernesto Valverde's side came from 2-0 down to maintain their unbeaten season 
  • Messi had been left on the bench but came on with 32 minutes remaining
  • Before the change, Sevilla are believed to have asked him to go easy on them

Published: 05:22 EDT, 3 April 2018 | Updated: 05:26 EDT, 3 April 2018

The Sevilla bench 'asked Lionel Messi to go easy on them' minutes before the Argentina forward came on to rescue a point for Barcelona in Saturday's 2-2 draw in La Liga.

The Catalan giants maintained their unbeaten league season in thrilling fashion on Saturday, when they came from two goals down at Ramon Sanchez Pizjuan Stadium. 

Luis Suarez and Messi both netted in the final few minutes as Ernesto Valverde's side made it 30 matches without a league defeat in this campaign.

Messi had been left on the bench ahead of Barcelona's Champions League quarter-final first leg against Roma on Wednesday.

And before being brought on with 32 minutes to play, the Argentina shared a joke with the Sevilla bench – who are believed to have asked him to go easy on them.

'Eh, tranquilo, eh, (go easy)' one member of the club's staff was filmed saying to the forward as he returned to the Barcelona bench following his warm up.

Messi was left on the bench ahead of Barcelona's Champions League quarter-final with Roma

Valverde's final instructions to the forward were also captured on film during the 2-2 draw

Messi responded with a laugh but there was only one side smiling half an hour later after he netted in the 89th minute to rescue a point for Valverde's side.

The Argentina forward replaced Ousmane Dembele on 58 minutes and his final instructions from Valverde were also captured on film.

'Tell (Philippe Coutinho) to go to the right,' Valverde told his forward before asking him: 'Unless you want to go there?'
