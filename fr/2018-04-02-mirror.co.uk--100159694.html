Chelsea and Tottenham fans clash in violent scenes after London derby

Chelsea and Tottenham fans clashed in violent scenes following the London derby at Stamford Bridge.

Spurs ran out 3-1 winners in their first victory at the champions' home in 28 years before ugly scenes followed.

Police provided a barrier between fans, with the visiting supporters outnumbering the Blues.

Projectiles, including a traffic cone, were launched from both sides before some control was established.

Spurs fans then collectively began to sing in the direction of the outnumbered Chelsea following.

Additional police officer were then summoned with extra distance between the groups of supporters provided.

Spurs fans were in high spirits after Christian Eriksen and Dele Alli's double provided Mauricio Pochettino's side with their historic victory.

The three points leaves Spurs eight points ahead of their rivals in fourth with just seven matches to go.

Both sides have a game in hand on Liverpool, who are two and 10 points ahead of the London sides.

Another meeting could occur this season though, should Spurs overcome Manchester United and Chelsea get past Southampton in the FA Cup semi-finals.
