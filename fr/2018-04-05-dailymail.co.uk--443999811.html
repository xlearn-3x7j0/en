Phil Neville spoke to Man United about attempt to set up women's team

  • Manchester United are waiting to find out if their application is successful
  • Phil Neville: It is only right that there is a women's team at Manchester United 
  • Neville spoke to United chief Ed Woodward about the attempts to set up a team
  • Neville progressed through youth ranks at United and played 386 senior games 
  • England women's boss says coaching is best feeling he's had in his career 

Published: 19:33 EDT, 4 April 2018 | Updated: 20:21 EDT, 4 April 2018

England women’s manager Phil Neville has been speaking to Manchester United chief Ed Woodward about his former club’s attempt at setting up their own women’s team.

Neville, whose Lionesses face Wales in a World Cup qualifier at St Mary’s Stadium tomorrow, feels the Women’s Super League ‘brand’ would benefit significantly from United’s involvement.

‘They are in the bidding process now but it is only right that there is a women’s team at Manchester United,’ Neville said. ‘I spoke to Ed Woodward the other day and he is very positive about it.

Phil Neville has spoken to Manchester United about their attempts to set up a women's team

‘They are the biggest club in the world and in terms of a brand for the WSL it would be a tremendous story. But they have got to look at the other WSL models and match what they have got — the Arsenals, the Man Cities, the Readings.

‘These are established brands and it is not going to be easy for them to get the bid. There are two WSL leagues and they are up against some strong competition with 14 clubs bidding. But the women’s game it would be an unbelievable sign.’

United are waiting to find out if their application is successful and Neville added: ‘You have got to fit certain criteria. You cannot just come in and start playing on a park pitch. You have got to have a certain infrastructure in place.

Neville disclosed that coaching is the best feeling he has had in his career

‘I would have thought that Manchester United would want to do it properly. Ed Woodward told me that the other day and I am sure they will.’

More than 25,000 are expected to watch England and Wales in Southampton tomorrow and Neville says standing on the touchline is the best feeling he has had in his career.

‘The USA game (in the final of the SheBelieves Cup) was the pinnacle,’ Neville said. ‘I was up against the No 1 team and the No 1 coach in the world and that is where I had always wanted to be. It just felt right.

‘I’ve always wanted to be a manager. It was purely being a manager, being in charge, being in that technical area, making substitutions. It was a better feeling than anything I’ve ever experienced.

‘I told the players after the game and I think they were surprised.’
