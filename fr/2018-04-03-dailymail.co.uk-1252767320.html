Jurgen Klopp prepares Liverpool at Melwood ahead of Manch City clash

  • Jurgen Klopp prepares his side ahead of the biggest game of their season against Manchester City
  • Liverpool stars were training at Melwood on Tuesday evening before their Champions League quarter-final
  • The all English-tie means there can only be one English side that progress to the semi-finals

Published: 13:48 EDT, 3 April 2018 | Updated: 14:35 EDT, 3 April 2018

Jurgen Klopp prepared his players for the biggest game of their season so far when they took to the training pitch at Melwood on Tuesday evening.

Liverpool's players have been gearing up for visit of Manchester City in the first-leg of their Champions League quarter-final, which kicks off at 19:45 on Wednesday.

Klopp watched on from the sidelines with assistant manager Zeljko Buvac as they finalised their plans to beat Pep Guardiola's side.

The players were put through their paces at Melwood as they carried out some light-fitness drills on Tuesday evening ahead of the big game at Anfield.

Many of the team looked as if they mean business with serious faces throughout and they know they will need to give everything they have to beat the Premier League leaders.

Klopp will take inspiration from his sides 4-3 win over Manchester City at Anfield earlier in the season, inflicting their only defeat in the league.

Jurgen Klopp told reporters on Tuesday: ‘Manchester City are quite a special team who will be absolutely deserved champions of the Premier League. But it is not about being the best but being perfectly prepared for the next game to be your best. It is about the moment.

‘Hoping for a clean sheet against Man City does not make much sense. It is 100 per cent they will have a shot on target in 95 minutes. You need the balls to use the space. We have to be 100 per cent in all parts, offence and defence.

‘We cannot avoid everything but with our skills we can cause them problems so that makes the game interesting.'
