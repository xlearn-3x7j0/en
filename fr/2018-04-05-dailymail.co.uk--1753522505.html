Russia wants to revive 'grid girl tradition' in Formula One

  • Russia wants to bring grid girls back to Formula One for the Russian Grand Prix
  • Grid girls ended in February after F1 officials said it wasn't 'appropriate' anymore
  • It's was replaced with 'grid kids' which allows youngsters to feature instead
  • Russia's deputy Prime Minister disagrees with the switch labelling it as 'wrong'

Published: 10:18 EDT, 5 April 2018 | Updated: 10:26 EDT, 5 April 2018

Russia wants to bring grid girls back to Formula One, barely two months after the series ended the practice.

Deputy Prime Minister Dmitry Kozak, who oversees the Russian Grand Prix, said: 'If we can reach an agreement we will revive this tradition in September.

According to Russian news agency Interfax, the deputy Prime Minister boasted: 'Moreover, our girls are the most beautiful.'

Russia wants to bring grid girls back to Formula One for the Russian Grand Prix in September

Russia's deputy Prime Minister Dmitry Kozak (R) hopes they can reach an agreement with F1

F1 said in January the practice of using grid girls - paid models in uniform - wasn't 'appropriate or relevant' anymore. 

Their role has since been given to 'grid kids' scheme, young drivers from local motorsport clubs.

Kozak disagrees with the switch, saying: 'It's wrong at races to lead out children, who are frightened of mechanical things.'

The grid kids initiative received a mixed reaction when it was introduced in Melbourne in March.

The 'grid kids' initiative began in Melbourne but it received a mixed reaction

McLaren driver Stoffel Vandoorne said he enjoyed the kids' enthusiasm but overall it was 'okay'.

However, Sir Jackie Stewart, the triple world champion who was in Melbourne, said: 'I saw nothing wrong with having grid girls and nor does my wife. The idea that grid girls put off women drivers is baloney.

'If we had women, the viewing numbers would go up.'
