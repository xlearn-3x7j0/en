Man, 38 accused of kidnapping and assaulting a Utah girl, 17  

Phoenix, Arizona, police charged 38-year-old Raymond Burk with human trafficking of a child and aggravated kidnapping on Monday

An Arizona man who allegedly kidnapped a Utah girl and held her captive referred to her as his 'pet,' according to court documents. 

Raymond Burk, 38, was accused of picking up the 17-year-old teen from her home in St. George, Utah, and holding her hostage for a week in Phoenix, Arizona. 

Burk is believed to have met the alleged victim when she posted in 2016 that she was 'depressed and wanted someone to pick her up,' Q13 Fox reports.  

'The man [Burk] told the child victim that he would take away her depression and she would be happy with him. The man said he would take child victim to his house in Leeds, Utah, and have her home in two days,' the probable cause statement reads. 

And when the teen shared with Burk that she was 17, he allegedly told her that was 'even better.' 

Burk picked the girl up from her father's home on June 2, 2016 and took her to a bank so that she could withdraw money. 

After driving approximately 45 minutes, the girl began to realize that something wasn't quite right about the arrangement. 

'Leeds, Utah, is only about 30 minutes from St. George, Utah,' the statement adds. 'Child victim asked Burk if they were still going to Leeds. Burk replied, "I lied. I live in Phoenix."'

And when the alleged victim asked if she could still come home in two days, Burk was said to have informed her that he planned on keeping her as a 'pet' indefinitely. 

The girl alleges that while they were in Phoenix, she was sexually assaulted multiple times and made to stay against her will. 

Whenever she asked to leave, Burk was said to tie her up to his bed with a rope and assault her. 

Eventually, the girl was able to get a hold of Burk's phone and used social media to send out a call for help. 

But when he learned that police were on to him, Burk allegedly tried to take the girl with him. The girl was able to contact officers and alert them as to what was happening, however. 

The 38-year-old was charged with human trafficking of a child and aggravated kidnapping on Monday.  

His next court appearance is slated for April 25th in Phoenix.
