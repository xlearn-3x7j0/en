San Francisco's 'Hot Cop' sentenced to nine months for hit-and-run

A San Francisco police officer who became known as the 'Hot Cop of the Castro' was sentenced on Wednesday to nine months in county jail for a hit-and-run accident that left two pedestrians injured.  

Christopher Kohrs, 40, was found guilty on March 15 in the November, 29, 2015, crash at an intersection in the North Beach neighborhood.

Police said Kohrs was driving with his brother and a friend in his vehicle when when slammed into two men as they crossed a street.

Kohrs’ attorney argued during the trial that the off-duty officer fled the scene because he felt threatened by an aggressive crowd that recognized him from past news coverage.

'They said, "It's the Hot Cop! F— the police!" defense lawyer Peter Furst told the court the crowd began chanting. 

Kohrs turned himself in about eight hours after the crash, frustrating prosecutors who said they could not collect evidence to determine if he was under the influence when he was behind the wheel. 

On Wednesday, the court heard from victim Victor Perez, who described his physical injuries and severe depression, reported KGO-TV.

At one point, he addressed Kohrs directly, telling the cop: 'I know you have the ability to change.'

When given a chance to speak, the officer said: ‘I made a split second decision. It was the wrong decision. I let fear overcome my mind ... I'm very sorry.’

His lawyer said he was pleased Kohrs won't be going to state prison and revealed that he will likely serve about half of his jail sentence, followed by three years of probation.

At the conclusion of the sentencing hearing, the judge had Kohrs remanded into custody without setting bail, pointing to the fact that he had fled thee the scene of the crash three years ago., reported CBS San Francisco. 

Kohrs had been driving his orange 2009 Dodge Charger and had his brother, Nathan, sitting in the back seat while pal, 45-year-old Norman Banks, sat in the front.

After leaving the Fort One Bar and Lounge at approximately 2am in the morning. Kohrs hit two men as they crossed the street, injuring both severely.

And instead of stopping to see if victims Victor Perez and Frank Vilches were fine, Kohrs ditched his car and friends and ran away, on foot.

Prosecutors added that he did technically have the green light but the grievance was that he took off.

Vilches suffered permanent disabling injuries and had medical bills well above $600,000, his lawyer attested. 

Kohrs had worked at the Park Station of the department for seven years. He has been on unpaid leave from the time of the incident.  

He settled a civil lawsuit filed by on the crash victims a year ago.
