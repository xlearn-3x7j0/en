Pep Guardiola make big promise ahead of Liverpool Champions League clash

Pep Guardiola has promised to deliver a Champions League classic - and claimed he would “scare” his players if he asked them to change their attacking style.

Guardiola admitted their tactics are “perfect” for Liverpool but is ready to serve up another thriller at Anfield and has no doubts that his Manchester City team is ready for the big occasion.

City’s last win at Anfield was in May 2003 and since then Liverpool have won 11 of their 17 encounters including the 4-3 victory in January which ended Guardiola’s unbeaten charge towards the Premier League title.

But rather than play safe in a more traditional European tie rather than an all-English affair, Guardiola made it perfectly clear that his team only knows how to play one way.

City boss Guardiola said: “If I said we are going to be different the players would look at me and say: ‘this guy is scary!’ It would be that we don’t trust in ourselves. It would be a big mistake.

“What I admire the most about the important teams - we were not able to do it in this situation last season. In the bad moments they are calm.

“Madrid, Barcelona, when the opponents are attacking they look like they are taking a cup of coffee because their chance is coming. When it comes we are not going to miss. That’s the big difference.

“Our strikers are 20 or 21 and this is only the second time they’ve played in this competition and maybe need more time. It’s a dream to be in the best eight teams of Europe and trying to compete to be in the last four.

“Of course I consider the value of Liverpool. I know their qualities, and I’m concerned of what they do. I’m not so arrogant to know what they do.

“But if you’re thinking we are going to defend 90 minutes and counter attack - that is not going to happen. If we defend 90 minutes it’s because Liverpool are unstoppable. But why should it change when we we are going well so far?

“I know for the way we play it’s perfect for Liverpool. They attack the space like any other team in the world - especially Mane, Firmino, Salah, they are so good. I know that.

“We are going to try to do something. I feel the best way to try to win is the way we do it since I’ve been here.”

Jurgen Klopp has an impressive head-to-head managerial record against Guardiola, with six wins, five defeats and a draw.

The Anfield atmosphere will also be a major test for City but, despite Liverpool fans promising to give the visitors a red-hot reception, it is not something unusual for Guardiola.

Guardiola added: “In the quarter finals, it is a mountain. It depends if you are able to overcome this situation knowing the opponent is one of the toughest in the world.

“In Barcelona and Madrid for important games, La Liga or semi-finals, there the police spread out the people. We cannot be too much in contact with the fans, but here the streets are narrow. I hope it’s going to be well and Liverpool fans can show what their history is.

“We’ve had one Champions League semi-final, they’ve won five. You have to feel you can do it, if you don’t it’s no change.

“I said to the players. People think how tough it is, but in Barcelona it’s the same - Barcelona, Bayern Munich, Madrid, Sevilla.

“They can harm us but we can harm them again. We are close to the Premier League title but in one or two games in this competition, anything can happen. If we face any top teams in this competition. Liverpool have quality, history but also they have great players as well.”
