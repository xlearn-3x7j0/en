Spotify investors take their profits after blockbuster listing

Published: 16:55 EDT, 4 April 2018 | Updated: 19:14 EDT, 4 April 2018

Slip: Spotify dropped 5 per cent in afternoon trading

Shares in music service Spotify have slipped after its blockbuster listing on the stock market.

The business, which lets users listen to 35m songs online, dropped 5 per cent as investors tried to decide its worth.

Spotify went public in New York for £21billion through a direct listing, which did not see any new stock sold to raise money. 

The fall means shares have dipped 15 per cent since the float on Tuesday, valuing it at £17.8billion. Bosses had warned that trading was likely to be volatile.

With no new stock issued for a set price, unlike normal initial public offerings, markets have less information than normal on which to decide what the existing shares are worth. 

The company is still valued at around £1.1billion.

Billionaire 35-year-old boss Daniel Ek has said he was not worried about any short-term fluctuations.
