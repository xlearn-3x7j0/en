Arsenal 'in pole position' to sign Bayer Leverkusen starlet

Arsenal are leading the race for Kai Havertz with Liverpool also interested.

The Bayer Leverkusen starlet, 18, has scored seven goals in 51 appearances for the Bundesliga outfit.

Southampton are also tracking his progress, report the Sun, though the Gunners remain in pole position.

Havertz is known to admire Mesut Ozil, which could factor into his decision to choose the Emirates as his next destination if a fee can be agreed.

Scouts watched the youngster in Germany under-19s' 5-2 defeat to Norway, with a bid set to be tabled this summer.

Calum Chambers insists Premier League remains important for Arsenal despite Europa League priority

Calum Chambers insists Arsenal won’t take their eye off the ball in the league, writes Mirror Football's Adrian Kajumba.

The Gunners have little to play for in the Premier League after falling well off the top four pace, 13 points behind fierce rivals Tottenham.

Their priority has now become winning the Europa League with that competition offering Arsene Wenger’s men their only realistic route back into the Champions League.

After seeing off Stoke on Sunday to stay sixth Arsenal face CSKA Moscow in a quarter-final first leg tie on Thursday and defender Chambers said: “We want to finish as high as we can in the league so we are going to keep fighting until the end of the season with that.

“We want to win every game so at the moment we are going to prepare for every game and try to get the results we need from each game.

“You could let your mind drift to the Europa League.

“But we are a professional side and do want to finish as high as we can in the league so these league games are still important for us.

“We got the job done today so now we can put that to one side and focus on Thursday.”

Max Allegri puts Arsenal and Chelsea on alert by claiming he will manage abroad when he leaves Juventus

Max Allegri has put Arsenal and Chelsea on alert after claiming he will manage abroad when he quits Juventus, writes Mirror Football's Aaron Flanagan.

The Italian is looking good to win his fourth Serie A title in charge of the Old Lady, with his side sitting four points clear of Napoli.

Allegri, 50, has been constantly mooted as a potential successor to either Arsene Wenger or Antonio Conte.

Wenger could walk away from Arsenal at the end of the season, with club chiefs already considering alternative options following a disastrous campaign.

Chelsea look set to miss out on the top four, which would inevitably mark the end of Conte's time at Stamford Bridge.

And Allegri could be the man to fill their shoes.

"Certainly I will go abroad," he told the Telegraph . "In Italy, finished."
