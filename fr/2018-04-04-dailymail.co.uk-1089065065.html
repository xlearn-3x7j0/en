Liverpool host Manchester City in the quarter finals of the Champions League on Wednesday night at Anfield.

Both sides come into the fixture on the back of victories in the Premier League and will be aiming to gain the upper hand ahead of the second leg. 

City last reached the last eight of the Champions League in 2016 before making it to the semi-finals, while the Reds haven't got to this stage since 2009.

The atmosphere is building just nicely indeed outside Anfield

This is a huge game in the history of both clubs and Liverpool's fans have done everything possible to give their players a lift ahead of kick-off.

Hundreds of them have lined the streets in anticipation of the team bus arriving at Anfield.

There is plenty riding on this one tonight. Not too long before the action starts.

Why Liverpool may have the edge ahead of this evening?

There is no question that Manchester City being without Sergio Aguero is major blow.

Aguero combined goals and assists total for City this season in all competitions is 37. Only Liverpool's Mohamed Salah has done better on 47. 

Salah is the most deadly player of each of the two teams and his sensational form is only likely to continue against a City defence that is often suspect when defending pace.
