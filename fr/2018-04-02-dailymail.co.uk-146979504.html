Real Madrid squad and Florentino Perez head to Italy for Juventus game

  • Real Madrid take on Juventus on Tuesday in the first leg of the last-eight tie
  • A host of Madrid players posted pictures on Instagram as they left Spain
  • The players made their way to the plane all wearing matching Hugo Boss suits 

Published: 06:16 EDT, 2 April 2018 | Updated: 06:19 EDT, 2 April 2018

The Real Madrid squad and President Florentino Perez travelled to Italy on Monday morning ahead of their mouth-watering quarter-final clash against Juventus in the Champions League.

Madrid take on the Turin side in the first leg in what is a repeat of the 2017 Champions League final on Tuesday night. 

A host of Madrid players, who all wore matching navy Hugo Boss suits, posted pictures on Instagram of them departing Spain.

Sergio Ramos posted a photo on Instagram alongside President Florentino Perez before flight

Isco, who scored a hat-trick for Spain last Tuesday, looked laid back with his headphones in

Defender Raphael Varane also posted a photo as the Real Madrid squad departed for Italy

Sergio Ramos looked relaxed with his headphones in as he made his way to the plane and alongside him was Perez. 

Isco, who scored a hat-trick against Argentina in Spain's 6-1 victory last Tuesday, looked laid back also with his headphones in. 

Defender Raphael Varane posted a photo on the social media platform heading for the plane and behind him was Gareth Bale.

On Madrid's official Instagram account there was a photo of Isco and Keylor Navas on the story

The club's official Twitter account also posted a picture of Varane and Karim Benzema on the plane with the caption: 'We're on our way to Turin!'

It is a big nine days coming up for Zinedine Zidane's side as after Tuesday's match, Madrid return to league action with a derby against Atletico Madrid on Sunday, before the return leg at the Santiago Bernabeu on April 11.

But Madrid come into the Champions League tie in fine form after winning all of their games in March and Madrid also came out on top in the final last year against the Serie A side 4-1. 

Madrid had announced their 24-man squad for the last-eight match earlier in the day.
