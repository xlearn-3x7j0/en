Rhode Island DENIES 'offering Rick Pitino $2million annually'

The University of Rhode Island is requesting that an internet report claiming the school nearly hired embattled Hall of Fame coach Rick Pitino for $2 million a season be taken down.

According to GoLocalProv.com, Pitino, the fired former Louisville coach, was close to signing a new deal with the Rams until the school President weighed in and killed the verbal agreement. 

'On Monday, April 2, 2018, GoLocalProv posted a story claiming, "President Dooley kills hiring of Pitino at URI,"' read the URI statement. 'This story is false. University President David M. Dooley and Athletic Director Thorr Bjorn have not spoken with Rick Pitino. Athletic Director Bjorn never presented an offer to President Dooley related to Rick Pitino. 

'The University takes journalistic integrity very seriously and denies the validity of this story in its entirety,' the statement continued. 'The University has requested the story be removed from the GoLocalProv website.'

Pitino, who was forced out at Louisville after the program was recently implicated in multiple scandals, would have replaced now-former Rams coach Dan Hurley, who recently signed at the University of Connecticut.

According to the report, athletic director Thorr Bjorn approached URI President David Dooley about the deal, but Pitino's connection to an ongoing FBI probe into corruption within college basketball recruiting circles became a sticking point.   

The 65-year-old Pitino, who took rival Providence College to the Final Four in 1987, has not been charged with any crime in the joint FBI-Justice Department investigation. However, he allegedly had knowledge and directed payments to potential recruits, according to an indictment that was unsealed on November 8.

Brian Bowen's family was allegedly paid in exchange for his commitment to Louisville 

According to that indictment, former sports agency representative Christian Dawkins claimed that he requested Pitino call Adidas executive James Gatto to ask for money in order to bribe the family of top recruit Brian Bowen. Pitino allegedly agreed.

In an affidavit presented by his attorney, Pitino claimed he 'had no part - active, passive or through willful ignorance' in the alleged bribery scheme.

Publicly, Pitino claimed to be pleasantly surprised when Bowen shocked the college basketball world in June by choosing Louisville over Arizona, which many believed was his preferred destination all along.

Pitino's Cardinals were also stripped of their 2013 national championship title as well their 2012 Final Four appearance after the NCAA found that a member of the coaching staff provided strippers and prostitutes for players and recruits between 2011 and 2015.

Louisville is the first Division I men's basketball program to vacate a national title during the Final Four era, according to ESPN.
