Shoppers break glass door to get to cut-price Victoria's Secret bras

  • Chaotic scenes were captured on camera in Kota Kinabalu, north east Malaysia
  • The popular lingerie shop's sale saw bras reduced from £14.53 to just £5.33  
  • Footage shows staff trying to close safety barrier but shoppers break through it

Published: 16:09 EDT, 4 April 2018 | Updated: 19:13 EDT, 4 April 2018

This is the shocking moment frantic shoppers break down a glass security door in a bid to get their hands on cut-price Victoria's Secret Lingerie. 

Smartphone footage from the underwear store in Kota Kinabalu, north east Malaysia shows staff desperately trying to shut the door as they try to control overwhelming crowds. 

But despite their best efforts the crazed customers manage to break down the safety screen and storm into the shop. 

The Asian Victoria's Secret outlet was offering a special discount on their Fresh Escape Mist Collection, pricing it at 29 MYR (£5.33) instead of the usual 79 MYR (£14.53).   

Famous Victoria's Secret models include sisters Bella and Gigi Hadid, Alessandra Ambrosio and Adriana Lima.  

The shop is seen overrun with clients as they dash from display to display trying to get their hands on the reduced stock.   

After the clip went viral online, commentators have slammed the shoppers, accusing them of acting in an uncivilised and pushy manner in their determination to take advantage of a bargain.

One posting on social media blamed store managers for failing to anticipate the high demand and coming up with a strategy to deal with it.

'Pity the women who are actually 100% supporting your product. They shouldn't be treated like this,' she said.

Long lines formed outside the store before the promotion began at 10am that day. But it was reportedly forced to close ahead of schedule due to such high demand. 

MailOnline has contacted Victoria's Secret for comment.
